### Pipeline Mechanics

- **Browse Feed:** Personalised recommendations on homepage. Driven by viewer watch history, topic affinity, and session signals. New channels enter via topic match, not subscriber count.
- **Search:** Keyword matching + engagement signals. Title, description, tags all indexed. Watch time after click is the strongest ranking signal for search results.
- **Suggested:** Sidebar and end-screen recommendations. Driven by co-watch patterns (viewers who watched X also watched Y). Most important discovery surface for small channels.

### Longform-Specific

- Videos over 8 minutes qualify for mid-roll ads — YouTube actively promotes content that enables this
- AVD matters more than total watch time for algorithm ranking
- Returning viewers signal high satisfaction — the algorithm weights repeat viewership heavily
- First 30 seconds determine whether YouTube continues promoting a video
- Session continuation (viewer watches another video after yours) is a strong positive signal

### Satisfaction Signals

- Likes, shares, and comments relative to views
- Survey responses (YouTube occasionally surveys viewers about satisfaction)
- Low dislike-to-view ratio
- Viewers adding video to playlists

### Reported Thresholds

- **AVD:** No fixed percentage — relative to similar content in the topic niche. Aim for top quartile of similar-length videos in your topic.
- **CTR:** 4-10% is typical. Above 10% is exceptional. Below 2% signals thumbnail/title mismatch. CTR naturally decreases as reach expands beyond core audience.

### Small Channel Notes

- YouTube tests new channels via small audience samples. If the test group shows strong CTR + AVD, the algorithm expands reach. A 462-sub channel can reach millions if per-impression metrics are strong. Focus on CTR (thumbnail + title) and first-30-second retention.
