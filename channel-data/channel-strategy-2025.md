## 🎬 CONTENT STRATEGY

### Video Format (Proven):
**Length:** 8-12 minutes (sweet spot for retention)
**Structure:**
1. **Hook (0:00-0:15):** Modern political figure using historical myth
2. **Payoff (0:45):** Primary document contradicting myth
3. **Pattern (2:00-8:00):** 3 interconnected claims with evidence
4. **Modern Stakes (8:00-10:00):** Why accurate history matters NOW
5. **CTA (10:00-12:00):** Sources in description, subscribe

**Retention Optimization:**
- First payoff at 0:45 (prevents 1:11 drop-off)
- Micro-hooks every 2 minutes
- Visual variety every 30-45 seconds
- "But it gets worse" transitions
- Because/therefore connectors

**Visual Strategy:**
- 60-70% talking head (you are the authority)
- 15-20% maps (evidence)
- 10-15% primary documents (smoking guns)
- 5% modern footage (news hooks)

### Topic Selection Framework:

**Formula That Works:**
"[Colonial/Historical Decision] from [X] years ago is still [causing deaths/conflicts/disputes] today"

**High-Performance Topics:**
1. **Territorial Disputes** (Best Overall)
   - Modern news hook + historical evidence
   - Maps + primary sources
   - Clear geopolitical relevance

2. **Fact-Checking Politicians** (High CTR)
   - Timely (election seasons, current events)
   - Controversial but evidence-based
   - Strong subscriber conversion

3. **Colonial History Myths** (Core Niche)
   - Underserved on YouTube
   - Primary source verification
   - Modern consequence connection

4. **Archaeological Evidence** (Strong CTR)
   - DNA studies, excavations
   - Challenges widely-held beliefs
   - Scientific credibility

### Upload Strategy:
- **Frequency:** 2-3 videos per week (when active)
- **Best Days:** Tuesday-Thursday
- **Best Time:** 2-4 PM EST
- **Avoid:** Friday-Sunday (lower engagement)

### Shorts Strategy (Revised):
- **Current Approach:** Standalone shorts = low conversion
- **Better Approach:** Clips from long-form driving to main content
- Repurpose 5-10 segments per long-form video
- Use as discovery traffic, not primary content

## 📈 OPTIMIZATION OPPORTUNITIES

### Thumbnail Strategy:
**Current:** Varies in effectiveness
**Recommended:**
- Show primary document image
- Text: "[POLITICIAN] vs. THE DOCUMENTS"
- Face + document split screen
- High contrast, readable at small size
- Test A/B with VidIQ

### Title Optimization:
**Winners:**
- "JD Vance's Colonial Myths | I Checked the Documents" (11.21% CTR)
- "Aztec Child Sacrifice: What DNA Actually Shows" (8% CTR)

**Formula:**
"[BOLD CLAIM/FIGURE] | [EVIDENCE PROMISE]"

**Avoid:**
- Generic "The History of X"
- Clickbait without payoff
- Overly academic phrasing

### Description SEO:
**Essential Elements:**
- First 2 sentences hook (visible before "show more")
- Timestamps for key evidence (0:45, 2:30, etc.)
- Complete source list (builds authority)
- Related video links
- Keywords naturally integrated

### Comment Engagement:
**Strategy:**
- Lead with claim, then correction
- Cite 2-4 credible sources
- Respectful on sensitive topics
- Default CTA: "Full breakdown in video. Sources in description."
- See youtube-comment-response-guide.md

## ⚠️ RISK MANAGEMENT

### Platform Challenges:
**Reality:** Educational history content faces systematic hostility
- Automated demonetization (30-70% of videos)
- False copyright claims
- Appeals take 14+ days
- Algorithm can't distinguish education from glorification

**Defensive Strategy:**
1. **Revenue Diversification (Essential):**
   - Don't rely solely on YouTube ads
   - Build Patreon/membership EARLY
   - Consider sponsorships (educational tools)
   - Merchandise potential

2. **Self-Censorship (Necessary Evil):**
   - Use illustrations instead of graphic photos
   - Add verbose disclaimers
   - Avoid certain imagery (even in education)
   - Frame controversies carefully

3. **Community Building:**
   - Discord/owned platforms
   - Email list for loyal fans
   - Direct communication channel
   - Protection against algorithm changes

### Content Approach:
**Frame as "What We Didn't Learn" NOT "You've Been Lied To"**
- Soft framing converts better long-term
- Aggressive framing generates clicks but damages trust
- Lead with curiosity, not accusation
- Exception: Genuinely egregious myths warrant stronger language

**Balance Controversy:**
- Mix lower-risk content (ancient civilizations, medieval life)
- Use revenue to subsidize important but risky topics
- Don't compromise core mission
- Build authority before tackling most controversial
