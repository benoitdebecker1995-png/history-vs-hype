## Five myths circulating online with debunk potential

**MYTH 1: "The 1899 arbitration was a Russian-British conspiracy" (HIGHEST circulation)**

**Where it's spreading:** Official Venezuelan government websites, state media, Maduro's public statements, Venezuelan educational curriculum since the 1950s. Washington Post confirms: "Everyone agrees on this... Those in the government and those in the opposition studied with the same books."

**Why it's false:** Severo Mallet-Prevost waited 45 years to make allegations (memorandum written 1944, published posthumously 1949), provided zero evidence beyond speculation, yet Venezuela fully accepted and honored the award for 62 years (1899-1962). In 1941, Venezuelan Foreign Minister stated the frontier was "well defined and a closed issue." The "fraud" claim emerged only when Britain began independence negotiations in 1962. Most damning: Mallet-Prevost received the Venezuelan Order of the Liberator in 1944 for his work—decades after supposedly witnessing fraud.

**Debunk impact:** Excellent visual opportunity—show timeline of 62 years of Venezuelan acceptance versus sudden "discovery" of fraud when oil and independence made territory valuable.

**MYTH 2: "Border clashes are happening between Venezuelan and Guyanese forces" (1M+ views)**

**Where it's spreading:** X/Twitter blue-checked account "Clash Report" (334K followers) posted video claiming fighting on Venezuela-Guyana border, received 1M+ views before community note added.

**Why it's false:** The footage shows fighting between Colombian guerrillas (ELN and FARC) vs. Clan del Golfo drug cartel in Briceno, Antioquia, Colombia. Originally reported by Noticias Caracol on November 22, 2023. Fighters wear ELN armbands, speak with Colombian accents, in mismatched uniforms. Geolocation confirms Colombia location.

**Debunk impact:** Perfect example of recycled conflict footage phenomenon—can show original Colombian broadcast versus false viral posts.

**MYTH 3: "Venezuelans are crossing rivers to vote in Guyana's Essequibo"**

**Where it's spreading:** Venezuelan Vice President Delcy Rodríguez (official government account) circulated video claiming people crossing Essequibo River to participate in Venezuelan election process.

**Why it's false:** Footage actually shows passengers boarding boat in Georgetown (Guyana's capital) en route to Vreed-en-Hoop, hundreds of miles from disputed territory. Stabroek Market clearly visible in background (iconic Georgetown landmark). Guyana Chronicle on-the-ground reporting confirmed zero Venezuelan electoral activity in Region One.

**Debunk impact:** Government disinformation at highest levels—can identify Stabroek Market in video and compare Georgetown location to actual border.

**MYTH 4: "The Geneva Agreement prohibits ICJ jurisdiction"**

**Where it's spreading:** Maduro's letters to UN Secretary-General (March 2025), Venezuelan Ministry of Foreign Affairs, diplomatic channels.

**Why it's false:** ICJ explicitly ruled on this December 18, 2020, rejecting Venezuela's jurisdictional objections. Geneva Agreement Article IV(2) provides for UN Secretary-General to choose means of settlement—UN Secretary-General António Guterres chose ICJ on January 30, 2018. The Agreement lists judicial settlement as one of the means.

**Debunk impact:** Can quote actual Geneva Agreement text versus Venezuelan misrepresentation—classic "treaty shopping."

**MYTH 5: "The referendum showed overwhelming Venezuelan support"**

**Where it's spreading:** Venezuelan National Electoral Council claimed 95-98% support with 10.5 million voters participating in December 3, 2023 referendum.

**Why it's false:** Multiple international observers reported near-empty polling stations. UK Guardian: "Few voters could be seen at voting centres." Opposition estimated 10% actual turnout versus official 51% claim. DFRLab documented coordinated bot activity amplifying referendum hashtags. Photos of empty polling stations contradict government claims.

**Debunk impact:** Visual evidence available—empty polling station photos versus government claims, bot network analysis showing artificial amplification.
