Add detection heuristics after the format template:

**How to Identify Cut Candidates:**

Priority 1 (Easy Cuts):
- Repeated explanations (saying same thing twice)
- Filler phrases ("So basically...", "What I mean is...")
- Unnecessary context that doesn't advance argument
- Tangents that don't connect to main thesis
- Pauses longer than 2 seconds

Priority 2 (Moderate Cuts):
- Examples beyond first two (diminishing returns)
- Historical context that could be summarized
- Quotes that could be shortened without losing meaning

Priority 3 (Hard Cuts):
- Sections that feel like "darlings" (author loves them, audience doesn't need them)
- Secondary arguments that weaken focus
- Evidence stacking beyond proof point

DO NOT CUT:
- Opening hook (first 60 seconds)
- Thesis statements and reframes
- Primary source evidence
- Pattern interrupts every 90-120 sec
- Call to action

This gives Claude concrete criteria for categorization rather than just format.
