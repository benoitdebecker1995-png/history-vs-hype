**Gathered:** 2026-01-23
**Status:** Ready for planning

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Systematic tracking of what works for top history creators — capturing techniques and identifying underserved topics/angles. This phase delivers:
- COMP-01: Technique tracking (document what works for top history creators)
- COMP-02: Gap identification (find underserved topics/angles in the niche)

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Creator Scope
- **Two tiers of creators:**
  - Similar size (1K-50K subs): Analyze for breakout videos and replicable patterns
  - Large channels (500K+): Study what already works at scale
- **Starting list:** 5 style models (Kraut, Alex O'Connor, Knowing Better, Shaun, Fall of Civilizations) + RealLifeLore/Wendover + CaspianReport
- **No cap on total creators tracked** — expand organically as relevant channels are discovered
- **Active discovery is part of the system** — research and identify best creators to learn from (editing, scriptwriting, data presentation)
- **Discovery signals:** Outlier videos, high retention, fast growth rate — any exceptional performance indicator
- **Non-history channels:** Include when techniques are relevant and applicable to this channel

### Technique Organization
- **Primary lookup method:** By use case ("I need a strong opening")
- **Categories:** Script phases — opening hooks, building arguments, presenting evidence, closing
- **Each technique includes:** Transcript excerpt showing the actual words used
- **Effectiveness rating:** Track which techniques worked when tried
- **Measurement:** Retention data when available + personal assessment always (both)

### Gap Identification Criteria
- **"Underserved" means any of:**
  - Low competition (few videos exist)
  - Missing angle (videos exist but miss primary-source / myth-busting approach)
  - Poor quality (existing videos are shallow, inaccurate, or outdated)
- **Channel DNA filter:** Prioritize history-first, myth-busting, primary-source topics — but note interesting exceptions
- **Discovery sources:** All available — competitor analysis, search data, comment mining
- **Prioritization:** Composite score combining demand + competition + channel fit
- **Modern hooks:** Note when present (bonus), but don't require them

### Maintenance Cadence
- **Gap analysis refresh:** Quick check before each video when selecting next topic
- **Technique tracking:** Integrated into workflow
  - Capture: During research phase, note techniques competitors used on similar topics
  - Evaluate: After publishing, assess how techniques performed
- **Creator monitoring:** Claude's discretion on frequency

### Claude's Discretion
- Monitoring frequency for tracked creators
- Technique freshness management (when to surface untried techniques)
- Specific thresholds for outlier/growth detection
- Implementation of composite scoring formula

</decisions>

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- "Best creators to follow if I want to be successful on YouTube in my niche" — user wants active research to discover who to learn from, not just tracking a static list
- Transcript excerpts preferred over timestamps — actual words used are more valuable than video links
- Effectiveness tracking creates a learning loop — this isn't just documentation, it's an improvement system
