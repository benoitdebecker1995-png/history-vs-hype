### **MYTH 1: "FRANCE BROUGHT CIVILIZATION" (2:30 - 4:45)**
[VO with historical images]

Let's start with the development myth. The idea that French colonial presence—and its continuation through institutions like the CFA franc—has benefited African countries economically.

**[Graphics: Colonial-era "civilizing mission" propaganda images]**

This narrative is so deeply embedded that even today, defenders of French policy argue that the CFA franc provides monetary stability and that French institutions prevent economic chaos.

**[ON CAMERA - slight pause for emphasis]**

But here's what they don't tell you about what happened when an African country actually tried to choose genuine independence.

**[VO with map focusing on Guinea]**

1958. Charles de Gaulle offers African colonies a choice: partnership with France or immediate independence. Only one country votes "Non"—Guinea, under Sékou Touré.

**[Graphics: Voting results showing Guinea's rejection]**

France's response revealed the true nature of their "civilizing mission." First, they stripped Guinea of everything—telephones, light bulbs, even medical supplies. But that wasn't enough.

**[Document close-up visual effect]**

French intelligence launched something called "Operation Persil"—a secret program designed to destroy Guinea's economy from within.

**[Graphics: Printing press imagery, counterfeit currency]**

According to economists Fanny Pigeaud and Ndongo Samba Sylla, who documented this operation using admissions from French officials: "France used the printing presses of the SDECE"—that's French intelligence—"to create false Guinean banknotes, which were then poured en masse into the country."

**[Pause for impact]**

"The result was the collapse of the Guinean economy."

**[Economic decline graphics]**

The French official quoted in their research boasted: "The operation was a total success: Guinea's economy, already very weak, never fully recovered."

**[ON CAMERA]**

Let me be clear about what this means. When Guinea chose independence, France didn't just withdraw aid—they engaged in deliberate economic warfare. They flooded the country with counterfeit money to destroy its currency and economy.

**[Brief pause]**

This is France's "civilization"—punishing any African nation that dared choose genuine sovereignty.

**[Military hardware montage]**

France deployed overwhelming force—5,100 troops, fighter jets, drones, the latest military technology. They had every advantage you could imagine.

**[ON CAMERA]**

So what happened after nearly a decade of French military presence in the world's supposed terrorism hotspot?

**[Graphics: Before/after violence statistics]**

According to researchers from the UK's Centre for European and International Studies, the intervention was "nothing other than failure." Quote: "Attacks perpetrated by these groups have increased substantially, leading to tens of thousands of fatalities."

**[Map showing regional spread]**

Not only did the violence continue—it spread. "Militants have spread to neighbouring countries and now threaten West African coastal states."

**[Coup announcement footage]**

And rather than strengthening democracy, "inside Mali, the military launched two coups and supplanted the former democratic government."

**[VO with protest footage]**

But here's the most damning part. The Malian Prime Minister, representing the current military government, justified their coup by pointing to this exact failure: "Hundreds and hundreds of Malians have been massacred over the years despite the presence of over 50,000 soldiers"—including French forces.

**[ON CAMERA]**

Think about that irony. France intervened with overwhelming military superiority to fight terrorism in what's now the world's deadliest terrorist region. After a decade of presence, terrorist violence increased, democracy collapsed, and the military leaders who overthrew the government explicitly cited French failure as their justification.

**[Graphics: Resource allocation vs. outcomes comparison]**

This isn't just tactical failure—it's strategic defeat on every measurable level.

**[Graphics: Coordinated actions chart]**

**[ON CAMERA]**

This isn't random instability. It's coordinated resistance to a specific target.

**[VO with Alliance of Sahel States imagery]**
