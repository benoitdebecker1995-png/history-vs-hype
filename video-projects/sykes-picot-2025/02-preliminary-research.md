## 1. SMOKING GUN EVIDENCE (Primary Sources)

### A. THE SYKES-PICOT AGREEMENT (May 1916)

**Source:** Yale Avalon Project (https://avalon.law.yale.edu/20th_century/sykes.asp)

**Territorial Zones Proposed:**
- **Blue Area** (France): Cilicia, coastal Syria, Lebanon, Galilee - "direct or indirect administration"
- **Red Area** (Britain): Southern Mesopotamia - equivalent control to France's blue area
- **Area A** (French influence): Syrian hinterland - "independent Arab state or confederation" under French priority
- **Area B** (British influence): Territory east of Jordan River, Negev - Arab state under British priority
- **Brown Area** (International): Sanjak of Jerusalem region - "international administration"
- **Russia:** Armenian provinces (Erzurum, Trebizond, Van, Bitlis)

**Critical Detail:** Agreement states "the annexed map" repeatedly - but the map is NOT included in many archive versions, creating confusion about exact boundaries.

**Limitations in Agreement:**
- Baghdad railway "shall not be extended southwards beyond Mosul" (Area A)
- "Nor northwards beyond Samarra" (Area B)

### B. EVIDENCE OF PRE-COLONIAL IRAQ & SYRIA

**Source:** Jadaliyya - "Lines Drawn on an Empty Map" (https://www.jadaliyya.com/Details/32140)

**Key Finding:** 1893 Ottoman map shows:
- Three administrative provinces: Basra, Baghdad, Mosul
- Label "al-`Iraq al-`Arabi (Arab Iraq)" stretching across Basra and Baghdad
- 19th century Ottomans used "Iraq" to designate a geographical region

**Implication:** Iraq existed as a recognized geographical/administrative concept BEFORE 1916 Sykes-Picot.

### C. ACTUAL BORDER FINALIZATION DATES

**Iraq Borders:**
- 1922: Lines "start to look something like present-day Iraq"
- **June 5, 1926:** Turkey-Iraq Frontier Treaty - Mosul's inclusion finalized by League of Nations
- **1932:** Iraq-Syria northern border agreement (6-year negotiation process)
- 1930s+: Continued border conflicts with Iran
- **October 3, 1932:** British Mandate ends, Iraq joins League of Nations

**Syria-Palestine Border:**
- December 23, 1920: Franco-British Convention (broad terms)
- June 1, 1921: Paulet-Newcombe Commission begins work
- February 3, 1922: Final report signed
- **March 7, 1923:** British/French governments approve - formal agreement finalized

**Palestine-Transjordan Separation:**
- March 17, 1921: Cairo Conference - Churchill decides to separate Transjordan
- March 31, 1921: Article 25 of mandate approved - Jordan River as border
- April 1921: Abdullah appointed Emir of Transjordan
- June 3, 1922: Churchill White Paper
- **August 12, 1922:** League of Nations approves Transjordan exclusion from Jewish settlement provisions

### D. CONTRADICTORY BRITISH COMMITMENTS

**COMMITMENT 1: Hussein-McMahon Correspondence (July 1915 - March 1916)**
- 10 letters exchanged
- British promise: Arab independence in vaguely-defined territory
- To whom: Sharif Hussein of Mecca
- In exchange for: Arab Revolt against Ottomans
- Ambiguity: What exactly was included? Palestine? Syria?

**COMMITMENT 2: Sykes-Picot (May 1916)**
- British-French zones of influence
- Contradicts: Hussein-McMahon by dividing Arab territory between European powers
- Secret agreement (not revealed to Arabs until 1917)

**COMMITMENT 3: Balfour Declaration (November 2, 1917)**
- Promise: "National home for Jewish people" in Palestine
- Contradicts: BOTH Hussein-McMahon AND Sykes-Picot
- Third party claim to same territory

**RESOLUTION ATTEMPT: San Remo Conference (April 19-26, 1920)**
- Allocated mandates: Britain (Palestine & Iraq), France (Syria & Lebanon)
- Used Sykes-Picot as framework but modified significantly
- Boundaries "to be determined by Principal Allied Powers" - not final

### E. TREATY OF SÈVRES (1920) - FAILED ATTEMPT & LOCAL RESISTANCE

**CRITICAL CLARIFICATION:** Sèvres was **signed but NEVER RATIFIED** - its failure actually **STRENGTHENS** the thesis.

**Source:** Wikipedia, Treaty of Sèvres

**What Happened:**
- **August 10, 1920:** Treaty of Sèvres signed at Sèvres, France
- Attempted to formalize the partition of Ottoman Empire
- Included harsh terms for Turkey (loss of Anatolia, Armenia, Kurdistan)
- **Turkey REJECTED it** - refused to ratify

**Turkish War of Independence (1919-1923):**
- Mustafa Kemal Atatürk led nationalist resistance
- Turkish forces defeated Greek, Armenian, and French forces
- **FORCED complete renegotiation** of the settlement

**Treaty of Lausanne (July 24, 1923):**
- **REPLACED Sèvres entirely**
- Recognized modern Turkish borders
- Turkey regained significant territory
- Completely different settlement than Sèvres proposed

**Why This Matters for Your Video:**

1. **Shows Process Was Contested:** European powers TRIED to impose borders (Sèvres) but COULDN'T
2. **Demonstrates Local Agency:** Turkish resistance successfully changed the entire settlement
3. **Proves 15-Year Timeline:** From Sykes-Picot 1916 → Sèvres 1920 (failed) → Lausanne 1923 → borders finalized 1926-1932
4. **Strengthens "No Single Document" Argument:** Even formal treaties failed; outcomes resulted from years of negotiation and resistance

**Script Use (2-3 sentences):**
"Britain and France tried to formalize their plans in the Treaty of Sèvres, signed August 1920. Turkey rejected it. Under Atatürk, they fought a war of independence and forced a complete renegotiation—the Treaty of Lausanne in 1923. That's three years of war that changed the entire settlement."

## 2. PATTERN ANALYSIS (Systematic Issue)

### Pattern: Britain's Contradictory Promises (1915-1920)

**Incident 1 (1915-1916):** Hussein-McMahon
- Promise: Arab independence
- Recipient: Arab leaders
- Goal: Secure Arab Revolt against Ottomans

**Incident 2 (May 1916):** Sykes-Picot
- Promise: Franco-British zones of control
- Recipient: France (and Russia)
- Goal: Divide Ottoman spoils, prevent French objections
- **CONTRADICTION:** Can't have independent Arab state AND Franco-British zones

**Incident 3 (Nov 1917):** Balfour Declaration
- Promise: Jewish national home in Palestine
- Recipient: Zionist movement
- Goal: Gain Jewish support, post-war claim to Palestine
- **CONTRADICTION:** Can't have Arab state, Franco-British zones, AND Jewish homeland in same territory

**Incident 4 (April 1920):** San Remo tries to reconcile
- Result: Mandate system created
- Used Sykes-Picot framework but modified
- Arabs feel betrayed (no independence)
- Boundaries still "to be determined"

**Incident 5 (August 1920):** Treaty of Sèvres - FAILED
- European attempt to formalize partition
- **Turkey REJECTS and refuses to ratify**
- Turkish War of Independence (1919-1923)
- **LOCAL RESISTANCE WORKS** - forces renegotiation

**Incident 6 (July 1923):** Treaty of Lausanne
- **REPLACES Sèvres entirely**
- Turkey regains territory
- Completely different settlement
- Proves local agency mattered

**Incident 7 (1923-1932):** Actual borders finalized
- Syria-Palestine: 1923
- Iraq-Turkey (Mosul): 1926
- Iraq-Syria: 1932
- Result: 15+ years of negotiations, NOT one 1916 map

**Escalation:**
1915 → One promise (Arabs)
1916 → Two promises (Arabs + France)
1917 → Three promises (Arabs + France + Zionists)
1920 → San Remo mandates (contradictions institutionalized)
1920 → Sèvres attempted, FAILED (Turkey resists)
1923 → Lausanne (Turkey wins concessions)
1926-1932 → Final borders negotiated

**Systematic Issue:** British making incompatible commitments to secure short-term wartime advantages + underestimating local resistance = 15-year contested process, NOT one 1916 blueprint.

- Conclusion: "Sykes-Picot is a symptom of the Middle East's fundamental problem, not its cause"
- Research: Used declassified British and French archives

- Multiple competing maps exist (1916-1932) - no single "creation moment"
- Ottoman maps from 1893 show "Arab Iraq" label
- Iraq-Syria border under ISIS control "looks more like Sykes-Picot" than actual modern borders
- Modern Iraq borders finalized 1926-1932, NOT 1916

- "Natural borders didn't exist" - sects and tribes overlapped
- "Any external plan would have failed" - Ottoman collapse caused conflicts

- Sykes-Picot's actual influence "is disputed"

### Key Academic Point:

The "Sykes-Picot myth" has become "firmly associated with almost all geopolitical predicaments Middle Eastern states face" (scholarly consensus on myth's power, if not accuracy).

Hundreds of scholarly works added in 2016 centennial, with debate intensifying.

### What Historians AGREE On:

1. Sykes-Picot WAS a colonial carve-up
2. It DID influence subsequent events
3. British made contradictory promises
4. BUT: Actual borders came from 15+ years of negotiations, local resistance, and multiple agreements
5. Blaming "Sykes-Picot alone" oversimplifies

## 4. COUNTER-ARGUMENTS TO ADDRESS

### Arguments SUPPORTING "Sykes-Picot Caused Conflicts":

**1. San Remo Used Sykes-Picot as Framework**
- True: April 1920 mandates followed Sykes-Picot zones broadly
- Counter: But boundaries weren't finalized until 1920s-1930s

**2. Straight-Line Borders Ignored Ethnic Realities**
- True: Some borders (especially deserts) drawn with rulers
- Counter: But many borders followed Ottoman administrative divisions

**3. Created Unstable Multi-Ethnic States**
- True: Iraq included Kurds, Sunnis, Shias in one state
- Counter: Ottoman Empire also governed these groups together

**4. European Powers Imposed Against Local Wishes**
- True: 1920 Damascus Congress wanted unified Arab state
- Counter: But local actors (Hashemites, tribes, nationalists) also shaped outcomes

### Legitimate Criticisms (MUST Acknowledge):

- ✅ British DID make contradictory promises (undeniable)
- ✅ Colonial powers DID prioritize their interests over local populations
- ✅ Mandate system WAS a form of continued colonialism
- ✅ Borders DO contribute to modern conflicts (though not sole cause)

### Where My Thesis Could Be Too Strong:

- If sources show Sykes-Picot was MORE determinative than I claim
- If 1920s negotiations simply ratified 1916 plan
- If local agency was minimal

**NOTE:** Research shows local agency WAS significant (Mosul question, border negotiations lasted years, local resistance mattered).
