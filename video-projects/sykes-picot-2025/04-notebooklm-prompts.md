- To whom: Sharif Hussein of Mecca
- In exchange for: Arab Revolt against Ottomans
- Ambiguities: What exactly was promised?

**COMMITMENT 2: Sykes-Picot (May 1916)**
- Promise: British and French zones of influence
- To whom: France (and Russia)
- Contradicts: Hussein-McMahon by dividing Arab territory
- What changed: Secret carve-up vs. Arab independence

**COMMITMENT 3: Balfour Declaration (November 1917)**
- Promise: "National home for Jewish people" in Palestine
- To whom: Zionist movement
- Contradicts: Both Hussein-McMahon AND Sykes-Picot
- What changed: Third party claim to same territory

**PATTERN ESCALATION:**
4. San Remo (1920) - How did Britain/France try to reconcile these contradictions?
5. Arab resistance - What did local populations do?
6. Final outcomes (1920s) - Which promise "won"? Who was betrayed?

For the pattern:
- Chronological progression showing escalating contradictions
- Systematic issue: Britain making incompatible promises
- Consequences: Which led to current conflicts?
- Quote 3-5 historians analyzing this pattern

This structure will keep viewers engaged: "So they promised X to Arabs... but THEN they promised Y to France... but WAIT, it gets worse - they ALSO promised Z to Zionists..."
