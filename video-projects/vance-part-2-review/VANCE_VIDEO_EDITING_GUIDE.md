## 🎬 VISUAL RHYTHM STRATEGY

### Typical 30-Second Pattern:
```
1. Host on camera (10 sec) - establishing claim/setup
2. Cut to document/graphic (5 sec) - evidence
3. Back to host (8 sec) - interpretation
4. Cut to document/graphic (4 sec) - additional evidence
5. Back to host (3 sec) - transition to next point
```

### Pattern Interrupt Schedule:
- Every 20-30 seconds: Change visual (you → document → you → map → you)
- Every 45-60 seconds: Change subject matter (claim 1 → modern stakes → claim 2)
- Every 90-120 seconds: Change emotional tone (analytical → urgent → conversational)

### Pacing Notes:

**FAST PACING (First 20 seconds):**
- Cold open: Vance clip (8 sec) → YOU (5 sec) → Brief document flash OR stay on you (5 sec)
- Quick cuts, high energy
- Goal: Hook retention fast

**MEDIUM PACING (01:00:22-01:05:00):**
- Claims 1, 2 & 3: Evidence presentation
- Mix of you (8-12 sec) and documents (4-6 sec)
- Goal: Maintain engagement while delivering substance

**Note:** Your actual footage is simpler and tighter than the script - you go straight from cold open into claim 1 without a separate preview section. This is GOOD - keeps momentum.

**STRATEGIC HOLDS (Key Evidence):**
- DNA study results: Hold 6-8 seconds as you list findings
- Cambridge quote: Hold 5-6 seconds for full quote
- Maps showing geographic problems: Hold 5-6 seconds

**FASTER PACING (Montages):**
- Quote bombardment (Furman, Davis, Pope): Rapid succession, 3-4 sec each

## 🎓 EDITING TIPS FOR THIS VIDEO

### Keep It Simple
- You're building trust through evidence, not flashy effects
- Clean cuts > fancy transitions
- Documents speak for themselves - don't over-animate

### Respect the Evidence
- When showing a document, give viewers time to see it (5-6 seconds)
- Zoom to relevant sections when possible
- Don't cut away too fast - frustrates viewers

### Maintain Authority
- Default to YOU on camera for analysis and interpretation
- Only cut away to show proof
- Come back to you after showing evidence to explain what it means

### Watch for These Common Mistakes
- **Too much B-roll:** If you're on camera less than 50%, you've over-cut
- **Text overlays covering your face:** Keep text in bottom third or sides
- **Inconsistent volumes:** Documents with music should be -20dB below voice
- **Rushed evidence:** Don't flash documents too fast to read

### Test Your Work
- Watch the full video without editing timeline visible
- Ask: "Can I follow the argument?"
- Ask: "Does each visual ADD information or just decorate?"
- If a cut feels unnecessary, remove it
