## DESCRIPTION

The 1916 Sykes-Picot Agreement didn't draw today's Middle East borders - here's the evidence that proves otherwise.

Everyone blames Sykes-Picot for "carving up the Middle East with a ruler" - ISIS cited it, Kurdish activists point to it, Palestine analysts invoke it constantly. But when you look at the actual documents? The borders we see today weren't finalized until 10-15 years later, through a contested process involving oil deals, local uprisings, and League of Nations commissions.

This video uses primary sources - the original Hussein-McMahon correspondence, Sykes-Picot Agreement text, Balfour Declaration, San Remo Conference records, and League of Nations archives - to show what actually happened.

📍 WHAT THIS VIDEO COVERS:
• Why the 1916 Sykes-Picot map doesn't match modern borders
• Britain's three contradictory promises about the same land (1915-1917)
• The smoking gun: French negotiator admitting Arab promises were lies
• How borders were actually drawn (1920-1932) through oil negotiations
• Local resistance that changed the map: Turkey 1923, Iraq 1920
