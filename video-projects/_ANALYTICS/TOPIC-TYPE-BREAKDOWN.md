## CROSS-CATEGORY PATTERNS

### Pattern 1: The "Reframing Effect"
**Same topic, different framing, different results:**
- African coups as "geopolitics": 619 views
- African coups as "colonial legacy": 25 views
- **Lesson:** Frame colonial legacy as territorial disputes or geopolitics when possible

### Pattern 2: The "Compilation Curse"
**Single specific myth > compilation:**
- 5 Big Myths Israel-Palestine: 586 views
- 10 Myths Middle East: 90 views
- 10 Myths Israel-Palestine: Would likely underperform too
- **Lesson:** Focus beats breadth

### Pattern 3: The "Evidence Hook"
**Scientific proof drives CTR:**
- DNA Proves Aztec Sacrifice: 8.0% CTR
- Archaeological Artifacts Israel: 2.7% CTR
- Just "myths about": 1.5% average CTR
- **Lesson:** "DNA/Archaeological Evidence Proves [X]" is a winning title formula

### Pattern 4: The "Retention ≠ Views Paradox"
**High retention doesn't guarantee discovery:**
- Vikings Female Commanders: 18.24% retention, 626 views ✓
- KGB Palestinians: 11.72% retention, 148 views ✗
- How a 1947 Drawing: 13.62% retention, 90 views ✗
- **Lesson:** Retention helps promotion AFTER clicks. Optimize for CTR first, retention second.

### Pattern 5: The "Named Figure Multiplier"
**Named person in title >> generic claim:**
- JD Vance Claims: 11.21% CTR, 936 views
- Generic genocide debate: 2.0% CTR, 689 views
- Putin specific claims: 6.56% CTR (shorts)
- "Why countries changed minds": 2.72% CTR
- **Lesson:** "[Name] Claims [X]. Here's The Evidence." is the best title formula
