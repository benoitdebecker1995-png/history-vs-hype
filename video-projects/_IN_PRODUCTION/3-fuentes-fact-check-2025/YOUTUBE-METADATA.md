PRIMARY SOURCES:

HOLOCAUST DOCUMENTATION:

1. Korherr Report (March 23, 1943)
   - Nazi SS statistician Dr. Richard Korherr to Heinrich Himmler
   - 16-page report on "Final Solution to the Jewish Question"
   - Documents 4 million Jews "evacuated" by December 1942
   - Available: German History in Documents and Images (GHDI)
   - URL: germanhistorydocs.org

2. Höfle Telegram (January 11, 1943)
   - Encrypted Nazi radio message, decoded by British intelligence
   - SS officer Hermann Höfle to Adolf Eichmann
   - Death counts for Operation Reinhard camps in 1942:
     * Belzec: 434,508
     * Sobibor: 101,370
     * Treblinka: 71,355
     * Majdanek: 24,733
     * Total: 1,274,166
   - Discovered 2000 in UK Public Record Office
   - URL: sobibor.org/en/the-hofle-telegram/

3. Auschwitz-Birkenau Crematorium Documentation
   - SS Central Building Administration blueprints
   - Karl Bischoff letter to Hans Kammler (June 28, 1943)
   - Documents 52 crematorium muffles/ovens:
     * Crematorium II: 15 muffles
     * Crematorium III: 15 muffles
     * Crematorium IV: 8 muffles
     * Crematorium V: 8 muffles
     * Original crematorium: 6 muffles
   - Official capacity: 4,416 corpses/day
   - Topf & Sons engineering specifications
   - URL: hdot.org/debunking-denial/ab3-german-documents-ovens/

4. Einsatzgruppen Reports
   - Mobile killing units documentation
   - 1.3+ million shot in Eastern Europe (not cremated in ovens)
   - US Holocaust Memorial Museum Encyclopedia
   - URL: encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/einsatzgruppen

FOUNDING FATHERS DOCUMENTATION:

1. First Continental Congress Petition to the King (October 26, 1774)
   - Peaceful diplomatic attempt to resolve grievances
   - Language affirming loyalty to King George III
   - Available: Avalon Project, Yale Law School
   - URL: avalon.law.yale.edu/18th_century/contcong_10-26-74.asp

2. Olive Branch Petition (July 5-8, 1775)
   - Drafted by John Dickinson, signed by Second Continental Congress
   - Sent AFTER Lexington & Concord battles (April 1775)
   - Final attempt to avoid war with Britain
   - Affirmed American loyalty, entreated king to prevent conflict
   - Available: Founders Online, Library of Congress
   - URL: founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-01-02-0114

3. King George III's Proclamation of Rebellion (August 23, 1775)
   - Declared colonists in open rebellion
   - Issued BEFORE receiving Olive Branch Petition
   - Refused to consider peace terms
   - Available: Avalon Project
   - URL: avalon.law.yale.edu/18th_century/proc1775.asp

4. Declaration of Independence (July 4, 1776)
   - Explicit language: "In every stage of these Oppressions We have Petitioned for Redress in the most humble terms"
   - "Our repeated Petitions have been answered only by repeated injury"
   - Came AFTER 2 years of failed diplomatic efforts
   - Available: National Archives
   - URL: archives.gov/founding-docs/declaration-transcript
