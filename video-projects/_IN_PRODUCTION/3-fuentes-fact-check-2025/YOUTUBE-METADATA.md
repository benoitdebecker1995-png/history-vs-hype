## DESCRIPTION

On October 27, 2025, Tucker Carlson hosted a two-hour interview with Nick Fuentes without raising his documented history of false historical claims. This video fact-checks two of those claims using primary sources.

---

**THE HOLOCAUST DOCUMENTS:**

**The Höfle Telegram** (January 1943) – An encrypted Nazi message showing 1,274,166 people deported to four death camps in one year. British intelligence decoded it in 2000. The telegram tracks arrivals at Belzec, Sobibor, Treblinka, and Majdanek—all camps that were part of Aktion Reinhardt, the Nazi operation to exterminate Polish Jews.

**The Korherr Report** (March 1943) – SS statistician Richard Korherr's report to Heinrich Himmler recording over 1.8 million Jews given "special treatment" (Sonderbehandlung)—Nazi bureaucratic code for killing—through December 1942 alone. The Nazis documented mass murder while hiding it in euphemistic language.

**Auschwitz-Birkenau Construction Records** – Architectural plans from the SS Central Building Administration showing 52 incineration chambers (muffles) across five crematoria, not the "15 ovens" Fuentes claimed in his 2019 video. The blueprints prove his math was based on a number he invented.

**Einsatzgruppen Report 106** (October 1941) – Operational Situation Report documenting 33,771 Jews killed at Babi Yar ravine near Kiev on September 29-30, 1941. This wasn't hidden—it was routine paperwork filed with Berlin headquarters, complete with location, date, method, and body count.

Most Holocaust victims weren't cremated at all. The Einsatzgruppen mobile killing units shot over a million Jews in Eastern Europe, and the three death camps named in the Höfle Telegram used mass graves and open-air burning. Witness testimony from Nazi guards, perpetrator confessions, and forensic excavations all confirm this.

**THE FOUNDING FATHERS TIMELINE:**

Fuentes has claimed the Founding Fathers attacked the British before declaring independence, making them aggressors—like the people who stormed the Capitol on January 6th. The documentary record shows otherwise.

**April 1774:** First Continental Congress petitions King George III for redress of grievances.

**April 19, 1775:** British troops fire first shots at Lexington and Concord. Colonists return fire.

**July 5, 1775:** The Olive Branch Petition. Written THREE MONTHS after British troops fired on colonists, the petition still addresses the King as "your Majesty's faithful subjects" and begs for peaceful resolution.

**August 23, 1775:** King George III rejects the petition and declares the colonies in "open and avowed rebellion."

**July 4, 1776:** Declaration of Independence—nearly 15 MONTHS after fighting began. The Declaration explicitly states: "In every stage of these Oppressions We have Petitioned for Redress in the most humble terms: Our repeated Petitions have been answered only by repeated injury."

The Founders didn't attack first. They exhausted every peaceful option for over a year after British troops opened fire.
