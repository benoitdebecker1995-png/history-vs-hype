## SEGMENT 2: HOLOCAUST MATH - SOURCE RECOMMENDATIONS

### PRIMARY DOCUMENTS (Free - Upload to NotebookLM)

**Priority: CRITICAL**

1. **Korherr Report (March 23, 1943)**
   - Source: germanhistorydocs.org (GHDI)
   - Format: PDF download available
   - What to upload: Full English translation
   - Why: Nazi SS official statistics proving 4M by Dec 1942
   - Status: ✅ FREE, publicly accessible

2. **Höfle Telegram Analysis**
   - Source: sobibor.org/en/the-hofle-telegram/
   - Format: Web page with full decoded text
   - What to upload: Copy text into .txt or .pdf
   - Why: Encrypted Nazi communication, 1.27M in 4 camps in 1942
   - Status: ✅ FREE

3. **Auschwitz-Birkenau Blueprints Documentation**
   - Source: hdot.org (Holocaust Denial on Trial)
   - URL: hdot.org/debunking-denial/ab3-german-documents-ovens/
   - Format: Web article with images
   - What to upload: Save as PDF with images
   - Why: SS blueprints, crematorium specifications, 52 ovens documented
   - Status: ✅ FREE

4. **Einsatzgruppen Reports**
   - Source: US Holocaust Memorial Museum
   - URL: encyclopedia.ushmm.org
   - Format: Encyclopedia entries + report excerpts
   - What to upload: Save relevant articles as PDFs
   - Why: Mobile killing units, 1.3M shot (not cremated)
   - Status: ✅ FREE

### BOOKS WORTH PURCHASING (Priority Order)

**Tier 1: ESSENTIAL (Buy These)**

1. **"The Destruction of the European Jews" by Raul Hilberg**
   - 3-volume definitive work
   - ~$80-120 (check used copies)
   - Why: Comprehensive documentation using Nazi bureaucratic records
   - NotebookLM value: High - exhaustive primary source citations
   - **Recommendation: BUY** - The gold standard reference

2. **"Anatomy of the Auschwitz Death Camp" edited by Yisrael Gutman & Michael Berenbaum**
   - ~$40-60
   - Why: Scholarly essays on camp operations, crematorium engineering
   - NotebookLM value: High - technical details on cremation capacity
   - **Recommendation: BUY** - Direct rebuttal to "oven math" claims

3. **"Nazi Mass Murder: A Documentary History of the Use of Poison Gas" by Eugen Kogon, Hermann Langbein, Adalbert Rückerl**
   - ~$35-50
   - Why: Primary Nazi documents on gas chamber operations
   - NotebookLM value: High - engineering specifications, witness testimony
   - **Recommendation: BUY** - Technical documentation

**Tier 2: VERY USEFUL (Buy If Budget Allows)**

4. **"The Wannsee Conference and the Final Solution" by Mark Roseman**
   - ~$20-30
   - Why: Analysis of planning documents for systematic killing
   - NotebookLM value: Medium-High - Context for Korherr Report
   - **Recommendation: Consider** - Adds important context

5. **"Holocaust: The Nazi Persecution and Murder of the Jews" by Peter Longerich**
   - ~$30-40
   - Why: Comprehensive narrative with extensive documentation
   - NotebookLM value: Medium-High - Good overview with sources
   - **Recommendation: Consider** - Strong academic work

**Tier 3: NICE TO HAVE (Skip If Budget Tight)**

6. **"The Holocaust by Bullets" by Patrick Desbois**
   - ~$20-30
   - Why: Einsatzgruppen mass shooting documentation
   - NotebookLM value: Medium - Complements "not all cremated" argument
   - **Recommendation: Skip for now** - Free sources cover this

7. **"The Origins of the Final Solution" by Christopher Browning**
   - ~$25-35
   - Why: Evolution of Nazi killing policy
   - NotebookLM value: Medium - Historical context
   - **Recommendation: Skip for now** - Not essential for math debunk

## SEGMENT 3: FOUNDING FATHERS - SOURCE RECOMMENDATIONS

### PRIMARY DOCUMENTS (Free - Upload to NotebookLM)

**Priority: CRITICAL**

1. **Journals of the Continental Congress**
   - Source: founders.archives.gov
   - Format: Searchable digital archive
   - What to upload: October 1774 proceedings, July 1775 proceedings
   - Why: Official record of petition decisions
   - Status: ✅ FREE, National Archives

2. **First Continental Congress Petition to the King (Oct 26, 1774)**
   - Source: masshist.org, archives.gov
   - Format: Full text available
   - What to upload: Complete petition text
   - Why: First peaceful attempt
   - Status: ✅ FREE

3. **Olive Branch Petition (July 5-8, 1775)**
   - Source: loc.gov (Library of Congress)
   - Format: Full text + manuscript images
   - What to upload: Complete text, background notes
   - Why: CRITICAL - peace attempt AFTER bloodshed
   - Status: ✅ FREE

4. **Declaration of Independence (July 4, 1776)**
   - Source: archives.gov
   - Format: Full text + analysis
   - What to upload: Complete text, focus on grievance section
   - Why: Explicit statement on "repeated petitions"
   - Status: ✅ FREE

5. **George III's Proclamation of Rebellion (August 23, 1775)**
   - Source: National Archives (UK), avalon.law.yale.edu
   - Format: Full text
   - What to upload: Complete proclamation
   - Why: Proves King rejected peace before receiving Olive Branch
   - Status: ✅ FREE

### BOOKS WORTH PURCHASING (Priority Order)

4. **"The Radicalism of the American Revolution" by Gordon S. Wood**
   - ~$15-25
   - Why: Pulitzer Prize winner, explores Revolutionary ideology
