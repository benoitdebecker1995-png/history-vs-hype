## SEGMENT 2 (3:00-7:00): Holocaust Math Debunk
**NOTEBOOKLM NOTEBOOK #1: "Holocaust Documentation"**

This segment needs deep sourcing. These sources go into NotebookLM.

### PRIMARY DOCUMENTS (FREE - Must Upload)

**1. Korherr Report**
- Full title: "Die Endlösung der Judenfrage" (The Final Solution to the Jewish Question)
- Date: March 23, 1943
- Author: SS statistician Dr. Richard Korherr to Heinrich Himmler
- Key stat: 4 million Jews "evacuated" by December 1942
- **Source:** germanhistorydocs.org (GHDI - German History in Documents and Images)
- **Format:** PDF download, English translation
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Full PDF

**2. Höfle Telegram Analysis**
- Date: January 11, 1943
- Type: Encrypted Nazi radio message, decoded by British intelligence
- From: SS Sturmbannführer Hermann Höfle
- To: Adolf Eichmann
- Key stat: 1,274,166 deaths in 4 Operation Reinhard camps in 1942 alone
  - Belzec: 434,508
  - Sobibor: 101,370
  - Treblinka: 71,355
  - Majdanek: 24,733
- **Source:** sobibor.org/en/the-hofle-telegram/
- **Format:** Web page with full decoded text + analysis
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Save page as PDF

**3. Auschwitz Crematorium Technical Documentation**
- SS Central Building Administration blueprints
- Karl Bischoff letter to Hans Kammler (June 28, 1943)
- Engineering specs: 52 crematorium ovens (NOT 15)
  - Crematorium II: 15 muffles
  - Crematorium III: 15 muffles
  - Crematorium IV: 8 muffles
  - Crematorium V: 8 muffles
  - Original crematorium I: 6 muffles
- Official SS capacity: 4,416 corpses/day
- **Source:** hdot.org/debunking-denial/ab3-german-documents-ovens/
- **Format:** Comprehensive web page with images
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Save entire page as PDF with images

**4. Einsatzgruppen Overview**
- Mobile killing squads in Eastern Europe
- Documented 1.3+ million shot (NOT cremated)
- Daily execution reports sent to Berlin
- **Source:** USHMM encyclopedia (encyclopedia.ushmm.org)
- **Format:** 2-3 key articles
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Save as PDFs
  - "Einsatzgruppen" main article
  - "Mobile Killing Units" article

**PRIMARY DOCUMENTS SUBTOTAL:** 4-6 sources (all FREE)

## SEGMENT 3 (7:00-9:30): Founding Fathers Timeline
**NOTEBOOKLM NOTEBOOK #2: "Founding Fathers Diplomacy"**

This segment is straightforward. Primary documents are self-explanatory.

### PRIMARY DOCUMENTS (FREE - Must Upload)

**1. First Continental Congress Petition (October 26, 1774)**
- Petition to King George III
- Content: Colonists assert loyalty, ask for grievance redress
- **Source:** founders.archives.gov OR masshist.org (Massachusetts Historical Society)
- **Format:** Full text (1-2 pages)
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Complete petition text as PDF

**2. Olive Branch Petition (July 5-8, 1775)**
- Drafted by: John Dickinson
- Date adopted: July 5, 1775
- Date signed: July 8, 1775
- Context: Sent AFTER Lexington & Concord battles
- Content: "Final attempt to avoid war," affirmed loyalty to Britain
- **Source:** loc.gov (Library of Congress - George Washington Papers)
- **Format:** Full text + manuscript images
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Full text + LOC analysis page as PDF

**3. Declaration of Independence (July 4, 1776)**
- Focus: Grievance section, "repeated petitions" passage
- Key quote: "In every stage of these Oppressions We have Petitioned for Redress in the most humble terms: Our repeated Petitions have been answered only by repeated injury"
- **Source:** archives.gov (National Archives)
- **Format:** Full text
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Full text as PDF (focus on grievances section)

**4. King George III's Proclamation of Rebellion (August 23, 1775)**
- Content: King declares colonists in rebellion BEFORE receiving Olive Branch Petition
- Context: Refused to read Olive Branch
- **Source:** avalon.law.yale.edu (Avalon Project)
- **Format:** Full text
- **Upload to NotebookLM:** Complete proclamation as PDF

**PRIMARY DOCUMENTS SUBTOTAL:** 4 sources (all FREE)
