## 📋 SCRIPT WRITING RULES FOR NEXT VIDEO

### Rule 1: ONE CONCEPT = ONE POWERFUL MENTION + ONE CALLBACK
Don't repeat the same concept more than twice in a 10-minute video.

**Example:**
- ✅ Introduce: "The Nazis documented their genocide with bureaucratic precision"
- ✅ Callback (middle): "Remember that bureaucratic record-keeping?"
- ❌ Don't repeat this concept 4+ times

### Rule 2: VARY YOUR VOCABULARY
Never use the exact same phrase more than twice.

**Bad:**
- "Deportation records, statistical reports" (x4)

**Good:**
- "Deportation records and statistical reports"
- "Nazi paperwork"
- "Their administrative files"
- "Bureaucratic documentation"

### Rule 3: SHOW, DON'T SAY
If a document is on screen, you don't need to SAY "document."

**Bad:**
"Here's a document. This document shows... The document proves... Documentary evidence..."

**Good:**
"Here's the Höfle Telegram. It shows 1.27 million deportations. The numbers prove Fuentes wrong."

### Rule 4: CUT YOUR CONCLUSIONS IN HALF
Your first draft conclusion is probably twice as long as it needs to be.

**Bad conclusion (repetitive):**
"The Nazis documented. Deportation records. Statistical reports. The Nazis documented. The evidence shows. Documentary evidence. They documented."

**Good conclusion (tight):**
"The Höfle Telegram. The Korher Report. The blueprints. The Nazis kept records. Fuentes ignores them. That's not revisionism—that's lying."

### Rule 5: USE THE "THREE MENTION RULE"
For any piece of evidence (like the Höfle Telegram):
1. **First mention:** Introduce it fully (what it is, why it matters)
2. **Second mention:** Quick callback in the middle ("Remember those deportation numbers?")
3. **Third mention:** Include in conclusion montage (visual, no narration needed)

**Don't mention it 6 times.**

### Rule 6: CALLBACKS SHOULD BE BRIEF
When referencing something you already explained, make it SHORT.

**Bad callback (too long):**
"And remember that encrypted telegram? The one from January 11, 1943? With the deportation records showing 1.2 million people sent to death camps in one year? That telegram?"

**Good callback (concise):**
"Remember those deportation numbers? Four times Fuentes' estimate. In one year."

## 🎯 SPECIFIC IMPROVEMENTS FOR NEXT SCRIPT

### Before Writing:
1. **List your evidence** - Write down all primary sources you'll reference
2. **Mention each 2-3 times MAX** - No more
3. **Create a "phrase variety list"** - 5 different ways to say "documents," "evidence," "records"
4. **Outline your conclusion FIRST** - Keep it to 60-90 seconds

### While Writing:
1. **Use Ctrl+F to search for repeated phrases**
   - Search: "deportation records"
   - Search: "documentary evidence"
   - Search: "documents"
   - If any phrase appears 4+ times, vary the language

2. **Read your conclusion out loud**
   - Does it feel repetitive?
   - Are you restating things you already said?
   - Can you cut 30%?

### After First Draft:
1. **Count references to each piece of evidence**
   - Höfle Telegram: Aim for 3 mentions, not 6
   - Korher Report: 2-3 mentions MAX
   - Each document: 2-3 mentions

2. **Identify your "thesis phrase"**
   - Example: "erasing documentary evidence"
   - Use it ONCE in the intro
   - Use it ONCE in the conclusion
   - Don't repeat it 5 times
