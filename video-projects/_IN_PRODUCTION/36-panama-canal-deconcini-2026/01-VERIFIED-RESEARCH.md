### Modern Hook Statement
**The clause:** The 1978 DeConcini Reservation to the Panama Canal Neutrality Treaty reserves the US right to use military force in Panama to keep the canal open.

**Who invokes it today:**
- US politicians threatening to "take back" the canal (Trump, Dec 2024 / Jan 2025)
- Panamanian sovereignty advocates
- International law scholars analyzing US interventionism

**Modern consequence:** The same legal instrument that Panama protested in 1978 and that shadowed the 1989 invasion is still in effect. Trump's 2025 rhetoric about reclaiming the canal revives the question of whether the US ever truly relinquished control.

**Hook strength (1-5):** 5/5 - Active in current political discourse, massive search volume, primary documents available, clear "one clause" hook

### Why This Matters in 2025-2026
Trump's December 2024 / January 2025 statements about "taking back" the Panama Canal made global headlines. Panama's President Mulino pushed back. But almost nobody read the actual treaty to see what legal rights the US retained. The DeConcini Reservation is the document that answers this question — and it's been hiding in plain sight for 47 years.
