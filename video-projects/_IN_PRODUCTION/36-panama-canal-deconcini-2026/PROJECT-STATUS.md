## Project Brief

### The Story

In 1977, Jimmy Carter and Omar Torrijos signed two treaties to transfer the Panama Canal to Panama. But during Senate ratification in 1978, Senator Dennis DeConcini (D-AZ) added a reservation to the Neutrality Treaty — a single clause reserving the United States' right to use military force in Panama to keep the canal open. Panama protested. The Senate vote passed 68-32, one vote above the two-thirds threshold.

The DeConcini Reservation is still in legal effect. In 1989, the US invaded Panama (Operation Just Cause). In 2025, Trump threatened to "take back" the canal. The same 1978 clause shadows every US-Panama interaction.
