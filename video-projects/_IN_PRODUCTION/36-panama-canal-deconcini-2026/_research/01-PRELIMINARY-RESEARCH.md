## 1. The DeConcini Reservation (1978) — CORE DOCUMENT

### Exact Text Found

❓ **NEEDS VERIFICATION against Congressional Record:**

> "If the Canal is closed, or its operations are interfered with, the United States of America and the Republic of Panama shall each independently have the right to take such steps as each deems necessary, in accordance with its constitutional processes, including the use of military force in the Republic of Panama, to reopen the Canal or to restore the operations of the Canal, as the case may be."

**Adopted by Senate:** March 15, 1978 (vote: 75-23)
**Attached to:** Treaty Concerning the Permanent Neutrality and Operation of the Panama Canal (the "Neutrality Treaty")
**Author:** Senator Dennis DeConcini (D-AZ)
**Replaced:** The "Clements Clause" (earlier, weaker version)

❓ **KEY QUESTIONS FOR VERIFICATION:**
- Is this the complete text or abridged?
- Was there additional qualifying language?
- What was the Clements Clause it replaced?

### The Leadership Amendment

❓ **NEEDS EXACT TEXT:**

The Leadership Amendment was added alongside DeConcini to provide political cover. It incorporated language from an October 1977 joint statement by Carter and Torrijos, explicitly setting out US rights to defend canal neutrality after 2000.

- Co-sponsors: 76 senators
- Purpose: To soften DeConcini by reaffirming the treaty didn't grant intervention rights in Panama's internal affairs

❓ **VERIFY:** Did the Leadership Amendment legally modify or limit DeConcini?

### Torrijos Counter-Reservation

❓ **NEEDS EXACT TEXT:**

**Key finding:** Torrijos opened his counter-reservation by repeating the DeConcini text word-for-word, then added three paragraphs.

**First paragraph (preliminary):**
> "The Republic of Panama agrees to the exchange of the instruments of ratification of the aforementioned Neutrality Treaty on the understanding that there are positive rules of public international law contained in multilateral treaties to which both the Republic of Panama and the United States of America are Parties..."

**Context:** Carter suggested Torrijos write his own counter-reservation after Torrijos threatened to renounce the treaties entirely. Torrijos insisted intervention was only acceptable if "specifically invited."

❓ **CRITICAL QUESTION:** Does Torrijos's counter-reservation legally nullify the DeConcini Reservation? Legal scholars appear divided.

**Instruments of ratification signed:** June 16, 1978 (ceremony in Panama City, both Carter and Torrijos present)

## 2. The 1977 Torrijos-Carter Treaties

### Two Separate Treaties

❓ **VERIFY exact provisions:**

**Treaty 1: The Panama Canal Treaty**
- Canal Zone ceases to exist: October 1, 1979
- Full canal transfer to Panama: December 31, 1999
- Phased handover of facilities and operations
- Joint administration during transition period

**Treaty 2: Treaty Concerning the Permanent Neutrality and Operation of the Panama Canal (Neutrality Treaty)**
- Canal permanently neutral
- Open to vessels of all nations
- US retains right to defend neutrality
- **DeConcini Reservation attached to THIS treaty**
- Remains in effect "in perpetuity" (unlike Treaty 1 which expired 1999)

### Negotiation Details

- **Negotiations resumed:** February 15, 1977
- **Completed:** August 10, 1977
- **US negotiators:** Ambassador Ellsworth Bunker, Sol Linowitz
- **Panama negotiators:** Romulo Escobar Bethancourt
- **Signed:** September 7, 1977 (both treaties)
- **Location:** OAS headquarters, Washington DC

### Senate Ratification

- **Neutrality Treaty ratified:** March 16, 1978 (68-32) — ONE vote above 2/3 threshold
- **Panama Canal Treaty ratified:** April 18, 1978 (68-32) — identical margin

❓ **VERIFY:** Who were the 68 and 32? Any notable crossover votes?

## 3. The 1903 Hay-Bunau-Varilla Treaty

### How the US Got the Canal

❓ **VERIFY full sequence:**

**Timeline:**
1. **Early 1903:** US negotiates Hay-Herran Treaty with Colombia for canal rights
2. **August 12, 1903:** Colombian Congress rejects Hay-Herran Treaty
3. **November 3, 1903:** Panama declares independence from Colombia (US tacitly supported)
4. **November 13, 1903:** US formally recognizes Republic of Panama
5. **November 18, 1903:** Hay-Bunau-Varilla Treaty signed (15 DAYS after independence)

### Philippe Bunau-Varilla

❓ **VERIFY these claims:**
- **Nationality:** French citizen (NOT Panamanian)
- **Background:** Engineer who worked on French canal project, major financial stakeholder
- **Key claim:** Had not been on the Isthmus of Panama since 1887 — 17 years before signing
- **Appointed:** Panamanian ambassador to the United States (despite being French)
- **Never returned** to Panama after signing

❓ **CONTESTED:** Was Bunau-Varilla authorized by Panama's government? Sources suggest he signed before Panama's own delegation arrived in Washington.

### Key Treaty Provisions (from Yale Avalon Project)

**Article II:** Grants US "use, occupation and control" of a 10-mile-wide zone "in perpetuity"
**Article III:** US receives "all the rights, power and authority" it would possess "if it were the sovereign" — explicitly excluding Panama's sovereign exercise
**Article V:** US monopoly on canal construction and operation "in perpetuity"
**Article XIV:** $10 million upon ratification + $250,000/year starting 9 years later
**Article XXIII:** US authorized to employ armed forces and establish fortifications

❓ **KEY PHRASE:** "as if it were the sovereign" — this is the phrase that defined 74 years of US control

## 5. 1999 Canal Transfer

❓ **VERIFY details:**

- **Date:** December 31, 1999, at noon
- **Ceremony:** Attended by former President Carter, Panamanian President Mireya Moscoso
- **What transferred:** All canal operations, facilities, infrastructure
- **What remained:** US retained rights under Neutrality Treaty (which has no expiration)
- **Operational continuity:** Panama Canal Authority (ACP) took over

### Post-Transfer Performance

❓ **VERIFY statistics:**

**FY 2024 (most recent):**
- Total revenue: $4.99 billion
- Toll revenue: $3.18 billion
- Canal transit services: $1.66 billion
- Profit: $3.44 billion (68.9% of revenue)
- Ship transits: 9,944 (down 21% from FY23 due to drought)
- Average capacity: 38 vessels/day (normal), 25.9/day (drought period)

❓ **KEY CONTEXT:** Panama manages the canal successfully and profitably — relevant to Trump's "take back" rhetoric
