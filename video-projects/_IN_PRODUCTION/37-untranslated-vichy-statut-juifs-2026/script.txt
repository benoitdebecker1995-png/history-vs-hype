COLD OPEN

This is a law that stripped French citizens of their jobs, their rights, and their place in public life — based on their grandparents' race. It was written in French. And if you don't read French, you've probably never read it.

The United States Holocaust Memorial Museum describes its provisions. Yad Vashem summarizes them. Academic books quote sections of it. But a readable, clause-by-clause English translation of the full text? I couldn't find one. So I made one.

And then there's something else — a draft of this law, covered in handwritten annotations from the French head of state himself. Those annotations have never been translated or walked through in English. They prove he didn't just sign this law. He personally made it worse.

I'm going to show you both.

WHY THIS DOCUMENT MATTERS

On October 3rd, 1940, the Vichy government published the Statut des Juifs — the Jewish Statute. Ten articles. It created a racial definition of who counted as Jewish. It banned Jews from almost every position of public influence. And it applied those bans across the entire French empire.

For decades, the official French position was that Germany imposed this. After the war, Vichy's own Justice Minister — a man named Joseph Barthélemy — said, and this is a direct quote: "This legislation is not a French initiative. It is entirely of German origin."

The archives prove that was a lie.

How do we know? Three things.

First, the armistice France signed with Germany in June 1940 said nothing about Jews. Historians Marrus and Paxton went through both the German and French archives. Their conclusion: "Close study of German and French archives reveals no German demand in 1940 that Vichy adopt an anti-Jewish policy."

Second, France was already passing anti-Jewish measures before Germany issued anything. In July 1940, they restricted public service jobs to people born of French fathers. In August, they restricted the medical profession. In late August, they repealed the Marchandeau Law — the law that had made antisemitic attacks in the press illegal. All of this before any German ordinance on Jews.

Third, when Germany finally did act — on September 27th, 1940 — what they issued was limited. A census. Property registration. Only in the occupied zone. And they used a religious definition of who was Jewish. Six days later, France responded with something far broader. A racial definition. A full professional purge. Applied across the entire empire.

France didn't follow Germany. France went further.

ARTICLE 1: THE DEFINITION

The first thing the law does is define its target. And the word it uses is "race." Not religion. "Race juive" — the Jewish race.

If you had three Jewish grandparents, you were counted as a Jew. Doesn't matter if you were baptised Catholic. Doesn't matter if you'd never set foot in a synagogue. Two Jewish grandparents? You could escape the definition — unless your spouse was Jewish. Then you were fully Jewish under the law.

And notice — the law never defines what makes a grandparent Jewish. The definition is circular. That's not sloppy drafting. A vague definition gave authorities maximum discretion.

Marrus and Paxton: "Vichy's Statut des juifs went further than the German ordinance. Whereas the German ordinance spoke only of religion, the Vichy law spoke openly of race."

ARTICLE 2: THE PURGE

Article 2 is the ban list. And when you read it, you understand the scale.

Head of State. Every court, from the supreme court down to the local magistrate. Every elected assembly. All prefects. All police. All diplomats. All colonial governors. All military officers. All teachers. Any director of a company receiving public money.

That's not a ban on a few senior positions. That's a systematic removal from every institution that exercises authority, shapes culture, or controls information.

By 1942, roughly 3,400 public servants had been dismissed in mainland France. Another 2,500 in Algeria.

ARTICLE 5: WHO TELLS THE STORIES

Article 5 bans Jews — "without condition or reservation" — from directing, managing, or editing any newspaper, magazine, or periodical. Then cinema: directors, producers, screenwriters. Then radio.

No war-hero exceptions. No exemptions at all. The only thing Jews could still publish was scientific journals.

That one exception tells you everything about the law's purpose. A Jewish journalist isn't banned because they're bad at their job. They're banned because they shape what French people think. This is about controlling who tells France its stories.

ARTICLE 9: THE EMPIRE

One sentence. And it extends the entire law across the French empire.

In Morocco, 32 of 37 Jewish lawyers were removed from the bar. Four days after this statute, a separate law stripped over a hundred thousand Algerian Jews of the citizenship they'd held for seventy years.

But it didn't stop at North Africa. French administrators applied this law everywhere — including territories thousands of miles from the nearest German soldier. In French Indochina, they counted 140 Jews. French West Africa, 110. Martinique, 36. Guadeloupe, 22. Madagascar, 26.

There was no military requirement to register 22 people in Guadeloupe. There were no German troops in Martinique. This wasn't geopolitics. This was administrative zeal.

Marrus and Paxton call this the "definitive proof" that Vichy's antisemitism was indigenous. No German pressure. No German troops. Just French administrators, French law, French zeal.

THE DRAFT

That's the law. Now I want to show you something that was hidden for seventy years.

In 2010 — seventy years to the day after the statute was signed — a lawyer named Serge Klarsfeld held a press conference at the Mémorial de la Shoah in Paris. He had a typed draft of the statute, stamped "DOCUMENT CONFIDENTIEL." Covered in handwritten annotations. Handwriting experts confirmed they were Philippe Pétain's — the head of the Vichy state.

I have this document. I've translated his notes.

PÉTAIN'S ANNOTATIONS

Every Teacher

The typed draft listed specific educational administrators — rectors, inspectors, school directors. Pétain wrote in the margin: "tout personnel enseignant." All teaching personnel. Not just the people who run the schools. Every teacher at every level.

Every Elected Office

The typed draft banned Jews from specific named institutions. Pétain added: "toutes assemblées issues de l'élection." All assemblies arising from election. Every municipal council, every departmental council, every elected body in France and its empire.

The Village Courts

The draft banned Jews from the high courts. Pétain extended it to the "justices de paix" — the village magistrate courts. The lowest level of French justice.

The One He Crossed Out

And then there's the change that matters most.

The typed draft — already harsh — had a protection clause. Condition (a) read: "être descendant de Juifs nés français ou naturalisés avant l'année 1860."

"To be a descendant of Jews born French or naturalized before the year 1860."

That's an eighty-year grandfather clause. If your family had been French since before Napoleon the Third, you were protected. These were France's most established, most assimilated Jewish families. Families who'd been French for generations.

Pétain drew a line through the whole sentence.

One pen stroke. Eighty years of citizenship, erased. It didn't matter if your great-grandfather fought for France. If you fit the racial definition, you were out.

Not a single annotation on this document softened the law. Every mark expanded who it targeted or removed a protection. He added professions. He closed loopholes. He erased the one clause that would have protected France's oldest Jewish families.

Klarsfeld noted: "The main argument of Pétain's defenders was to say that he protected French Jews. This argument has now fallen."

CONCLUSION

After the war, Charles de Gaulle — the leader of the Free French resistance, and France's first post-war president — declared all of Vichy's laws null and void. His position was simple: Vichy was never the real France. The Republic had just been interrupted. It went to sleep in 1940, woke up in 1944. And if Vichy wasn't the real France, then France bore no responsibility.

That was the official position for fifty years.

It held until 1995. Jacques Chirac, standing at the site where French police had arrested over thirteen thousand Jews in 1942, said what no French president had said before:

"France, the home of the Enlightenment and of the rights of man, delivered those she should have protected to their executioners."

Not Vichy. France.

I want to be fair about one thing. Seventy-five percent of Jews living in France survived the war. That's a higher rate than almost any other occupied country. But that survival wasn't because of this law. It was because of ordinary French people who chose to protect their neighbours despite what their government was doing.

The law we just read didn't protect anyone.
