## Academic Sources [A]

### [A1] Paxton, Robert O. *Vichy France: Old Guard and New Order, 1940-1944*
- **Publisher:** Columbia University Press, 1972 (revised 2001)
- **Key chapters:** Chapter on anti-Jewish legislation, French initiative thesis
- **Relevance:** THE foundational work proving Vichy acted independently. Paxton's central finding: France didn't need German pressure to persecute Jews.
- **NotebookLM filename:** `[A1] Paxton-Vichy-France.pdf`
- **Priority:** CRITICAL — the book that changed everything about Vichy scholarship

### [A2] Marrus, Michael R. and Robert O. Paxton. *Vichy France and the Jews*
- **Publisher:** Stanford University Press, 1981 (revised 1995)
- **Key chapters:** Analysis of the Statut des Juifs drafting process, Alibert's role, Pétain's involvement
- **Relevance:** The definitive academic study of Vichy anti-Jewish policy. Goes further than *Vichy France* on the specific legislation.
- **NotebookLM filename:** `[A2] Marrus-Paxton-Vichy-Jews.pdf`
- **Priority:** CRITICAL — detailed analysis of the statute itself

### [A3] Klarsfeld, Serge. *Vichy-Auschwitz: Le rôle de Vichy dans la solution finale de la question juive en France*
- **Publisher:** Fayard, 1983-1985 (2 volumes)
- **Key chapters:** Documentation of Vichy's role in deportations; includes primary documents
- **Relevance:** Klarsfeld is the archivist who discovered the Pétain annotations. His document collection is unparalleled.
- **NotebookLM filename:** `[A3] Klarsfeld-Vichy-Auschwitz.pdf`
- **Priority:** HIGH — primary document compiler, discoverer of Pétain draft
- **Note:** In French. May need to use relevant excerpts.

### [A4] Jackson, Julian. *France: The Dark Years, 1940-1944*
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press, 2001
- **Key chapters:** Chapters on Vichy ideology, the statut des Juifs, collaboration vs resistance
- **Relevance:** Modern synthesis incorporating Paxton's work + newer archival discoveries. Balanced assessment.
- **NotebookLM filename:** `[A4] Jackson-France-Dark-Years.pdf`
- **Priority:** HIGH — the modern standard synthesis

### [A5] Weisberg, Richard H. *Vichy Law and the Holocaust in France*
- **Publisher:** New York University Press, 1996
- **Key chapters:** Legal analysis of Vichy anti-Jewish legislation
- **Relevance:** Treats the laws as legal texts (not just historical events) — perfect for clause-by-clause analysis
- **NotebookLM filename:** `[A5] Weisberg-Vichy-Law.pdf`
- **Priority:** HIGH — legal analysis perspective matches our format

### [A6] Poznanski, Renée. *Jews in France during World War II*
- **Publisher:** Brandeis University Press/University Press of New England, 2001
- **Relevance:** Social history of how the laws affected daily life
- **NotebookLM filename:** `[A6] Poznanski-Jews-France-WWII.pdf`
- **Priority:** MEDIUM — humanizes the legal provisions

### [A7] Rousso, Henry. *The Vichy Syndrome: History and Memory in France since 1944*
- **Publisher:** Harvard University Press, 1991
- **Relevance:** How France remembered (and suppressed memory of) Vichy — explains why the myth persisted
- **NotebookLM filename:** `[A7] Rousso-Vichy-Syndrome.pdf`
- **Priority:** MEDIUM — explains the myth's persistence
