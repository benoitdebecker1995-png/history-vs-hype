# Pétain's Handwritten Annotations on the Draft Statut des Juifs

**Source:** Two-page typed draft with handwritten annotations, discovered by Serge Klarsfeld, announced October 3, 2010 at the Mémorial de la Shoah.
**Images:** `documents/Statut_des_Juifs_-_page_1.jpg`, `documents/Statut_des_Juifs_-_page_2.jpg`

---

## How to Read This Document

The draft is a **typed document** with **handwritten annotations in ink** (Pétain's hand). Red pencil marks "Projet" (Draft) across the top-left corner. The stamp reads "DOCUMENT CONFIDENTIEL."

Every single handwritten change **made the law harsher**. Not one annotation softened it.

---

## PAGE 1 — Articles 1 and 2

### Title
- **Typed:** LOI PORTANT STATUT DES JUIFS
- **Handwritten (red):** "Projet" (Draft) — stamped diagonally across top-left
- **Stamp:** "DOCUMENT CONFIDENTIEL" (top-left, partially visible)

### Article 1 (Definition of "Jewish")
- **Typed text:** "Est regardé comme juif, pour l'application de la présente loi, toute personne issue de trois grands-parents de race juive ou de deux grands-parents de la même race, si son conjoint lui-même est juif."
- **Pétain's annotations:** None visible on Article 1 in this draft.
- **Note:** However, scholars confirm the final published version matches this text. The racial definition — defining Jewishness by grandparents' "race" without defining how *their* race was determined — was already in the typed draft.

### Article 2 (Professional Exclusions) — HEAVILY ANNOTATED

The typed draft lists positions banned to Jews. Pétain's handwriting **adds** entire categories:

#### Annotation 1: Expanding the judiciary ban
- **Typed draft ends at:** "...Tribunaux de 1ère instance et toutes juridictions d'ordre professionnel."
- **Pétain adds in handwriting:** "justices de paix" (Justices of the Peace)
- **Translation:** Pétain personally extended the ban from higher courts down to the lowest level of local justice — the village magistrate courts where ordinary French people settled everyday disputes.
- **This appears in the final law:** Yes — "Justices de Paix" is in the published Article 2.

#### Annotation 2: Banning Jews from ALL elected bodies
- **Pétain adds in handwriting:** "toutes assemblées issues de l'élection"
- **Translation:** "all assemblies arising from election"
- **Significance:** The typed draft banned Jews from specific named institutions. Pétain's handwriting added a **blanket ban on all elected assemblies** — municipal councils, departmental councils, any elected body whatsoever. This is a catch-all that ensures no elected position anywhere in France or its empire could be held by a Jew.
- **This appears in the final law:** Yes — "toutes assemblées issues de l'élection" is in the published Article 2, paragraph 1.

#### Annotation 3: Adding colonial inspectors
- **Pétain adds in handwriting near the colonial officials section:** "inspection des colonies"
- **Translation:** "colonial inspectorate"
- **Significance:** The typed draft already banned Jews from being Governors-General and Secretaries-General of colonies. Pétain added the inspectorate — the officials who *oversaw* colonial governance. This closed a loophole: Jews couldn't govern colonies AND couldn't inspect how colonies were governed.
- **This appears in the final law:** Yes — "inspecteurs des colonies" is in the published Article 2, paragraph 3.

#### Annotation 4: Expanding the teaching ban
- **Pétain adds in handwriting near education section:** "tout personnel enseignant" (all teaching personnel)
- **Translation:** The typed draft listed specific educational administrators (Rectors, Academy Inspectors, School Directors). Pétain's handwriting broadened this to **all teaching staff** — not just administrators, but every teacher at every level.
- **This appears in the final law:** Yes — "Membres des corps enseignants" (Members of the teaching corps) became its own paragraph (Article 2, paragraph 4) in the published version.

## PAGE 2 — Articles 3, 4, and 5 (renumbered)

### Header annotation
- **Pétain writes at top:** "paragraphe 6 de l'art 2"
- **Translation:** "paragraph 6 of article 2"
- **Significance:** Pétain reorganized the structure. What was originally a separate Article 3 (about state-subsidized enterprises) got **absorbed into Article 2** as paragraph 6. This made Article 2 even more comprehensive — a single massive article covering ALL professional exclusions.

### Original Article 3 → Became Article 2, paragraph 6
- **Typed:** "ARTICLE 3. Les Juifs ne peuvent remplir les fonctions d'administrateur, de Directeur, de Secrétaire général dans les entreprises bénéficiaires de concessions ou de subventions accordées par une collectivité publique..."
- **Pétain crosses out "3"** and annotates it as paragraph 6 of Article 2.
- **In the final law:** This became Article 2, paragraph 6: "Administrateurs, directeurs, secrétaires généraux dans les entreprises bénéficiaires de concessions ou de subventions accordées par une collectivité publique, postes à la nomination du Gouvernement dans les entreprises d'intérêt général."

### Original Article 4 → Became Article 3 (THE MOST SIGNIFICANT CHANGE)

- **Typed:** "ARTICLE 4." — Pétain **crosses out "4"** and writes **"3"**
- **Typed heading:** "L'accès et l'exercice de toutes les fonctions publiques autres que celles énumérées aux articles 2 et 3 ne sont ouverts aux Juifs que s'ils peuvent exciper des conditions suivantes :"

#### THE DELETED PROTECTION — Condition (a):
- **Typed:** "a) être descendant de Juifs nés français ou naturalisés avant l'année 1860;"
- **Pétain STRIKES THROUGH THE ENTIRE LINE**
- **Translation of what was deleted:** "a) to be a descendant of Jews born French or naturalized before the year 1860;"
- **What this means:** The original draft included a **grandfather clause** protecting established French Jewish families — those whose ancestors had been French citizens since before 1860 (predating even the Third Republic). These were families who had been French for 80+ years, fully integrated into French society. **Pétain personally crossed this out.** In one pen stroke, he eliminated the distinction between "old French Jews" and recent immigrants. The protection that would have shielded the most assimilated, longest-established Jewish families in France — gone by Pétain's hand.
- **In the final law:** This condition does NOT appear. The final Article 3 lists only military service exceptions (war citations, Légion d'honneur). The 1860 protection was erased.

#### Remaining conditions (kept but renumbered):
- **b)** "avoir été cité au cours de la campagne 1914-1918 ou au moins être titulaire de la carte de combattant 1914-1918." — KEPT (became final Article 3, condition 1)
- **c)** "avoir été cité à l'ordre du jour au cours de la campagne 1939-1940." — KEPT (became condition 2)
- **d)** "être décoré de la Légion d'Honneur à titre militaire ou de la médaille militaire." — KEPT (became condition 3)
- **Pétain also adds:** "de guerre" after a reference — specifying these must be *wartime* decorations, not peacetime honors.

### Original Article 5 → Became Article 4

- **Typed:** "ARTICLE 5." — Pétain **crosses out "5"** and writes **"4"**
- **Typed text about liberal professions:** "...sont permis aux Juifs dans une proportion fixée, pour chaque catégorie, par règlements d'Administration publique."
- **Pétain adds handwriting between lines:** "s'il y a lieu" and additional qualifying text
- **Translation:** "if there is reason to" — This annotation appears to modify the quota system for liberal professions, potentially making it more restrictive.
- **Additional handwritten text appears to add:** language about the quotas being mandatory rather than optional ("n'avait fait pour à mourir" — partially legible, suggests strengthening enforcement language).

## Summary: Every Change Made It Worse

| # | What Pétain Changed | Effect | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Added "Justices de Paix" | Extended judiciary ban to lowest courts | **HARSHER** |
| 2 | Added "toutes assemblées issues de l'élection" | Blanket ban on all elected office | **HARSHER** |
| 3 | Added "inspection des colonies" | Closed colonial governance loophole | **HARSHER** |
| 4 | Added "tout personnel enseignant" | Expanded from administrators to ALL teachers | **HARSHER** |
| 5 | Reorganized Art. 3 → Art. 2 §6 | Made Article 2 a comprehensive exclusion list | **STRUCTURAL** |
| 6 | **STRUCK OUT the 1860 protection** | Removed protection for established French Jewish families | **HARSHER** |
| 7 | Added "de guerre" qualifier | Narrowed military exception to wartime only | **HARSHER** |
| 8 | Modified quota language (Art. 5→4) | Strengthened enforcement of professional quotas | **HARSHER** |

**Not a single annotation softened the law. Every mark Pétain made expanded who it targeted or removed a protection.**

## For the Script — Key Visual Moments

1. **The strikethrough of the 1860 clause** — Most powerful visual. A single pen stroke erasing 80 years of citizenship.
2. **"toutes assemblées issues de l'élection"** — Show the handwriting adding a blanket ban on elected office.
3. **"tout personnel enseignant"** — From banning school directors to banning every teacher.
4. **The red "Projet"** — Proof this was a working draft, not a final copy.
5. **"DOCUMENT CONFIDENTIEL"** — This was meant to stay hidden.

## Note on Authentication

Serge Klarsfeld discovered this document and announced it on October 3, 2010, the 70th anniversary of the statute, at the Mémorial de la Shoah in Paris. The handwriting has been confirmed as Pétain's. The document is held at the Mémorial de la Shoah.
