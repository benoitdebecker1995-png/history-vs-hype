# Translation — Loi du 3 octobre 1940 portant statut des Juifs

**Source Language:** French
**Translator:** Claude (manual clause-by-clause)
**Date:** 2026-02-19
**Document Context:** Vichy France anti-Jewish statute, October 3, 1940

---

## Article premier

### Original
> Est regardé comme juif, pour l'application de la présente loi, toute personne issue de trois grands-parents de race juive ou de deux grands-parents de la même race, si son conjoint lui-même est juif.

### Translation
For the application of the present law, any person descended from three grandparents of the Jewish race, or from two grandparents of the same race if their spouse is themselves Jewish, is regarded as Jewish.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"race juive" (Jewish race):** The statute uses biological/racial language — "race" — not religious language. This is a racial classification system, not a religious one.
2. **"Est regardé comme" (Is regarded as):** Legal fiction language — the state defines who "is" Jewish regardless of self-identification, religious practice, or belief.
3. **"conjoint" (spouse):** The spousal clause means someone with only 2 Jewish grandparents becomes legally Jewish through marriage — marriage to another Jew triggers reclassification.

> **SURPRISE (MAJOR):** The law never defines what makes a *grandparent* Jewish. The racial definition is circular — "Jewish" is defined by descent from people who are "Jewish." This logical impossibility was deliberate: it gave authorities maximum discretion in application.

## Article 2

### Original
> L'accès et l'exercice des fonctions publiques et mandats énumérés ci-après sont interdits aux Juifs :
> 1. Chef de l'État, membre du gouvernement, Conseil d'État, Conseil de l'Ordre national de la Légion d'honneur, Cour de Cassation, Cour des comptes, Corps des Mines, Corps des Ponts et Chaussées, Inspection générale des Finances, Cours d'appel, Tribunaux de première instance, Justices de Paix, toutes juridictions d'ordre professionnel et toutes assemblées issues de l'élection ;
> 2. Agents relevant, du, département des Affaires étrangères, secrétaires généraux des départements ministériels, directeurs généraux, directeurs des administrations centrales des ministères, préfets, sous-préfets, secrétaires généraux des préfectures, inspecteurs généraux des services administratifs au ministère de l'Intérieur, fonctionnaires de tous grades attachés à tous services de police ;
> 3. Résidents généraux, gouverneurs généraux, gouverneurs et secrétaires généraux des colonies, inspecteurs des colonies ;
> 4. Membres des corps enseignants ;
> 5. Officiers des Armées de terre, de Mer et de l'Air ;
> 6. Administrateurs, directeurs, secrétaires généraux dans les entreprises bénéficiaires de concessions ou de subventions accordées par une collectivité publique, postes à la nomination du Gouvernement dans les entreprises d'intérêt général.

### Translation
Access to and exercise of the public functions and offices listed below are forbidden to Jews:

1. Head of State, member of the government, Council of State, Council of the National Order of the Legion of Honor, Court of Cassation, Court of Auditors, Corps of Mines, Corps of Bridges and Roads, General Inspectorate of Finance, Courts of Appeal, Courts of First Instance, Justices of the Peace, all professional courts and all elected assemblies;

2. Officials of the Department of Foreign Affairs, secretaries-general of ministerial departments, directors-general, directors of central ministry administrations, prefects, sub-prefects, secretaries-general of prefectures, inspectors-general of administrative services at the Ministry of the Interior, civil servants of all grades attached to all police services;

3. Residents-general, governors-general, governors and secretaries-general of the colonies, inspectors of the colonies;

4. Members of teaching bodies;

5. Officers of the Army, Navy, and Air Force;

6. Administrators, directors, secretaries-general in enterprises receiving concessions or subsidies from a public authority, positions appointed by the Government in enterprises of general interest.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"Conseil d'État" (Council of State):** France's highest administrative court and legal advisory body to the government — one of the most powerful institutions in the French state.
2. **"Cour de Cassation" (Court of Cassation):** France's highest court of appeal for civil and criminal matters — equivalent to a supreme court.
3. **"Corps des Mines / Corps des Ponts et Chaussées":** Elite technical civil service corps — France's most prestigious administrative bodies, producing the country's top engineers and administrators.
4. **"préfets" (prefects):** Government-appointed administrators of departments (regional units) — direct representatives of central state authority throughout France.
5. **"toutes assemblées issues de l'élection" (all elected assemblies):** This bans Jews from ALL elected office — municipal councils, departmental councils, parliament. Total political exclusion.
6. **"entreprises d'intérêt général" (enterprises of general interest):** Covers public utilities, transport, infrastructure — any business serving the public interest, even if privately owned.

> **SURPRISE (MAJOR):** The scope is totalizing. This isn't just "senior government positions" — it covers everything from the Head of State down to local justices of the peace, from the military officer corps to teachers, from diplomats to police officers of "all grades." Sub-section 6 extends the ban into the private sector wherever public money or government appointments are involved. The phrase "toutes assemblées issues de l'élection" eliminates democratic participation entirely.

## Article 3

### Original
> L'accès et l'exercice de toutes les fonctions publiques autres que celles énumérées à l'art. 2 ne sont ouverts aux Juifs que s'ils peuvent exciper de l'une des conditions suivantes :
> 1. Être titulaire de la Carte de combattant 1914-1918 ou avoir été cité au cours de la campagne 1914-1918 ;
> 2. Avoir été cité, à l'ordre du jour au cours de la campagne 1939-1940 ;
> 3. Être décoré de la légion d'honneur à titre militaire ou de la Médaille militaire.

### Translation
Access to and exercise of all public functions other than those listed in Article 2 are open to Jews only if they can invoke one of the following conditions:

1. To hold the Veteran's Card of 1914-1918 or to have been mentioned in dispatches during the 1914-1918 campaign;
2. To have been mentioned in dispatches during the 1939-1940 campaign;
3. To be decorated with the Legion of Honor for military service or with the Military Medal.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"exciper de" (invoke/plead):** Formal legal term — places the burden on the Jewish person to PROVE their exception. Guilty until proven worthy.
2. **"Carte de combattant" (Veteran's Card):** Official French veteran's identification card — proof of combat service.
3. **"cité à l'ordre du jour" (mentioned in dispatches):** A specific military honor — official recognition of distinguished service in combat.

> **SURPRISE (NOTABLE):** Article 3 reveals the law's cynical architecture. The "exceptions" in Article 3 apply only to lower-level public functions NOT already banned by Article 2. A decorated Jewish war hero could still serve as a minor civil servant — but could never be a teacher, a judge, a prefect, an officer, or hold elected office. The exceptions are designed to create an illusion of fairness while the core exclusion remains total.

## Article 4

### Original
> L'accès et l'exercice des professions libérales, des professions libres, des fonctions dévolues aux officiers ministériels et à tous auxiliaires de la justice sont permis aux juifs, à moins que des règlements d'administration publique n'aient fixé pour eux une proportion déterminée. Dans ce cas, les mêmes règlements détermineront les conditions dans lesquelles aura lieu l'élimination des juifs en surnombre.

### Translation
Access to and exercise of the liberal professions, independent professions, functions assigned to ministerial officers and to all auxiliaries of justice are permitted to Jews, unless public administrative regulations have fixed a determined proportion for them. In that case, the same regulations shall determine the conditions under which the elimination of excess Jews shall take place.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"professions libérales" (liberal professions):** In French law, these are self-employed professionals: doctors, lawyers, architects, accountants, pharmacists — the educated middle class.
2. **"officiers ministériels" (ministerial officers):** Court-appointed legal officials: notaries, bailiffs, auctioneers, court clerks — officers of the court who exercise delegated state authority.
3. **"auxiliaires de la justice" (auxiliaries of justice):** Lawyers, legal clerks, and all other persons whose profession involves assisting the courts.
4. **"proportion déterminée" (determined proportion):** A quota system — the state reserves the right to cap how many Jews can practice any given profession.
5. **"l'élimination des juifs en surnombre" (the elimination of excess Jews):** The word "élimination" in this legal context means removal/expulsion from the profession. The bureaucratic euphemism is chilling in retrospect — "excess Jews" to be "eliminated."

> **SURPRISE (MAJOR):** The language of Article 4 is remarkable. It uses the word "élimination" — elimination — to describe removing Jews from their professions. While in 1940 legal French this meant "removal," the word choice is devastating in historical context. The article also creates a blank-check quota system: the government can set ANY proportion it wants, at ANY time, for ANY profession, through mere administrative regulation — no further legislation needed.

## Article 5

### Original
> Les juifs ne pourront, sans condition ni réserve, exercer l'une quelconque des professions suivantes :
> Directeurs, gérants, rédacteurs de journaux, revues, agences ou périodiques, à l'exception de publications de caractère strictement scientifique. Directeurs, administrateurs, gérants d'entreprises ayant pour objet la fabrication, l'impression, la distribution, la présentation de films cinématographiques; metteurs en scène et directeurs de prises de vues, compositeurs de scénarios, directeurs, administrateurs, gérants de salles de théâtres ou de cinématographie, entrepreneurs de spectacles, directeurs, administrateurs, gérants de toutes entreprises se rapportant à la radiodiffusion. Des règlements d'administration publique fixeront, pour chaque catégorie, les conditions dans lesquelles les autorités publiques pourront s'assurer du respect, par les intéressés, des interdictions prononcées au présent article, ainsi que les sanctions attachées à ces interdictions.

### Translation
Jews may not, without condition or reservation, exercise any of the following professions:

Directors, managers, editors of newspapers, magazines, agencies, or periodicals, with the exception of publications of a strictly scientific character. Directors, administrators, managers of enterprises engaged in the manufacture, printing, distribution, or presentation of cinematographic films; film directors and directors of photography, screenwriters, directors, administrators, managers of theaters or cinemas, entertainment producers, directors, administrators, managers of all enterprises related to radio broadcasting.

Public administrative regulations shall determine, for each category, the conditions under which public authorities may ensure compliance by the relevant parties with the prohibitions pronounced in the present article, as well as the sanctions attached to these prohibitions.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"sans condition ni réserve" (without condition or reservation):** Unlike Articles 3-4, there are NO exceptions here — no war hero exemption, no quota. Total ban.
2. **"caractère strictement scientifique" (strictly scientific character):** The single exception — Jews can still edit scientific journals. Everything else in media and culture is forbidden.
3. **"metteurs en scène" (directors/stage directors):** Covers both theater and film directors.
4. **"compositeurs de scénarios" (screenwriters):** Literally "composers of scenarios" — covers all scriptwriting.

> **SURPRISE (NOTABLE):** Article 5 is specifically about controlling narrative and public discourse. Jews are banned from every position that shapes what French people read, watch, hear, or see performed. Newspapers, magazines, cinema, theater, radio — the entire media landscape. The sole exception for "strictly scientific" publications reveals the law's purpose: it's not about professional competence, it's about controlling who shapes French culture and opinion.

## Article 6

### Original
> En aucun cas, les juifs ne peuvent faire partie des organismes chargés de représenter les professions visées aux articles 4 et 5 de la présente loi ou d'en assurer la discipline.

### Translation
Under no circumstances may Jews be members of organizations charged with representing the professions referred to in Articles 4 and 5 of the present law or with ensuring their discipline.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"En aucun cas" (Under no circumstances):** Absolute prohibition — no exceptions whatsoever, not even the war-hero exception from Article 3.
2. **"organismes chargés de représenter" (organizations charged with representing):** Professional associations, bar associations, medical boards, press councils — the self-governing bodies of each profession.
3. **"assurer la discipline" (ensure discipline):** Professional disciplinary bodies — those that regulate standards and can sanction members.

> **SURPRISE (NOTABLE):** Even where Jews are technically still permitted to practice a profession (under Article 4's quota system), they cannot participate in governing those professions. A Jewish doctor allowed to practice under the quota cannot sit on the medical board. A Jewish lawyer cannot join the bar council. This ensures Jews have no voice in the rules governing their own professional survival.

## Article 7

### Original
> Les fonctionnaires juifs visés aux articles 2 et 3 cesseront d'exercer leurs fonctions dans les deux mois qui suivront la promulgation de la présente loi. Ils seront admis à faire valoir leurs droits à la retraite, s'ils remplissent les conditions de durée de service ; à une retraite proportionnelle, s'ils ont au moins quinze ans de service ; ceux ne pouvant exciper d'aucune de ces conditions recevront leur traitement pendant une durée qui sera fixée, pour chaque catégorie, par un règlement d'administration publique.

### Translation
Jewish civil servants covered by Articles 2 and 3 shall cease exercising their functions within two months following the promulgation of the present law. They shall be entitled to claim their pension rights, if they meet the conditions of length of service; to a proportional pension, if they have at least fifteen years of service; those unable to invoke either of these conditions shall receive their salary for a period to be determined, for each category, by a public administrative regulation.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"cesseront d'exercer" (shall cease exercising):** Mandatory termination — not voluntary resignation. The state fires them.
2. **"deux mois" (two months):** The timeline: from publication on October 18, 1940, affected Jews had until approximately December 18, 1940 to leave their positions.
3. **"droits à la retraite" (pension rights):** The law provides for pensions — this is the bureaucratic machinery of orderly dispossession. You're fired, but here's your paperwork.
4. **"retraite proportionnelle" (proportional pension):** Reduced pension for those with 15+ years of service but not enough for a full pension.

> **SURPRISE (NOTABLE):** The pension provisions are the law's most insidious feature. By providing for orderly retirement, the statute presents itself as a reasonable administrative measure rather than persecution. "We're not punishing you — we're retiring you early with benefits." This bureaucratic veneer made the law easier for French institutions to implement without moral crisis. The two-month deadline also means: by Christmas 1940, every Jewish teacher, judge, police officer, and military officer in France was gone.

## Article 8

### Original
> Par décret individuel pris en Conseil d'état et dûment motivé, les Juifs qui, dans les domaines littéraires, scientifiques, artistique ont rendu des services exceptionnels à l'État français, pourront être relevés des interdictions prévues par la présente loi.
> Ces décrets et les motifs qui les justifient seront publiés au Journal officiel.

### Translation
By individual decree issued by the Council of State and duly justified, Jews who, in the literary, scientific, or artistic domains, have rendered exceptional services to the French State, may be relieved of the prohibitions provided for by the present law.

These decrees and the grounds justifying them shall be published in the Journal officiel.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"décret individuel" (individual decree):** Case-by-case exceptions — not a category exemption but a personal, named exception for specific individuals.
2. **"dûment motivé" (duly justified):** The grounds must be stated — the exception must be publicly defended.
3. **"services exceptionnels" (exceptional services):** Not merely "good service" but "exceptional" — an extraordinarily high bar.
4. **"pourront être relevés" (may be relieved):** "May" not "shall" — discretionary, not automatic. Even exceptional service doesn't guarantee exemption.

> **SURPRISE (NOTABLE):** The exception clause covers only literary, scientific, and artistic achievement — not military service, not commerce, not public administration. A Jewish war hero who lost a leg at Verdun gets no exception under Article 8 (only the limited Article 3 exception for lower-level posts). But a Jewish novelist who won a literary prize *might* get an individual exemption — if the Council of State decides to grant one, publishes its reasons, and can justify "exceptional services to the French State." The publication requirement means any exempted Jew is publicly identified.

## Article 9

### Original
> La présente loi est applicable à l'Algérie, aux colonies, pays de protectorat et territoires sous mandat.

### Translation
The present law is applicable to Algeria, to the colonies, protectorate countries, and mandated territories.

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"l'Algérie" (Algeria):** Algeria was legally part of metropolitan France (divided into three départements), not a colony. Listing it separately emphasizes that the law applies to Algeria's large Jewish population specifically.
2. **"colonies" (colonies):** French colonial empire: Indochina, West Africa, Equatorial Africa, Madagascar, etc.
3. **"pays de protectorat" (protectorate countries):** Morocco, Tunisia, and others where France exercised control while maintaining nominal local sovereignty.
4. **"territoires sous mandat" (mandated territories):** League of Nations mandates: Syria and Lebanon, Cameroon, Togo — territories France administered under international authority.

> **SURPRISE (MAJOR):** In one sentence, Article 9 extends racial exclusion across the entire French empire — from Hanoi to Dakar, from Beirut to Antananarivo. This is not a law for metropolitan France alone. It applies to Algeria (where the Crémieux Decree of 1870 had granted citizenship to Algerian Jews — that decree was separately revoked four days later on October 7). It applies to mandated territories that France administered under League of Nations authority — meaning France used international trusteeship to impose racial law. The global scope reveals this as an imperial project of racial classification.

## Article 10

### Original
> Le présent acte sera publié au Journal officiel et exécuté comme loi de l'État.
>
> Fait à Vichy, le 3 octobre 1940.
> Philippe Pétain.
>
> Par le Maréchal de France, chef de l'État français : Le vice-président du conseil, Pierre Laval.
> Le garde des sceaux, ministre secrétaire d'État à la justice, Raphaël Alibert.
> Le ministre secrétaire d'État à l'intérieur, Marcel Peyrouton.
> Le ministre secrétaire d'État aux affaires étrangères, Paul Baudoin.
> Le ministre secrétaire d'État à la guerre, Général Huntziger.
> Le ministre secrétaire d'État aux finances, Yves Bouthillier.
> Le ministre secrétaire d'État à la marine, Amiral Darlan.
> Le ministre secrétaire d'État à la production industrielle et au travail, René Belin.
> Le ministre secrétaire d'État à l'agriculture, Pierre Caziot.
>
> (Journal Officiel du 18 Octobre 1940)

### Translation
The present act shall be published in the Journal officiel and executed as a law of the State.

Done at Vichy, October 3, 1940.
Philippe Pétain.

By the Marshal of France, Head of the French State: The Vice-President of the Council, Pierre Laval.
The Keeper of the Seals, Minister Secretary of State for Justice, Raphaël Alibert.
The Minister Secretary of State for the Interior, Marcel Peyrouton.
The Minister Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs, Paul Baudoin.
The Minister Secretary of State for War, General Huntziger.
The Minister Secretary of State for Finance, Yves Bouthillier.
The Minister Secretary of State for the Navy, Admiral Darlan.
The Minister Secretary of State for Industrial Production and Labor, René Belin.
The Minister Secretary of State for Agriculture, Pierre Caziot.

(Journal Officiel of October 18, 1940)

**Legal Terms:**
1. **"Maréchal de France, chef de l'État français" (Marshal of France, Head of the French State):** Pétain's self-given title — not "President of the Republic" but "Head of the French State," reflecting the abolition of the Third Republic.
2. **"Garde des sceaux" (Keeper of the Seals):** Traditional title of France's Minister of Justice — literally, the keeper of the state seal used to authenticate laws. Raphaël Alibert, who drafted this statute.

> **SURPRISE (NOTABLE):** Nine ministers co-signed. This was not one man's decision or a quiet bureaucratic act — it was a collective act of the entire Vichy cabinet. Every major ministry: Justice, Interior, Foreign Affairs, War, Finance, Navy, Industry, Agriculture. The collective signature means collective responsibility. René Belin, the Minister of Industrial Production and Labor, was notably a former trade union leader — even the labor left of the Vichy government signed.

## Translation Summary

**Total Articles:** 10
**Translation Method:** Clause-by-clause with full document context
**Surprises Found:** 4 major, 5 notable

### Key Translation Observations

1. **"Race" not "Religion":** The law consistently uses "race juive" (Jewish race), never "religion juive" (Jewish religion). This is a racial statute, not a religious restriction.

2. **"Élimination" in Article 4:** The use of "élimination des juifs en surnombre" (elimination of excess Jews) — while meaning professional removal in 1940 legal context — is the language of the statute itself.

3. **Escalating totality:** Articles progress from definition (1) → total exclusion from power (2) → narrow exceptions (3) → quota control of professions (4) → total media ban (5) → ban from professional governance (6) → implementation timeline (7) → narrow individual exceptions (8) → imperial application (9) → collective authorization (10).

4. **The exception architecture:** The law is designed to appear measured. Exceptions exist — but each one is narrower than it seems. Article 3 exceptions don't apply to Article 2 positions. Article 8 exceptions are individual, discretionary, and public. The structure creates a veneer of reasonableness over total exclusion.

5. **No German fingerprints:** Nothing in this text references Germany, the armistice, or the occupation. It reads as a purely French law, because it was.
