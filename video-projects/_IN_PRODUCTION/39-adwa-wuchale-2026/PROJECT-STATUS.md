## The Core Story

**The document:** Article 17 of the Treaty of Wuchale (1889), written in both Amharic and Italian.

**What it reveals:**
- **Amharic version:** Ethiopia "may, if he desires to" (*yichalachewal*) use Italian mediation in foreign affairs — optional.
- **Italian version:** Ethiopia "is obliged to" conduct all foreign affairs through Italy — mandatory protectorate.
- **Count Antonelli** drafted BOTH versions. Spoke BOTH languages fluently. Knew Menelik didn't read Italian.
- Italy immediately notified European powers that Ethiopia was now an Italian protectorate.
- Menelik discovered the discrepancy, refused to renew the treaty (1893), repudiated it entirely.
- Italy invaded. Ethiopia won at the **Battle of Adwa (March 1, 1896)** — the only African army to defeat a European colonial force in the age of empire.

**Modern relevance:**
- Adwa Day is Ethiopia's national holiday (March 1)
- The battle is the symbolic founding event of Pan-Africanism
- Marcus Garvey, Kwame Nkrumah, and Haile Selassie all cited it
- The African Union headquarters is in Addis Ababa — partly because of this legacy
- Decolonization discourse still invokes Adwa as proof African resistance was possible
