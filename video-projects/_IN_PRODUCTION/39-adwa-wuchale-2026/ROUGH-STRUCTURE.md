## ACT 1: THE TREATY TRAP (1:00-4:00)

### 1A. Context: The Scramble for Africa reaches Ethiopia (1:00-2:00)
- 1880s — Europe is carving up Africa. Berlin Conference just happened.
- Italy occupies Eritrea (1890) — wants to push south into Ethiopia
- Ethiopia under Emperor Menelik II — shrewd diplomat, modernizing the military
- Menelik is playing European powers against each other (buying weapons from France and Russia)
- **B-ROLL:** Map of colonial Africa c. 1890. Italian colonial territory in Eritrea. Portrait/painting of Menelik II.

### 1B. Count Antonelli and the negotiation (2:00-3:00)
- Pietro Antonelli — grandson of a Vatican Cardinal, Italian diplomat since age 26
- Fluent enough in Amharic to negotiate, but — critically — fluent enough to know the versions differed
- Sent to get maximum concessions from Menelik
- The provisional draft was written in Rome before Antonelli arrived — the protectorate clause was already there
- **KEY REVEAL:** The treaty itself claims "the two versions agreeing perfectly with each other" — a lie written into the document
- **B-ROLL:** Treaty text showing the "agreeing perfectly" clause. Portrait of Antonelli.

### 1C. Article 17 — clause by clause (3:00-4:00)
- **THE CORE SCENE:** Split-screen, Italian left, English-of-Amharic right
- Italian: "consente di servirsi" — "consents to use" → legally mandatory
- Amharic: "yichalachewal" — "may, if he desires" → clearly optional
- One verb. Two meanings. The difference between sovereignty and protectorate.
- Italy immediately notified Britain, France, Germany, Russia that Ethiopia was now an Italian protectorate (October 1889)
- **Pattern interrupt:** "So what happens when Menelik finds out?"
- **B-ROLL:** Split-screen treaty comparison. Map of Europe with arrows showing Italy's notification to capitals.

## ACT 2: MENELIK'S RESPONSE (4:00-6:30)

### 2A. The discovery (4:00-5:00)
- Early 1890 — Menelik contacts European leaders to announce his accession as Emperor
- European correspondents inform him: "Your Majesty, Italy says all your diplomatic relations go through Rome."
- September 27, 1890 — Menelik writes directly to King Umberto I, rejecting protectorate status
- **Key quote opportunity:** Find Menelik's actual words to Umberto (❓ in Jonas/Zewde)
- **B-ROLL:** Map showing diplomatic correspondence arrows. Portrait of King Umberto I.

### 2B. The circular letter (5:00-5:45)
- April 10, 1891 — Menelik sends circular letter to ALL European heads of state
- Defines Ethiopia's borders. Asserts full sovereignty. Says: I am not Italy's subject.
- **THE POWER MOVE:** He's doing exactly what Article 17's Italian version said he couldn't — conducting foreign affairs independently
- **B-ROLL:** Show the actual circular letter text (available at Somalitalk)

### 2C. Repudiation and the road to war (5:45-6:30)
- February 1893 — Menelik formally repudiates the entire Treaty of Wuchale
- Declares it null and void due to the Article 17 discrepancy
- Italy refuses to accept — doubles down on protectorate claim
- Diplomatic breakdown → border skirmishes → full invasion by 1895
- **B-ROLL:** Map showing Italian military advance from Eritrea into Ethiopian territory.

## ACT 3: THE BATTLE OF ADWA (6:30-9:00)

### 3A. Setup — two armies (6:30-7:30)
- Italy commits ~15,000-17,000 troops under General Baratieri (Italian soldiers + Eritrean askari)
- Ethiopia mobilizes 73,000-100,000+ troops — NOT a ragtag militia
- Menelik has been buying modern rifles from France and Russia for years
- Empress Taytu Betul commands her own contingent (~5,000 infantry + 600 cavalry)
- She organized logistics AND devised the strategy to cut off Italian water supply at Mekelle
- **Steelman moment:** "Italy wasn't fighting a pre-industrial army. They were fighting a state that had been preparing for this since the treaty was signed."
- **B-ROLL:** Map of troop movements. Illustration/painting of Ethiopian army. Portrait of Empress Taytu.

### 3B. March 1, 1896 (7:30-8:30)
- Italian forces advance on three columns — lose contact with each other in mountainous terrain
- Ethiopian forces exploit the gaps — surround and destroy Italian columns in detail
- ~6,000 Italian and colonial troops killed. Nearly 4,000 captured.
- **~70% of the Italian force — killed or captured in a single day.**
- General Baratieri survives but is recalled to Italy in disgrace
- **Precision note:** "The Zulu defeated the British at Isandlwana in 1879. But Britain came back with 17,000 troops and won the war. After Adwa, Italy didn't come back. They signed a treaty instead."
- **B-ROLL:** Battle map showing the three Italian columns and Ethiopian envelopment. Contemporary illustration of the battle.

### 3C. Treaty of Addis Ababa — October 1896 (8:30-9:00)
- Italy recognizes full Ethiopian sovereignty
- Nullifies the Treaty of Wuchale entirely
- Italy retains Eritrea — but abandons all claims to Ethiopia
- "The protectorate that existed only in Italian was over."
- **B-ROLL:** Map showing final borders. Treaty document if available.

## ACT 4: THE LEGACY — WHY THIS STILL MATTERS (9:00-11:00)

### 4A. Pan-Africanism's founding moment (9:00-10:00)
- Adwa didn't just save Ethiopia. It became the symbolic proof that African sovereignty was possible.
- Marcus Garvey's UNIA movement drew on Ethiopian symbolism (Psalm 68:31)
- Kwame Nkrumah cited Garvey as his greatest influence — statue at AU HQ reads "Ethiopia shall stretch forth her hands to God"
- When the OAU was founded in 1963, they chose Addis Ababa. Not because of geography. Because Ethiopia was the only African nation that was never colonized. Because of Adwa.
- Rastafari movement — Ethiopia as the promised land, divinely protected. Rooted in Adwa's legacy.
- **B-ROLL:** Haile Selassie's 1936 League of Nations speech (footage exists). OAU/AU headquarters. Adwa Victory Memorial Park in Addis Ababa.

### 4B. The modern paradox — Tigray (10:00-10:45)
- (HANDLE WITH CARE — brief, factual)
- Adwa Day is still Ethiopia's national holiday. March 1. Schools closed. Parades in Menelik Square.
- In 2024, a new Adwa Victory Memorial Museum opened — 31,000 visitors in its first month.
- But Adwa is in Tigray. During the Tigray War (2020-2022), the town of Adwa itself was occupied.
- The government invoked Adwa as a symbol of Ethiopian unity — while waging war in the very region where the battle took place.
- "The legacy of Adwa is still being contested — not by Italy, but by Ethiopians deciding what that legacy means."
- **B-ROLL:** Modern Adwa Day celebrations. Adwa Museum exterior.
