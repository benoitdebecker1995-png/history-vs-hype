## 1. THE TREATY TEXT

### Total Articles
The Treaty of Wuchale contains **20 articles** total, covering perpetual peace and friendship (Article 1), consuls (Article 2), boundary demarcation (Article 3), trade regulations, and concluding with ratification provisions (Article 20).

### Article 17 — The Critical Discrepancy

**Italian version (original Italian):**
> "Sua Maesta il Re dei Re d'Etiopia consente di servirsi del Governo di Sua Maesta il Re d'Italia per tutte le trattazioni di affari che avesse con altre potenze o governi."

**English of Italian version:** Ethiopia's sovereign *consents to use* ("consente di servirsi") the Italian government for *all* business with other powers or governments. Italian legal interpretation: Ethiopia **must** conduct foreign affairs through Italy = **protectorate**.

**English of Amharic version:**
> "His Majesty the King of Kings of Ethiopia **can** use the Government of His Majesty the King of Italy for all business with other powers or governments."

The Amharic uses permissive language — the Emperor **may** use Italian diplomatic services if he chooses. An **option**, not a mandate.

**The core linguistic issue:** Italian "consente di servirsi" was interpreted as mandatory, while the Amharic equivalent (*yichalachewal*) conveyed optionality.

## 2. COUNT PIETRO ANTONELLI

- **Born:** April 29, 1853, Rome
- **Died:** January 11, 1901
- **Family:** Grandson of **Cardinal Giacomo Antonelli** (Secretary of State to Pope Pius IX)
- **Career:** Italian diplomat, explorer, politician
- **First expedition to Ethiopia:** 1879-1881
- **Role:** Italian ambassador at court of Menelik II; chief architect/negotiator of Treaty of Wuchale

### Amharic Fluency — CONTESTED
Per Encyclopaedia Africana and Sewasew Ethiopian encyclopedia: Antonelli **knew Amharic** and reportedly accepted the Amharic reading of Article 17, but **did not change the Italian version**. Some sources describe him as "lacking full Amharic proficiency" — working knowledge but not fully fluent.

**Critical detail:** He understood enough to know the versions differed. This raises the deliberate ambiguity question.

### Instructions from Italy
The provisional draft was **"presumably written in Italy before the beginning of the negotiations"** and already included the protectorate provision. This suggests the clause was an intentional Italian strategic objective, not an accident.

## 4. THE BATTLE OF ADWA (March 1, 1896)

### Force Sizes

**Ethiopian Forces:**
- ~82,000 soldiers with swords and rifles, 20,000 spearmen, 8,000 cavalry
- Range in sources: 73,000 to 100,000+
- Armed with **modern rifles imported from France and Russia**

**Italian Forces:**
- ~14,500 men under General Oreste Baratieri
- Mix of Italian soldiers and locally recruited **askari** (Eritrean colonial troops)
- Hampered by poor reconnaissance and unfamiliarity with terrain

### Casualties — Scholarly Range

| Category | Killed | Captured |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Italian/Colonial | ~6,000 total (300 officers, 4,600 Italian rank, ~1,000 askari) | 1,865 Italians + 2,000 askari |
| Ethiopian | 4,000-7,000 killed (Berkeley estimates 7,000) | N/A |

**~70% of Italy's forces were killed or captured.**

**Sensitive detail:** 800 captured Eritrean askari had their right hands and left feet amputated (regarded as traitors to Africa). Italian prisoners were generally spared. ❓ VERIFY this in Jonas — sensitive claim needs academic sourcing.

### Key Commanders

**Ethiopian:**
- **Emperor Menelik II** — Supreme commander
- **Empress Taytu Betul** — Commanded ~5,000 infantry + 600 cavalry; organized logistics; devised strategy to cut off Italian water supply at Battle of Mekelle
- **Ras Makonnen Wolde Mikael** — Father of future Haile Selassie
- **Ras Alula Engida (Abba Nega)** — Legendary military commander
- **Fitawrari Gebeyyehu** — Killed in battle

**Italian:**
- **General Oreste Baratieri** (1841-1901) — Governor/military commander of Eritrea

### "Only African Victory" — PRECISION CHECK

**Claim as commonly stated is IMPRECISE.** The Zulu defeated the British at **Isandlwana (January 22, 1879)** — killed ~800 British soldiers. BUT:
- Britain launched a 9-month counteroffensive with 17,000 troops and won the Anglo-Zulu War by August 1879
- Zululand was subsequently colonized

**CORRECT CLAIM:** Ethiopia is the only African nation that **decisively defeated a European colonial army AND maintained its independence as a result**. Adwa was a **war-ending** victory that **preserved sovereignty**. Other African resistances (Ashanti, Mahdist Sudan, Zulu) achieved tactical victories but ultimately lost to colonial conquest.

- **Psalm 68:31** ("Ethiopia shall stretch forth her hands unto God") was the most popular text at UNIA meetings
- UNIA had an official catechism: "that Negroes will set up their own government in Africa with rulers of their own race"
- Garvey conflated "Ethiopia" with "Africa" as a whole — Ethiopia as divinely protected
- **Later criticism:** Garvey became critical of Haile Selassie during 1935 Italian invasion

- Statue at African Union HQ bears inscription: **"Ethiopia shall stretch forth her hands to God. Africa Must Unite."**

### Haile Selassie
- Central role in founding the OAU in 1963 in Addis Ababa

- Opened Ethiopia's borders to exiled African nationalists
- **1936 speech to League of Nations** (June 30, 1936) — one of most famous speeches of 20th century
- Available at Library of Congress: https://www.loc.gov/item/2021667904/

### African Union HQ in Addis Ababa
- **OAU founded May 25, 1963** with 33 signatory governments
- Ethiopia chosen because:
  1. **Never colonized** (thanks to Adwa) — unique symbolic legitimacy
  2. Haile Selassie's diplomatic role
  3. Ethiopia provided "historic legitimacy and diplomatic space"
- Adwa not **explicitly** cited in OAU founding documents, but Ethiopia's uncolonized status (direct consequence of Adwa) was the underlying reason
- **Adwa Victory Memorial Park** stands at center of Addis Ababa

### Rastafari Connection
- Emerged in Jamaica following Haile Selassie's coronation in 1930
- Roots in 18th-century Ethiopianism among enslaved Africans
- Garvey's prophecy ("Look to Africa for the crowning of a Black king") considered foundational
- Haile Selassie venerated as "Jah"
- Ethiopia's independence (secured at Adwa) central to Rastafari theology
- 1935-36 Italian invasion intensified Rastafari identification with Ethiopia

## 6. MODERN RELEVANCE

### Adwa Victory Day
- **National holiday** observed **March 2** (some sources say March 1)
- First official celebration during Haile Selassie's reign
- Parades, military performances, patriotic songs at **Menelik Square** in Addis Ababa
- Schools, banks, post offices, government offices closed
- **2024:** Adwa Victory Memorial Museum inaugurated February 11, 2024 by PM Abiy Ahmed — 31,000+ visitors in first month
- **2025 (129th anniversary):** Celebrated under theme **"Adwa: The Victory of Black People"**

### The Tigray War Paradox (2020-2022) — POWERFUL MODERN ANGLE

- **Adwa is located in the Tigray region**
- Tigray War fought between Ethiopian federal government + Eritrea vs. TPLF
- **Adwa was one of the first targets** in PM Abiy Ahmed's military operation (November 2020)
- Reports of **30+ civilians massacred by Eritrean soldiers** in Adwa town itself
- **"Adwa, not AGOA" propaganda campaign** — pro-government social media used Adwa to frame Western criticism as neo-colonial interference
- Academic study: Kebede & Pohjonen (2025) in *Media, War & Conflict* documented how pro-government Twitter campaigns invoked Adwa while obscuring atrocities
- **Critical paradox:** Government invoked Adwa as symbol of Ethiopian unity while waging war in the very region where the battle took place, against people whose ancestors fought at Adwa
- *Ethiopia Insight* (May 2025): "Adwa is not the only oppressive Ethiopian myth" — critical examination of how narrative has been instrumentalized
