## PROJECT OVERVIEW

**Concept:** Evidence-based myth-busting about the Industrial Revolution (1760-1840)

**Format:** 6-8 min long-form + 6 shorts (VidIQ clipping tool)

**Angle:** "Both extremes wrong" - Popular narrative says "progress and prosperity," counter-narrative says "exploitation and misery" - reality is nuanced

**Primary Sources:** Factory records, wage data, life expectancy statistics, child labor parliamentary reports, technological innovation records

**Examples:** Steam engine productivity gains vs. child labor conditions, rising real wages vs. horrific working conditions, increased life expectancy (eventually) vs. initial urban squalor

## KEY POSITIONING (CRITICAL)

**VidIQ Insight:** This should be positioned as **"data vs. modern myths about industrial capitalism"** NOT political advocacy.

**Frame as:**
- Popular view: "Industrial Revolution brought prosperity and progress"
- Counter-narrative: "It was just exploitation and child labor"
- Primary sources show BOTH narratives cherry-pick
- Reality: Productivity soared AND working conditions were brutal (both true)

**DO NOT frame as:**
- Pro-capitalism propaganda ("Industrial Revolution was great!")
- Anti-capitalism propaganda ("Industrial Revolution was evil!")
- Taking sides in modern political debates

**DO frame as:**
- "Here's what the wage data and factory records actually show"
- "Both modern narratives cherry-pick the history"
- Neutral economic/historical analysis
