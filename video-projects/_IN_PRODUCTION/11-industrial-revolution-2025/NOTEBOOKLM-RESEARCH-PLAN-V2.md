#### 7. **"A Farewell to Alms: A Brief Economic History of the World" - Gregory Clark (2007)**
**Author:** Gregory Clark
**Publication:** Princeton University Press, 2007
**Status:** Important BUT 18 years old - controversial thesis, note critiques

**What to Extract:**
- Malthusian trap explanation
- Real wage series 1200-1900
- Why productivity gains didn't immediately raise wages
- "Engels Pause" concept (wage stagnation 1760-1820)

**Modern Context:**
- Clark's thesis on genetic/cultural selection has been heavily criticized
- Wage data still valuable
- "Engels Pause" concept accepted but explanations debated
- Use for data, acknowledge controversial interpretation

**Specific Prompts:**
1. "What is Clark's 'Malthusian trap' explanation for wage stagnation 1760-1820, and how have recent scholars (2010+) responded to or revised this thesis?"
