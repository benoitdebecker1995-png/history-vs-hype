# Modern Relevance of the Flat Earth Myth (2023-2025)

**Research Date:** 2025-12-31
**Purpose:** Document current usage of the medieval flat earth myth in contemporary discourse

---

## 1. New Atheist Discourse

### Ongoing Usage (2024)
Despite being thoroughly debunked by historians, the medieval flat earth myth **continues to appear in atheist critiques of religion** in 2024.

**Key Finding:** The myth represents "outdated myths backed by garbled evidence peddled by non-historians who have irrelevant authority by merit of being scientists and who are motivated by an emotionally-driven ideological bias against religion."

### Academic Analysis
- The myth "is a central piece in the contemporary larger myth of the science and faith conflict"
- Used as historical "evidence" that religion inherently opposes scientific progress
- Perpetuated despite repeated historical debunking since at least 1920

### Specific Examples
**"History for Atheists" Response (2024):**
- Atheist activists continue to peddle the myth that the Medieval Church taught the earth was flat
- The myth keeps appearing in New Atheist critiques despite being "patent nonsense"
- [Source: History for Atheists podcast, iHeart 2024](https://www.iheart.com/podcast/269-history-for-atheists-79894635/episode/the-medieval-flat-earth-myth-79898520/)

**Academic Critiques (2024):**
- March 2024: Article "Science Series: The Flat Earth Myth" addresses ongoing misuse
- [Source: Is Christianity True?](https://ischristianitytrue.wordpress.com/2024/03/28/science-series-the-flat-earth-myth/)

## 2. Conspiracy Theory Communities

### Modern Flat Earth Movement (2024)
The modern flat earth movement has **surged on social media platforms**, particularly TikTok, despite scientific consensus.

### Social Media Spread
**University of Cincinnati Study (March 2024):**
- Flat Earth misinformation spreads rapidly on social media
- Short clips and edited videos make ordinary features of the world look suspicious
- **Algorithms push clips to viewers who already distrust institutions**
- [Source: UC News](https://www.uc.edu/news/articles/2024/03/flat-earth-misinformation-spreads-on-social-media-says-ucs-jeffrey-blevins.html)

**TikTok Presence:**
- Active hashtags and viral content featuring pilots, celebrities
- "Conspiracy culture performs well on any social media platform"
- Taglines about Flat Earth keep viewers engaged "because it's just too weird not to watch"
- [Source: Rolling Stone analysis of TikTok trends](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/flat-earth-conspiracy-theory-podcast-1311262/)

### The Ironic Twist
**Modern flat-earthers cite the medieval myth to support their claims**, yet that very myth was invented by 19th-century scientific rationalists—their supposed "enemies."

- Rationalists like Draper (1874) and White (1896) created the myth to make religion look backward
- By claiming "the Church used to teach flat earth," conspiracy theorists fall for **anticlerical propaganda** designed to make believers look ignorant
- [Source: Multiple historical analyses](https://www.mdpi.com/2313-5778/8/2/32)

## 3. Educational Debates and Textbook Controversies

### Ongoing Textbook Problem
While no specific 2023-2024 controversy focused solely on medieval flat earth beliefs, the myth **persists in popular culture and some educational materials**.

**Historical Literacy Crisis:**
- Misconception that medieval people believed earth was flat continues into 21st century
- **Virtually every thinker of the 1,000-year medieval period affirmed spherical earth**
- Historians note "there was scarcely a Christian scholar of the Middle Ages who did not acknowledge [Earth's] sphericity"
- [Source: Multiple historical scholarship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myth_of_the_flat_Earth)

### Science Education Response (December 2024)
**Emphasis on Critical Thinking:**
- Science education now emphasizes critical thinking and evidence-based reasoning
- Teaching students how to evaluate claims helps counter misinformation
- Scientists and educators engage with flat earthers to promote scientific literacy
- [Source: Facts.net, December 2024](https://facts.net/earth-and-life-science/35-facts-about-flat-earth-myth/)

### Texas Textbook Controversy (2023)
While not about medieval myths specifically, the 2023 Texas textbook battles show **ongoing tensions between science education and ideological pressures**:
- Republican-controlled Texas State Board of Education rejected most proposed textbooks for 8th grade
- Only 5 of 12 textbooks approved (focused on climate change and evolution, not flat earth)
- Demonstrates broader pattern of textbook politicization
- [Source: Texas Tribune, November 2023](https://www.texastribune.org/2023/11/17/texas-climate-textbooks-education-SBOE/)

## 5. Academic Research (2023-2024)

### MDPI Study (March 2024)
"The 'Global' Deception: Flat-Earth Conspiracy Theory between Science and Religion"
- Examined modern flat-earth discourse from 2016-2023
- **Key finding:** In the United States, flat-earth narrative shows accentuated religious character attributable to conservative Christianity
- American flat earthers base arguments on biblical text
- Consider criticisms as expressions of secularist thought opposed to Christian faith
- [Source: MDPI Genealogy journal](https://www.mdpi.com/2313-5778/8/2/32)

### Topics in Cognitive Science (2024)
"Conspiracy Theory as Individual and Group Behavior: Observations from the Flat Earth International Conference"
- Analyzed flat earth beliefs as group phenomenon
- Documented community dynamics and social reinforcement
- [Source: Wiley Online Library](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/tops.12662)

## 6. Why the Myth Persists (2024 Analysis)

### Community and Belonging
**Not About Evidence:**
- Flat Earth claims often survive **not because they have strong evidence**
- Offer a **feeling of belonging**
- Frame themselves as truth seekers who stand apart from mainstream thinking
- [Source: Multiple 2024 analyses](https://www.uc.edu/news/articles/2024/03/flat-earth-misinformation-spreads-on-social-media-says-ucs-jeffrey-blevins.html)

### Broader Trend
- Part of broader trend of distrust, conspiracy theories, rejection of scientific evidence
- Continues to generate viral content across platforms despite being scientifically debunked

---

## Modern Consequences: Why This Myth Matters

### 1. Erodes Trust in Historians
When people believe the medieval myth, they're less likely to trust **actual historical expertise** about other topics.

### 2. Fuels Science vs. Religion Conflict
The myth **perpetuates false warfare narrative** between science and religion, obscuring their complex historical relationship.

### 3. Gateway to Conspiracy Thinking
Believing one debunked historical myth can open pathways to **believing other conspiracy theories**.

### 4. Undermines Science Education
When people think medieval scholars were ignorant, they're more susceptible to **modern anti-science movements**.
