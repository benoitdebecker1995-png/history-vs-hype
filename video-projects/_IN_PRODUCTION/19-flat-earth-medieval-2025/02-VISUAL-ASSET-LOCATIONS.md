## 1. Bede's "De temporum ratione" - The "Playing Ball" Quote

### Primary Manuscript: St. Gall, Stiftsbibliothek, Cod. Sang. 250

**Manuscript Details:**
- **Date:** Compiled in latter half of 9th century
- **Location:** Abbey library of Saint Gall
- **Content:** 645-page astronomical and computistical encyclopedia in Latin
- **Bede's text location:** Pages 164-425
- **Significance:** "Numerous, detailed illustrations" including spherical earth proofs

**Digital Access:**
- **Direct URL:** https://www.e-codices.unifr.ch/en/list/one/csg/0250
- **Platform:** e-codices (Virtual Manuscript Library of Switzerland)
- **Status:** FREE ACCESS - Complete digital reproduction with scholarly descriptions
- **IIIF-compliant:** Also viewable via Biblissima platform
- **Alternative URL:** https://iiif.biblissima.fr/collections/manifest/3ba842503f55578a1c06f845947f15179d02e6fe

**Specific Pages for B-roll:**
- Individual pages available on Flickr (e-codices account):
  - Page 485: https://www.flickr.com/photos/e-codices/7846498836
  - Page 515: https://www.flickr.com/photos/e-codices/7846507254
  - Page 483: https://www.flickr.com/photos/e-codices/7846496456/
  - Page 481: https://www.flickr.com/photos/e-codices/7846492148/

**What to Show:**
- Tracking shot over Latin text in Chapter 32
- Close-up on "instar potius pilae" (like a ball)
- Carolingian minuscule script from 9th century
- Astronomical diagrams

**Copyright:** Public domain (9th century manuscript)

**Sources:**
- [e-codices main page](https://www.e-codices.unifr.ch/en/list/one/csg/0250)
- [Codex Sangallensis 250 - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Codex_Sangallensis_250)

## 2. Sacrobosco's "Tractatus de Sphaera" - Ship and Mast Diagram

### Primary Manuscript: Modena, Biblioteca Estense Universitaria

**Manuscript Details:**
- **Shelfmark:** α.X.2.14 = Lat.209
- **Alternative name:** "De Sphaera Estense" or "Sphaerae coelestis et planetarum descriptio"
- **Creator:** Cristoforo de Predis
- **Date:** c. 1450-1460
- **Location:** Milan, Italy
- **Content:** Astronomical and cosmological illustrations related to Sacrobosco's work

**Digital Access:**
- **Platform:** Estense Digital Library
- **URL:** https://edl.cultura.gov.it/
- **Search term:** α.X.2.14 or Lat.209
- **Status:** Recently launched digital platform

**Stanford University Exhibit:**
- **Direct link:** https://exhibits.stanford.edu/medieval/catalog/112-25169
- **Collection:** Medieval Collections at Stanford - Spotlight Exhibits
- **Provides:** High-quality images and scholarly description

**Alternative Access:**
- **Wikimedia Commons:** Category:De sphaera mundi
  - URL: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:De_sphaera_mundi
  - Multiple manuscript versions available
  - Public domain images

**What to Show:**
- 13th-century diagram of ship approaching land
- Signal visible from masthead but not from deck
- "Bulge of the water" illustration
- Lunar eclipse showing curved Earth shadow

**Copyright:** Public domain (15th century manuscript)

**Sources:**
- [Stanford Medieval Collections Exhibit](https://exhibits.stanford.edu/medieval/catalog/112-25169)
- [Estense Digital Library announcement](https://pro.europeana.eu/post/bringing-the-past-back-to-life-with-the-estense-digital-library)
- [Wikimedia Commons - De sphaera mundi category](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:De_sphaera_mundi)

## 4. Hildegard of Bingen's Cosmos (Scivias)

### The Lost Original

**Important Context:**
- **Original manuscript:** Formerly Wiesbaden, Hessische Landesbibliothek, Hs. 1
- **Date:** Last quarter of 12th century
- **Status:** **DESTROYED in WWII**
  - Transferred to Dresden in 1942 for safety
  - Lost in chaos of war
  - Original NO LONGER EXISTS

### Available Reproductions

**Black-and-White Photographs (1920):**
- **Created:** 1920 (before destruction)
- **Quality:** Not modern standards, but exist
- **URL 1:** http://www.kulturelles-erbe-koeln.de/documents/obj/00043147
- **Note:** Check if URL still active

**Color Facsimile (1927-1933):**
- **Created:** Abbey of St. Hildegard, Eibingen
- **Date:** 1927-1933
- **Status:** Faithful illuminated copy made BEFORE original was destroyed
- **Current location:** Abbey of St. Hildegard
- **URL:** http://www.abtei-st-hildegard.de/die-scivias-miniaturen/
- **Note:** Primary source for color reproductions today

**Digital Resources:**
- **Healthy Hildegard:** Scivias summary and images
  - URL: https://www.healthyhildegard.com/scivias-illustrations/
- **Early Music America:** "Illuminating Hildegard's Scivias"
  - URL: https://www.earlymusicamerica.org/web-articles/illuminating-hildegards-scivias/

**What to Show:**
- Wiesbaden MS B, fol. 14r (from facsimile)
- Egg-shaped universe with spherical Earth at center
- Vivid, multi-colored oval cosmos
- Layers: pure ether → dark fire (ignis niger) → bright fire shell

**Copyright:** Facsimile from 1927-1933 may have restrictions; check with Abbey

**Sources:**
- [Scivias - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scivias)
- [Cambridge Companion to Hildegard analysis](https://resolve.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/cambridge-companion-to-hildegard-of-bingen/picturing-hildegard-of-bingens-sight-illuminating-her-visions/5D9625583B52BB5C29ABE4FEE051C55F)

## 5. T-O Maps from Isidore of Seville

### Wikimedia Commons (Best Source)

**Main Category:**
- **URL:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:T_and_O_map
- **Content:** Multiple digitized medieval manuscripts
- **Status:** Public domain

**Specific Example:**
- **Description:** 11th-century copy of Isidore of Seville's writings (AD 560-636)
- **Features:** Mappa mundi with East at top, Red Sea in red
- **Source:** PICRYL - https://picryl.com/media/isidore-seville-mappamundi-9d3523

**First Printed Edition:**
- **Date:** 1472 (Augsburg)
- **Significance:** First map included in a printed book
- **Source:** History of Information - https://www.historyofinformation.com/detail.php?id=1446

**Norman B. Leventhal Map Center:**
- **Content:** Unsigned and untitled T-O world map
- **URL:** https://collections.leventhalmap.org/search/commonwealth:q524n595p
- **Status:** High-quality digitization

**What to Show:**
- Split-screen: T-O map on one side
- Accompanying text from Isidore's Etymologies Book III, Chapter 32 on other side
- Text: "sphere of the sky" with Earth "equally enclosed on all sides"
- Demonstrate it's iconographic diagram of habitable quarter (oikoumene)

**Key Context for Script:**
- T = Mediterranean, Nile, Don (Tanais) dividing continents
- O = encircling ocean
- NOT a literal flat disc, but diagram of inhabited world on sphere

**Copyright:** Public domain (medieval and early modern)

**Sources:**
- [Wikimedia Commons - T and O map category](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:T_and_O_map)
- [T and O map - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T_and_O_map)

## 6. Additional Visual Evidence from NotebookLM Research

### Ebstorf Map (13th Century)
**Description:** Entire world depicted as body of Christ
- Christ's head at top (East/Paradise)
- Hands extending North and South
- Feet at bottom (West/Gibraltar)
**Note:** Destroyed in WWII bombing of Hanover, but reproductions exist

### Nicole Oresme Miniature (Paris MS fr. 565)
**Description:** Oresme in his study next to large armillary sphere
**Significance:** Proves 14th-century scholars worked within spherical framework
**Source:** Medieval and Early Modern Science Vol. 2, p. 882

### Macrobius "Radial Rainfall" Diagram
**Manuscript:** MS Egerton 2976, fol. 49v
**Location:** British Library
**Content:** Rain falling toward center of circular Earth
**Significance:** Demonstrates medieval understanding of radial gravity
