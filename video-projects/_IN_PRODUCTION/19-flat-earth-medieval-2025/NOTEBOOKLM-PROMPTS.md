## SECTION A: CORE THESIS VERIFICATION

### Prompt A1: Russell's Central Argument
**For:** Russell's *Inventing the Flat Earth*

> "Summarize Jeffrey Burton Russell's central thesis in 'Inventing the Flat Earth.' What evidence does he provide that the flat-earth myth was invented in the 19th century? Who does he identify as the key figures responsible for creating and spreading this myth? Include specific page references."

**Expected output:** Confirmation of Irving, Letronne, Draper, White as myth-makers; evidence that medieval scholars accepted spherical Earth.

---

### Prompt A2: The "Five Flat-Earthers" Claim
**For:** Russell's *Inventing the Flat Earth*

> "Russell claims he found only five identifiable flat-earthers in the entire medieval period. Who were they? What were their dates and works? Why does Russell argue they were marginal figures with little influence? What evidence supports their marginality?"

**Expected output:** Names (Lactantius, Cosmas, possibly 2-3 others); evidence they were ignored/ridiculed.

---

### Prompt A3: Lindberg and Numbers on Medieval Knowledge
**For:** Lindberg's *Beginnings of Western Science*; Numbers' *Galileo Goes to Jail*

> "What do David Lindberg and Ronald Numbers say about medieval scholars' knowledge of Earth's shape? Find their statement that 'there was scarcely a Christian scholar of the Middle Ages who did not acknowledge [Earth's] sphericity.' What evidence do they cite for this claim?"

**Expected output:** Direct quote with context; list of medieval scholars who accepted sphericity.

## SECTION B: MEDIEVAL PRIMARY SOURCES

### Prompt B1: Bede's Explicit Statement
**For:** Bede's *De Temporum Ratione*

> "Find and analyze Bede's statement about Earth's shape in 'De Temporum Ratione' (The Reckoning of Time, 725 CE). He reportedly wrote that Earth is 'like a ball, not a shield.' What is the full context of this statement? What other astronomical knowledge does Bede demonstrate in this work? Why is this significant for understanding medieval cosmology?"

**Expected output:** Full quote in context; evidence of sophisticated astronomical understanding; significance for debunking myth.

---

### Prompt B2: Sacrobosco's Influence
**For:** Sacrobosco's *De Sphaera Mundi*; Grant or Lindberg

> "Analyze John of Sacrobosco's 'De Sphaera Mundi' (c. 1230) as evidence of medieval astronomical education. How does Sacrobosco describe Earth's shape? How widely was this text used in medieval universities? What statistics exist on manuscript copies and printed editions? Why does this work disprove the flat-earth myth?"

**Expected output:** Quotes from text; statistics (300+ manuscripts, 359+ editions); evidence of universal university adoption.

---

### Prompt B3: Aquinas as Witness
**For:** Aquinas's *Summa Theologica*

> "Find Thomas Aquinas's statement in the Summa Theologica where he uses Earth's sphericity as an example of established scientific fact. What is the full context? Why is it significant that Aquinas used spherical Earth as an *illustration* rather than a claim requiring defense?"

**Expected output:** Direct quote; analysis showing sphericity was uncontroversial.

---

### Prompt B4: Dante's Cosmology
**For:** Grant or Lindberg on medieval cosmology

> "Analyze how Dante's 'Divine Comedy' (c. 1320) demonstrates medieval knowledge of spherical Earth. What references does Dante make to different stars visible in the Southern Hemisphere, changing sun positions, and different time zones? Why is this significant evidence for widespread lay knowledge of Earth's shape?"

**Expected output:** Specific references from Purgatorio; analysis of cosmological assumptions.

## SECTION C: THE RARE EXCEPTIONS

### Prompt C1: Lactantius in Context
**For:** Russell or Numbers

> "Analyze Lactantius (c. 250-330) as a flat-earth advocate. Why did he reject spherical Earth? What was his attitude toward Greek philosophy? How was he received by other Church Fathers and medieval scholars? Why do historians argue he was marginal and uninfluential?"

**Expected output:** Context for Lactantius's views; evidence of his rejection by mainstream Christianity.

---

### Prompt C2: Cosmas Indicopleustes's Irrelevance
**For:** Russell, Lindberg, or Cosmas's *Christian Topography*

> "Analyze why Cosmas Indicopleustes (fl. 540s) is not evidence of medieval flat-earth belief. When was his 'Christian Topography' written? In what language? When was it first translated into Latin? When was it first known in Western Europe? What did David Lindberg mean when he said Cosmas is 'the only medieval European known to have defended a flat earth cosmology' yet his work was unknown in the medieval West?"

**Expected output:** Dates showing Cosmas was unknown until 1706 (Latin) and 1897 (English); evidence he cannot represent medieval Western belief.

## SECTION D: ORIGIN OF THE MYTH

### Prompt D1: Washington Irving's Invention
**For:** Irving's *Columbus* (1828); Russell

> "Analyze Washington Irving's 1828 biography of Columbus as the origin of the flat-earth myth. What scene did Irving invent involving Columbus at Salamanca? What does Irving claim about medieval clerics opposing Columbus? What was the actual historical dispute about Columbus's voyage (hint: distance to Asia, not Earth's shape)? Why did Irving's fictional account become accepted as history?"

**Expected output:** Specific passages from Irving; comparison with actual historical record; analysis of why fiction became fact.

---

### Prompt D2: Letronne's Misrepresentation
**For:** Russell

> "Analyze Antoine-Jean Letronne's 1834 work 'On the Cosmographical Ideas of the Church Fathers.' What claims did Letronne make about Augustine, Ambrose, and Basil? Were these claims accurate? What was Letronne's ideological motivation? How did his work combine with Irving's to create the flat-earth myth?"

**Expected output:** Specific false claims; evidence Church Fathers accepted sphericity; analysis of anti-religious motivation.

---

### Prompt D3: Draper and the Conflict Thesis
**For:** Draper's *Conflict Between Religion and Science* (1874); Russell or Numbers

> "Analyze how John William Draper's 'History of the Conflict Between Religion and Science' (1874) embedded the flat-earth myth in popular culture. What does Draper claim about medieval belief in flat Earth? How did his 'conflict thesis' framework use the flat-earth myth? Why did his book become so influential (50 editions in 50 years)? What was his ideological agenda?"

**Expected output:** Specific passages; analysis of conflict thesis; evidence of widespread influence.

---

### Prompt D4: White's Codification
**For:** White's *Warfare of Science with Theology* (1896); Russell or Numbers

> "Analyze how Andrew Dickson White's 'A History of the Warfare of Science with Theology in Christendom' (1896) gave the flat-earth myth scholarly appearance. What references does White provide? Why is his work described as 'more propaganda than history' by modern scholars? What was White's motivation as founder of Cornell University?"

**Expected output:** Specific claims; analysis of White's methodology; evidence of bias.

---

## SECTION E: THE MYTH'S SPREAD AND PERSISTENCE

### Prompt E1: Timeline of Myth Propagation
**For:** Russell; Gould

> "Trace the timeline of how the flat-earth myth spread through education and popular culture. Russell found that 'very few texts mentioned the flat-earth myth before 1870, but almost all texts after 1880 featured the legend.' What caused this shift? How did the myth move from specialized books (Irving, Draper, White) to general textbooks?"

**Expected output:** Specific timeline; analysis of transmission mechanisms.

---

### Prompt E2: Gould's "Double Myth" Analysis
**For:** Gould's "Late Birth of a Flat Earth" essay

> "Analyze Stephen Jay Gould's concept of the 'double myth' in his essay 'The Late Birth of a Flat Earth.' What are the two myths Gould identifies? How does he connect the flat-earth myth to broader narratives about the 'Dark Ages' and 'science vs. religion'? Why does Gould argue the myth persists?"

**Expected output:** Explanation of double myth; connection to broader historiographical issues.

---

### Prompt E3: Modern Persistence
**For:** Numbers' *Galileo Goes to Jail*

> "Why does the flat-earth myth persist despite scholarly debunking since at least 1991 (Russell) and arguably since 1920? What factors contribute to its persistence in textbooks, media, and popular culture? What does this tell us about how historical myths spread and survive?"

**Expected output:** Analysis of persistence factors; media/educational inertia.

---

## SECTION F: CONTINUITY FROM ANTIQUITY

### Prompt F1: Greek Knowledge Transmission
**For:** Lindberg's *Beginnings of Western Science*

> "Trace the transmission of knowledge about Earth's sphericity from ancient Greece through the Middle Ages. Start with Pythagoras and Aristotle, move through Eratosthenes's calculation, Ptolemy's Almagest, and show how this knowledge was preserved through Late Antiquity (Boethius, Martianus Capella), early medieval encyclopedists (Isidore, Bede), Arabic translations, and finally medieval university curriculum. What does this transmission chain tell us about intellectual continuity?"

**Expected output:** Detailed transmission chain with dates and key figures.

---

### Prompt F2: Medieval University Curriculum
**For:** Grant's *Foundations of Modern Science*; Lindberg

> "Analyze how medieval university curriculum taught astronomy. What was the quadrivium? What role did astronomy play? What texts were used (especially Sacrobosco)? How did this curriculum ensure that educated medieval Europeans knew Earth was spherical? What percentage of university students would have been exposed to this teaching?"

**Expected output:** Structure of quadrivium; role of Sacrobosco; evidence of widespread education.
