## PART 1: VIDIQ PROMPTS (With Documented Limitations)

### Understanding VidIQ Limitations

**What VidIQ is good at:**
- Keyword volume data (how many people search for terms)
- Competition scores (how saturated a keyword is)
- Title character count optimization
- Tag suggestions based on search data
- A/B testing framework

**What VidIQ is NOT good at:**
- Understanding niche documentary audiences (trained on mass-market YouTube)
- Predicting YOUR channel's performance (your 83-scored title got 21x more views than a 97-scored title)
- Documentary tone evaluation (will suggest clickbait patterns)
- Differentiating between "click" optimization and "brand" optimization

**Your filter:** Use VidIQ for DATA, not STRATEGY. Extract the numbers, apply your own judgment.

## PART 2: AI THUMBNAIL WORKFLOW (Gemini/Nano Banana)

### Why Use AI for Thumbnails (and Why NOT)

**Use AI for:**
- Background textures (aged paper, parchment, medieval aesthetic)
- Concept exploration (try 5 compositions before committing)
- Element generation (textures, patterns, lighting effects)
- Color palette testing

**DO NOT use AI for:**
- Final thumbnail (viewers detect AI slop)
- Text generation (AI is terrible at text)
- Faces (uncanny valley, damages credibility)
- The primary visual element (document, book cover, etc.)

**Your workflow:**
1. Generate backgrounds/textures with AI
2. Source real historical images (manuscripts, book covers)
3. Composite in Photoshop
4. Add text manually in Photoshop (you control typography)

---

### Risk Assessment: Will AI Thumbnails Hurt Your Channel?

**YouTube Algorithm:**
- YouTube has NOT publicly penalized AI-generated thumbnails
- No evidence of detection/demotion systems for AI images
- Thumbnail performance is based on CTR, not AI detection

**Audience Perception (The Real Risk):**
- Your audience is educated males 25-44 seeking documentary content
- This demographic is MORE likely to notice and dislike obvious AI art
- "AI slop" perception damages credibility for evidence-based channel
- Risk is audience trust, not algorithm penalty

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Use AI for ELEMENTS (backgrounds, textures), not full thumbnails
- Always composite with real source material
- Final product should look "designed," not "generated"
- Text MUST be manually added in Photoshop

### What NOT to Prompt

**AVOID these prompts:**
- "Medieval scholar" or any people (AI faces hurt credibility)
- "Flat earth" imagery (will generate globe/disk comparisons that look cheap)
- "Columbus at Salamanca" (will look like AI slop immediately)
- Any text (AI text is always wrong)
- "Documentary thumbnail" (too generic, will look templated)

---

### Thumbnail Workflow: Step by Step

**Step 1: Generate AI backgrounds (Gemini)**
- Run 3-5 texture prompts
- Select best options
- Export at highest resolution

**Step 2: Source real historical images**
- Anderson textbook page (1880 or 1898) from archive.org or university library
- Medieval manuscript from e-codices.ch or Wikimedia
- Book covers (Irving, Draper, White) from archive.org

**Step 3: Composite in Photoshop**
- Layer AI background texture (bottom)
- Place historical source image (middle)
- Add highlights/shadows manually
- Apply color grading for consistency

**Step 4: Add text in Photoshop**
- Font choice: Documentary serif (not AI-generated fonts)
- Keep to 3-5 words maximum
- Ensure readability at small sizes
- Test at thumbnail preview size

**Step 5: A/B test concepts**
- Create 2-3 variants
- Test in VidIQ thumbnail tester if available
- Get feedback before publishing
