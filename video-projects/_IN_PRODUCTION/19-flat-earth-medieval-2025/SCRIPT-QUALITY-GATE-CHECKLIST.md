# Script Quality Gate Checklist - Final Verification Before Filming

**Purpose:** GO/NO-GO checklist before declaring script film-ready
**Use:** After incorporating third audio, run through this entire checklist
**Standard:** ALL items must pass ✅ before filming

---

## SECTION 1: RETENTION FORMULA VERIFICATION

### Opening Hook (First 60 Seconds)
- [ ] **Visual contrast present** (textbook 1850 vs 1890, or equivalent visceral demonstration)
- [ ] **Authority marker within 22 seconds** ("I went through the manuscripts/read the documents")
- [ ] **Specific references** (not vague: "Bede from 725 AD," "Sacrobosco from 1230," not "medieval texts")
- [ ] **Preview of argument** (three perpetrators named OR key evidence shown)
- [ ] **Stakes stated clearly** (still taught today, damage ongoing, etc.)
- [ ] **Total opening time: ~60 seconds** (not longer - retention drops)

**RED FLAGS:**
- ❌ Opens with abstract philosophy or modern crisis stats
- ❌ Opens with geopolitical drama (warship, politician quotes)
- ❌ Takes >25 seconds to establish authority marker
- ❌ Vague references ("historians say," "some scholars")

---

### Pattern Interrupts (Every 2-3 Minutes)
- [ ] **Visual anchor at 2:00-2:30** (manuscript, graphic, document)
- [ ] **Visual anchor at 4:00-4:30**
- [ ] **Visual anchor at 6:00-6:30**
- [ ] **Visual anchor at 8:00-8:30**
- [ ] **Visual anchor at 10:00-10:30**
- [ ] **Visual anchor at 12:00-12:30**
- [ ] **Visual anchor at 14:00-14:30** (if video goes this long)

**Each anchor should be:**
- Manuscript display (Bede, Sacrobosco, etc.)
- OR book cover (Irving, Draper, White)
- OR infographic (timeline, calculation error)
- OR on-screen quote with attribution

**RED FLAGS:**
- ❌ Extended talking head sections >3 minutes without visual
- ❌ Abstract philosophy sections without document anchors
- ❌ Political drama/news commentary stretches

---

### Early Payoff
- [ ] **Answer given in first 60 seconds** ("Medieval people knew earth was spherical, here's who lied")
- [ ] **Rest of video PROVES the answer** (not mystery-building)

**RED FLAGS:**
- ❌ Withholding conclusion until end
- ❌ "You won't believe what I found..." without revealing it

---

## SECTION 2: SPEAKING PATTERNS & DELIVERY

### User's Natural Patterns Present
- [ ] **Immediate term definitions** ("Estoppel—a legal rule that says...")
- [ ] **Embedded explanations** (after complex quote: "In simpler terms...")
- [ ] **Concrete specifics** (never vague: "Salamanca commission of 1486, led by Hernando de Talavera" not "a commission")
- [ ] **Short declarative emphasis** ("They knew." "Irving invented it." "Britain never built it.")
- [ ] **Dash-separated clarifications** ("The globus cruciger—literally 'cross-bearing orb'—appeared in royal art...")

---

### Spoken Delivery Optimization
- [ ] **Contractions used** (it's/they're, not it is/they are)
- [ ] **Date formats conversational** ("June 16th, 2014" not "June 16, 2014")
- [ ] **Quote attributions natural** ("Bede wrote..." not "Quote: [text]")
- [ ] **Natural connectors present** ("So..." "And..." "But..." at sentence starts)
- [ ] **Sentences short enough for single breath** (no run-ons requiring pause mid-sentence)
- [ ] **Rhetorical questions for pacing** (not just statements)

**TEST:** Read entire script aloud. Mark any:
- [REWORD] - Awkward phrasing
- [SHORTEN] - Can't finish in one breath
- [ADD PAUSE] - Need natural break

## SECTION 4: SIMPLIFICATION DETECTION

### Territorial/Boundary Claims (N/A for this video, but check anyway)
- [ ] No vague "they claim the territory" (would need: which specific boundaries, how much)
- [ ] No percentages without sourcing (e.g., "90% of the land")

---

### Temporal Accuracy
- [ ] **Present-tense statements are current** (not: "Modern flat earthers believe..." if you mean 2024-2025 specifically)
- [ ] **Past-tense statements have dates** (not: "Medieval people knew" → "By 725 AD, Bede wrote...")
- [ ] **"Still" claims are verified current** ("still taught today" → verified with 2020s examples)

---

### Attribution Specificity
- [ ] **Every "according to X" has full name + work + year**
  - ✅ "According to Jeffrey Burton Russell in Inventing the Flat Earth (1991)..."
  - ❌ "According to historians..."

- [ ] **Every historical event has date**
  - ✅ "The Salamanca commission of 1486..."
  - ❌ "The Salamanca commission..."

- [ ] **Every book mentioned has author + year**
  - ✅ "Washington Irving's 1828 biography of Columbus..."
  - ❌ "A book about Columbus..."

---

### Contested Claims Marked
- [ ] **Lactantius/Cosmas outliers**: Acknowledged as exceptions (5 total vs hundreds)
- [ ] **T-O maps**: Explained as "quarter of sphere" not whole planet
- [ ] **Any debate among historians**: Both sides presented

**RED FLAGS:**
- ❌ Absolutist language ("all historians agree")
- ❌ Sweeping claims ("nobody believed")
- ❌ Ignoring counter-evidence

## SECTION 5: DOCUMENTARY TONE MAINTENANCE

### Channel DNA Compliance
- [ ] **History-first, not geopolitics-first** (70%+ historical sources/events, 30%- modern relevance)
- [ ] **Document-focused, not news-focused** (primary sources shown, not political commentary)
- [ ] **Educational, not confrontational** (explaining, not attacking)
- [ ] **Forensic investigation, not political debate**

---

### Avoiding Clickbait Language
**Scan script for these violations:**
- ❌ "You won't BELIEVE..."
- ❌ "SHOCKING truth..."
- ❌ "They don't want you to know..."
- ❌ "This will BLOW YOUR MIND..."
- ❌ All-caps for emphasis (use italics in writing, vocal emphasis when speaking)

**Acceptable documentary language:**
- ✅ "The documents show..."
- ✅ "Here's what the evidence reveals..."
- ✅ "I went through the manuscripts..."
- ✅ "This is what I found..."

---

### Avoiding Political Punditry
- [ ] **No modern politicians as main subjects** (historical figures only)
- [ ] **No takes on current policy debates** (historical context for beliefs, not advocacy)
- [ ] **No partisan language** ("the left," "the right" unless historically specific)

**Exception:** Modern flat earthers can be mentioned as CONSEQUENCE of myth, not as political figures

## COMMON FAILURE POINTS (From Past Videos)

### Red Flag #1: Unverified Claims Snuck In
**Example:** "January 6 defendants cited Founding Fathers in court" (which defendants? which cases?)
**Fix:** Find specific case, or DELETE claim

### Red Flag #2: Vague Attributions
**Example:** "Historians say medieval people knew..."
**Fix:** "According to Jeffrey Burton Russell in Inventing the Flat Earth (1991), page 2..."

### Red Flag #3: Extended Philosophy Without Documents
**Example:** 5 minutes on "why people believe myths" without evidence
**Fix:** Cut to 2 minutes, anchor in textbook survey data

### Red Flag #4: Opening Too Slow
**Example:** 90 seconds before authority marker ("I read the documents")
**Fix:** Authority marker within 22 seconds (proven by retention data)

### Red Flag #5: Competing with Wrong Channels
**Example:** Spending 3 minutes on physics proofs (laser over water)
**Fix:** That's Professor Dave's niche. You expose FABRICATION, not prove science.
