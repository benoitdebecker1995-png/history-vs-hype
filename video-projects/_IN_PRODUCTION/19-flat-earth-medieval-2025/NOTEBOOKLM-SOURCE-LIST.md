## UPDATED THESIS (2025-12-31)

**New approach:** "You've probably heard medieval people didn't think Earth was flat. But have you ever seen the evidence?"

**Three-Act Structure:**
1. **Act 1: The Evidence You've Never Seen** - SHOW the actual manuscripts (Bede, Sacrobosco, Isidore)
2. **Act 2: How the Myth Was Invented** - Irving, Draper, White's 19th-century fabrication
3. **Act 3: Why the Myth Persists** - Psychology of misinformation + modern flat-earth conspiracy connection

**Source priorities updated accordingly:**
- **Recent scholarship prioritized** (2010-2025) where available
- **Harrison (2015), Ungureanu (2019)** for conflict thesis - most recent work
- **Classic authorities retained** where still standard (Grant, Lindberg for medieval cosmology)
- **Visual evidence emphasis** - Need specific manuscript images, not just citations
- **Meta-analysis focus** - Understanding WHY myths persist, not just debunking

**Note on Source Dates:**
- **Medieval cosmology:** Grant (1996) and Lindberg (2007) remain THE authorities - not superseded
- **Conflict thesis:** NEW scholarship 2015-2019 available (Harrison, Ungureanu)
- **Primary sources:** Date of critical edition matters, not age of original text
