## Core Thesis (UPDATED 2025-12-31)

**You've probably heard that medieval people didn't think the Earth was flat. But have you ever seen the evidence?**

This video goes beyond the debunking to **SHOW the actual medieval sources** proving they knew Earth was spherical, then asks a deeper question: *Why does this myth persist despite being debunked since 1991?*

**Three-Act Structure:**

**ACT 1: The Evidence You've Never Seen**
- Show actual medieval manuscripts (Bede, Sacrobosco, Isidore)
- Display spherical Earth diagrams from medieval universities
- Demonstrate that this wasn't secret knowledge—it was taught in every university for 400 years

**ACT 2: How the Myth Was Invented**
- Washington Irving's 1828 fabrication of the Columbus-Salamanca scene
- Draper and White's "conflict thesis" propaganda (1874-1896)
- How the myth spread through textbooks 1860-1890

**ACT 3: Why the Myth Persists**
- Psychology of misinformation: why false history sticks
- The myth's utility for anti-religious AND anti-medieval narratives
- Connection to modern flat Earth conspiracy movement
- Why "common knowledge" is hard to dislodge even after scholarly debunking

**Both Extremes Wrong Framework:**
- Extreme A: "Medieval people were ignorant flat-earthers" — FALSE, invented in 19th century
- Extreme B: "The Church suppressed knowledge of the spherical Earth" — FALSE, the Church taught spherical Earth through its universities

**Modern Relevance Hook:**
How does a historical myth survive 30+ years of scholarly debunking? Understanding this helps explain modern conspiracy persistence.

## Target Audience & Hook Angle

**Audience:** Males 25-44, educated, interested in history myth-busting, skeptical of popular narratives

**Hook Angle Options:**
1. **The Evidence Challenge:** "You've heard medieval people knew Earth was round. But have you ever seen the proof? Here's a manuscript from 725 CE..."
2. **The Meta-Question:** "This myth was debunked in 1991. Why do people still believe it in 2025?"
3. **The Document First:** [OPEN ON medieval spherical Earth diagram] "This is what they actually taught. So why do you think they believed in a flat Earth?"

**Why This Updated Thesis Performs Better:**
- **Respects viewer intelligence** - Doesn't assume they're ignorant of the debunking
- **Shows, doesn't tell** - Visual primary sources (channel differentiator)
- **Asks deeper question** - "Why does the myth persist?" is more sophisticated than "the myth is false"
- **Modern conspiracy connection** - Links to flat Earth resurgence, distrust of expertise
- **Intellectual honesty** - Acknowledges the debunking exists but most people haven't seen the actual evidence
