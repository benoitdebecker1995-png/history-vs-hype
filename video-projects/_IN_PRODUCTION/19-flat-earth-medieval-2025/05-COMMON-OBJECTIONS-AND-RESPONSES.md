# Common Objections to Medieval Spherical Earth Evidence

**Research Date:** 2025-12-31
**Purpose:** Document typical objections when presenting evidence and how historians respond

---

## Objection 1: "But T-O Maps Look Flat!"

### The Objection:
"I've seen medieval world maps that are circles. They clearly show a flat disc, not a sphere."

### What They're Referring To:
T-O maps (mappa mundi) from medieval manuscripts, particularly those associated with Isidore of Seville.

### The Historical Response:

**What T-O Maps Actually Were:**
- **Iconographic diagrams**, not literal geographic representations
- Showed only the **habitable quarter (oikoumene)** of a spherical globe
- Appeared in manuscripts **alongside texts** explicitly describing spherical earth

**The Evidence:**
1. **Isidore's own text** (Etymologies, Book III, Chapter 32):
   - "The sphere of the sky is a certain shape that is round in form, and its center is the earth, which is **equally enclosed on all sides**"
   - T-O map appears ON THE SAME PAGE as this spherical description

2. **What the T and O represent:**
   - T = Mediterranean, Nile, Don (Tanais) dividing continents
   - O = encircling ocean **around the known inhabited lands**
   - NOT the entire planet, just the inhabited portion

3. **Context matters:**
   - These were **spiritual/moral diagrams**
   - East at top (Garden of Eden)
   - Jerusalem at center (theological, not geographic claim)
   - Used for teaching biblical geography, not navigation

**The Analogy:**
"Looking at a T-O map and concluding medieval people thought the earth was flat is like looking at a London Tube map and concluding modern people think the city is made of colored spaghetti. It's a **schematic representation**, not a literal map."

**Sources:**
- [T and O map - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T_and_O_map)

## Objection 2: "What About Lactantius and Cosmas?"

### The Objection:
"Lactantius and Cosmas Indicopleustes were Church figures who taught flat earth. That proves the Church promoted it."

### The Historical Response:

**They Were Isolated Outliers:**

**Lactantius (c. 245–325):**
- Professional rhetorician, NOT a theologian
- His works were eventually **condemned as heretical**
- St. Jerome praised his **rhetoric** but criticized his theology: "failed to affirm our doctrines"
- Ridiculed spherical earth in "On the False Wisdom of the Philosophers"
- **NO medieval scholars followed his cosmology**

**Cosmas Indicopleustes (fl. 540s):**
- Byzantine merchant-turned-monk
- Wrote in **Greek** (not Latin)
- Christian Topography **NOT translated into Latin until 1706**
- Only **2-3 full medieval manuscripts exist**
- **Unknown in medieval West** for over 1,000 years
- 9th-century Patriarch Photius dismissed him as a **"fabulist rather than trustworthy authority"**

**The Statistics:**
- Historian Jeffrey Burton Russell identifies **exactly 5 flat-earth advocates** in first 1,500 years of Christianity
- Versus **hundreds of spherical-earth proponents** in same period
- Lactantius and Cosmas = **extreme exceptions**

**The Analogy:**
"Citing Lactantius and Cosmas as proof that medieval Christians believed in flat earth is like citing the Flat Earth Society as proof that modern Americans believe it. Yes, they existed. No, they weren't representative."

**Sources:**
- [Inventing the Flat Earth, p. 23-24, 32, 35]
- [Flat Earth or round sphere, p. 366, 381]
- [The Christian Topography of Cosmas, p. 1638]

## Objection 3: "Columbus Had to Fight the Church"

### The Objection:
"Everyone knows Columbus had to prove to the Catholic Church that the earth was round. That's why they opposed his voyage."

### The Historical Response:

**The Salamanca Dispute Was About DISTANCE, Not SHAPE:**

**Who Was There:**
- NOT "shaved monks" or the Inquisition
- **Hernando de Talavera** (Queen's confessor, later Archbishop)
- Professors of astronomy, geography, mathematics
- **Rabbi Abraham Zacuto** (famous Jewish astronomer)
- Ad hoc committee of **experts**, not clerics

**What They Argued:**
1. Earth's sphericity = **NOT disputed** (both sides agreed)
2. Earth's circumference = **DISPUTED** (Columbus said 21,000 miles, scholars said closer to 25,000)
3. Distance to Asia = **DISPUTED** (Columbus said 2,500 miles, scholars said ~12,500 miles)

**Who Was Right:**
The **scholars at Salamanca** were correct:
- Earth IS about 25,000 miles around
- Asia IS about 12,500 miles away going west
- Columbus's ships **couldn't carry enough supplies** for that distance

**What Saved Columbus:**
The Americas happened to be sitting **exactly where Columbus thought Asia was** (about 4,000 miles from Spain).

**Where the Myth Comes From:**
- **Washington Irving's 1828 biography** (A History of the Life and Voyages of Christopher Columbus)
- Irving **invented** the scene of Columbus facing bigoted monks
- Historian Samuel Eliot Morison called it **"pure moonshine"**
- But Irving's dramatic story was so compelling, it entered textbooks as "fact"

**Sources:**
- [Inventing the Flat Earth, p. 52-54, 624-627]
- [Flat Earth or round sphere, p. 364-370]
- NotebookLM: Salamanca Commission research

## Objection 4: "The Bible Says the Earth Is Flat"

### The Objection:
"The Bible has passages about the 'four corners of the earth' and the 'circle of the earth.' Medieval Christians took this literally, so they must have believed in flat earth."

### The Historical Response:

**Medieval Scholars Distinguished Literal from Figurative:**

**Augustine's Warning (De Genesi ad litteram, Book I, Chapter 19):**
> "Usually, even a non-Christian knows something about the earth, the heavens, and the other elements of this world... now it is a **disgraceful and dangerous thing** for an infidel to hear a Christian, presumably giving the meaning of Holy Scripture, **talking nonsense on these topics**."

**Key Point:** Augustine explicitly warned Christians NOT to use Bible as science textbook in **4th century**.

**How Medieval Scholars Read "Problem" Passages:**

**"Circle of the earth" (Isaiah 40:22):**
- Hebrew word "חוּג" (khug) can mean sphere or circle
- Medieval scholars read it as **sphere**
- Augustine: "A skin may be stretched into the shape of a sphere, **as with a leather ball**"

**"Four corners" (Revelation 7:1):**
- Read as **figurative language**
- Like we say "the sun rises" without meaning geocentrism
- Medieval scholars: Obviously metaphorical for "all directions"

**The Evidence:**
Every major medieval theologian accepted spherical earth:
- **Augustine** (4th century): Confirmed spherical earth
- **Bede** (8th century): "Like a ball, being equally round in all directions"
- **Aquinas** (13th century): Used spherical earth as **uncontroversial example** in Summa Theologica

**The Analogy:**
"Medieval Christians reading 'four corners of the earth' literally would be like modern people hearing 'the sun rises' and concluding we believe in geocentrism. They understood figurative language."

**Sources:**
- [When Science and Christianity Meet, p. 550]
- [Inventing the Flat Earth, p. 86-87]
- NotebookLM: Augustine primary source quote

## Objection 5: "But People Were Ignorant in the Dark Ages"

### The Objection:
"Medieval people didn't have science. They were backward and superstitious. Of course they believed silly things like flat earth."

### The Historical Response:

**The "Dark Ages" Label Is Itself a Myth:**

**Who Invented "Dark Ages":**
- **Petrarch** (ca. 1340): Wanted to glorify classical antiquity and his own era
- Defined period between Roman Empire and his time as "dismal stagnation and gloom"
- This was **propaganda**, not objective history

**What Was Actually Happening:**
1. **Universities Founded:** Paris (1150), Oxford (1167), Cambridge (1209)
2. **Required Curriculum:** 30% focused on natural world, including astronomy
3. **Standard Textbook:** Sacrobosco's De Sphaera (survives in **hundreds of manuscripts**)
4. **Student Numbers:** 250,000+ in German universities alone (1350-1500)
5. **Preservation Work:** Monasteries copied and preserved classical texts

**The Evidence They Knew Spherical Earth:**
- **Bede (725):** Explicitly taught spherical earth in monastery schools
- **Sacrobosco (c. 1230):** Standard university text provided **sensory proofs** for globe
- **Aquinas (c. 1265):** Used spherical earth as non-controversial example
- **Dante (c. 1320):** Entire Divine Comedy assumes spherical earth geography

**Who Was Actually "Ignorant":**
19th-century polemicists who **didn't read medieval sources** before claiming medieval people believed flat earth.

**The Quote:**
Historian Jeffrey Burton Russell: "All educated people throughout Europe knew the earth's spherical shape and its approximate circumference."

**Sources:**
- [The Beginnings of Western Science, p. 161]
- [Galileo Goes to Jail, p. 21, 28]
- [Inventing the Flat Earth, p. 2]

## Objection 6: "Why Would Historians Lie About This?"

### The Objection (from modern flat-earthers):
"You're saying historians made this up? Why would they lie? There must be a conspiracy."

### The Historical Response:

**It Wasn't a Conspiracy, It Was 19th-Century Propaganda:**

**Who Created the Myth (and Why):**

**1. Washington Irving (1828):**
- Fiction writer, not historian
- Wanted to make Columbus story **more dramatic**
- Invented Salamanca scene for **literary effect**
- Sold 10,000 copies immediately; story spread

**2. Antoine-Jean Letronne (1834):**
- French anticlerical academic
- Dedicated Voltairean who viewed Christianity as "intellectual night"
- Used flat earth myth to attack Church credibility
- Provided "scholarly" weight to Irving's fiction

**3. John William Draper (1874):**
- Author: History of the **Conflict** between Religion and Science
- Angry about 1870 Papal Infallibility declaration
- Used flat earth myth as "proof" of Church's anti-science stance
- **50 printings in 50 years**, translated into 10+ languages

**4. Andrew Dickson White (1896):**
- Founder of Cornell (first secular US university)
- Under attack from religious critics
- Wrote A History of the **Warfare** of Science with Theology
- Used flat earth myth to "smite his enemies with history"
- "Horde of footnotes" created "illusion of meticulous scholarship"

**Why It Stuck:**
- Appeared in textbooks between 1870-1920
- Reinforced "Conflict Thesis" (science vs. religion)
- Satisfied cultural "presentism" (congratulating modern era)
- Simple, dramatic story beats complex reality

**Modern Irony:**
Modern flat-earthers claiming "they lied about medieval flat earth" are **citing the anticlerical propaganda** designed to make religion look stupid.

**Sources:**
- [Inventing the Flat Earth, p. 43-76]
- [History of the Conflict, Draper 1874]
- [A History of the Warfare of Science, White 1896]

## Objection 7: "It's Just a Harmless Myth"

### The Objection:
"Who cares if people get medieval history wrong? It doesn't affect anything important."

### The Historical Response:

**This Myth Has Real Consequences:**

**1. Undermines Trust in Expertise:**
- If historians are "wrong" about this, why trust them on anything?
- Creates opening for denialism (Holocaust, slavery, etc.)

**2. Perpetuates Science vs. Religion Warfare:**
- Forces false choice between faith and reason
- Obscures actual complex historical relationship
- Makes productive dialogue nearly impossible

**3. Teaches Bad Epistemology:**
- "Common knowledge" over verified sources
- "Sounds right" over rigorous research
- Dramatic stories over documentary evidence

**4. Gateway to Conspiracy Thinking:**
- "They lied about this, what else are they lying about?"
- Erosion of trust in institutions
- Radicalization pathway

**The Stakes:**
- 15% decline in trust in science (2020-2023)
- 27% of people have little confidence in scientists
- Flat earth conspiracy overlaps with other dangerous conspiracies

**Why Correction Matters:**
Teaching people to check sources, question popular narratives, and trust evidence over assumptions = skills that transfer to:
- Evaluating news
- Assessing scientific claims
- Understanding political rhetoric
- Navigating social media

## Summary: Pattern of Objections

### Common Thread:
Most objections stem from **not reading primary sources** and **trusting popularized narratives** over academic research.

### How Historians Respond:
1. **Show the primary sources** (Bede, Sacrobosco, Aquinas, etc.)
2. **Explain the context** (T-O maps, biblical interpretation, etc.)
3. **Demonstrate the fabrication** (Irving, Draper, White)
4. **Reveal the stakes** (Why getting history right matters)

### The Core Message:
**The evidence for medieval spherical earth belief is overwhelming. The evidence for widespread flat earth belief is nonexistent.**

Those who claim otherwise are either:
- Unaware of the primary sources
- Repeating 19th-century propaganda
- Motivated by ideological agenda
