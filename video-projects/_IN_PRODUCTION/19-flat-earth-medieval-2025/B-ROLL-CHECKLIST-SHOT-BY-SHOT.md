## SHOT-BY-SHOT SCRIPT INTEGRATION

**For each manuscript/document, use this pattern:**

1. **Introduce verbally**: "Here's what Bede wrote in 725 AD..."
2. **Show full manuscript**: 2 seconds (establish it's real)
3. **Zoom to relevant text**: Latin visible
4. **Overlay English translation**: 3-4 seconds
5. **Hold with attribution**: 1-2 seconds
6. **Continue speaking**: Don't wait for visual to finish

**Timing:** Quote should appear ON SCREEN while you're reading it aloud, not before or after.

## EDITING SOFTWARE SETTINGS

**For Text Overlays:**
- Font: Helvetica or Roboto (clean, readable)
- Size: Large enough to read at 480p (mobile test)
- Color: White text on semi-transparent dark overlay (readability)
- Duration: Quote stays on screen while being read + 1-2 seconds after
- Animation: Simple fade in/out (not distracting)

**For Manuscript Display:**
- High resolution (download max quality from e-codices)
- Zoom smoothly (no jumpy cuts)
- Allow parchment texture to show (proves authenticity)
- Keep Latin text visible (shows you're not hiding anything)

**For Book Covers:**
- Full title page first (2 sec)
- Then zoom to key details (author, date, provocative words like "CONFLICT")
- Hold long enough for viewers to screenshot if desired
