## FORMATTING SPECIFICATIONS

### On-Screen Text Guidelines:

**Font:**
- Sans-serif, highly readable (Helvetica, Arial, Roboto)
- White text on dark semi-transparent overlay
- OR black text on light semi-transparent overlay (depending on background)

**Size:**
- Large enough to read on mobile (test at 480p)
- 2 lines maximum per screen
- If quote is longer, split into multiple screens

**Timing:**
- Display WHILE you're reading it aloud (not before, not after)
- Stay on screen 1-2 seconds after you finish reading
- Don't rush—give viewers time to read AND absorb

**Attribution:**
- Smaller text below quote
- Format: "Author Name, *Book Title* (Year), Page X"
- Can be briefer on screen than full citation

### Example Format:

```
[LARGE TEXT - CENTER SCREEN]
"The earth is not merely circular like a shield...
but resembles more a ball, being equally round in all directions"

[SMALLER TEXT - BOTTOM]
Bede, The Reckoning of Time (725 AD)
```

## USAGE STRATEGY

### When to Show Quotes:

1. **Medieval Evidence (Act 2)**: Show ALL manuscript quotes
   - Bede, Isidore, Sacrobosco, Martianus, Augustine
   - This is your proof—make it visual

2. **Fabrication Timeline (Act 4)**: Show book titles, NOT long quotes
   - Irving, Draper, White book covers
   - Brief attributions, let visuals do work

3. **Modern Data (Act 6)**: Show statistics as graphics
   - Trust decline, gateway effect data
   - Make numbers pop visually

4. **Objections (throughout)**: Show comparative data
   - T-O map explanation, Lactantius/Cosmas stats
   - Side-by-side comparisons work well

### When NOT to Show Quotes:

- Don't over-display—not every sentence needs text
- Your talking head is the authority—quotes support, not replace
- Use quotes for KEY moments, not constant overlay
