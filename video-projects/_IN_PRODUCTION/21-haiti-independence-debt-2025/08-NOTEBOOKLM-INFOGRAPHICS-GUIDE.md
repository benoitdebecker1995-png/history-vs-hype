## NOTEBOOKLM CAPABILITIES (2025-2026)

### What NotebookLM CAN Do Now

**Direct Infographic Generation:**
- NotebookLM launched Infographics feature powered by "Nano Banana Pro" (Gemini's image model)
- Can generate visual summaries directly from your uploaded sources
- Outputs: PNG files, downloadable
- Customization: orientation (square/portrait/landscape), detail level, custom prompts

**Data Extraction:**
- Analyze uploaded PDFs, Google Docs, text files
- Provide citations with exact page numbers
- Ground all outputs in YOUR sources (not web data)

### Limitations to Know

| Limitation | Impact | Workaround |
|------------|--------|------------|
| **Watermark** | NotebookLM logo on all infographics | Remove in Photoshop, or use Ultra tier ($250/mo) |
| **Usage Limits** | Free: few generations per notebook | Prioritize which infographics to generate |
| **Capacity Issues** | Feature sometimes restricted due to demand | Try during off-peak hours, have manual backup plan |
| **Text Quality** | AI-generated text can be imperfect | Verify all text, may need manual correction |
| **Style Control** | Limited compared to manual design | Use as starting point, refine in Photoshop |

**Source:** [NotebookLM Limits Explained](https://medium.com/ai-quick-tips/notebooklm-limits-explained-free-vs-pro-what-you-actually-get-1625db4ac6dc), [Google NotebookLM Help](https://support.google.com/notebooklm/answer/16758265?hl=en)

### INFOGRAPHIC 1: Payment Timeline (1825-1947)

**Prompt:**
```
Create a horizontal timeline infographic showing Haiti's 122-year debt payment history from 1825 to 1947.

Include these key dates with brief descriptions:
- January 1, 1804: Independence
- April 17, 1825: France's ordinance (150 million francs demanded)
- July 3, 1825: French fleet arrives (14 ships, 528 cannons)
- February 12, 1838: Debt reduced to 90 million francs
- 1898: 50% of budget goes to debt
- 1914: 80% of budget goes to debt, USS Machias gold seizure
- 1922: National City Bank acquires debt
- 1947: Final payment

Use a dark, documentary color palette (navy, gold, cream). Make it suitable for YouTube B-roll at 1920x1080. Emphasize that this is 122 YEARS of payments.
```

**Orientation:** Landscape
**Detail Level:** Detailed

### INFOGRAPHIC 2: Budget Drain (50% → 80%)

**Prompt:**
```
Create a "by the numbers" infographic showing how Haiti's debt consumed its national budget.

Key statistics to visualize:
- 1898: 50% of Haiti's budget went to debt service
- 1914: 80% of Haiti's budget went to debt service
- What was crowded out: education, healthcare, infrastructure "went practically unfunded throughout the Nineteenth Century"

Use pie charts or bar graphs to show the before/after. Color scheme: use red/orange for debt payments, muted colors for remaining budget. Make the 80% figure visually dominant.

Target audience: educated viewers who want to understand economic mechanisms.
```

**Orientation:** Square
**Detail Level:** Standard

### INFOGRAPHIC 3: Claimants Pyramid (25,838 People)

**Prompt:**
```
Create a pyramid/funnel infographic showing how France's 90 million franc indemnity was distributed among 25,838 colonist claimants.

Structure from top to bottom:
- TOP (smallest): 86 individuals received 1,512-3,396 francs each
- BOTTOM (largest): 9,450 individuals received only 1-7.50 francs each
- Total claimants: 25,838
- Total properties adjudicated: 10,345

The visual should make clear that a tiny elite received most of the money while thousands received almost nothing. Use a wealth inequality visual style - gold at top, gray at bottom.

Source: Beauvois p.124
```

**Orientation:** Portrait
**Detail Level:** Detailed

### INFOGRAPHIC 4: Haiti vs Britain Comparison

**Prompt:**
```
Create a comparison infographic showing the DIRECTION of money flow in two abolition scenarios:

LEFT SIDE - BRITAIN (1833):
- Arrow: GOVERNMENT → SLAVEHOLDERS
- Amount: £20 million
- Who paid: British taxpayers
- Recipients: ~4,000 slaveholders

RIGHT SIDE - HAITI (1825):
- Arrow: FORMERLY ENSLAVED → SLAVEHOLDERS
- Amount: 90 million francs
- Who paid: Descendants of enslaved people
- Recipients: 25,838 colonist claimants

Use contrasting colors. Make the arrows large and clear. The key insight: Haiti is the ONLY country where the enslaved paid reparations to their enslavers.

Documentary style, muted colors, emphasis on the reversal.
```

**Orientation:** Landscape
**Detail Level:** Standard

### INFOGRAPHIC 5: Duvalier vs France Comparison

**Prompt:**
```
Create a bar chart infographic comparing two forms of extraction from Haiti:

BAR 1 - FRENCH DEBT EXTRACTION:
- Amount: $560 million (2022 dollars)
- Period: 1825-1947 (122 years)
- Source: NYT "The Ransom" investigation

BAR 2 - DUVALIER FAMILY THEFT:
- Amount: $1.65 billion
- Period: 1957-1986 (29 years)
- Source: US Department of Commerce

Include text: "Internal corruption exceeded external extraction in raw dollars - but the debt created the conditions for kleptocracy."

This is the steelman acknowledgment. Honest comparison, documentary tone.
```

### INFOGRAPHIC 6: Money Flow Diagram

**Prompt:**
```
Create a flow diagram showing where Haiti's debt payments actually went:

FLOW:
Haiti → French Treasury → French Banks (Paravey, CIC, Rothschild) → French Economy

Key facts to include:
- Paravey bank became shareholder of Banque de France (Oosterlinck p.1264)
- CIC profits from Haiti helped finance the Eiffel Tower (NYT)
- Investors in National Bank of Haiti cleared 15% returns while French investments averaged 5% (NYT)
- 1922: National City Bank (US) acquired all Haitian debts

Use arrows to show money flowing OUT of Haiti. Dark background, gold/yellow for money flow arrows.
```

**Orientation:** Landscape
**Detail Level:** Detailed

### INFOGRAPHIC 7: Boyer's Three Questions

**Prompt:**
```
Create a text-focused infographic showing President Boyer's three manipulative questions to his secret council on July 7, 1825.

Context: Boyer did NOT show the council the actual French ordinance. Instead, he asked three leading questions designed to get approval.

Questions (translate to English, keep formal tone):
1. "Can the King of France, in recognizing Haiti's independence, reserve commercial advantages for French trade?"
2. "If we accept this ordinance, could France use it against us in future dealings with other nations?"
3. "However, since independence is finally recognized, would we not risk losing forever the chance to conclude this great affair by rejecting it?"

Note at bottom: "Boyer concealed the actual terms. Haitians only learned the full debt amount months later when French newspapers arrived."

Source: Ardouin p.350-351

Documentary style, aged paper texture, formal typography.
```

**Orientation:** Portrait
**Detail Level:** Detailed
