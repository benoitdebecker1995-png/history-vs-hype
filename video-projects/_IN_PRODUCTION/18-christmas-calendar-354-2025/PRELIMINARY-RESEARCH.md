## THE PRIMARY SOURCE: CHRONOGRAPHY OF 354

### Basic Facts
- **Full name:** Chronography of 354 / Codex-Calendar of 354 / Calendar of Philocalus
- **Date:** 354 AD
- **Compiler:** Furius Dionysius Filocalus (Roman calligrapher)
- **Commissioner:** Valentinus (wealthy Roman Christian aristocrat)
- **Original:** LOST - only Renaissance copies survive
- **Best copies:**
  - Vienna manuscript (partial)
  - Vatican manuscripts
  - 17th century copies of lost Carolingian copy

### Why It Matters
- **Earliest mention of December 25 as Christ's birth** (in Depositio Martyrum section)
- **Earliest mention of December 25 Sol Invictus festival** (in calendar section)
- Both in SAME document - can't prove which came first
- Critical evidence for Christmas origins debate

## THE TWO DECEMBER 25 ENTRIES

### Entry 1: The Pagan Calendar (Part 6)
**Location:** Section VI - the Roman calendar of festivals
**Text:** "VIII Kal. Ian. N·INVICTI·CM·XXX"
**Translation:** "8th day before Kalends of January. Birth of the Unconquered. 30 chariot races."
**Meaning:**
- VIII Kal. Ian. = December 25 (Roman date counting)
- N·INVICTI = Natalis Invicti = "Birthday of the Unconquered [Sun]"
- CM·XXX = Circenses Missus XXX = 30 chariot races scheduled
- This is a PAGAN festival entry - Sol Invictus

### Entry 2: The Christian List (Part 10)
**Location:** Section X - Depositio Martyrum (list of martyr death dates + Christ's birth)
**Text:** "VIII kal. Ian. natus Christus in Betleem Iudeae"
**Translation:** "8th day before Kalends of January. Christ born in Bethlehem of Judea"
**Meaning:**
- Same date (December 25)
- Christian entry for Christ's nativity
- Listed alongside martyr commemorations
- This is the EARLIEST written evidence of Dec 25 Christmas

### The Problem
- Same document, same year (354 AD)
- We can't tell from this which tradition is older
- The document records BOTH - doesn't claim one borrowed from other

## THE COMPETING THEORIES

### Theory 1: Christians Borrowed from Sol Invictus
**The Claim:**
- Emperor Aurelian established Sol Invictus as official cult in 274 AD
- He (allegedly) set December 25 as the main festival
- Christians adopted this date to ease pagan conversion
- "Christmas is just rebranded paganism"

**The Problems:**
- ⏳ VERIFY: Did Aurelian actually establish Dec 25 as Sol Invictus festival?
- No evidence of Dec 25 Sol festival BEFORE the Chronography of 354
- Aurelian's Sol temple dedication was October 25, not December 25
- The "borrowing" theory assumes facts not in evidence

### Theory 2: The Calculation Hypothesis
**The Claim:**
- Christians calculated Christ's birth date independently
- Based on belief that great figures die on their conception date
- March 25 = date of crucifixion (and thus conception/Annunciation)
- Add 9 months → December 25

**The Evidence:**
- This reasoning appears in early Christian writers
- ⏳ VERIFY: Which writers? Exact quotes?
- Hippolytus of Rome (c. 204 AD) may mention this calculation
- African Christian tradition (De Pascha Computus, 243 AD)

**The Problems:**
- The calculation seems contrived to modern ears
- But it's what early sources actually say
- Doesn't prove they DIDN'T also borrow from pagans

### Theory 3: Winter Solstice Convergence
**The Claim:**
- December 25 is close to winter solstice (Dec 21-22)
- "Birth of the sun" / "light returning" symbolism universal
- Both pagans and Christians independently found this date meaningful
- Not borrowing, but parallel development

**The Evidence:**
- Symbolism makes sense for both traditions
- Solstice celebrations widespread in many cultures
- But this is speculation, not documented

### Theory 4: History of Religions School (Older View)
**The Claim:**
- Late 19th/early 20th century scholars assumed religious syncretism
- Christianity borrowed extensively from "mystery religions"
- Christmas from Sol Invictus, Easter from spring fertility cults, etc.

**The Problems:**
- Much of this scholarship is now questioned
- Often assumed parallels without proving direction of influence
- Steven Hijmans and others have critiqued the Sol Invictus claims

## SOL INVICTUS - WHAT WE ACTUALLY KNOW

### The Cult
- **Sol Invictus** = "The Unconquered Sun"
- Roman sun god cult
- Became prominent under Aurelian (emperor 270-275 AD)
- Temple dedicated in Rome 274 AD

### The December 25 Question
- **The claim:** Aurelian established Dec 25 as Sol Invictus birthday
- **The evidence:** Chronography of 354 (80 years later)
- **The problem:** No contemporary source from Aurelian's time mentions Dec 25
- ⏳ VERIFY: Steven Hijmans's research on this

### What Hijmans Argues
- Steven Hijmans (classicist) has argued:
  - No evidence Dec 25 was Sol Invictus festival before 354 AD
  - Aurelian's temple dedication was October 25, not December 25
  - The "Sol Invictus origin of Christmas" may be backwards
  - Christians may have had Dec 25 first, pagans added it later

## THE "VIII KAL. IAN." DATING

### How Roman Dates Worked
- Romans counted backwards from three fixed points each month:
  - Kalends (1st)
  - Nones (5th or 7th)
  - Ides (13th or 15th)
- "VIII Kal. Ian." = 8th day before January 1 = December 25

### Why This Matters
- Shows the document is using standard Roman calendar
- December 25 in both entries is the same actual date
- Not a translation issue
