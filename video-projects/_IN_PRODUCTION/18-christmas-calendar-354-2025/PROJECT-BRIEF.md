## THE MYTH TO ADDRESS

### The Internet Claim
"December 25 was originally the Roman festival of Sol Invictus (the Unconquered Sun). Christians stole the date to make conversion easier."

### The Nuanced Reality
1. The Chronography of 354 is the EARLIEST document mentioning both:
   - December 25 as "N·INVICTI" (Birth of the Unconquered [Sun])
   - December 25 as Christ's birth (in a separate section)

2. But here's the problem: Both entries are in the SAME document from 354 AD
   - We don't have earlier evidence of Sol Invictus on Dec 25
   - We don't have earlier evidence of Christmas on Dec 25
   - So which came first? The document doesn't tell us

3. The scholarly debate:
   - **History of Religions school:** Christians borrowed from pagans
   - **Calculation hypothesis:** Christians calculated Dec 25 from March 25 (Annunciation/crucifixion)
   - **Modern consensus:** It's complicated, evidence is thin either way

### Your Angle
Don't take sides in the culture war. Show the document. Explain what it actually says. Let viewers see that "Christmas was stolen from pagans" is oversimplified - but so is "Christmas has nothing to do with paganism."

## THE CHRONOGRAPHY OF 354 - KEY DETAILS

### What It Is
- **Full name:** Chronography of 354 / Codex-Calendar of 354
- **Compiler:** Furius Dionysius Filocalus (calligrapher)
- **Patron:** Valentinus (wealthy Roman Christian)
- **Date:** 354 AD
- **Original:** Lost - we have later copies (Renaissance manuscripts)

### Contents (16 parts)
1. Dedications and planetary tables
2. Illustrations of emperors
3. List of consuls
4. Easter table
5. List of city prefects
6. **THE CALENDAR** (12 months with festivals) ← KEY SECTION
7. Illustrations of months
8. Natales Caesarum (imperial birthdays)
9. List of Roman bishops
10. **Depositio Martyrum** (death dates of martyrs)
11. Depositio Episcoporum (death dates of bishops)
12. World chronicle
13. Chronicle of the city of Rome
14. List of regions of Rome
15. List of notable buildings
16. Notitia (administrative list)

### The December 25 Entries

**In Part 6 (Calendar) - December section:**
- "VIII Kal. Ian. N·INVICTI·CM·XXX"
- Translation: "8th day before the Kalends of January. Birth of the Unconquered. 30 chariot races."
- This is the PAGAN entry - Sol Invictus festival

**In Part 10 (Depositio Martyrum) - also December:**
- "VIII kal. Ian. natus Christus in Betleem Iudeae"
- Translation: "8th day before Kalends of January. Christ born in Bethlehem of Judea"
- This is the CHRISTIAN entry - earliest mention of Dec 25 Christmas

### The Forensic Question
- Same document, same year (354 AD)
- One section has pagan festivals, another has Christian dates
- Which tradition is older? The document doesn't tell us
- But it DOES show both existed by 354 AD
