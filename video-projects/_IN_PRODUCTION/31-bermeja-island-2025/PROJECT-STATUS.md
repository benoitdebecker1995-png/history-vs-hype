## Project Brief

### The Story

In 1539, Spanish cartographer Alonso de Santa Cruz documented an island called "Bermeja" off the Yucatan Peninsula. For the next 400+ years, mapmakers copied this island onto their charts without verifying it existed.

In 2000, when Mexico and the US negotiated oil rights in the Gulf of Mexico's "Western Gap," Mexico couldn't prove Bermeja existed. Without the island, Mexico's Exclusive Economic Zone shrank—and with it, access to an estimated 22.5 billion barrels of oil.

The 2009 UNAM study concluded: the island never existed. It was a cartographic error replicated for over 400 years.
