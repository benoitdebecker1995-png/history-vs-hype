## SECTION 5: The Verdict

### ✅ CLAIM 8: Cartographic Error Conclusion (VERIFIED)

**UNAM Conclusion:**
> "The island does not exist at the charted coordinates (22° 33' N, 91° 22' W)"

**Mechanism:** The "habit of replication" - once an island entered cartographic records, it was endlessly copied without verification, gaining "the prestige of tradition and legitimacy."

**Supporting evidence:**
- 1775 and 1789 Spanish expeditions failed to find it
- 1885 US Hydrographic Office called it "more than doubtful"
- Multiple searches found nothing
- Geological data shows stable sediments for 5,300+ years

## STEELMAN: The "It Existed" Argument

**For fairness, here's the strongest case that Bermeja was real:**

1. **Detailed descriptions:** 1540 positioning data (23° N, 14 leagues from Cape San Antón, 55 leagues from Alacranes Reef) seems too precise for pure fiction

2. **Consistent cartography:** Unlike some phantom islands that shifted location, Bermeja was drawn in approximately the same place for centuries

3. **UNAM nuance:** Dr. Alcántara Ayala acknowledged the historical accounts are too "precise" and "detailed" to easily dismiss

4. **Possible explanations if real:**
   - Located elsewhere in the Gulf (positional error)
   - Consumed by sea (erosion, rising sea levels)
   - Submerged (British 19th century maps marked it as "sunk")

**Verdict:** Likely a cartographic error, but UNAM scientists allow for the possibility it existed elsewhere.
