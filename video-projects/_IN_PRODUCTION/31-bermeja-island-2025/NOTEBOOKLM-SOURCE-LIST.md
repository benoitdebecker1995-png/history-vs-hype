## PRIMARY DOCUMENTS

### P1: 2000 US-Mexico Maritime Treaty
**URL:** https://www.state.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/US_Mexico_2000_withExtension.pdf
**Format:** PDF
**Why:** The actual treaty that divided the Western Gap. Check if Bermeja is mentioned.

### P2: Congress.gov Treaty Document
**URL:** https://www.congress.gov/treaty-document/106th-congress/39/document-text
**Format:** Web page (copy text or print to PDF)
**Why:** Full treaty text with congressional context.

### P3: UNAM 2009 Press Release
**URL:** https://www.dgcs.unam.mx/boletin/bdboletin/2009_374.html
**Format:** Web page (Spanish - copy text)
**Why:** Official scientific conclusion that island never existed.

### P4: 1539 Santa Cruz Atlas
**URL:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2021668468/
**Format:** Digital images
**Why:** First map showing Bermeja. Look for "El Yucatán e Islas Adyacentes" section.

### P5: State Dept Limits in the Seas No. 45
**URL:** https://www.state.gov/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/LIS-45.pdf
**Format:** PDF
**Why:** Official US-Mexico maritime boundary documentation.

## ACADEMIC BOOKS

### A1: The Phantom Atlas
**Author:** Edward Brooke-Hitching
**Publisher:** Chronicle Books, 2016
**Why:** Comprehensive study of phantom islands and cartographic errors. Author is Fellow of the Royal Geographical Society.
**Look for:** May include Bermeja entry, methodology of how phantom islands persisted on maps.

### A2: Phantom Islands of the Atlantic
**Author:** Donald S. Johnson
**Publisher:** Walker & Company, 1994
**Why:** Academic treatment of phantom islands, includes chapter on cartographic inaccuracies in Age of Discovery.
**Look for:** "Mapping the Unknown Seas" chapter on why errors persisted.

### A3: Phantom Islands: In Search of Mythical Lands
**Author:** Dirk Liesemer
**Publisher:** Haus Publishing / University of Chicago Press, 2019
**Why:** Scholarly treatment of 30 phantom islands with documented discovery and disproval.

### A4: Lost Islands
**Author:** Henry Stommel
**Publisher:** Dover Publications, 2017
**Why:** Detailed scholarly text with maps and referenced historical narratives.

## ACADEMIC JOURNAL ARTICLES

### J1: US-Mexico Continental Shelf Boundary
**Journal:** American Journal of International Law (Cambridge University Press)
**Author:** Sean D. Murphy
**Volume:** Vol. 95, Iss. 2 (April 2001), pp. 393-394
**URL:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-journal-of-international-law/article/abs/usmexico-continental-shelf-boundary-in-gulf-of-mexico/

### J2: Transboundary Hydrocarbon Reservoirs Agreement
**Journal:** Journal of World Energy Law & Business (Oxford Academic)
**Author:** José Luis Herrera Vaca
**Volume:** Vol. 5, Iss. 3 (September 2012), pp. 235-247
**Why:** Legal framework for oil exploitation in Western Gap.

### J3: Mexico's Continental Shelf Extension
**Journal:** Mexican Law Review (Elsevier/ScienceDirect)
**URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1870057816300312
**Why:** Explains 62/38% split and legal basis for boundary.

## WHAT TO LOOK FOR

### In the Treaty (P1, P2, P5):
- [ ] Any mention of "Bermeja" or "Isla Bermeja"
- [ ] How EEZ boundaries were calculated
- [ ] The 1.4 nautical mile buffer zone provision
- [ ] Oil/gas drilling moratorium terms

### In the UNAM Study (P3):
- [ ] Exact methodology (ship name, dates, equipment)
- [ ] The "5,300 years" finding - exact quote
- [ ] Names of scientists involved
- [ ] Geological evidence (or lack thereof)

### In the Santa Cruz Atlas (P4):
- [ ] Exact description of Bermeja
- [ ] Coordinates or positioning given
- [ ] Any physical description (size, color, features)

### In Academic Books (A1-A4):
- [ ] How phantom islands persisted for centuries
- [ ] Why cartographers copied without verifying
- [ ] Precedents for Bermeja (other phantom islands)
- [ ] Academic framework for understanding cartographic errors

### In Journal Articles (J1-J3):
- [ ] Legal analysis of Western Gap division
- [ ] How EEZ is calculated under international law
- [ ] Whether Bermeja factored into negotiations
- [ ] Oil reserve estimates with citations
