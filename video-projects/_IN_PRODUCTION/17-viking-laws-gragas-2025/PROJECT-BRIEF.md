## THE MYTHS TO ADDRESS

### Myth 1: Vikings Were Lawless Barbarians
**The claim:** Vikings were savage raiders with no civilization, just violence.
**The evidence:**
- Grágás contains 400+ pages of detailed legal procedures
- The Althing (est. 930 AD) is the oldest parliament still in existence
- Lawspeaker was one of the most powerful positions in society
- Disputes were resolved through arbitration, not just combat

### Myth 2: Vikings Solved Everything With Violence
**The claim:** If Vikings had a problem, they just killed each other.
**The evidence:**
- Grágás has elaborate compensation tables for injuries
- Blood feuds were regulated by law (limited retaliation)
- Outlawry was the ultimate punishment - exile, not execution
- Holmgang (duel) had strict rules and was a legal procedure

### Myth 3: Viking Society Was Primitive
**The claim:** Vikings had no sophisticated institutions.
**The evidence:**
- Complex property law (inheritance, land rights)
- Marriage contracts with divorce provisions
- Detailed commercial law (trade, contracts)
- Procedural law (how to bring cases, evidence rules)

## GRÁGÁS - KEY DETAILS

### What It Is
- "Grey Goose Laws" - unclear why called this
- Oral law of Iceland, memorized by lawspeakers
- Written down after Christianization (c. 1117-1118)
- Reflects pre-Christian Viking Age legal traditions
- Two main manuscripts survive (13th century copies)

### Entertaining Provisions to Highlight

**Insult Laws (Níð):**
- Specific fines for calling a man "beardless"
- Fines for calling someone a coward
- Fines for implying a man was sexually passive
- Composing insulting poetry (níðvísur) was a serious crime

**Violence Regulations:**
- Detailed compensation for specific injuries (eye, hand, etc.)
- Different penalties based on victim's status
- Rules about when killing was justified
- Blood feud limitations

**Property & Commerce:**
- Inheritance rules
- Land boundary disputes
- Livestock theft compensation
- Trade contract enforcement

**Procedural Law:**
- How to summon someone to court
- Evidence requirements
- Witness rules
- Appeal procedures

### The Althing
- Established 930 AD at Þingvellir (Thingvellir)
- Annual gathering for two weeks each summer
- All free men could attend
- Laws recited from memory by Lawspeaker
- Disputes adjudicated
- Still exists today (Icelandic parliament)
