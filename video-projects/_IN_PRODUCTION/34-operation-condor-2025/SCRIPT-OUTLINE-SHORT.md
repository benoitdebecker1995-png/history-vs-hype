## STRUCTURE

### [0:00-0:45] HOOK - The Denial

**Visual:** Black screen, then text appearing: "For decades, they said it was a conspiracy theory."

**Script direction:**
- Open with the DENIAL, not the history
- Establish that governments, CIA, dictators ALL denied Operation Condor existed
- Frame it as "leftist propaganda" that officials dismissed

**Key beat:** "Six South American dictatorships secretly coordinating assassinations across borders? With CIA support? That was the claim. And for twenty years, anyone who said it out loud was called paranoid."

**Transition:** "Until December 22nd, 1992. In a police station in Paraguay."

---

### [0:45-2:00] THE MAN - Almada's Story

**Visual:** Photo of Almada (if available), map of Paraguay

**Script direction:**
- Introduce Martín Almada - NOT as a hero, but as a man searching for his own file
- Former teacher, arrested 1974 on charges of "intellectual terrorism"
- Tortured for nearly four years
- **The cruelest detail:** Police called his wife, told her he was dead, played recordings of him being tortured. She died from the psychological stress.

**Key beat:** "After the dictatorship fell in 1989, Almada returned to Paraguay with one goal: he wanted to find his file. He wanted to understand what had been done to him and why."

**Transition:** "Then he got a tip."

---

### [2:00-3:30] THE DISCOVERY - December 22, 1992

**Visual:** Map showing Lambaré suburb, exterior of building (if available), recreation of scene

**Script direction:**
- Build tension: whistleblower (former policeman who wanted a job for his son) provides hand-drawn map
- Almada, Judge José Agustín Fernández, and a TV crew arrive at police station
- Police try to block them. Shouting. Tension.
- The judge threatens contempt of court
- **The moment:** A formidable padlock. A crowbar. The lock breaks.

**Key beat:** "What they found inside wasn't just Almada's file. It was rooms - plural - stacked floor to ceiling with documents. Three to four tons of paper. The bureaucratic memory of a dictatorship that truly believed it would never be held accountable."

**Visual opportunity:** Show the actual archive photos if available - the mountain of paper

**Transition:** "They called it the Archives of Terror."

---

### [3:30-5:00] THE RECEIPTS - What Was Inside

**Visual:** Document images, the founding charter, Kissinger cable

**Script direction:**
- Walk through what they found:
  1. **The founding charter** - signed November 28, 1975, with signatures of intelligence chiefs from five countries
  2. **The invitation letter** from Manuel Contreras proposing "something similar to Interpol, but dedicated to subversion"
  3. **Operational documents** labeled for distribution to "Condor" - proof of prisoner exchanges, coordinated kidnappings
  4. **The Kissinger connection** - documents that corroborated what declassified U.S. cables would later confirm: the CIA knew from the beginning

**Key beat:** "This wasn't a conspiracy theory. It was a filing system. They kept receipts for their murders."

**Evidence display:** Show specific documents on screen with translations

**Transition:** "And once the files were public, the denials stopped working."

---

### [5:00-6:30] THE RECKONING - Justice Arrives

**Visual:** Courtroom footage, conviction headlines, perpetrator photos

**Script direction:**
- The immediate aftermath: Paraguay had no amnesty laws - torturers went to prison
- The ripple effect: These files became evidence in trials across the world
  - Judge Baltasar Garzón used them to arrest Pinochet in London (1998)
  - Argentine courts convicted 15 officers in 2016
  - Italian courts sentenced perpetrators to life in 2021
- **The numbers:** 107 convictions across 45 trials. 262 victims with some measure of justice.

**Key beat:** "Almada went looking for his own file. He found proof of an entire conspiracy. And that proof is still being used in courtrooms today."

---

### [6:30-7:30] CLOSE - The Lesson

**Visual:** Return to Almada, then documents, then modern day

**Script direction:**
- Almada's legacy: He died in April 2024. The trials continue.
- **The 50th anniversary:** November 28, 2025 marks 50 years since Operation Condor was founded. Cases are still pending.
- **The uncomfortable question:** What other archives are sitting in dusty basements? Cairo? Moscow? Langley?

**Final beat:** "They thought the files would never be found. They were wrong."

**End card:** "If you want to know more about what was IN those files - the assassinations, the death flights, the CIA cables - let me know in the comments."
