## AUTOMOTORES ORLETTI

### Location and Physical Description
- **Address:** Venancio Flores 3.519/21, Floresta neighborhood, Buenos Aires
- **Across from:** Sarmiento railway
- **Structure:** Two-story former automobile repair shop
- **Ground Floor:** Old workshop, holding area for prisoners in inhuman conditions
- **Upper Floor:** Office (with Adolf Hitler portrait), cells, large salon for torture/interrogation
- **Environment:** Prisoners heard passing trains and children playing at neighboring elementary school
- **Access:** Large metallic sliding shutter door, password "Operación Sésamo" (Open Sesame)
- **Nicknames:** "El Jardín" (The Garden), "El Taller" (The Garage), "La Cueva de la Vía" (The Railway Cave)

### Management
- **Control:** Argentine State Intelligence Secretariat (SIDE), Department of Tactical Operations I (OT 18)
- **Leadership:** Aníbal Gordon ("El Jefe") - civilian SIDE employee with criminal record, former Triple A death squad leader
- **Military Commander:** SIDE chief General Otto Paladino

### Multinational Participation
- **Argentina:** SIDE agents (Eduardo Ruffo, Miguel Ángel Furci), Intelligence Battalion 601, PFA officers
- **Uruguay:** Primary base for SID and OCOA officers including Major José Nino Gavazzo, Lieutenant Colonel Manuel Cordero, Major Enrique Martínez
- **Chile:** DINA agents including Michael Townley, Exterior Brigade members
- **Other:** Bolivia and Paraguay military personnel

### Prisoner Experience
- Near-starvation (eating only three times in ten days)
- Blindfolded, handcuffed, shackled in freezing environment
- **Torture methods:** "La Parrilla" (electric grill), "Submarino" (immersion in excrement/urine), hanging by hooks, beatings, systematic sexual torture

### Survivor Testimonies
**Enrique Rodríguez Larreta Piera (Uruguayan journalist):** Described being kidnapped, tortured with electric shocks, hearing Uruguayan officers like Gavazzo using numeric aliases ("302")

**Sara Méndez:** Had her 21-day-old son stolen at moment of abduction. Gavazzo mocked her: "this war is not against children"

**Luis Taub:** Guards held "asado" (barbeque) to celebrate successful raids while prisoners listened from cells

### Statistics
- **Capacity:** ~300 individuals passed through during 6 months (May-November 1976)
- **Survival rate:** One of lowest of any Argentine secret prison

### Notable Cases
- **Julién-Grisonas Children:** Anatole (4) and Victoria (19 months) held after parents murdered, later abandoned in Valparaíso
- **Gelman Family:** Marcelo killed, dumped in cement drum. María Claudia (pregnant wife) taken to Uruguay, gave birth, then murdered
- **Cuban Diplomats:** Jesús Cejas Arias and Crescencio Galañena Hernández tortured to death for JCR information
- **Uruguayan Legislators:** Zelmar Michelini and Héctor Gutiérrez Ruiz executed, left in car trunk
- **PVP Leaders:** Gerardo Gatti and León Duarte ransomed for millions before being disappeared

### Closure
Shut down after José Ramón Morales and Graciela Vidaillac escaped through upper-story window in November 1976, exposing the facility

## ASSASSINATION OF GENERAL CARLOS PRATS (1974)

### Basic Facts
- **Date:** September 30, 1974, early morning hours
- **Location:** Palermo district, Buenos Aires, Malabia Street near Libertador Avenue
- **Method:** Remote-control car bomb, ~2 pounds C4 plastic explosives
- **Device placement:** Michael Townley personally slid under vehicle in garage, affixed to transmission support bar
- **Detonation:** CB radio transmitter modified with tone device

### Perpetrators
- **Lead operatives:** Michael Townley and wife Mariana Callejas (DINA Exterior Department agents)
- **Local support:** Enrique Arancibia Clavel (DINA liaison Buenos Aires), Argentine neofascist group Milicia (Triple A affiliate), Juan Martín Ciga Correa
- **Orders from:** DINA Chief Manuel Contreras, overseen by Pedro Espinoza and Raúl Iturriaga Neumann

### Evidence
- Townley confessions to U.S. and Argentine authorities (1990s)
- Four boxes of secret DINA documents seized from Arancibia Clavel (surveillance reports, correspondence with DINA Santiago)
- Recognized as first major international assassination by Condor prototype before formal naming

### Outcome
- **General Carlos Prats:** Killed instantly, blown out of car
- **Sofía Cuthbert (wife):** Trapped in vehicle, burned to death

### Convictions
- **Enrique Arancibia Clavel:** Life imprisonment (Argentina, 2000)
- **Manuel Contreras:** Life imprisonment (Chile, 2008)
- **Pedro Espinoza and Raúl Iturriaga Neumann:** 15-20 years

- **Date:** October 6, 1975, evening

- **Both survived** but permanently injured

- **Ana Fresno (wife):** Permanently paralyzed (bullet hit spine)

### Convictions
- **Manuel Contreras:** 20 years in absentia (Italy, 1995)
- **Raúl Iturriaga Neumann:** 18 years in absentia (Italy, 1995)
- **Michael Townley:** 15 years in absentia (remained in U.S. Witness Protection)

## DEATH FLIGHTS (VUELOS DE LA MUERTE)

### Overview
Cornerstone of "industrial repression" - allowed regimes to dispose of opponents while maintaining "plausible deniability" (no body = "absent forever" not murdered)

### Perpetrator Testimonies

**Adolfo Scilingo (Argentine Navy captain, ESMA):**
- Televised confession March 1995
- Admitted to two flights in 1977
- Personally pushed 30 naked, drugged individuals into Atlantic Ocean
- Death-flight duty rotated among nearly all naval officers (collective complicity)
- "Recurring nightmare" of first flight - slipped and nearly fell out with struggling prisoner

**Víctor Ibáñez (Argentine Army sergeant, Campo de Mayo):**
- Television appearance April 1995
- Confessed to loading prisoners onto planes, throwing into sea
- "Lost count" of how many flights

**Chilean Pilots:**
- 2003: Five pilots indicted for "Operation Puerto Montt"
- Transported prisoners, attached iron girders to bodies, dropped from helicopters into Pacific

**Jorge Tróccoli (Uruguayan Navy Captain):**
- Admitted awareness of flights
- Expressed preference for shooting, called flights "not a combat solution"

### Operational Mechanics
1. **The Ruse:** Prisoners told they were being "transferred" to rehabilitation camps or receiving "vaccinations"
2. **Drugging:** Sodium pentothal ("Pen-Naval") administered to make drowsy
3. **Second dose:** Medical doctors administered onboard to ensure full unconsciousness
4. **Disposal:** Victims undressed before being pushed out
5. **Sinking:** Stomach cavities slit open to prevent floating - called "fish food" (comida de pescado)

### Locations and Aircraft
- **Argentina:** ESMA, El Palomar Air Force Base; Navy Skyvans, Army C-130s
- **Uruguay:** First clandestine flight (vuelo cero) 1974 used Fairchild
- **Chile:** Helicopters for sea disposal or Andean peaks

### Estimated Victims
- **ESMA:** 1,500-2,000 (Scilingo estimate, 1976-1977)
- **Campo de Mayo:** ~2,300 (Ibáñez estimate)
- **Chile:** 400-500 (Judge Guzmán investigation)
- **Argentina total:** Up to 30,000 disappeared, significant percentage via death flights

### How Information Emerged
- **Washed-up bodies:** 1976 onwards, Uruguayan side of Río de la Plata
- **December 1977:** Bodies of activists including two French nuns and Mothers of Plaza de Mayo founder washed ashore Buenos Aires Province
- **Survivor observations:** Witnessed preparations; clothing/effects reappeared in camp storerooms after "transfers"
- **1984 CONADEP report (Nunca Más):** First official state document recording testimony
- **1995 "Scilingo Effect":** Confessions provided "factual spine" for judicial progress

## COLONIA DIGNIDAD

### Overview
- **Name:** Sociedad Benefactora y Educacional Dignidad
- **Location:** 37,000-acre German immigrant enclave, rural Parral, Seventh Region of Chile
- **Founded:** 1961 by Paul Schafer (fugitive fleeing West Germany child sexual abuse charges)
- **Description:** "State within a state" with own security, property, political alliances
- **U.S. State Department:** Described as having "neo-Nazi tendencies"

### DINA Relationship
- Served as "DINA's closest collaborator"
- **Visitors:** Manuel Contreras and Augusto Pinochet both personal visitors
- **Direct Command:** DINA regional intelligence brigade operated from Colony-owned house in Parral
- **Foreign Intelligence Bridge:** Facilitated exchange of "subversive" lists between German BND and DINA

### Operational Role
- **Clandestine detention/extermination:** Prisoners transferred to "disappear" - up to 112 held in 1977
- **Training:** DINA agents received specialized interrogation/torture training from former Nazi officials
- **Communications Hub:** Housed massive radio installation - central transceiver for DINA foreign apparatus (Condortel)
- **Weapons Development:** Linked to biological/chemical weapons. DINA chemist Eugenio Berríos worked there. Pinochet "very excited" about Sarin nerve gas laboratory

### Victims
**Boris Weisfeiler:** U.S. citizen, Penn State professor, disappeared 1985 while backpacking. Military informant "Daniel" confessed: detained by army patrol, classified as "Soviet spy," turned over to Colony security chief, interrogated, executed with shot to neck

**Jorge Fuentes Alarcón:** Chilean MIR leader, JCR courier - survivors reported man with Brazilian accent during torture at DINA/Colony-linked site

**Juan René Muñoz Alarcón:** DINA agent who provided "desperate" declaration to Vicariate of Solidarity confessing participation in Colony disappearances

**Osvaldo Romo:** Notorious DINA torturer confirmed in testimony that Colony was torture/disappearance site

### Legal Accountability
- **Judge Juan Guzmán Tapia:** Led investigation, 2002 property search (Weisfeiler case)
- **Contreras and Espinoza:** Charged with kidnappings/murders involving Colony
- **Osvaldo Romo:** Extradited from Brazil, sentenced 2004
- **Rafael Gonzalez:** Indicted 2003 as accomplice in Charles Horman murder
- **Paul Schafer:** Eventually faced trial for human rights abuses and child molestation

## CONDOR CODE SYSTEM

### Country Designations (Condortel)
- **Condor 1:** Argentina
- **Condor 2:** Bolivia
- **Condor 3:** Chile
- **Condor 4:** Paraguay
- **Condor 5:** Uruguay
- **Condor 6:** Peru (joined 1978)
- **Condor 7:** Ecuador (joined 1978)

Note: Brazil provided technological support rather than primary numerical identifier initially

### Prisoner Fate Codes (DINA Card File)
- **"Puerto Montt":** Prisoner marked "to be killed on land"
- **"La Moneda":** Prisoner "to be killed by being dropped from airplane into sea"
- Prisoners slated for these fates categorized as "incapable of rehabilitation" (irrécupérable)

### Other Operational Codes
- **"Phase III":** Most secret level - special teams for international assassinations
- **"Teseo":** Top-secret assassination unit (Argentina, Chile, Uruguay) for "liquidating" European targets
- **"Operation Code 12":** Assassination disguised as "contrived accident"
- **"Packages" (Paquetes) / "Merchandise" (Mercadería):** Kidnapped human beings
- **"Oscar":** Code names for Uruguayan OCOA officers in Argentine prisons (Oscar 1 = highest ranking)
- **"300 Series":** Numerical aliases for Uruguayan SID officers (302 = Gavazzo, 303 = Cordero)
- **"PEN-Naval":** Sodium pentothal for death flight drugging

### Condortel Technical Details
- Originally used telexes in primitive codes
- Upgraded to Swiss-made cryptographic machines (from Brazil)
- Later: Hagelin Crypto H-4605 equipment (from Argentina)
- **Panama "Parent Station":** U.S. military communications facility in Canal Zone served as encrypted hub

### Day-to-Day Functioning
- Formal telex requests between nations via numerical designation
- Example: "Condor 1" (Argentina) requests records on Uruguayan detainee from "Condor 5" (Uruguay)
- **Condoreje (Condor Axis):** Buenos Aires office processed Condortel intelligence into operational orders for raids/kidnappings

## FBI ROLE AND ROBERT SCHERRER

### Robert Scherrer Background
- **Position:** FBI Legal Attaché, U.S. Embassy Buenos Aires (starting 1972)
- **Coverage:** Argentina, Chile, Paraguay, Uruguay, Bolivia
- **Reputation:** "One-man intelligence station" - deep network within local police/intelligence
- Made establishing relationship with DINA Chief Contreras a personal priority
- Understood mentality of Latin American secret police, traded jokes with torturers while hating their methods

### The Jorge Fuentes Alarcón Case (May 1975)
- First major instance of documented U.S. official participation in proto-Condor operations
- **June 6, 1975:** Scherrer sent formal letter to Chilean General Ernesto Baeza notifying of Fuentes's arrest in Asunción
- **Content shared:** Fuentes's true identity, JCR courier role, names and addresses from address book (contacts in New York, Dallas, Puerto Rico)
- Promised to share FBI domestic investigation results
- **Key point:** FBI shared intelligence knowing it was obtained under torture

### Other FBI Cooperation
- Routinely advised Chilean investigative police regarding interrogation results from Paraguay
- 1975 Conference of American Armies: Southern Cone governments proposed FBI maintain more open exchange on "terrorists" in U.S.
- **December 1976:** FBI Director Clarence Kelly sent thank-you note to Paraguayan torturer Antonio Campos Alum for "cooperation"
- **Carlos the Jackal:** Scherrer claimed Fuentes/Santucho interrogation intelligence led to French DST raid on Carlos's Paris apartment (June 1975)

### CHILBOM Investigation (Post-Letelier)
- **Code name:** CHILBOM
- Fundamental shift from cooperation to adversarial investigation of DINA
- **September 28, 1976:** Scherrer sent top-secret report correctly defining Operation Condor and Phase III
- Solved murder by tracing Paraguayan passport photos (copied by Ambassador Landau) identifying Townley and Fernández Larios
- Led to first international prosecution of Pinochet regime

## JULIÉN CHILDREN - FULL STORY

### Parents
- **Mario Roger Julién and Victoria Lucía Grisonas:** Uruguayan political activists, PVP founding members
- Living in exile in Buenos Aires

### The San Martín Raid (September 26, 1976)
- Large Argentine-Uruguayan military task force raided family home
- Neighbors witnessed Mario shielding children in bathtub during gunfight
- Mario captured and murdered after attempting to flee
- Victoria Grisonas dragged by hair, thrown on pavement, taken away
- Officers refused to let neighbors care for toddlers

### Detention and Rendition
- **Orletti:** Children held ~10 days while mother tortured
- **~October 7:** Transferred to second prison in SID building, Montevideo
- **Late December 1976:** Flown to Chile via small plane

### Anatole's Memory
"I remember being on a small plane and someone telling me 'go to the cockpit.' That was the first time I saw the shape of the plane controls, and I also stared at the Andes, with their snow-capped mountain tops, which I had never seen before."

### Discovery in O'Higgins Square (December 22, 1976)
- **Morning:** Toddlers abandoned in O'Higgins Square, Valparaíso, Chile
- Stepped out of black car with tinted windows
- Spent entire day alone and holding hands, playing on playground rides
- **Evening:** Rides owner called Carabineros when no one came
- Local press noted "River Plate" accents (Argentina/Uruguay)

### Adoption and Identification
- Placed in Playa Ancha orphanage
- 1977: Adopted by Jesús Larrabeiti (dental surgeon) and Silvia Yáñez
- Grandmother María Angélica Cáceres never stopped searching

**1979 Breakthrough:**
- Chilean social worker in Venezuela identified siblings from published photos
- July 1979: Letter from France alerted UNHCR Santiago
- Compromise reached: Children stay with adoptive family in Chile, maintain contact with biological relatives in Uruguay

### Legal Proceedings
- **2009-2021:** Five trials in Montevideo and Buenos Aires
- **Convictions:** Eight Uruguayan and seven Argentine officers for parents' murder and children's abduction
- **Orletti V (2020):** Four intelligence officers sentenced to life for homicide and concealment
- **Inter-American Court (2021):** Condemned Argentina for parents' disappearance and breach of children's rights

## ARCHIVES OF TERROR DISCOVERY - DETAILED

### The Discovery
- **Date:** December 22, 1992
- **Location:** Police facility (Department of Production) in Lambaré, suburb of Asunción, Paraguay
- **Specific location:** Rear room on second floor

### Key Figures
**Dr. Martín Almada:**
- Paraguayan pedagogue and lawyer
- Victim of Stroessner dictatorship
- Arrested 1974, tortured, imprisoned nearly four years on "intellectual terrorism" charges
- Wife died from psychological stress (police called her, told her he was dead, played recordings of his torture)
- After Stroessner's fall (1989), returned and used "habeas data" constitutional provision to petition for his records

**The Whistleblower:**
- Former policeman who wanted a job for his son
- Provided tip to human rights activist/congressman Francisco de Vargas
- Gave Almada hand-drawn map of where documents had been moved

**Judge José Augustín Fernández:**
- Issued court order to search premises

### The Discovery Scene
- Police official claimed records destroyed
- Judge used constitutional authority to order entry
- Formidable padlock broken with crowbar
- Found rooms filled floor to ceiling with documents

### Document Details
- **Volume:** 3-4 tons of paper; 593,000 pages when microfilmed (some sources say 700,000)
- **Source:** Complete records of Department of Investigation of Capital Police (DIPC), formerly headed by torturer Pastor Coronel
- **Contents:** Ringed binders, chronological interrogation reports, jailhouse logbooks, surveillance tapes, photos, rap sheets with photos/fingerprints of Paraguayan and foreign prisoners

### Critical Finds
- **Founding charter of Operation Condor**
- **Original invitation from Manuel Contreras** to 1975 meeting
- **Meeting agenda**
- Documents from 1976-1977 explicitly labeled for distribution to "Condor"
- Proof of Phase II prisoner exchanges and coordinated transnational operations

### Immediate Aftermath
- News broke days before Christmas 1992, circulated worldwide
- **Paraguay prosecutions:** Pastor Coronel and General Benito Guanes Serrano imprisoned (no amnesty laws in Paraguay)
- **February 1993:** Chilean government under Aylwin formally requested all Condor-related information
- **Protection:** Judge Fernández, activists, reporters formed human chain to move documents to Palace of Justice for safekeeping

### Use in Trials
- Transformed Condor from suspected conspiracy to settled legal fact
- Foundation for Judge Baltasar Garzón's investigations (led to 1998 Pinochet arrest in London)
- Used by Judge Rodolfo Canicoba Corral in landmark Argentine Condor trial
- **2014:** Relative provided "Rollo 143" (1,000+ documents) to Argentine court after Paraguay failed to respond
- Used in trials in Italy and France
