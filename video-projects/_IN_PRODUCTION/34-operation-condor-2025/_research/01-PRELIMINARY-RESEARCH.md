## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Operation Condor was a secret intelligence alliance formed on **November 28, 1975** in Santiago, Chile, between six South American military dictatorships (Chile, Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay, Bolivia, Brazil) to coordinate assassinations, kidnappings, and torture of left-wing opponents across borders and internationally. The CIA provided communications equipment and had knowledge of operations. The network resulted in at least **805 confirmed victims** of transnational repression between 1969-1981.

**Modern stakes:** As of 2024, trials are ongoing with over 100 people convicted so far. Declassified documents continue to reveal deeper CIA involvement than previously known.

## KEY VERIFIED FACTS (Preliminary - Need Academic Verification)

### Founding

- **Date:** November 28, 1975
- **Location:** Santiago, Chile, at the War College building
- **Attendees:** Intelligence chiefs from Chile (DINA), Argentina, Bolivia, Paraguay, Uruguay
- **Purpose stated:** Create "something similar to INTERPOL" for tracking "subversives"
- **Naming:** Proposed by Uruguayan delegation after Chile's national bird

**Sources for verification:**
- [National Security Archive declassified documents](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/events/operation-condor-1975-1980)
- [Operation Condor documentation trove](https://nsarchive2.gwu.edu/NSAEBB/NSAEBB514/)

### Participating Countries

1. **Chile** - Coordinating center, led by DINA (secret police)
2. **Argentina** - Major participant
3. **Uruguay** - Active participant
4. **Paraguay** - Participant
5. **Bolivia** - Participant
6. **Brazil** - Joined later, limited participation

### CIA Role

- **1976:** CIA documented second Condor meeting (May 31-June 2, 1976)
- **Equipment:** CIA provided communications and coordination infrastructure
- **Knowledge:** State Department knew of operations as early as August 1976
- **Kissinger intervention:** On September 16, 1976, Henry Kissinger blocked State Department démarche warning against Condor operations
  - Just **5 days before** Letelier assassination

**Sources:**
- [CIA documentation](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/chile/2016-09-23/cia-pinochet-personally-ordered-letelier-bombing)
- [Kissinger role in blocking warnings](https://theintercept.com/2023/06/16/henry-kissinger-assassination-orlando-letelier-chile/)

### Orlando Letelier Assassination (High-Profile Case)

- **Date:** September 21, 1976
- **Location:** Washington, D.C. (Sheridan Circle, Embassy Row)
- **Time:** 9:35 AM
- **Method:** Car bomb placed under vehicle
- **Victims:** Orlando Letelier (Chilean diplomat/economist) + Ronni Moffitt (American colleague)
- **Perpetrators:** Michael Townley (DINA operative) + Cuban exiles recruited in U.S.
- **Who ordered it:**
  - 1987 CIA report: Pinochet personally ordered it
  - 2023 Townley confession: DINA chief Manuel Contreras ordered it
  - Both may be true (Pinochet → Contreras → Townley)

**Why it matters:** This was state terrorism on U.S. soil, demonstrating Condor's international reach.

**Sources:**
- [CIA conclusion on Pinochet's role](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/chile/2016-09-23/cia-pinochet-personally-ordered-letelier-bombing)
- [Washington Post investigation](https://www.washingtonpost.com/sf/national/2016/09/20/this-was-not-an-accident-this-was-a-bomb/)

### Victim Statistics

- **Confirmed victims:** At least 805 victims of transnational repression (1969-1981)
- **2016 trial victims:** 173 victims from Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Paraguay, Peru, Uruguay
- **Countries affected:** Operations in Europe (France, Italy, Portugal) and U.S.

**Source:** [Academic study on transnational repression](https://academic.oup.com/isq/article/68/2/sqae035/7637878)

### Modern Justice (2016-2024)

- **2016 Buenos Aires trial:** 15 convicted (including former dictator Reynaldo Bignone, Uruguayan colonel Manuel Cordero)
- **Total convictions to date:** Over 100 people sentenced across 40 trials
- **Ongoing (2024-2025):** 8 trials in progress (7 in Uruguay, 1 in Italy)
- **Recent convictions:** Troccoli convicted for murders of Rafaela Filipazzi, José Agustín Potenza, Elena Quinteros

**Sources:**
- [2016 trial verdict](https://plancondor.org/en/node/1476)
- [Trial accountability analysis](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/latamcaribbean/2019/08/01/argentinas-operation-condor-trial-opened-up-new-paths-to-accountability-for-past-atrocities-in-south-america-and-beyond/)

### Declassified Documents

- **2016:** Obama-Macri agreement released 43,000 declassified U.S. documents to Argentina
- **2023-2024:** National Security Archive continues releasing documents
- **Key revelations:** Deeper CIA coordination than previously documented
