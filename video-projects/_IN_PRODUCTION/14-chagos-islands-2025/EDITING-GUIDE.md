## EDITING PHILOSOPHY

**The Golden Rule:** If the B-roll doesn't make your argument stronger, stay on camera.

**B-roll is EVIDENCE, not decoration.**

Use B-roll when:
- Showing PRIMARY SOURCE DOCUMENTS (memos, treaties, ICJ ruling text)
- Displaying geographic locations (Indian Ocean maps, Diego Garcia)
- Providing visual proof of deportation (testimony quotes, eyewitness accounts)
- Showing historical photos (Ramgoolam father/son, Lancaster House)
- Displaying institutional evidence (ICJ building, UN votes)

Stay on camera when:
- Making arguments and interpretations ("This is where it gets uncomfortable for everyone")
- Explaining causation and connecting events
- Presenting steelman arguments ("To be fair to the UK position...")
- Building emotional weight (deportation narrative)
- Delivering rhetorical questions and pivots
