## CORE THESIS

**The Double Betrayal:** The UK committed the original wrong (illegal detachment, forced deportation). Mauritius committed the second betrayal (trading away Diego Garcia return rights for sovereignty and money). The Chagossians remain sidelined by both.

## SECTION 2: The UK/US Position (Steelman)

### Military Value of Diego Garcia

**Operations Supported:**
- Afghanistan invasion - B-1, B-2 stealth bombers, B-52s flew from atoll, dropped more ordnance than any other squadrons
- Iraq invasions (twice)
- Gulf War 1990 - 18 prepositioned cargo ships arrived Saudi Arabia 8 days after deployment orders
- Counter-piracy in Gulf of Aden
- Ongoing surveillance, reconnaissance, logistics
- Threatens Iran, China, Russia
- Supports Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty (seismic monitoring)
- Global Positioning System (GPS) support

**Strategic Value:**
- "One of the world's great natural harbors in its protected lagoon"
- Can host aircraft carrier task force + large cargo ships
- Enough land for large airstrip
- 7,000 miles closer to Central Asia/Persian Gulf than continental US bases
- "The single most important military facility we've got" in region
- Fills "power vacuum" after British withdrawal East of Suez
- US military aim: "to run the planet from Guam and Diego Garcia"

### UK Legal Arguments at ICJ

1. **Continuous sovereignty since 1814** (Treaty of Paris)
2. **Administrative dependency** - Chagos "has never been part of Mauritius," merely managed for "administrative convenience"
3. **Constitutional detachment** - Royal Prerogative allows detaching territory from one colony to another
4. **Valid consent** - Mauritian negotiating team included leaders of 5 major parties, "democratically elected," had "sufficient mandate"
5. **Conditional arrangement** - UK committed to return when "no longer needed for defence purposes"
6. **Subsequent reaffirmation** - Mauritius reaffirmed agreement through own laws and Constitution
7. **ICJ jurisdiction** - Advisory Opinion "not legally binding," referral was "misuse of powers"

### Judge Donoghue's Dissent (the 1 in 13-1)

**Core argument:** ICJ should have refused to give Advisory Opinion

**Reasoning:**
- Matter is a "quintessentially bilateral... dispute over territorial sovereignty"
- General Assembly request "has the effect of circumventing the absence of United Kingdom consent" to judicial settlement
- This "undermin[es] the integrity of the Court's judicial function"
- Court's ruling shows its stated principle of declining jurisdiction when consent is circumvented was "hollow"
- Questions of decolonization and sovereignty could not be separated
- ICJ decided "very issues that Mauritius has sought to adjudicate" without UK consent

### UK Acknowledgment of Moral Wrong
> "The UK fully accepts that the manner in which the Chagossians were removed... and the way they were treated thereafter, was shameful and wrong, and it deeply regrets that fact."

**Note:** UK denies legal culpability but acknowledges moral failure.

## SECTION 3: The Mauritius Critique

### 1965 Deal Benefits to Mauritius
- Secured independence
- Received £3 million payment
- Later received £650,000 grant (1972) for Chagossian resettlement - never implemented
- PM Ramgoolam told Parliament: "we had no choice"
- Critics: Ramgoolam effectively "sold" the islands

### Mauritius Treatment of Chagossians (1973-present)
- Complicit in displacement
- Left Chagossians "languishing on the beach"
- £650,000 resettlement scheme never implemented
- Chagossians faced chronic impoverishment
- Remained at bottom of Mauritian society
- 58% unemployment rate for Chagossian women (1980s surveys)
- Widespread ethnic and racial discrimination

### 2025 Treaty Critique
- Mauritius agreed to bar Diego Garcia resettlement for 99 years
- Chagossians excluded from negotiations ("state-to-state basis")
- Mauritius prioritized sovereignty + £3.4B over Chagossian return rights
- Resettlement clause is "in substance declaratory" - "does not secure the right to return"
- No guarantee £40M trust fund will be Chagossian-controlled

### The "Double Betrayal" Frame
| Actor | Betrayal | Result |
|-------|----------|--------|
| UK | Illegal expulsion, "wrongful act" | Secured military base, discharged financial obligation via Trust Fund |
| Mauritius | Accepted exclusion of Chagossians, barred Diego Garcia return for 99 years | Gained sovereignty + £3.4B, sacrificed full right of return |

## SECTION 4: Chagossian Positions (Divided)

### Support for 2025 Deal
- **Chagos Refugees Group (CRG)** led by Olivier Bancoult
- Called it "historic day for us"
- Welcomes completion of Mauritian decolonization
- Aligns with ICJ finding that self-determination belonged to "colonial unit as a whole"

### Opposition to 2025 Deal
- **Chagossian Voices** and other groups
- Oppose denial of full right of return to Diego Garcia
- Sought recognition as "indigenous people" before UN
- UN experts: "grave concern" that agreement "fails to guarantee" Chagossian rights

### What Chagossians Actually Want
1. **Full return to Diego Garcia** - core demand, not just outer islands
2. **Compensation and control** - reparations + control over £40M Trust Fund
3. **Recognition** - distinct identity, unconditional rights to live on islands
4. **Self-determination** - Judge Sebutinde argued Chagossians possess "societal/cultural identity that separates them from wider Mauritian people"

## SECTION 5: Legal Nuances

### Why ICJ Ruled Detachment Unlawful
- NOT because of generalized coercion (duress under Vienna Convention only prohibits military/physical force)
- Applied "heightened scrutiny" standard for colonial agreements
- Consent must represent "free and genuine expression of the will of the people concerned"
- UK conditioned independence (a fundamental right) on surrender of territory
- This subverted the true will of the colonial people

### Decolonization vs. Bilateral Dispute
- UK framed as "bilateral sovereignty dispute"
- ICJ ruled it was fundamentally about decolonization
- Decolonization = multilateral obligation owed to entire international community (erga omnes)
- Requires UK to cease unlawful administration regardless of Mauritius consent

### Self-Determination vs. Resettlement
- ICJ confirmed territorial integrity as corollary of Mauritius's self-determination
- But relegated Chagossian resettlement to UN General Assembly for "modalities"
- Success depends on how Mauritius handles these secondary human rights obligations

## SECTION 6: Comparisons

### Cyprus Sovereign Base Areas
- UK retained Akrotiri and Dhekelia when Cyprus gained independence
- Population NOT deported
- UK continues to engage with local government
- **Contrast:** Chagos required "strategic population cleansing"

### Gibraltar
- UK retains control (disputed by Spain)
- Gibraltarian people determine territory's future via self-determination
- **Contrast:** In Chagos, territorial integrity of colonial unit overrode local population's rights

### Mayotte (France)
- France separated Mayotte from Comoros in 1975
- Mayotte voted to stay with France
- **Contrast:** In Chagos, no referendum held, ICJ ruled this invalidated detachment

### Other Strategic Expulsions
- **Bikini Atoll:** US displaced population for nuclear testing
- **Thule, Greenland:** US secret agreement with Denmark to remove 150 Inughuit
- **Kwajalein, Marshall Islands:** Thousands moved to small island, "ghetto of the Pacific"
- **Pattern:** Systematic displacement of small, lightly populated islands under colonial control, often non-Western populations

## SECTION 9: Causal Sequence

**[Cold War Anxiety]** → consequently → **[Strategic Island Concept]**
- 1956 Suez Crisis questioned Britain's long-term Indian Ocean control
- US feared "power vacuum" as British influence waned
- Non-Western world "growing increasingly unstable"
- Western bases vulnerable to "local non-Western opposition"

**[Strategic Island Concept]** → which meant that → **[British Colonies Targeted]**
- Most suitable Indian Ocean islands controlled by UK
- UK facing financial crisis, looking to cut East of Suez costs
- UK could handle "political heat" and "dirty work of expulsion"

**[British Colonies Targeted]** → thereby → **[Chagos Selected]**
- "Minimal population, isolation, present status, historical and ethnic factors"
- "Neglected minor backwater"
- "One of the world's great natural harbors"
- Aldabra rejected due to "Aldabra Affair" (scientists concerned about tortoises/birds)

**[Chagos Selected]** → as a result → **[Deportation Decided]**
- US demanded "exclusive control (without local inhabitants)"
- Permanent inhabitants could lead to UN scrutiny, "threaten the life of the base"
- UK policy: "maintain the fiction that inhabitants are not permanent"
