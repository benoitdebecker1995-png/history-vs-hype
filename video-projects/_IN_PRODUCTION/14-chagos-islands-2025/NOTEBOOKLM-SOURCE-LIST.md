## PRIORITY 5: ACADEMIC BOOKS ⭐ KEY FOR AUTHORITY

### 12. *Island of Shame* by David Vine ⭐ CRITICAL
**What:** THE definitive academic book on Diego Garcia and Chagossian deportation
**Author:** David Vine, Professor of Anthropology, American University
**Publisher:** Princeton University Press (2009)
**ISBN:** 978-0691149837
**Amazon:** https://www.amazon.com/Island-Shame-Secret-History-Military/dp/0691149836
**Why Critical:**
- Based on declassified documents and interviews with exiled islanders
- First major book to reveal US-UK conspiracy to expel Chagossians
- Academic authority you can cite throughout video
**Extract:**
- Deportation details with primary sources
- US false claim to Congress that islands had "no permanent inhabitants"
- Interview testimonies

### 13. *The International Court of Justice and Decolonisation* (Cambridge, 2021)
**What:** Edited academic volume on the Chagos ICJ ruling
**Editors:** Thomas Burri and Jamie Trinidad
**Publisher:** Cambridge University Press
**URL:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/international-court-of-justice-and-decolonisation/C7013E53429C9C42EA75B21A5CB07252
**Why Important:**
- 13 chapters analyzing ICJ reasoning
- Legal scholars' perspectives on self-determination
- Academic context for your legal claims
**Extract:**
- Court's legal reasoning explained
- Consequences for international law
- Expert analysis of decolonization rules

### 14. *Silence of the Chagos* by Shenaz Patel
**What:** Literary/historical account of Chagossian experience
**Publisher:** Restless Books (2019)
**URL:** https://restlessbooks.org/bookstore/silence-of-the-chagos
**Why Useful:**
- Written from Chagossian perspective
- Translated from French (Mauritian author)
- Cultural identity and justice themes
**Extract:**
- Personal narratives
- Cultural impact of deportation
