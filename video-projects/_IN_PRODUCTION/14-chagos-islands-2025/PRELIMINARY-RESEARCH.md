## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The UK detached the Chagos Archipelago from Mauritius in 1965, three years before Mauritius gained independence. PM Harold Wilson threatened Mauritian PM Ramgoolam with "independence or no independence" to force consent. Between 1967-1973, the UK forcibly deported 1,500-2,000 Chagossians to make way for a US military base on Diego Garcia. In 2019, the ICJ ruled the UK's administration "unlawful." In 2025, the UK agreed to return sovereignty to Mauritius - but excluded Chagossians from negotiations, and the new Mauritian PM has rejected the deal terms.

**"Both Extremes Wrong" Angle:**
- UK says: "Legal process, defense necessity, we're returning it now"
- Mauritius says: "Colonial theft, sovereignty is ours"
- Reality: UK used documented duress AND Mauritius excluded the actual indigenous people from 2025 negotiations

## SECTION 1: THE 1965 DETACHMENT

### The Duress - "Frighten Him with Hope"

**The Smoking Gun Memo (September 22, 1965):**

Sir Oliver Wright, Private Secretary to PM Harold Wilson, prepared a note that read:

> "Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam is coming to see you at 10:00 tomorrow morning. **The object is to frighten him with hope: hope that he might get independence; Fright lest he might not unless he is sensible about the detachment of the Chagos Archipelago.**"

**Source:** [UK pressured Mauritius into giving up Chagos Islands, court told - ITV News](https://www.itv.com/news/2018-09-03/uk-pressured-mauritius-into-giving-up-chagos-islands-court-told)

### The Lancaster House Meeting (September 23, 1965)

PM Harold Wilson told PM Ramgoolam:

> "In theory, there were a number of possibilities. **The Premier and his colleagues could return to Mauritius either with Independence or without it.** On the Defence point, Diego Garcia could either be detached by order in Council or with the agreement of the Premier and his colleagues. The best solution of all might be Independence and detachment by agreement."

**Translation:** "Agree to give up Chagos or you won't get independence."

**Legal Finding:** Judges Kateka and Wolfrum at the Permanent Court of Arbitration found that Wilson's statement "amounted to duress."

Sir Anerood Jugnauth testified to the ICJ that "Mr Ramgoolam understood Mr Wilson's words 'to be in the nature of a threat.'"

### The £3 Million Payment

The UK paid Mauritius £3 million "in recognition of the detachment" - effectively purchasing the islands under coercion.

The UK also promised to return the islands "when no longer required for defence purposes."

## SECTION 2: THE DEPORTATION (1967-1973)

### Who Were the Chagossians?

The Chagossians are an Afro-Asian ethnic group descended from:
- Freed African slaves brought in late 18th century
- Indian and Malay workers

Under international law, they are **the indigenous peoples of the Chagos Archipelago** - the earliest human settlers.

**Population in 1965:** 1,500-1,750 people (about 1,360 on the islands)

### How They Were Removed

**The Steamer Trap (1969):**
Starting March 1969, Chagossians visiting Mauritius for supplies found they could no longer board the steamer home. Told their work contracts had "expired." Left stranded - homeless, jobless, no support.

**The Threats:**
- Told they would be "shot or bombed" if they didn't leave
- An American official told one resident: "If you don't leave you won't be fed any longer"

**The Dog Massacre:**
Sir Bruce Greatbatch ordered all dogs on Diego Garcia killed. Over 1,000 pets were gassed with exhaust fumes from military vehicles.

**Final Removal:**
- Main forced removal: July-September 1971
- Last group removed from Peros Banhos: April 1973

**Source:** [Expulsion of the Chagossians - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expulsion_of_the_Chagossians)

### Human Rights Watch Verdict (2023)

> "The forced displacement of the Chagossians and ongoing abuses amount to **crimes against humanity** committed by a colonial power against an indigenous people."

**Source:** [HRW Report - "That's When the Nightmare Started"](https://www.hrw.org/report/2023/02/15/thats-when-nightmare-started/uk-and-us-forced-displacement-chagossians-and)

## SECTION 3: THE 2019 ICJ ADVISORY OPINION

### The Ruling (February 25, 2019)

**Vote:** 13-1 majority

**Key Finding #1 - Unlawful Detachment:**
> "The process of decolonization of Mauritius was not lawfully completed when that country acceded to independence."

**Key Finding #2 - Ongoing Wrongful Act:**
> "The United Kingdom's continuing administration of Chagos Islands constitutes an international wrongful act for which it bears responsibility."

**Key Finding #3 - Obligation to End:**
> "The United Kingdom is under an obligation to bring to an end its administration of the Chagos Archipelago as rapidly as possible."

**Source:** [ICJ Advisory Opinion - Case 169](https://www.icj-cij.org/case/169)

### UN General Assembly Vote (May 22, 2019)

The GA adopted a resolution demanding UK withdrawal within 6 months.

**Vote:** 116 in favor, 6 against (Australia, Hungary, Israel, Maldives, UK, US), 56 abstentions

**UK Response:** Ignored it. Foreign Office minister Alan Duncan stated the ruling was "merely an advisory opinion and not a legally binding judgment."

## SECTION 4: THE 2025 TREATY

### Terms of the Agreement (Signed May 22, 2025)

**Sovereignty:**
- Mauritius gains full sovereignty over Chagos Archipelago
- UK retains authority over Diego Garcia military base

**Diego Garcia Lease:**
- Initial period: 99 years
- Extendable by 40 years (if both agree)
- Then extendable again

**Financial Terms:**
- £101 million/year average for 99 years (£3.4 billion total)
- £40 million trust fund for Chagossians
- £45 million/year for 25 years for Mauritius development projects

**Resettlement:**
- Mauritius can resettle Chagossians on ALL islands EXCEPT Diego Garcia
- Diego Garcia remains off-limits

**Source:** [UK Government Treaty Publication](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ukmauritius-agreement-concerning-the-chagos-archipelago-including-diego-garcia-cs-mauritius-no12025)

### New PM Rejects Deal (December 2024)

Navin Ramgoolam (elected November 2024) rejected the terms:

> "The draft deal would not produce the benefits that the nation could expect."

**Concerns:**
- 99-year lease allegedly discussed as 200 years
- Financial terms inadequate
- Negotiations reopened

**Current Status:** Renegotiating as of December 2024. Treaty signed May 2025 despite initial rejection.

**Source:** [The National - UK's Chagos deal rejected by new Mauritius PM](https://www.thenationalnews.com/news/uk/2024/12/18/uks-chagos-deal-rejected-by-new-mauritius-prime-minister/)

### UN Experts Criticize Treaty (June 2025)

> "Agreement between Mauritius and UK **fails to guarantee rights of Chagossians** including their right to return to Diego Garcia, effective remedy and reparations, and their cultural rights."

**Source:** [OHCHR Press Release](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-releases/2025/06/agreement-between-mauritius-and-uk-fails-guarantee-rights-chagossians-say-un)
