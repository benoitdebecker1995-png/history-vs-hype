## PRIMARY SOURCES (SMOKING GUNS)

### 1. "Frighten Him with Hope" Memo (September 22, 1965)
> "The object is to frighten him with hope: hope that he might get independence; Fright lest he might not unless he is sensible about the detachment of the Chagos Archipelago."
- Sir Oliver Wright, Private Secretary to PM Harold Wilson

### 2. Lancaster House Threat (September 23, 1965)
Harold Wilson to PM Ramgoolam:
> "The Premier and his colleagues could return to Mauritius either with Independence or without it."

### 3. ICJ Advisory Opinion (February 25, 2019)
> "The United Kingdom's continuing administration of Chagos Islands constitutes an international wrongful act."
- 13-1 majority

### 4. Human Rights Watch (2023)
> "The forced displacement of the Chagossians and ongoing abuses amount to crimes against humanity."

### 5. UN Experts (June 2025)
> "Agreement between Mauritius and UK fails to guarantee rights of Chagossians."
