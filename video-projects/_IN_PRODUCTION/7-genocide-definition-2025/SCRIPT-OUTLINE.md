### SECTION 3: PRIMARY SOURCE - UN Convention Text (1:30-3:30) [~220 words]

**THIS IS THE CORE - PRIMARY SOURCE HEAVY**

**Visual:** B-roll - UN Genocide Convention text on screen

**Script:**
"In 1948, the United Nations defined genocide in the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide. Article II is the part that matters. Let me read it to you.

[TEXT ON SCREEN - Article II displayed]

'Genocide means any of the following acts committed with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial or religious group, as such:

(a) Killing members of the group;
(b) Causing serious bodily or mental harm to members of the group;
(c) Deliberately inflicting on the group conditions of life calculated to bring about its physical destruction in whole or in part;
(d) Imposing measures intended to prevent births within the group;
(e) Forcibly transferring children of the group to another group.'

Notice what's NOT in there. Numbers. There's no minimum death count. The Holocaust killed six million. The Rwandan genocide killed 800,000. But the Convention doesn't say 'genocide requires X deaths.'

What it does require is two things: specific acts AND intent to destroy a group.

That second part—intent—is where everything falls apart."

### SECTION 4: THE INTENT REQUIREMENT (3:30-4:30) [~110 words]

**Visual:** Talking head → B-roll examples

**Script:**
"Here's why intent matters. Civilian deaths in war? Horrible. War crime? Possibly. Genocide? Only if there's intent to destroy that group.

Ethnic cleansing—forcibly removing people from territory? Also horrible. Also a war crime. But it's not genocide unless the intent is to destroy the group, not just move them.

This is what courts look for: orders, plans, statements showing intent to destroy a group 'as such.' Not just kill people in war. Not just gain territory. Destroy the group itself.

That's a high bar. And it's supposed to be."

### SECTION 5: EXAMPLES - Applying the Standard (4:30-6:30) [~220 words]

**Visual:** Maps + talking head

**Script:**
"So let's apply this. Four cases people argue about.

**Rwanda, 1994.** Hutu extremists killed 800,000 Tutsis in 100 days. Radio broadcasts said 'cut down the tall trees.' Lists of Tutsi families. Roadblocks checking ID cards. The International Criminal Tribunal found this was genocide. Why? Clear intent to destroy Tutsis as a group.

**Bosnia, 1995.** Srebrenica. Serbian forces killed 8,000 Bosnian Muslim men and boys. The ICJ ruled this was genocide—not the entire Bosnian war, but Srebrenica specifically. Why? Evidence showed intent to destroy Bosnian Muslims 'in part.'

**Xinjiang, 2010s-present.** China detained over a million Uyghurs in camps, forced sterilizations documented, cultural destruction. Some legal scholars say Article II(d)—preventing births—applies. China denies intent. No international court has ruled yet. The debate is exactly about intent.

**Gaza, 2023-2024.** Over 40,000 dead. South Africa brought a case to the ICJ claiming genocide. Israel denies intent to destroy Palestinians as a group, says it's targeting Hamas. The Court hasn't issued a final ruling. Legal experts are divided—not on whether it's horrific, but on whether it meets the Convention's definition.

Notice the pattern. The question is never 'is this bad.' The question is 'does it meet the legal standard.'"

### SECTION 6: WHY IT MATTERS (6:30-7:30) [~110 words]

**Visual:** Talking head

**Script:**
"So why does this matter? Because words have power. If we call everything genocide, the word loses meaning. If we call nothing genocide, we can't prevent it.

The Convention exists for a reason. It's not just about punishing crimes after they happen. Article I says countries have a duty to prevent genocide when they see it forming.

But you can't prevent something if you can't define it. And you can't hold people accountable if you're using the term politically instead of legally.

The next time you see 'genocide' in a headline, ask: are the five acts happening? Is there intent to destroy a group? What do the courts say?"

**Retention Trigger (7:00):** "Article I says countries have a duty to prevent"
