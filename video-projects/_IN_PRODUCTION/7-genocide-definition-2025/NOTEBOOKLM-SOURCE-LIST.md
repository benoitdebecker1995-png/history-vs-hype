## PRIMARY SOURCES (Tier 1 - Must Have)

### 1. UN Genocide Convention (1948)
**Source Type:** International Treaty (Public Domain)
**Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL - This is THE primary source
**URL:** https://www.un.org/en/genocideprevention/genocide-convention.shtml
**Alternative:** https://treaties.un.org/Pages/ViewDetails.aspx?src=TREATY&mtdsg_no=IV-1&chapter=4

**What to Download:**
- Full text of Convention (PDF)
- Article II (full text - this is what you'll display on screen)
- Article I (prevention obligation)

**Why Critical:**
- You'll read Article II verbatim in the video
- Need exact legal language for on-screen display
- This is the foundation of the entire video

---

### 2. ICJ Ruling: Bosnia v. Serbia (2007)
**Source Type:** International Court of Justice Judgment
**Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL
**URL:** https://www.icj-cij.org/case/91
**Case Name:** Application of the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide (Bosnia and Herzegovina v. Serbia and Montenegro)
**Date:** February 26, 2007

**What to Download:**
- Full judgment (PDF - warning: 171 pages)
- Summary of judgment (shorter, key findings)
- Srebrenica section (paragraphs 297-297)

**Why Critical:**
- This is the leading ICJ case defining "intent to destroy in part"
- Ruled Srebrenica WAS genocide
- Shows how courts apply Article II in practice

**Key Quote to Extract:**
> "The intent must be to destroy at least a substantial part of the particular group."

---

### 3. ICTR Ruling: Prosecutor v. Akayesu (1998)
**Source Type:** International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda
**Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL
**URL:** https://unictr.irmct.org/en/cases/ictr-96-4
**Case Number:** ICTR-96-4-T
**Date:** September 2, 1998

**What to Download:**
- Judgment summary
- Section on definition of genocide (paragraphs 495-508)

**Why Critical:**
- First international conviction for genocide since 1948
- Clear example of "intent to destroy" evidence (radio broadcasts, lists, etc.)
- Rwanda is your clearest "YES, this was genocide" example

**Key Evidence Types:**
- Radio broadcasts ("cut down the tall trees")
- Government-issued lists of Tutsi families
- Roadblocks checking ethnic ID cards

---

### 4. ICJ Provisional Measures: South Africa v. Israel (2024)
**Source Type:** ICJ Order (Ongoing Case)
**Priority:** 🟡 HIGH
**URL:** https://www.icj-cij.org/case/192
**Case Name:** Application of the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide in the Gaza Strip
**Date:** January 26, 2024 (provisional measures)

**What to Download:**
- Provisional measures order (PDF)
- Summary of South Africa's application
- Summary of Israel's response

**Why Important:**
- Most current/relevant example (2023-2024)
- Shows ongoing legal debate about intent
- You'll say "ICJ hasn't issued final ruling" - this proves it

**What NOT to Say:**
- ❌ "The ICJ ruled it is genocide" (FALSE - provisional measures ≠ final ruling)
- ✅ "The ICJ issued provisional measures and hasn't ruled on the merits"
