## THE COMMENT TO FACT-CHECK

**Common YouTube Comment Argument:**
> "Guatemala has claim to Belize because the Maya lived there before the British arrived"

**Why This Needs Fact-Checking:**
- Technically true (Maya did live in Belize pre-British)
- BUT the conclusion is false (this doesn't give Guatemala legal claim)
- Guatemala doesn't even use this argument at the ICJ

---

## RESEARCH FINDINGS (Preliminary)

### What Guatemala ACTUALLY Argues at ICJ:
✅ **Uti possidetis juris** - Inherited Spain's territorial rights (1821)
✅ **1859 treaty breach** - Britain didn't fulfill obligations
❌ **NOT Maya heritage** - Official arguments based on Spanish colonial inheritance

### Maya Demographics Reality:
| Country | Maya Population | % of Population |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Guatemala | ~7 million | 41-43% |
| Belize | ~50,000 | 10-12% |
| Mexico | ~2 million | ~1.5% |

**Key Insight:** Maya lived (and still live) across Guatemala, Belize, Mexico, Honduras, El Salvador. No single state can claim "Maya successor" status.

### Why the Comment Logic Fails:
1. **Guatemala doesn't use this argument** - If Maya heritage was strong legal claim, they'd use it at ICJ
2. **Maya lived everywhere** - Same logic would let Mexico claim Guatemala
3. **Maya still live in Belize** - 2015 Caribbean Court granted Maya land rights IN Belize
4. **Would break all borders** - "Indigenous people lived here first" would invalidate every modern state
