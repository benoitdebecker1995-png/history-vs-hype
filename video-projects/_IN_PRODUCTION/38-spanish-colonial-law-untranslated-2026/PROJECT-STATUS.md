## Modern Hook

**The myth:** "Spanish land grants were properly adjudicated by American courts"

**Who uses it today:** Legal scholars, Hispanic land grant communities in New Mexico/Colorado/Texas, indigenous rights advocates

**Modern consequence:** The Court of Private Land Claims (1891-1904) rejected over 90% of Spanish land grants covering 36 million acres. English-speaking judges couldn't read the Spanish legal framework. In 2002, the Colorado Supreme Court upheld descendants' access rights in *Lobato v. Taylor* — still being litigated as recently as 2018.

**Hook strength:** 4/5 — active legal disputes, living descendants, territorial dimension

## Intended Techniques

- **Opening:** Document-First (show the 4 volumes, then modern court case)
- **Structure:** "The Specific Site" pattern — one land grant case as microcosm, then zoom out
