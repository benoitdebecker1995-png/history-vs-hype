#### 1. **"The Cambridge History of Communism" (2017) - 3 Volumes**
**Editors:** Silvio Pons & Stephen A. Smith
**Publication:** Cambridge University Press, 2017
**Why Critical:**
- Most comprehensive recent academic synthesis
- Volume 1: World Revolution and Socialism in One Country 1917–1941
- Volume 2: The Socialist Camp and World Power 1941–1960s
- Volume 3: Endgames? Late Communism in Global Perspective, 1968 to the Present
- Multi-author, international perspectives
- Incorporates post-Cold War archival access

**What to Extract:**
- Volume 1, Introduction: Modern scholarly consensus on Marx vs. Lenin vs. Stalin
- Chapter on "Marxist Theory and Practice" - how academics now view the divergence
- Quantitative data on USSR economic performance vs. capitalist economies
- Recent interpretation of "dictatorship of proletariat"

**Specific Prompts:**

2. "What quantitative economic data does the Cambridge History provide comparing USSR performance to Western economies?"
3. "How do the editors explain why the 'withering away of the state' never occurred?"

**What to Extract:**

- What Marx actually predicted vs. what he left ambiguous
- Recent scholarly consensus on Marx's intent

**Specific Prompts:**

**Specific Prompts:**
