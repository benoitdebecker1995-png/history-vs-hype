## PRIMARY SOURCES (Tier 1 - Must Have)

### 1. The Communist Manifesto (1848)
**Source Type:** Primary political text
**Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL - This is Marx's foundational definition
**Authors:** Karl Marx & Friedrich Engels
**Date:** 1848

**Where to Find:**
- Marxists.org: https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1848/communist-manifesto/
- Project Gutenberg: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/61
- Public domain, fully available online

**What to Extract:**
- Marx's definition of communism as end-stage of history
- "Dictatorship of the proletariat" explanation
- Stateless, classless society vision
- Specific quotes on what communism IS and ISN'T
- 10 planks of immediate demands vs. final communist stage

**Why Critical:**
- Marx's own words defining communism
- Primary source you can display on screen
- Counters "nobody knows what Marx actually said" problem

---

### 2. Das Kapital, Volume 1 (1867) - Excerpts
**Source Type:** Primary economic/political theory
**Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL
**Author:** Karl Marx
**Date:** 1867

**Where to Find:**
- Marxists.org: https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1867-c1/
- Focus on Chapter 1 (Commodities), Chapter 32 (Historical Tendency of Capitalist Accumulation)
- No need for full text, just relevant excerpts on collective ownership

**What to Extract:**
- Collective ownership of means of production
- How Marx envisioned transition from capitalism to communism
- "From each according to ability, to each according to need"
- Critique of private property

**Why Critical:**
- More detailed explanation than Manifesto
- Economic theory behind communism
- Shows what Marx meant by "workers own means of production"

### 3. Soviet Constitution (1936 - Stalin Constitution)
**Source Type:** Primary legal document
**Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL
**Official Title:** Constitution (Fundamental Law) of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (1936)

**Where to Find:**
- Marxists.org: https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/stalin/works/1936/12/05.htm
- Seventeen Moments in Soviet History: http://soviethistory.msu.edu/1936-2/

**What to Extract:**
- Articles on state ownership of property (Article 4-12)
- Communist Party supremacy (Article 126)
- Central planning structure
- How USSR defined itself as "socialist state"

**Why Critical:**
- Shows what USSR actually implemented vs. Marx's theory
- Primary source for "USSR built centralized state, not stateless society"
- Can display on screen alongside Marx quotes for comparison

## SOURCE VERIFICATION NOTES

**Red Flags to Watch For:**
- ⚠️ Sources claiming "Marx wanted totalitarianism" (FALSE - he wanted stateless society)
- ⚠️ Sources claiming "USSR was exactly what Marx wanted" (FALSE - Marx wanted statelessness)
- ⚠️ Sources claiming "communism always leads to genocide" (moral claim, not definitional)
- ⚠️ Sources claiming "USSR wasn't communist at all" (FALSE - more nuanced than that)

**Green Flags (Trustworthy):**
- ✅ Primary sources (Marx texts, Soviet constitutions)
- ✅ Academic books with extensive citations (Service, Fitzpatrick, Singer)
- ✅ Sources that acknowledge difference between Marx's theory and USSR practice
- ✅ Sources that explain both what Marx wanted AND why it didn't happen
