## EDITING PHILOSOPHY

**The Golden Rule:** If the B-roll doesn't make your argument stronger, stay on camera.

**B-roll is EVIDENCE, not decoration.**

### Use B-roll when:
- Showing primary sources (1906 Constitution, British reports, CIA documents)
- Displaying maps (1907 Anglo-Russian zones, sphere of influence)
- Presenting quotes on screen (Abrahamian, Woodhouse, CIA history)
- Historical photos (Mossadegh, Mohammad Ali Shah, Majlis building)
- Timeline graphics (1906-1911, August 15-19 1953)
- Pattern diagram (the 5-step cycle)

- Making interpretive arguments ("This is what it established...")
- Rhetorical questions ("Do you see what happened here?")
- Personal observations ("I'll be honest—before I did this research...")
- Emotional emphasis ("Iran's first democracy—crushed.")
- Conclusions and thesis statements
- Transitions between acts

**Target Ratio:** 60% talking head, 40% B-roll (slightly more B-roll than usual due to heavy primary source content)
