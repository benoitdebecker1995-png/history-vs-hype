## ACT 2: THE BAIT AND SWITCH [4 min]

### The First Draft [1 min]

[VISUAL: Hassan Habibi / early constitutional assembly]

June 1979. Four months after the revolution. A draft constitution is published.

It was written by a committee of moderates—lawyers, academics, secular nationalists. And it looked remarkably like... a normal democracy.

Elected president. Powerful parliament. Independent judiciary. The clerics would have religious authority, but not political supremacy.

There was no mention of Velayat-e Faqih—the "guardianship of the jurist"—which would become the foundation of Supreme Leader authority.

So what happened?

### Khomeini's Rejection [1 min]

[VISUAL: Khomeini photo]

Khomeini rejected it. He insisted on an elected Assembly of Experts to write a new constitution.

Now, "elected assembly" sounds democratic. But here's the mechanism.

[VISUAL: Flowchart of constitutional process]

**The Bait and Switch:**

1. **First Draft** (June 1979): Written by moderates. No Supreme Leader.
2. **Khomeini's Rejection**: Insists on elected Assembly of Experts
3. **Rigged Procedures**: Electoral rules favor clerical party
4. **Outcome**: Clerical faction wins 55 of 73 seats
5. **Secular Boycott**: National Front and others refuse to legitimize the process
6. **New Constitution**: Assembly writes Velayat-e Faqih into the document
7. **Referendum**: Approved in December 1979—during hostage crisis fervor

The form was democratic. An elected assembly. A referendum.

The substance was the opposite. Procedures designed to produce a predetermined outcome.

### The Guardian Council - Spreadsheet [2 min]

[VISUAL: Guardian Council structure diagram]

And here's the mechanism they created—the reason Iranian elections don't work like you think they do.

**The Guardian Council:**

| Component | How Appointed | Power |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| 6 Clerics | Direct appointment by Supreme Leader | Veto laws violating "Islamic principles" |
| 6 Jurists | Nominated by judiciary chief (appointed by Leader), confirmed by Majlis | Veto laws violating constitution |
| **Combined** | 12 people, all traceable to Supreme Leader | Veto ANY legislation + Disqualify ANY candidate |

Do you see the closed loop?

The Supreme Leader appoints the clerics directly. The Supreme Leader appoints the judiciary chief who nominates the jurists. The Majlis confirms—but the Majlis members were already vetted by the Guardian Council.

Everyone who has power was selected by someone who was selected by the Supreme Leader.

The Shah had to use the army to shut down the Majlis. The Islamic Republic uses the Guardian Council to ensure the Majlis is pre-filled with loyalists before any election happens.

## ACT 3: DEBUNKING "JUST A THEOCRACY" [4.5 min]

### Where It's Right [1 min]

[VISUAL: "Just an Oppressive Theocracy" graphic]

Now let's be clear. The mechanisms of control are real.

Parsa documents what the Guardian Council actually does:

> "The council eliminated 99 percent of 3,000 reformists who had registered to participate in the 2016 Majles elections."

[SOURCE: Parsa, p.29 - SHOW ON SCREEN]

Ninety-nine percent. Disqualified before voters had a choice.

They've disqualified former President Rafsanjani. They disqualified Hassan Khomeini—Ayatollah Khomeini's own grandson.

The IRGC controls massive portions of the economy:

> "Foundations (Bonyads)... dominated some 40 per cent of the Iranian economy."

[SOURCE: Ansari, p.548]

If you oppose the regime, you may also be opposing your employer.

So the "oppressive theocracy" narrative is accurate about the mechanisms. Where does it go wrong?

### Where It's Wrong - Comparative [2 min]

[VISUAL: Comparative timeline]

It goes wrong by treating Iranian authoritarianism as cultural inevitability.

**Comparative Democratic Traditions:**

| Country | First Constitution | First Elected Parliament |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Iran | 1906 | 1906 |
| Turkey | 1876 (suspended) / 1924 | 1877 / 1923 |
| Egypt | 1923 | 1924 |
| Iraq | 1925 (British-imposed) | 1925 |
| Saudi Arabia | None | None |

Iran had a constitutional democracy in 1906. Not imposed by colonizers. Demanded by Iranians.

While Egypt's parliament was created under British supervision, Iran's was created against foreign opposition.

While Iraq's constitution was written by British mandate authorities, Iran's was written by Iranian revolutionaries.

The protests happening right now aren't an aberration. They're the continuation of a 120-year-old demand.

### The Evidence of Continuity [1.5 min]

[VISUAL: Protest timeline with slogans]

**1906:** 14,000 in British Embassy demanding parliament
**1951:** Unanimous Majlis vote for nationalization
**2009:** Three million in Tehran—"Where is my vote?"
**2022:** 150 cities, 30 provinces—"Woman, Life, Freedom"
**2025:** Bazaar merchants—traditional regime supporters—closing shops in protest

Abrahamian captures the shift in vocabulary:

> "By the end of the century, the key terms were demokrasi, pluralism, moderniyat, hoquq-e beshar (human rights), jam'eh-e madani (civil society)... average Iranians now consider themselves no longer mere subjects of the ruler but full citizens, irrespective of gender, with the inalienable right to participate in national politics."

[SOURCE: Abrahamian, p.6]

They don't say *ri'yat* anymore—subject. They say *shahrvandi*—citizen.

That's not cultural failure. That's the success of Iranian political culture expressing itself despite suppression.

## ACT 4: THE CYCLE REPEATS [4.5 min]

### The Protest Mechanism [2 min]

[VISUAL: Protest comparison table]

Let me show you the pattern. Not just what happened—how.

**2009 - Election Fraud:**
- Trigger: Disputed presidential election
- Response: 3 million march ("Where is my vote?")
- Mechanism: Basij street violence + mass arrests
- Outcome: 70-110 killed, leaders under house arrest to this day

**2019 - Fuel Prices:**
- Trigger: 50-200% price increase
- Response: Protests in 21 cities within hours
- Mechanism: Internet blackout + shoot-to-kill orders

The Supreme Leader's order, according to Reuters:

> "The Islamic Republic is in danger"

And authorities should "stop the protests by any means necessary."

[SOURCE: Reuters - SHOW ON SCREEN]

Amnesty documented 304-321 deaths by name. Reuters sources inside the government said 1,500.

**2022 - Mahsa Amini:**
- Trigger: Woman dies in morality police custody
- Response: 150 cities, all 30 provinces
- Mechanism: Executions, mass detention, UN finding of crimes against humanity
- Outcome: 500+ killed, 22,000 detained, 7 executed

### The 2025 Uprising [1.5 min]

[VISUAL: 2025 protest footage / economic data]

**2025-2026 - Economic Collapse:**
- Trigger: Rial collapses, food prices up 72%
- Response: All 31 provinces
- Mechanism: Ongoing
- Outcome: 3,400+ killed in two weeks

And here's what's different this time. The protests started not with students—but with bazaar merchants. The traditional middle class. People who were supposed to be regime supporters.

When the bazaaris close their shops, something has shifted.

### Why the Cycle Continues [1 min]

[VISUAL: Cycle diagram]

Every time: constitutional demands. Press freedom. Fair elections. Economic accountability. Women's rights.

Every time: the same response mechanism.

Ostovar describes how the Basij operates:

> "Images of basijis, many in plain clothes, beating protesters with iron rods, bats, and clubs were prolifically shared on social media... A subset of the militiamen began to use handguns. They seemed to fire indiscriminately into the crowds..."

[SOURCE: Ostovar, p.184]

The mechanisms evolved. Russian Cossacks in 1908 became Basij militias in 2009 became internet blackouts in 2019.

But the pattern remained: constitutional demands, violent suppression.

## ACT 5: THE ACTUAL TRUTH [3 min]

### What the Evidence Shows [1.5 min]

[VISUAL: Full 200-year timeline]

So what do the sources actually show?

Not "it's all America's fault"—that treats Iranians as passive victims and ignores the internal collaborators, the elite conflicts, the mechanisms that Iranians built to control other Iranians.

Not "Iran is just a theocracy"—that treats Iranian authoritarianism as cultural inevitability and erases 120 years of constitutional struggle.

The evidence shows a cycle:

1. **Constitutional demand** — Iranians build democratic institutions
2. **External or internal threat** — Those institutions threaten someone's power
3. **Domestic collaboration** — Internal elites destroy from within
4. **Violent suppression** — Constitutional government crushed
5. **Trauma and radicalization** — Which fuels the next demand

In 1907 and 1953, foreign powers provided the trigger. In 1979-1981, Iranian revolutionaries did it themselves.

The common thread isn't "foreign interference" or "Islamic culture." It's the refusal of those with power to accept constitutional limits on that power.

### Why Both Narratives Are Comforting [1 min]

[VISUAL: Split screen - both narratives]

Both narratives are comforting. That's why they persist.

"It's all America's fault" says the problem is external. Fix the external factor and Iran will be free. This is satisfying but disempowering—it means waiting for others to act.

"Iran is just a theocracy" says the problem is cultural. Nothing will change until Iranians change themselves. This is satisfying for outsiders—it means the West bears no responsibility and nothing needs to be done.

The evidence says something harder: external intervention AND internal mechanisms created a self-reinforcing cycle that Iranians keep trying to break.

That's not a narrative that assigns clean blame. That's not a narrative that offers easy solutions.

But it's what the documents show.

### The Question [30 sec]

Both the regime and its opponents use 1953. The regime uses it to justify suppression—"foreign hands are everywhere." The opposition uses it to explain their grievances—"we were robbed."

If everyone is trapped in the same story of betrayal, how do you break the cycle?
