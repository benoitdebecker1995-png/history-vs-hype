## Description

Iran had an elected parliament before the Russian Revolution, before the Ottoman constitution was restored, before most of the Middle East.

Then foreign powers destroyed it. Twice.

This video covers the first 120 years of Iran's fight for constitutional democracy—from the 1906 Constitutional Revolution through the 1953 CIA coup.

**What you'll learn:**
- How Iranian merchants, intellectuals, and clerics built a constitutional democracy in 1906
- Why Britain and Russia divided Iran between themselves in 1907 without consulting Iran
- How a Russian officer commanding Iranian troops bombarded Iran's parliament in 1908
- Why the CIA overthrew Mossadegh in 1953—and how they manufactured the "communist threat"
- The psychological legacy of foreign intervention that still shapes Iranian politics today

**Primary sources shown on screen:**
- 1906 Persian Constitution
- Anglo-Russian Convention of 1907 (sphere of influence maps)
- CIA declassified documents (TPAJAX operation)
- Mossadegh's trial statement
- British Foreign Office memos on oil control
