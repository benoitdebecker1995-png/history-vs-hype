## THE MYTHS TO ADDRESS

### Myth 1: Slaves Built the Pyramids
**The claim:** Hebrew slaves (or other enslaved peoples) built the pyramids under the whip.
**The evidence:**
- Papyrus Jarf shows organized workforce with rations and schedules
- Worker villages at Giza show evidence of medical care, beer rations, decent burials
- No contemporary Egyptian source mentions slave labor for pyramids
- Hebrew slavery narrative is from Exodus (written ~1000 years later, about different period)

### Myth 2: Aliens / Lost Technology
**The claim:** Humans couldn't have built pyramids with bronze tools.
**The evidence:**
- Papyrus Jarf details mundane logistics: boats, limestone, schedules
- Worker graffiti found inside pyramids ("Friends of Khufu Gang")
- Engineering methods documented: ramps, levers, copper tools
- Quarry marks match construction sequence

### Myth 3: The "Mystery" of Construction
**The claim:** We have no idea how they were built.
**The evidence:**
- Papyrus Jarf describes the supply chain in detail
- Unfinished pyramids show construction stages
- Tool marks, ramp remnants, worker villages all documented
- "Mystery" is manufactured for entertainment

## THE DIARY OF MERER - KEY DETAILS

### What It Is
- Oldest papyri ever discovered
- Logbook/diary format (like a ship's log)
- Written by Merer, an inspector/middle-manager
- Details limestone transport from Tura quarries to Giza

### Key Entries to Highlight
1. **Daily schedules:** "Day 1: Loading at Tura. Day 2: Sailing to the pyramid."
2. **Workforce organization:** Teams with names like "Companions of the Escort"
3. **Reference to Khufu:** "The noble Khufu" - direct contemporaneous mention
4. **Inspector Merer:** Named individual, not anonymous "slaves"
5. **Rations and logistics:** Food distribution, rest periods

### Discovery Story (2013)
- Found at Wadi al-Jarf, Red Sea coast
- Port facility used for copper mining expeditions
- Pierre Tallet (French Egyptologist) led excavation
- Published 2017 in full academic study
