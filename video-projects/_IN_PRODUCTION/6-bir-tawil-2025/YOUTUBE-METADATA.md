Why do Egypt and Sudan both REJECT Bir Tawil? This unclaimed land reveals how colonial borders created a geopolitical paradox nobody wants.

In 2014, Jeremiah Heaton planted a flag in this 800-square-mile desert to make his daughter a princess. CNN, TIME, and Newsweek covered it. But nobody explained WHY he could do this – or why international law means his "Kingdom of North Sudan" has no sovereignty.

Using the 1899 Anglo-Egyptian Condominium Agreement and the 1902 administrative boundary, this video explains the border dispute that created Bir Tawil – and why claiming it means losing the valuable Hala'ib Triangle (8,000 square miles with Red Sea coastline and manganese deposits).

From the Ababda tribe (never consulted in 1899 or 1902) to the violent 1994 military confrontation over Hala'ib, this is the colonial border paradox nobody talks about.
