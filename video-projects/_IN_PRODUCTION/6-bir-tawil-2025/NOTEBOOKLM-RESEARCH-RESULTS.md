## DETAILED VERIFICATION ANALYSIS

### HISTORICAL CLAIMS

**Claim 1: "The 1899 treaty set the border at the 22nd parallel"**
- **Verdict:** ✅ VERIFIED
- **Evidence:**
  - "An agreement between the UK and Egypt relating to the administration of Sudan defined 'Soudan' as the 'territories south of the 22nd parallel of latitude'"
  - Date: January 19, 1899
  - Signatories: Cromer and Butros Ghali Pasha
  - Created Anglo-Egyptian Condominium
- **On-screen quote ready:** "The 1899 Anglo-Egyptian Agreement defined 'Soudan' as the 'territories south of the 22nd parallel of latitude,' setting the initial political boundary"

**Claim 2: "The 1902 decree redrew the line based on tribal land use"**
- **Verdict:** ✅ VERIFIED
- **Evidence:**
  - Date: November 4, 1902
  - Rationale: "intended to reflect the actual use of the land by the tribes in the region"
  - Tribes mentioned: Ababda (Bir Tawil area), Beja (Hala'ib), Bisharin
  - Ababda tribe used Bir Tawil as "grazing land" - assigned to Egyptian administration
  - Beja/Bisharin in Hala'ib were "culturally closer to Khartoum" - assigned to Sudanese administration
- **Key distinction:** Administrative boundary (temporary, for governance) vs. Political boundary (permanent, for sovereignty)

**Claim 3: "This created two conflicting borders"**
- **Verdict:** ✅ VERIFIED
- **Evidence:**
  - "The unclaimed status results from a discrepancy between the straight political boundary established in 1899 and the irregular administrative boundary established in 1902"
  - The mechanism: Egypt claims Hala'ib using 1899 line → places Bir Tawil in Sudan
  - Sudan claims Hala'ib using 1902 line → places Bir Tawil in Egypt
  - Result: "two competing, historically valid colonial lines"
