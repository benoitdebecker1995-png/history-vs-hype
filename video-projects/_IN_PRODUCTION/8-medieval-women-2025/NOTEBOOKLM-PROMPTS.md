## PROMPT 1: Property Ownership - Legal Framework

**Objective:** Understand how property law worked for medieval women

**Prompt:**
```
Based on English common law and medieval legal codes, explain how property ownership worked for medieval women.

Please clarify:
1. What was "coverture" and how did it affect married women's property rights?
2. What rights did unmarried women (feme sole) have regarding property?
3. What rights did widows have compared to married women?
4. Could women inherit property? Under what circumstances?
5. Give specific examples from the sources of women owning/managing property

I need to explain why marital status was THE key variable for women's legal rights.
```

**What You'll Get:**
- Coverture definition (married woman's identity merges with husband's)
- Feme sole status (unmarried women = full legal person)
- Widow rights (often regained property after husband's death)
- Inheritance laws
- Specific examples (Alice de Bryene, etc.)

**Use In Video:**
- Section 3 (1:30-3:00): Property rights explanation
- Shows complexity: some rights, but stratified by marital status
