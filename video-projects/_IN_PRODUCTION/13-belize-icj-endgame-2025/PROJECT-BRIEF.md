### SECTION 2: Comparable Cases - What Actually Happened (2:00-5:00)

**Show how ICJ ruled in 3 similar cases:**

#### Case 1: Nicaragua vs Colombia (2012)
**Context:**
- 1928 Bárcenas-Esguerra Treaty (Colombia-Nicaragua)
- Nicaragua claimed treaty was void
- Similar to Guatemala claiming 1859 treaty void

**ICJ Ruling:**
- Treaty VALID despite Nicaragua's objections
- BUT Colombia violated Nicaragua's maritime rights anyway
- **Lesson:** Treaty breach ≠ automatic voidance

**Primary sources to show:**
- 2012 ICJ ruling excerpt (paragraphs 115-120 on treaty interpretation)
- Map showing maritime adjustment

**Parallel to Belize:**
- Guatemala claims 1859 treaty void (road breach)
- ICJ might say: "Treaty valid, but Belize owes compensation"

---

#### Case 2: Cameroon vs Nigeria (2002)
**Context:**
- Colonial boundary (British/German treaties)
- Nigeria had effective control of Bakassi Peninsula since 1960s
- Claimed possession = ownership

**ICJ Ruling:**
- Colonial treaty supersedes effective control
- Uti possidetis juris principle (colonial boundaries stick)
- Nigeria loses despite decades of occupation

**Primary sources to show:**
- 2002 ICJ ruling excerpt (paragraphs 28-30 on colonial boundaries)
- Map showing Bakassi Peninsula
- Historical colonial maps

**Parallel to Belize:**
- Belize has had effective control since 1859
- BUT if ICJ finds treaty void, effective control doesn't save them
- Precedent cuts both ways

---

#### Case 3: Burkina Faso vs Mali (1986)
**Context:**
- Colonial boundary dispute (French administration)
- Did administrative errors void boundaries?
- Similar to "Was Article 7 road material breach?"

**ICJ Ruling:**
- Minor treaty breaches don't void territorial boundaries
- Must be "material breach" (fundamental, goes to heart of treaty)
- Administrative issues ≠ material breach

**Primary sources to show:**
- 1986 ICJ ruling excerpt on material breach test
- Map showing Burkina Faso-Mali border

**Parallel to Belize:**
- Was Article 7 road "material" to the treaty?
- Guatemala says YES (essential compensation)
- Belize says NO (separate from boundary provisions)
- ICJ will decide based on Vienna Convention on Treaties

### SECTION 4: Realistic Outcomes (6:30-7:30)

**Outcome 1: Status Quo Affirmed (Most Likely)**
- Based on Cameroon v Nigeria precedent
- Colonial boundaries rarely redrawn
- ICJ: "Treaty valid, Britain breached road provision, but boundary stands"
- **Who wins:** Belize keeps territory, Guatemala loses claim

**Outcome 2: Compensation Order (Uncommon but Precedented)**
- Based on Nicaragua v Colombia approach
- ICJ: "Belize keeps territory, pays Guatemala for road value"
- Estimate: ~£18M (1859 road cost in today's money)
- **Who wins:** Split decision (Belize keeps land, pays money)

**Outcome 3: Boundary Adjustment (Unlikely)**
- ICJ has done this (Burkina Faso v Mali minor adjustments)
- But usually only for unclear colonial maps
- 1859 treaty boundary is crystal clear (Sarstoon River)
- **Who wins:** Unpredictable (but very unlikely scenario)

**What ICJ WON'T do:**
- "Give Guatemala half of Belize" (no precedent)
- Rule based on "fairness" (rules on law)
- Create new border from scratch (interprets existing treaties)
