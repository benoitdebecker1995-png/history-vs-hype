## NOTEBOOKLM RESEARCH PROMPTS

### PROMPT 1: ICJ Legal Framework - What Law Does the Court Apply?

```
Using the ICJ Statute (especially Article 38) and the case documents:

1. What sources of law does the ICJ use to decide territorial disputes?
2. In what order of priority? (treaties first, then custom, then principles?)
3. Can the ICJ rule based on "fairness" or "justice" if the law is unclear?
4. What does "ex aequo et bono" mean and when has the ICJ used it?

Provide specific quotes from Article 38 with article/paragraph citations.
```

**Why this matters:** Debunks myth that "ICJ rules on fairness" - establishes law-first framework

### PROMPT 2: Treaty Interpretation - Nicaragua v Colombia Analysis

```
Analyze the Nicaragua v. Colombia (2012) case:

1. What was Nicaragua's argument about the 1928 treaty?
2. How did the ICJ rule on treaty validity?
3. Did the ICJ find Colombia violated the treaty? If so, what was the consequence?
4. Key finding: Does treaty breach automatically void the treaty?
```

### PROMPT 3: Colonial Boundaries - Cameroon v Nigeria & Burkina Faso v Mali

```
Using Cameroon v. Nigeria (2002) and Burkina Faso v. Mali (1986):

1. What is the uti possidetis juris principle?
2. Why do international courts apply it to colonial boundaries?
3. In Cameroon v Nigeria: Did Nigeria's decades of effective control matter? Why/why not?
4. In Burkina Faso v Mali: What did "intangibility of frontiers inherited from colonization" mean?
5. Can effective possession override a colonial treaty?

Provide paragraph citations and direct quotes showing the Court's reasoning.
```

**Why this matters:** Belize has controlled territory since 1859, but does possession = ownership if treaty is void?

## CRITICAL REMINDERS

**For script writing:**
- Every legal claim needs specific case citation
- Show primary sources on screen (ICJ rulings, Article 60 text)
- Use "both extremes wrong" framework (Guatemala's assumptions vs. Belize's assumptions)
- Documentary tone: "The Court ruled..." not "I think..."
- Evidence-first: Show what ICJ did, not what it "should" do

**For fact-checking:**
- Verify every paragraph number citation
- Ensure quotes are verbatim
- Cross-check legal principles against multiple sources
- Document any areas where cases conflict or are ambiguous
