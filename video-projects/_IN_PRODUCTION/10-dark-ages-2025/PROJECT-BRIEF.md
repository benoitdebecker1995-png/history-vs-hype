## PROJECT OVERVIEW

**Concept:** Evidence-based myth-busting about the "Dark Ages" (Early Medieval Period, 500-1000 CE)

**Format:** 6-8 min long-form + 6 shorts (VidIQ clipping tool)

**Angle:** "Both extremes wrong" - Popular narrative says "backwards and ignorant," counter-narrative says "actually advanced Renaissance" - reality is complex

**Primary Sources:** Carolingian manuscripts, agricultural records, architectural evidence, literacy data, technological innovations

**Examples:** Charlemagne's educational reforms, medieval inventions (heavy plow, horseshoe, stirrup), monastic preservation of texts, BUT also literacy decline, urban collapse, loss of Roman infrastructure

---

## KEY POSITIONING (CRITICAL)

**VidIQ Insight:** This should be positioned as **"evidence vs. modern myths about medieval 'backwardness'"** NOT random trivia.

**Frame as:**
- Popular view: "Dark Ages were ignorant and backwards"
- Counter-narrative: "Actually it was a golden age of innovation"
- Primary sources show BOTH narratives oversimplify
- Reality: Some decline (literacy, cities), some innovation (agriculture, governance)

**DO NOT frame as:**
- "Dark Ages were actually awesome" (oversimplification)
- "Dark Ages were terrible" (also oversimplification)
- Taking sides in culture war about medieval Europe

**DO frame as:**
- "Here's what the archaeological and manuscript evidence shows"
- "Both modern narratives cherry-pick the history"
- Neutral historical analysis
