## PRIMARY SOURCES (Tier 1 - Must Have)

### 1. Carolingian Minuscule Manuscripts (8th-9th century)
**Source Type:** Primary manuscript evidence
**Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL - This is your visual proof of Carolingian Renaissance
**Examples:** Tours Gospels, Lorsch Gospels, Utrecht Psalter

**Where to Find:**
- Digital Vatican Library: https://digi.vatlib.it/
- Bibliothèque nationale de France (Gallica): https://gallica.bnf.fr/
- British Library Digitised Manuscripts: https://www.bl.uk/manuscripts/

**What to Extract:**
- Visual evidence of Carolingian script standardization
- Examples of manuscript production increase under Charlemagne
- Proof of text preservation (classical authors copied)

**Why Critical:**
- Primary visual evidence you can display on screen
- Shows "Dark Ages" had sophisticated intellectual culture
- Counters "complete ignorance" narrative

## SOURCE VERIFICATION NOTES

**Red Flags to Watch For:**
- ⚠️ Sources claiming "Dark Ages were completely ignorant" (FALSE - monasteries preserved learning)
- ⚠️ Sources claiming "Dark Ages were actually advanced" (FALSE - literacy and urban decline real)
- ⚠️ Exaggerated claims about "monks saved civilization" (overstated, though true they preserved texts)
- ⚠️ Claims without quantitative data (need specific numbers for literacy, population)

**Green Flags (Trustworthy):**
- ✅ Academic sources with data (literacy rates, population estimates)
- ✅ Primary manuscripts as evidence (Carolingian texts)
- ✅ Archaeological evidence (plow designs, architectural remains)
- ✅ Sources acknowledging both decline AND innovation
- ✅ Regional and temporal nuance (not uniformly "dark")
