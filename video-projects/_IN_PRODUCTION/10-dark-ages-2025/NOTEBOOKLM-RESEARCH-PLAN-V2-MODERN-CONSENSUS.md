## PRELIMINARY RESEARCH FINDINGS (To Verify/Expand with Modern Sources)

From internet research, we found these claims that need MODERN academic verification:

### 1. Terminology & Historiography
- ❓ Term "Dark Ages" coined by Petrarch (1330s)
- ❓ Modern historians reject it due to "value judgment"
- ❓ Prefer "Early Medieval Period"

### 2. Carolingian Renaissance
- ❓ Manuscript production: 500 (pre-750) → 7,000 (750-900)
- ❓ Admonitio generalis of 789 CE (educational reform decree)
- ❓ Alcuin of York systematized curriculum
- ❓ Carolingian minuscule developed ~778 CE

### 3. Agricultural Innovations
- ❓ Heavy plow increased productivity
- ❓ Horse collar doubled plowing speed
- ❓ Three-field system increased land use by 30%
- ❓ Overall productivity increase: 200%

**⚠️ NOTE:** The 200% figure comes from Lynn White (1962) - THIS NEEDS VERIFICATION with recent scholarship as White's thesis has been substantially revised.

### 4. Literacy Decline
- ❓ Roman Empire: 10-15% literacy
- ❓ Early Medieval: 1-5% literacy
- ❓ By 1000 AD: 1/10th of Roman levels

### 5. Urban Collapse
- ❓ Rome: 1M → 30K population
- ❓ Constantinople: 500K → 70K
- ❓ Italy: 7-8M → 3-4M (400-600 AD)

### 6. Monastic Preservation
- ❓ Benedict of Nursia founded Monte Cassino (529)
- ❓ Monks preserved most surviving classical Latin texts
- ❓ Carolingian Revival = "most important event in classical literary history"
