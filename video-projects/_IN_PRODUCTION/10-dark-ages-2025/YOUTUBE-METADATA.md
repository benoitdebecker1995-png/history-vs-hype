## DESCRIPTION

Were the "Dark Ages" really dark? This video uses quantitative manuscript data from academic sources to debunk the most persistent myth in medieval history. The evidence shows something surprising: the early Middle Ages produced 10x more manuscripts than the centuries before.

A March 2025 YouGov poll found 48% of Americans describe the Middle Ages as "dark" - but only 2% claim they actually know much about the period. 51% learn medieval history from movies, not historians. So I went to the academic sources.

📊 THE DATA:
• 6th-7th century: 24,191 manuscripts produced
• 8th-9th century (Carolingian Renaissance): 245,444 manuscripts
• That's a 915% increase

The lights didn't go out. They got brighter.
