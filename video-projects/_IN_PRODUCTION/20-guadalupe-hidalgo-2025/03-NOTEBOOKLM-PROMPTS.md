## Notebook 1: The Treaty & Negotiations

### Prompt 1.1: Article V Analysis
> Quote the exact text of Article V of the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo that describes the boundary. What specific geographic references does it make? What map does it reference?

### Prompt 1.2: The Disturnell Map Choice
> Why was the Disturnell map (1847) chosen as the reference for the treaty negotiations? Who made this decision? Were there other maps available?

### Prompt 1.3: Nicholas Trist's Role
> What was Nicholas Trist's role in negotiating the treaty? Why did he continue negotiations after being recalled by President Polk? What were his instructions regarding the boundary?

### Prompt 1.4: "Southern Boundary of New Mexico"
> The treaty references "the southern boundary of New Mexico" multiple times. According to the sources, how was this boundary defined? What did it mean in 1848 vs what surveyors found in 1850?

### Prompt 1.5: Treaty Ratification
> Were there any concerns raised during ratification about the boundary descriptions? Did anyone notice the potential problems with the Disturnell map?

## Notebook 2: The Boundary Dispute

### Prompt 2.1: Disturnell Map Errors (Exact Numbers)
> What were the EXACT errors in the Disturnell map regarding:
> 1. The position of El Paso del Norte (latitude and longitude)
> 2. The position of the Rio Grande
> Provide the coordinates shown on the map vs the actual coordinates.

### Prompt 2.2: Discovery of Errors
> When and how did the boundary commissioners discover the Disturnell map errors? What astronomical observations did they make? Who first noticed the discrepancy?

### Prompt 2.3: Bartlett-García Conde Compromise
> What did the Bartlett-García Conde Compromise (1850) propose? Why did US Commissioner Bartlett agree to it? Why did Congress reject it? How much territory was at stake?

### Prompt 2.4: Mesilla Valley Crisis
> Describe the Mesilla Valley dispute:
> 1. What happened when Mexico expelled Americans in 1853?
> 2. What did Governor Lane do in response?
> 3. How close did the two countries come to armed conflict?
> 4. How did President Pierce defuse the situation?

### Prompt 2.5: Survey Commission Politics
> What political conflicts arose within the US boundary commission? Why was Bartlett controversial? What role did Southern interests play in the dispute?

## Notebook 3: Gadsden Purchase

### Prompt 3.1: Santa Anna's Motivations
> Why did Santa Anna agree to sell Mexican territory in 1853? What was Mexico's financial situation? What domestic threats was he facing? Quote any primary sources on his reasoning.

### Prompt 3.2: US Motivations
> What did the United States want from the Gadsden Purchase beyond fixing the boundary dispute? What was the significance of the proposed southern railroad route? Who in the US government pushed for this?

### Prompt 3.3: Negotiation Details
> Describe the negotiations between James Gadsden and Santa Anna:
> 1. When did they meet?
> 2. What were the initial US demands?
> 3. How much territory did the US originally want?
> 4. How did the final deal differ from initial proposals?

### Prompt 3.4: The Money
> What happened to the $10 million payment?
> 1. How much did Santa Anna and his associates take?
> 2. What did Santa Anna do with the money?
> 3. How did Mexicans react to this?

### Prompt 3.5: Aftermath
> What were the consequences of the Gadsden Purchase for:
> 1. Santa Anna personally (exile?)
> 2. US-Mexico relations
> 3. The proposed railroad (was it built?)
> 4. The people living in the transferred territory?
