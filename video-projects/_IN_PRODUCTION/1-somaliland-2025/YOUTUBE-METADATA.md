## DESCRIPTION

In 1960, 35 countries recognized Somaliland as an independent state. Then they all forgot. This is the legal case the world pretends doesn't exist.

On June 26, 1960, the Somaliland Order in Council granted independence to British Somaliland—making it a recognized state for five days before a planned merger with Italian Somalia. But the merger treaty was never properly signed. In 1963, a British judge ruled the union had no legal basis. In 2005, the African Union's own fact-finding mission called Somaliland's case "historically unique and self-justified in African political history."

So why does the international community still refuse recognition?

### The Documents

**1960 Independence (June 26):** British Somaliland Order in Council 1960 granted full independence. 35+ states extended formal recognition through bilateral agreements, including the US, USSR, China, UK, France, Egypt, Ethiopia, Ghana, and Israel.

**The Failed Union (July 1):** Two separate parliaments passed two different union laws—neither signed by both parties. Somalia's Act of Union was approved "in principle" but never enacted into law. Legal scholar Eugene Cotran documented this defect in his 1961 analysis.

**1963 Court Ruling:** British Judge Hazelwood ruled that "in the absence of an Act of Union, the court had no jurisdiction over Somaliland." Somalia attempted retroactive validation through Law No. 5 (1961), but scholars debate whether you can retroactively cure a void agreement.

**2005 AU Investigation:** African Union fact-finding mission concluded: "The fact that the union between Somaliland and Somalia was never ratified makes Somaliland's search for recognition historically unique and self-justified in African political history." The AU recommended finding a "special method" to deal with this case. No action was taken.

**2024-2025 Developments:** Ethiopia signed a port access deal (January 2024) with language about "in-depth assessment towards recognition." Israel became the first UN member to formally recognize Somaliland (December 2025), triggering immediate AU rejection.

### Why This Matters

Somaliland isn't arguing it should become independent—it's arguing it already was. The documentary evidence supports this: the 1960 independence order, 35+ recognitions, the never-ratified treaty, the 1963 court ruling, and the AU's own 2005 investigation.

The question isn't whether the legal case exists. It's whether the international community will read the documents they already have.
