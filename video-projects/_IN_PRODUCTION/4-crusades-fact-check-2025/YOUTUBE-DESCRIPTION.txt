# YouTube Description - Jerusalem 1099: What Crusaders Really Wrote

## TITLE:
Jerusalem 1099: What Crusaders Really Wrote

## DESCRIPTION:

A primary-source fact-check of viral claims that the Crusades were "awesome" and purely defensive. Using only what crusaders themselves wrote about the 1099 siege of Jerusalem in the First Crusade. We read the medieval crusader chronicles (Fulcher of Chartres, Raymond d'Aguilers, Gesta Francorum) to see what really happened.

A modern TV host's "Deus Vult" tattoo and viral videos claiming the Crusades were awesome raise a simple question: what did crusaders themselves write? This video examines the original sources—not pundit takes—to fact-check claims that the Crusades were purely defensive wars against Muslim expansion.

Were the Crusades justified? Were they defensive or aggressive wars? The primary sources tell a more complex story than viral narratives suggest. From the Jerusalem 1099 massacre to the Fourth Crusade's sack of Constantinople to the Rhineland massacres, crusader chronicles document atrocities the "awesome Crusades" narrative omits.

This video doesn't rely on modern pundits or movie scenes. It walks through the original Latin texts, maps, and medieval illustrations to show how contemporaries described the First Crusade, the sack of Constantinople, and medieval massacres.
