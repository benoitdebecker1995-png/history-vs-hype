### Prompt 5A: Causation Fallacy - Europe Still Christian

```
A video claims "Europe was Christian when the Crusades were launched, and it's still majority Christian today 1000 years later" implying the Crusades preserved Christianity in Europe. Analyze this claim:

1. TIMELINE:
   - When did the Crusades effectively end? (1291 fall of Acre? Later campaigns?)
   - When did European secularization begin? (Enlightenment 1700s? 1800s-1900s?)
   - What is the time gap between these events?

2. ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATIONS: What other factors kept Europe Christian?
   - Geographic distance from Islamic centers
   - Political consolidation
   - Cultural/social factors
   - Economic factors
   - The fact that Islam never threatened to conquer ALL of Europe

3. COUNTEREVIDENCE: What Christian territory was LOST despite Crusades?
   - Anatolia (became Turkey)
   - Balkans (Ottoman conquest 1300s-1400s)
   - Did the Fourth Crusade WEAKEN Byzantine Empire and hasten its fall?

4. INTERNAL CHRISTIAN CONFLICTS:
   - Protestant Reformation (1517+)
   - Thirty Years' War (1618-1648)
   - Religious wars WITHIN Christianity

Is this a "post hoc ergo propter hoc" fallacy (after therefore because of)?
```
