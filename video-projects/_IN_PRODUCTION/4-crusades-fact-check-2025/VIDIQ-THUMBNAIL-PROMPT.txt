# VidIQ Thumbnail Concept Prompt - Crusades Fact-Check Video

## Video Context
- **Title:** Fact-Checking the "Awesome Crusades" Narrative
- **Topic:** Debunking viral video claiming Crusades were defensive and justified
- **Angle:** Primary sources show the massacres, sacks, and atrocities the viral video omits
- **Modern hook:** Pete Hegseth's "Deus Vult" tattoo + 5M view viral video
- **Target audience:** Males 25-44, intelligent, interested in history

## Thumbnail Requirements

**Style:** Documentary, evidence-based, professional (NOT clickbait)

**Key Elements to Include:**
1. **Medieval primary source imagery** - Show actual crusader chronicles or illuminated manuscripts
2. **Visual contrast** - "What they say" vs. "What crusaders wrote"
3. **Pete Hegseth reference** (optional) - His "Deus Vult" tattoo is the modern hook
4. **Text overlay** - Clear, readable, documentary tone

## Thumbnail Concept Options

### Option 1: Split Screen Contrast
- **Left side:** "CRUSADES WERE AWESOME" (quote from viral video)
- **Right side:** Medieval manuscript showing Jerusalem massacre scene
- **Center text:** "What the Sources Actually Say"
- **Color palette:** Muted medieval colors, parchment tones

### Option 2: Primary Source Focus
- **Background:** Fulcher of Chartres manuscript page (blurred)
- **Foreground text:** "10,000 Beheaded" / "They Did Not Spare Women and Children"
- **Attribution:** "- Fulcher of Chartres, Christian Crusader"
- **Bottom text:** "The Sources They Won't Show You"

### Option 3: Modern-Medieval Connection
- **Top half:** Pete Hegseth's "Deus Vult" tattoo (cropped from news photo)
- **Bottom half:** Medieval illustration of Jerusalem sack
- **Center text:** "What 'Deus Vult' Actually Meant"
- **Documentary framing:** Evidence-focused, not sensationalist

### Option 4: Chronicle Quote Emphasis
- **Background:** Medieval battle scene (illuminated manuscript)
- **Large quote overlay:** "Men rode in blood up to their knees"
- **Attribution:** "Raymond d'Aguilers, Crusader Eyewitness"
- **Bottom text:** "Fact-Checking the Crusades"

## Text Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use serif fonts (medieval/historical feel)
- Keep text readable at mobile size
- Quote actual primary sources
- Use documentary language

**DON'T:**
- Use clickbait phrases ("You Won't BELIEVE...")
- Make it look sensationalist
- Hide what the video is about
- Use excessive color saturation or effects

## Color Palette Suggestions
- Parchment beige (#F4E8D0)
- Medieval red (#8B0000)
- Dark brown text (#3E2723)
- Gold accents (#D4AF37)
- Avoid bright, modern colors

## Image Sources to Consider
- Medieval manuscript illuminations (public domain)
- Historical maps of Jerusalem
- Crusader chronicle pages
- Byzantine/Islamic art (to show both sides)

## Final Thumbnail Should Communicate:
1. This is a fact-check video
2. Using primary historical sources
3. Challenging a popular narrative
4. Professional, documentary approach
5. Not taking political sides, just examining evidence
