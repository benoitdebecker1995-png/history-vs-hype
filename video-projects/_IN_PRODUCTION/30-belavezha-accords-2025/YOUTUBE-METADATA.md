## Description

```
In 1996, the Russian parliament voted to declare the Belavezha Accords—the document that ended the Soviet Union—illegal.

In 2025, presidential adviser Anton Kobiakov went further: the USSR "never legally ceased to exist."

This video examines the actual document signed on December 8, 1991, and asks: was the dissolution of the Soviet Union legal?

THE EVIDENCE:
- The Preamble that declared the USSR "no longer exists"
- Article 5 on territorial integrity
- Article 14 that terminated all Soviet institutions
- The 1990 Secession Law the signers bypassed
- The March 1991 referendum they overruled (76.4% voted to preserve USSR)

THE VERDICT:
Under Soviet law, the Accords were almost certainly unconstitutional. But the USSR was already "a carcass" (Zubok). The real question is why Russia keeps relitigating 1991—and what they're using that argument to justify today.
```
