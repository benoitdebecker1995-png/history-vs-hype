## Project Brief

### The Story

In 1713, Britain and Spain signed the Treaty of Utrecht ending the War of Spanish Succession. Article X ceded Gibraltar to Britain — but with one condition: if Britain ever decides to transfer sovereignty, Spain must be given "first refusal."

For 310 years, this clause has sat in the background. But Brexit changed everything. Gibraltar voted 96% to remain in the EU but was forced out with the UK. Now, as the UK-Gibraltar relationship evolves and EU-Spain-UK negotiations continue, the question arises: does Article X still apply? And what legally constitutes a "transfer of sovereignty"?
