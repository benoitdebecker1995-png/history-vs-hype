## ACADEMIC SOURCES (University Presses)

### [A1] Dadson & Elliott - *Britain, Spain and the Treaty of Utrecht 1713-2013*
**Authors:** Trevor J. Dadson and J.H. Elliott (eds.)
**Publisher:** Routledge (2014)
**ISBN:** 978-0-367-88589-2 (hardback), 978-1-351-19133-3 (ebook)
**Why:** 12 eminent historians/legal experts from Spain and UK on the Treaty's political and legal consequences. Includes chapter by Michael Waibel comparing Gibraltar to Hong Kong, Falklands, Ceuta/Melilla
**Priority:** CRITICAL
**Estimated cost:** ~$50-70

**NotebookLM upload name:** `[A1] Dadson-Elliott-Treaty-Utrecht-2014.pdf`

---

### [A2] Trinidad - *Self-Determination in Disputed Colonial Territories*
**Author:** Jamie Trinidad (Wolfson College, Cambridge; called to Bar of Gibraltar 2005)
**Publisher:** Cambridge University Press (2018)
**Series:** Cambridge Studies in International and Comparative Law, No. 134
**ISBN:** 978-1-108-41818-8
**Pages:** 292
**Why:** ESSENTIAL — directly addresses self-determination vs. territorial integrity in Gibraltar, Falklands, Western Sahara, Chagos Islands. Author grew up in Gibraltar.
**Priority:** CRITICAL
**Estimated cost:** ~£85 / $110

**NotebookLM upload name:** `[A2] Trinidad-Self-Determination-Colonial-2018.pdf`

---

### [A3] Stockey & Grocott - *Gibraltar: A Modern History*
**Authors:** Gareth Stockey (University of Nottingham) and Chris Grocott (University of Lancaster)
**Publisher:** University of Wales Press (2012)
**ISBN:** 978-0-7083-2481-3
**Why:** First 21st-century treatment of Gibraltar's history. Covers 1704 capture through Brexit-era. Replaces older treatments (Hills 1974).
**Priority:** CRITICAL
**Estimated cost:** ~$40-60

**NotebookLM upload name:** `[A3] Stockey-Grocott-Gibraltar-Modern-History-2012.pdf`

---

### [A4] Constantine - *Community and Identity: The Making of Modern Gibraltar Since 1704*
**Author:** Stephen Constantine (Lancaster University)
**Publisher:** Manchester University Press (2009)
**ISBN:** 978-0-7190-8054-8 (paperback)
**Why:** How multi-ethnic Roman Catholic and Jewish population became settled in British Gibraltar. Identity formation from 1704 to present.
**Priority:** HIGH
**Estimated cost:** ~$40-60

**NotebookLM upload name:** `[A4] Constantine-Community-Identity-Gibraltar-2009.pdf`

---

### [A5] Canessa (ed.) - *Bordering on Britishness: National Identity in Gibraltar from the Spanish Civil War to Brexit*
**Editor:** Andrew Canessa (Professor of Anthropology, University of Essex)
**Publisher:** Palgrave Macmillan (2019)
**Series:** Palgrave Studies in European Political Sociology
**ISBN:** 978-3-319-99310-2 (ebook), 978-3-319-99309-6 (hardcover)
**Why:** Explores evolving Gibraltarian relationship with Britishness. Generational shifts: older (WWII evacuation = British), middle-aged (Gibraltarian-British), young (European/Mediterranean). Includes chapters by Grocott & Stockey.
**Priority:** HIGH
**Estimated cost:** ~$60-90

**NotebookLM upload name:** `[A5] Canessa-Bordering-Britishness-2019.pdf`

---

### [A6] Gold - *Gibraltar: British or Spanish?*

**Publisher:** Routledge (2005; reissued 2013)
