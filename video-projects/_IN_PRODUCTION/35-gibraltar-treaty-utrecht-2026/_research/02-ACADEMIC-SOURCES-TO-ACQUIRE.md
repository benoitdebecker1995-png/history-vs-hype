## CRITICAL PRIORITY (Buy First)

### 1. [A2] Jamie Trinidad - *Self-Determination in Disputed Colonial Territories*

- **Publisher:** Cambridge University Press (2018)
- **Series:** Cambridge Studies in International and Comparative Law, No. 134
- **ISBN:** 978-1-108-41818-8
- **Pages:** 292
- **Price:** ~£85 / $110
- **Author credentials:** Fellow of Wolfson College, Cambridge. Called to Bar of Gibraltar 2005. Grew up in Gibraltar.
- **Why this is #1:** Directly addresses the core legal tension of the video — self-determination vs. territorial integrity — using Gibraltar, Falklands, Western Sahara, and Chagos Islands as case studies. Written by someone who grew up in Gibraltar and practiced law there.
- **What to extract:** Legal framework for competing claims, scholarly analysis of UN Resolution 2353 logic, comparative analysis with Falklands/Chagos

---

### 2. [A1] Dadson & Elliott (eds.) - *Britain, Spain and the Treaty of Utrecht 1713-2013*

- **Publisher:** Routledge (2014)
- **ISBN:** 978-0-367-88589-2 (hardback), 978-1-351-19133-3 (ebook)
- **Price:** ~$50-70
- **Editor credentials:** J.H. Elliott is Regius Professor Emeritus of Modern History at Oxford — one of the greatest living historians of Spain
- **Why:** 12 eminent historians and legal experts (Spanish AND British) on the Treaty's political and legal consequences. Includes Michael Waibel's chapter comparing Gibraltar to Hong Kong, Falklands, Ceuta/Melilla.
- **What to extract:** Treaty interpretation debates, Article X scholarly analysis, Spanish and British perspectives on the same treaty text

---

### 3. [A3] Stockey & Grocott - *Gibraltar: A Modern History*

- **Publisher:** University of Wales Press (2012)
- **ISBN:** 978-0-7083-2481-3
- **Price:** ~$40-60
- **Author credentials:** Stockey = Lecturer in Modern Spanish, University of Nottingham. Grocott = Teaching Fellow in European Studies, Lancaster.
- **Why:** First 21st-century comprehensive treatment of Gibraltar. Replaces Hills (1974) and Jackson (1990). Weaves British, Spanish, AND Gibraltarian perspectives since 1704.
- **What to extract:** 1704 capture details, Franco blockade narrative, identity formation, post-2000 developments

---

## HIGH PRIORITY (Buy Second)

### 4. [A4] Stephen Constantine - *Community and Identity: The Making of Modern Gibraltar Since 1704*

- **Publisher:** Manchester University Press (2009)
- **ISBN:** 978-0-7190-8054-8 (paperback)
- **Price:** ~$40-60
- **Author credentials:** Senior Lecturer in History, Lancaster University
- **Why:** Shows how multi-ethnic Roman Catholic and Jewish population became "British Gibraltarian." Identity formation from 1704 to present. Focuses on civilian population rather than military/diplomatic history.
- **What to extract:** Demographic history, identity statistics, how Franco's blockade cemented British identity, ethnographic detail

---

### 5. [A5] Andrew Canessa (ed.) - *Bordering on Britishness*

- **Publisher:** Palgrave Macmillan (2019)
- **Series:** Palgrave Studies in European Political Sociology
- **ISBN:** 978-3-319-99310-2 (ebook)
- **Price:** ~$60-90
- **Editor credentials:** Professor of Anthropology, University of Essex. Led ESRC-funded oral history project on Gibraltarian identity.
- **Why:** Most recent academic treatment of Gibraltarian identity. Reveals generational shifts: older generation (British through WWII evacuation), middle-aged (Gibraltarian-British), young (European/Latin/Mediterranean). Pre-Brexit and Brexit-era analysis.
- **What to extract:** Oral history quotes, generational identity data, how "more British than British" evolved, Brexit anxieties

---

### 6. [A6] Peter Gold - *Gibraltar: British or Spanish?*

- **Publisher:** Routledge (2005; reissued 2013)
- **Series:** Routledge Advances in European Politics
- **ISBN:** 978-1-138-87447-3
- **Price:** ~$50-70
- **Author credentials:** Principal Lecturer, University of the West of England. Also wrote book on Ceuta/Melilla.
- **Why:** Most thorough study of Spain's post-1984 sovereignty campaign. Presents Spanish legal arguments from Spanish documents — essential for balanced coverage.
- **What to extract:** Spanish government position papers, Spanish legal interpretation of Article X, post-Franco diplomatic strategy

---

### 7. [A7] Howard S. Levie - *The Status of Gibraltar*

- **Publisher:** Routledge (2021 reissue; original Westview Press 1983)
- **ISBN:** 978-0-367-31182-7
- **Pages:** 258
- **Price:** ~£40 / $50
- **Author credentials:** Professor Emeritus of International Law
- **Why:** Detailed legal analysis of every interpretation of Article X — UK reading vs. Spanish reading vs. international law principles. Covers the 1960s-70s diplomatic escalation in detail.
- **What to extract:** Treaty interpretation methodology, competing legal readings of "propriety" and "territorial jurisdiction," isthmus dispute legal arguments

---

### 8. [J1] *The Round Table* Special Issue on Gibraltar (Vol. 110, No. 3, 2021)

- **Publisher:** Taylor & Francis
- **Price:** ~$30-50 (journal access)
- **Key articles to download:**
  - Joseph Garcia (Deputy Chief Minister of Gibraltar): "Gibraltar and the European Union: In and Out"
  - "Gibraltar in Spanish Foreign Policy: Brexit as an Opportunity for a New Approach"
  - "Gibraltar and the 'British Mediterranean': An Overview"
- **Why:** Post-Brexit perspectives from Gibraltarian government official, Spanish academics, and British scholars. Multi-perspective in one source.
- **What to extract:** Garcia's insider account, Spanish strategic thinking post-Brexit, scholarly framing of sovereignty evolution

## MEDIUM PRIORITY (Buy Third)

### 9. [A8] James Crawford - *The Creation of States in International Law* (2nd ed.)

- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (2006)
- **Price:** ~$70-100
- **Why:** Oxford authority on statehood and territorial disputes. Framework for understanding Gibraltar's unique status (not a state, not fully decolonized, not integrated into UK).
- **What to extract:** Legal principles for territorial acquisition, decolonization categories, prescriptive sovereignty

---

### 10. [A9] Antonio Cassese - *Self-Determination of Peoples: A Legal Reappraisal*

- **Publisher:** Cambridge University Press (1995)
- **Price:** ~$60-90
- **Why:** Foundational international law text. Explains why UN could prioritize territorial integrity over self-determination in some cases but not others.
- **What to extract:** Legal doctrine on when self-determination applies, "salt water" doctrine, exceptions for "implanted populations"

---

### 11. [A10] Peter Gold - *Europe or Africa? Ceuta and Melilla*

- **Publisher:** Liverpool University Press (2000)
- **Price:** ~$50-70
- **Why:** Spain holds African enclaves using the same arguments Britain uses for Gibraltar. The irony angle that makes the video sharper.
- **What to extract:** Spain's legal justification for Ceuta/Melilla, Morocco's counter-arguments, inconsistency in Spain's position

---

### 12. [J2] "Bottom-up Geopolitics and Everyday Brexits at the Gibraltar-Spain Border"

- **Journal:** Geopolitics (Taylor & Francis, 2020)
- **Price:** ~$15-30
- **Why:** Links 1969 border closure memory to Brexit-era anxieties. Studies the La Linea crossing as lived experience.
- **What to extract:** Ethnographic detail about border crossing experience, quotes from residents, 1969 vs. Brexit parallels

---

### 13. [J3] "The Status of the Disputed Waters Surrounding Gibraltar"

- **Journal:** International Journal of Marine and Coastal Law, Vol. 28, No. 4 (2013)
- **Publisher:** Brill
- **Price:** ~$15-30
- **Why:** Maritime dimension rarely covered — territorial waters dispute is separate from land sovereignty question.
- **What to extract:** UNCLOS analysis, UK vs. Spain maritime claims, strategic importance of Strait control

---

### 14. [J4] Del Valle Galvez - "Agreement of the Forum of Dialogue on Gibraltar"

- **Author:** Alejandro del Valle Galvez
- **Institution:** Real Instituto Elcano (2006)
- **Price:** FREE (open access)
- **Why:** Spanish academic perspective on 2006 Cordoba Agreement. Argues it ended "state of exception" in Spain-Gibraltar relations.
- **What to extract:** Spanish scholarly interpretation of 2006 agreement, shift from confrontation to dialogue
