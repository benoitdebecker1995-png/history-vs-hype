═══════════════════════
🗺️ KEY DOCUMENTS EXPLAINED
═══════════════════════

Sykes-Picot Agreement (1916)
Secret agreement between Britain and France dividing Ottoman territories into colonial zones. Often blamed for Middle East conflicts, but it didn't create Israel's current borders—it created European spheres of influence that eventually became British and French mandates.

UN Partition Plan - Resolution 181 (1947)
The last internationally agreed-upon border proposal: 55% for a Jewish state, 45% for an Arab state, Jerusalem under international control. Vote: 33-13-10. Accepted by Jewish community, rejected by Arab community. Never implemented—1948 war happened instead.

The Green Line (1949)
Armistice demarcation lines drawn after the 1948 Arab-Israeli War. Named for the green pencil used on maps. Served as de facto border 1949-1967. Still represents Israel's internationally recognized borders today, despite not being intended as permanent by Arab states.

UN Resolution 242 (1967)
Passed after Six-Day War. Called for Israel to withdraw "from territories occupied in recent conflict" and for nations to "live in peace within secure and recognized borders." International law principle: no state may acquire territory by force.

Oslo Accords (1993-1995)
Divided West Bank into Areas A (18%, Palestinian control), B (22%, shared control), and C (64%, Israeli control). Intended as transitional arrangement for 5 years pending final status agreement. 30 years later, Area C still under Israeli control.
