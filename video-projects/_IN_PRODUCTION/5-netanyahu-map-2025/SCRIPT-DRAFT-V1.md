**ON CAMERA:**

If that's true, a century of borders—from Sykes-Picot to the UN partition to the Green Line—would be obsolete.

## [0:45-1:30] CONTEXT - WHAT MAPS DID NETANYAHU SHOW? (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

Let's start with what Netanyahu actually did. In September 2024, he held a press conference showing a map with the West Bank completely integrated into Israel—no border lines, no distinction.

**B-ROLL:** Netanyahu's Sept 2 press conference map

When a reporter asked why the West Bank wasn't marked, he said "I was talking about Gaza."

**B-ROLL:** News headline quote

Three weeks later at the UN, he held up two maps. Again, no Green Line. No 1967 borders. Just one continuous territory labeled "Israel."

**B-ROLL:** UN speech Sept 27 (Netanyahu holding maps)

Then in February, the quote: "Our decisions have redrawn the map."

---

## [1:30-2:15] HISTORICAL SETUP - SYKES-PICOT (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

So have the borders actually changed? To answer that, we need to understand where these borders came from in the first place.

**B-ROLL:** Sykes-Picot Agreement document

In 1916, Britain and France secretly divided Ottoman territories with the Sykes-Picot Agreement. But here's the first myth: Sykes-Picot didn't create Israel's borders. It created colonial zones of influence.

**B-ROLL:** Sykes-Picot zones map - color-coded French (blue), British (red), International (brown)

France got Lebanon and Syria. Britain got what's now southern Israel, Jordan, and Iraq. Palestine was designated for "international administration" because of the holy sites.

---

## [2:15-3:00] HISTORICAL SETUP - UN PARTITION (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

The actual first attempt at borders came 30 years later. On November 29, 1947, the UN voted to partition Palestine into a Jewish state and an Arab state, with Jerusalem under international control.

**B-ROLL:** UN Resolution 181 map (checkerboard pattern)

The vote: 33 in favor, 13 against. The Jewish community accepted it as a legal basis for statehood. The Arab community rejected it.

**B-ROLL:** News headlines from 1948

The plan was never implemented. Instead, the 1948 war happened, and that war created the borders that still define this conflict today.

---

## [3:00-3:45] HISTORICAL SETUP - THE GREEN LINE (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

After the 1948 war, armistice agreements drew lines between Israel and its neighbors. These were called the Green Line—literally named because they were drawn in green pencil on maps.

**B-ROLL:** 1949 armistice map showing Green Line

This became Israel's internationally recognized border from 1949 to 1967. Then came the Six-Day War. Israel occupied the West Bank, Gaza, Golan Heights, and Sinai.

**B-ROLL:** 1967 war map showing territories

The UN Security Council passed Resolution 242, demanding Israel withdraw from occupied territories. That was 1967. Fifty-eight years ago.

**B-ROLL:** UN Resolution 242 document

---

## [3:45-4:30] HISTORICAL SETUP - OSLO ACCORDS (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

The Oslo Accords in the 1990s tried to create a path to peace. They divided the West Bank into three zones as a temporary measure.

**B-ROLL:** Oslo Areas A, B, C map

Area A: 18 percent, full Palestinian control. Area B: 22 percent, shared control. Area C: 64 percent, full Israeli control.

**B-ROLL:** Pie chart or percentages overlay

This was supposed to be transitional—five years maximum while they negotiated final borders. That was 30 years ago. Area C is still under Israeli control.

---

## [4:30-5:30] THE FACT-CHECK - WHAT CHANGED IN 2024-2025 (60 seconds, 110 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

So back to Netanyahu's claim. Did the map get redrawn in 2024 and 2025?

Legally? No. The Green Line is still the internationally recognized border. The UN, the International Court of Justice, and 140 countries recognize Palestinian claims to the West Bank and Gaza based on the 1967 lines.

**B-ROLL:** ICJ ruling headlines (2024), UN vote (Sept 2025), country recognition news

But on the ground? Absolutely.

**B-ROLL:** Settlement construction photos

In 2024, Israel declared a record 24,000 dunams of West Bank land as "state land"—double the total seized since Oslo.

In 2025, 50,000 new settlement units are on track for approval. That's four times the previous record.

**B-ROLL:** Settlement expansion statistics/maps

In May, Israel announced 22 new settlements—the largest expansion since 1993.

---

## [5:30-6:15] THE FACT-CHECK - ADMINISTRATIVE ANNEXATION (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

And it's not just settlements. Israel shifted control of Area C from military commanders to civilian officials directly under Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich.

**B-ROLL:** Smotrich photo/news

That's significant. It's not temporary military occupation anymore—it's permanent civilian administration. That's what annexation looks like without calling it annexation.

In July, the Knesset voted 71 to 13 on a symbolic resolution calling for sovereignty over the West Bank. Non-binding, but politically clear.

**B-ROLL:** Knesset vote headlines

---

## [6:15-7:00] THE FACT-CHECK - DE FACTO VS DE JURE (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

So here's what's happening. Netanyahu is conducting three types of annexation simultaneously.

**ON CAMERA (stay on camera for emphasis):**

One: Administrative annexation. Israeli civil law, Israeli officials, Israeli control of Area C.

Two: Physical annexation. Settlements, land seizures, making a Palestinian state physically impossible.

Three: Rhetorical annexation. Showing maps without borders, saying "we've redrawn the map," framing it as irreversible.

**B-ROLL:** Three maps side by side - old, Netanyahu's, UN-recognized

What he hasn't achieved yet is legal annexation—international recognition. The question is: which map wins?

---

## [7:00-7:30] WHY IT MATTERS - CONSEQUENCES (30 seconds, 55 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

This matters because 50,000 settlement units don't just change maps—they make a contiguous Palestinian state impossible. The West Bank is being fragmented into isolated cantons with no territorial connection.

**B-ROLL:** Fragmented West Bank map showing settlements

And Netanyahu's "redrawn map" rhetoric extends beyond the West Bank. He's claimed the wars with Hezbollah and Hamas have redrawn the regional map—including Lebanon and Gaza.

---

## [7:30-8:15] WHY IT MATTERS - INTERNATIONAL LAW (45 seconds, 83 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

The international response has been clear. In 2024, the International Court of Justice ruled Israel's occupation has crossed into illegal annexation.

**B-ROLL:** ICJ building/ruling

In September 2025, the UN General Assembly voted 142 to 10 demanding Israel end the occupation within 12 months.

**B-ROLL:** UN vote results

Ten countries—including the UK, France, and Canada—recognized Palestinian statehood with borders based on the Green Line.

**B-ROLL:** Recognition announcements

But Netanyahu called that "a reward for terrorism" and rejected the premise of a Palestinian state.

---

## [8:15-8:45] CONCLUSION - THE VERDICT (30 seconds, 55 words)

**ON CAMERA:**

So did Netanyahu redraw the map?

In international law: no. The borders haven't changed.

On the ground: yes. Administrative control, settlement expansion, and physical annexation are proceeding at record pace.

The legal map and the physical map are diverging. And that gap is where the conflict lives.
