# Optimized Grim Hollow Builds for Strength and Wisdom Characters

Your ranged-caster party desperately needs **healing and frontline presence**. The Inquisition Domain Cleric and Oath of Pestilence Paladin emerge as top-tier choices, providing heavy armor tanking, full healing capability, and unique mechanics that protect your arcane allies while filling critical skill gaps.

## Your party's critical deficiencies

With two warlocks and a wizard, you lack dedicated healing, melee presence to absorb damage, Athletics/Survival/Perception expertise, and battlefield control. Grim Hollow's brutal encounter design punishes parties without healers—resurrection magic is scarce in Etharis where gods are dead, making prevention essential. Your build must prioritize Constitution saves for concentration spells (Spirit Guardians, Bless) while providing emergency healing through Healing Word and Cure Wounds.

The ranged-damage dealers need a frontliner who can **lock down enemies** (preventing them from reaching casters), **absorb focused fire** (high AC and HP), and **maintain uptime** through self-sufficiency. This means heavy armor proficiency, strong Constitution, and either self-healing or massive durability.

## Top tier: Inquisition Domain Cleric

This Grim Hollow subclass was designed for anti-mage warfare and provides exactly what your party needs. **Witch Hunter's Strike** grants bonus force damage on weapon attacks (1d8, scaling to 3d8 at level 14) with additional damage against concentrating spellcasters—perfect for shutting down enemy casters before they hurt your allies. When you break concentration, you gain temporary HP, creating a virtuous cycle of aggression and durability.

The level 2 Channel Divinity **Spell Shield** is transformative for your composition. As an action, grant an ally 1d10 + cleric level temporary HP that provides **resistance to spell damage and advantage on saving throws against spells**. This directly protects your Warlocks and Wizard from the magical threats that would otherwise devastate them. At level 17, Supernal Safeguard allows multi-target protection.

**Rebuke Invoker** at level 6 punishes enemy spellcasters automatically—when an enemy within 60 feet casts a spell, you can use your reaction to force a Constitution save or deal 1d8 force damage per spell level plus your Wisdom modifier. This creates a constant threat zone around you, discouraging enemy casters from targeting your party.

Domain spells include detect magic, identify, silence, zone of truth, dispel magic, remove curse, locate creature, divination, banishment, and wall of force—offering investigation, anti-magic, and control tools your party completely lacks. Heavy armor and martial weapon proficiency let you function as a true tank from level 1.

**Progression for Inquisition Cleric:**

Start with Wisdom 16, Strength 14-16, Constitution 14. Take Resilient (Constitution) or War Caster at level 1 if playing Variant Human—concentration is your lifeline. At level 1, you have heavy armor, martial weapons, and Witch Hunter's Strike adding force damage to attacks. Level 2 grants Spell Shield to protect allies. Level 5 brings Spirit Guardians (3d8 radiant damage, half speed for enemies in 15-foot radius)—your signature spell that turns you into a walking hazard zone.

Level 6 adds Rebuke Invoker, creating anti-caster reaction pressure. Level 8 grants Divine Strike (+1d8 force damage, 2d8 at 14). Max Wisdom to 20 by level 12 through ASIs. Level 13 provides 7th-level spells including Resurrection. Consider starting Seraph transformation between levels 11-16 for flight mobility and reaction-based party support.

At level 20, you're casting 9th-level spells (Mass Heal), dealing 3d8 bonus force damage per hit with Witch Hunter's Strike, protecting multiple allies simultaneously with Spell Shield, and punishing every enemy spellcaster within 60 feet with Rebuke Invoker. Your saves are extraordinary (Wisdom + proficiency for Wisdom/Charisma, proficiency in Constitution for concentration), and Spirit Guardians remains your most-cast spell.

## Elite alternative: Oath of Pestilence Paladin

This Grim Hollow oath is mechanically overtuned—reviewers rated it "6/5" for power. Channel Divinity **Debilitating Fever** is a melee spell attack that auto-inflicts disease causing incapacitation for 1 minute (Constitution save ends). Note this has **no initial save**—hitting automatically inflicts the condition. This shuts down priority targets instantly.

**Entropic Infection** (also Channel Divinity) forces a Constitution save or strips necrotic resistance and adds +2d6 necrotic damage the first time the target takes necrotic damage each turn for 1 minute. This creates devastating combos with Warlock's Hex + Eldritch Blast or any necrotic damage source.

The level 7 **Aura of Rampant Sickness** (10 feet, 30 feet at 18) is absurdly strong: as a reaction, impose disadvantage on any ability check, attack roll, or saving throw from a creature in your aura. This functions as a shutdown tool against legendary resistance, can protect allies from attacks, and turns every enemy save into a disadvantaged roll—dramatically improving your Warlocks' save-or-suck spells.

Level 15 **Disgusting Resilience** lets you spend Hit Dice when reduced to 0 HP to subtract that amount from the damage. If you still die, your corpse explodes for 8d6 necrotic damage in a 20-foot radius. Level 20 Plaguebringer grants necrotic resistance, poison immunity, HP maximum can't be reduced, and creatures within 5 feet take necrotic damage equal to your Charisma modifier once per turn (passive damage aura stacking with Spirit Guardians if multiclassed).

Oath spells include bane, inflict wounds, acid arrow, ray of enfeeblement, flash fever, stinking cloud, blight, confusion, contagion, and insect plague—focusing on debuff, damage-over-time, and area denial.

**Progression for Oath of Pestilence Paladin:**

Start Strength 16, Constitution 14, Charisma 14, Wisdom 10. Take Brawny feat at level 1 (Variant Human) for +1d4 damage on Strength melee attacks and count as one size larger for carrying capacity. Level 2 grants Defense Fighting Style (+1 AC in armor) and Divine Smite. Level 3 provides Oath features and Channel Divinity. Level 5 brings Extra Attack—the mandatory breakpoint doubling your damage output.

Level 6 Aura of Protection adds your Charisma modifier to all saving throws for you and allies within 10 feet—this is transformative for your party, granting +2-3 to every save. Level 7 delivers Aura of Rampant Sickness. Max Strength to 20 by level 8-12. Level 11 Improved Divine Smite adds 1d8 radiant to every melee hit automatically.

Consider Lycanthrope transformation starting at levels 7-11. This grants +2 Strength, +1 Constitution, hybrid beast form with natural weapons, and Hunter's Howl (mark enemies for +1d6 damage). The transformation enhances your already formidable melee presence while adding scouting options through Kindred Form (full animal transformation).

By level 20, you're dealing automatic radiant damage, have multiple concentration-free disease effects, possess massive durability through Disgusting Resilience, grant party-wide save bonuses, and can impose disadvantage on any enemy action as a reaction.

## Strength build: Path of the Fractured Barbarian

This Grim Hollow subclass is "significantly overtuned" according to reviews. **Fractured Body** at level 3 transforms you into a grappling specialist with 1d8 magical unarmed strikes, 10-foot reach on unarmed attacks, ability to grapple Huge creatures, and bonus action unarmed strikes. Your appearance becomes completely unrecognizable while raging—perfect for Grim Hollow's dark themes.

**Brains & Brawn** at level 6 grants resistance to all damage types except psychic while raging. This is better than Bear Totem Barbarian (which gets this at level 3 but for fewer damage types early). When not raging, you gain psychic resistance and social advantages.

Level 14 **Better Half** prevents death once per day—when reduced to 0 HP, you drop to 1 HP instead and automatically rage if not already raging. Combined with Lycanthrope transformation's Unstoppable Rage (don't fall unconscious at 0 HP), you become nearly unkillable.

**Progression for Path of the Fractured Barbarian:**

Start Strength 16, Dexterity 14, Constitution 16, Wisdom 12. Take Brawny at level 1. Level 2 grants Reckless Attack and Danger Sense. Level 3 provides Fractured Body—now you're a grapple-focused controller with reach unarmed strikes. Level 4 +2 Strength (18). Level 5 Extra Attack and Fast Movement. Level 6 delivers Brains & Brawn (all damage resistance while raging)—major survivability spike.

Max Strength to 20 by level 8. Level 9 Brutal Critical. Level 12 take Great Weapon Master or boost Constitution. Start Lycanthrope transformation at levels 7-11 for +2 Strength (22 total), +1 Constitution, natural weapons stacking with unarmed strikes, and Hunter's Howl. Progress to Iron Pelt (resistance to non-magical, non-silvered physical damage) and Bestial Savagery (magical 1d8 unarmed strikes, +1 AC, immune to charmed/frightened).

By level 20 with Primal Champion (+4 Strength/Constitution, max 24), you have Strength 24, Constitution 21+, resistance to nearly all damage while raging, can grapple creatures up to Huge size with 10-foot reach, deal massive unarmed damage, and functionally can't die through Better Half + Lycanthrope features.

## Strength option: Bulwark Warrior Fighter

This Grim Hollow fighter excels at protecting allies and absorbing punishment. **Protective Taunt** at level 3 causes creatures you attack to have disadvantage on attacks not targeting you until your next turn—an excellent "sticky tank" feature. **Weather the Storm** grants temporary HP equal to fighter level at the end of each turn for 1 minute (uses scale with level).

Level 7 **Threatening Presence** creates a 30-foot area where creatures must make Wisdom saves (DC 8 + Constitution + proficiency) or have disadvantage on attacks not targeting you. You gain HP equal to your Constitution modifier per failed save. Level 10 **Aggressive Defense** lets you convert temporary HP to damage (1-for-1, up to half fighter level), creating offensive pressure from defensive resources.

Level 18 **Halt the Assault** allows reaction to redirect nearby attacks to yourself and gain resistance to that attack's damage—ultimate protection for allies.

**Progression for Bulwark Warrior Fighter:**

Start Strength 16, Constitution 16, Wisdom 12. Take Polearm Master at level 1 for bonus action attack (1d4) and reaction attack when enemies enter your reach. Level 2 Action Surge. Level 3 Bulwark Warrior features. Level 4 +2 Strength. Level 5 Extra Attack. Level 6 +2 Strength (20) or Sentinel (lock down enemies).

Level 11 provides third attack per Attack action—major damage increase. Level 12 take +2 Constitution or Great Weapon Master. Consider Seraph transformation at levels 11-16 for flight, Divine Retribution (grant allies extra attacks as reaction), and support capabilities that synergize with your protective role.

By level 20, you're making three attacks per turn, generating massive temporary HP, forcing disadvantage on all enemy attacks not targeting you in a 30-foot radius, converting temp HP to damage, and redirecting attacks to yourself with resistance.

## Wisdom build: Circle of Mutation Druid

This Grim Hollow druid is rated 5/5—the strongest druid option. **Circle Forms** at level 3 provides Moon Druid-level wild shape (CR = 1/3 × druid level) with bonus action transformation. **Mutate Shape** lets you spend spell slots for mutation points to enhance wild shapes: add multiattack, flight (60 feet), swim speed, darkvision, venomous attacks (+2d6 poison per hit), natural armor boost, amphibious, tremorsense, burrowing, and more.

This creates incredible versatility—need a flying scout? Take a CR 1 beast and add flight mutation. Need a tank? Take a bear with natural armor and venomous attacks. Need damage? Take a multiattack beast and add venom to every hit.

**Unnatural** at level 6 recovers a lower spell slot when wild shaping, your natural weapons deal force damage (bypassing resistance), and you gain advantage on Deception/Intimidation/Animal Handling. **Endless Evolution** at level 10 adds Wisdom modifier bonus mutation points, grants access to damage resistances, **allows spellcasting in wild shape**, and provides regeneration option.

Level 14 **Apex Predator** adds +1d8 damage to natural weapon attacks and lets you mutate ally beasts.

**Progression for Circle of Mutation Druid:**

Start Wisdom 16, Constitution 14, Dexterity 14. Take Resilient (Constitution) at level 1. Level 2 grants wild shape and spells. Level 3 provides Circle Forms and Mutate Shape—combine multiattack beasts with venomous attacks for 4d6+ poison damage per round. Level 4 +2 Wisdom (18).

Level 5 brings CR 1 wild shapes and access to flight mutation—you can now fly in beast form. Level 6 grants Unnatural (force damage, spell slot recovery). Level 8 +2 Wisdom (20). Level 10 delivers Endless Evolution—you can now cast spells while wild shaped, fundamentally changing your combat role.

At level 10+, your typical combat is: wild shape into bear (bonus action), cast Healing Word or Cure Wounds while maintaining beast form, attack with force-damage natural weapons enhanced by mutations, regenerate HP if damaged. You're a full caster who can also tank in melee with hundreds of beast HP.

Consider Seraph transformation at levels 11-16 for flight (stacking with flight mutation for redundancy), Divine Clemency (reaction healing), and Cleanse Affliction (remove conditions when healing).

By level 20, you're casting 9th-level spells, wild shaping into CR 6+ beasts with multiattack + venomous attacks + natural armor + resistances + spellcasting + regeneration, all while providing healing support.

## Wisdom alternative: Green Reaper Ranger

This Grim Hollow ranger conclave is rated 5/5 for poison specialization. **Envenomed Attack** at level 3 adds 1d6 poison damage to all weapon hits as a bonus action with no concentration (2d6 at level 11). **Toxic Tradecraft** spends spell slots to add poison conditions: **Befuddled** (target can't cast verbal spells), **Suffer** (ongoing poison damage when hit), or **Weaken** (vulnerability to poison damage)—all while auto-inflicting poisoned condition.

Level 7 **Poison Control** grants poison resistance, advantage against poisoned condition, and free Protection from Poison spell. **Variegated Vexations** at level 11 converts poison damage to necrotic or acid to bypass immunity/resistance.

The capstone is level 15 **Pain Tolerance**: as a reaction, gain temporary HP equal to incoming damage once per round. This effectively nullifies one attack per round, making you extraordinarily durable against multi-attack enemies.

**Progression for Green Reaper Ranger (Pure):**

Start Wisdom 16, Dexterity 16, Constitution 14. Take Sharpshooter at level 1. Level 2 grants Archery Fighting Style (+2 ranged attacks) and spells. Level 3 provides Envenomed Attack and Toxic Tradecraft. Level 4 +2 Wisdom (18). Level 5 Extra Attack—now dealing 2× (1d8+3 longbow + 1d6 poison + 1d6 Hunter's Mark) = 2d8+2d6+2d6+6 per round.

Level 7 grants Poison Control. Level 8 +2 Wisdom (20). Level 11 provides Variegated Vexations (converting poison to necrotic) and Envenomed Attack scales to 2d6—now dealing 2d8+4d6+2d6+6 before Sharpshooter. Level 12 take Alert or Resilient (Constitution).

Level 15 **Pain Tolerance**—you now negate one attack per round entirely with temporary HP. Combined with high AC and Wisdom, you're extremely difficult to kill. Consider Lycanthrope transformation for Kindred Form (scouting), Hunter's Howl (stacking damage marks), or Seraph for Bow of Celestial Judgment (radiant longbow).

By level 20, you're dealing 2d8+4d6+2d6 poison/necrotic per hit (doubled with Extra Attack, tripled if you take 3 Druid levels for multiattack beast forms), negating one attack per round with Pain Tolerance, bypassing poison immunity, and shutting down enemy casters with Befuddled toxin.

## Transformation selection: Lycanthrope for Strength builds

Lycanthrope requires Strength 13 and provides the best synergy for martial characters. Level 1 grants **Hybrid Transformation** (action to transform for Constitution modifier hours): AC = 10 + Constitution + Dexterity, 1d6 slashing claws, 1d8 + Strength piercing bite as bonus action. **Shapechanger's Form** provides +2 Strength, +1 Constitution (max 20).

The transformation save DC (8 + proficiency + Strength) scales naturally with your combat stats. Level 1 flaw is **Lust for the Hunt**—must succeed DC 10 Wisdom save each turn in hybrid form or lose control (attack nearest enemy, prioritizing helpless creatures). This is manageable with decent Wisdom and avoidable by exiting hybrid form.

Level 2 optimal boon is **Hunter's Howl**: bonus action mark prey within 60 feet for +1d6 damage on all melee hits plus advantage on Perception/Survival to track. Uses equal to Strength modifier per short rest. This stacks with Hunter's Mark for Rangers and doesn't require concentration. Alternative is **Iron Pelt** for resistance to non-magical, non-silvered physical damage (stacking with Barbarian rage).

Level 3 optimal boon is **Bestial Savagery**: unarmed attacks become 1d8 magical slashing, gain +1 AC, immune to charmed and frightened. Alternative is **Titanic Vigor** for HP maximum + (2 × character level) and +5 temporary HP per turn in hybrid form.

Level 4 choices include **Blood Frenzy** (bite damage becomes 2d8, can grapple on hit, advantage and extra Strength damage while grappling) or **Unstoppable Rage** (don't fall unconscious at 0 HP—still make death saves but keep fighting).

**Optimal timing:** Begin transformation at character levels 3-4 when you have Extra Attack foundation. Progress to Level 2 at character levels 7-9. Advance to Level 3 at character levels 11-13. Reach Level 4 at character levels 17-19.

**Milestones for progression:**
- Establish a pack of lycanthropes or defeat an Alpha Lycanthrope  
- Gain control of animalistic urges through trials
- Unleash the beast while retaining humanity (philosophical balance)
- Kill powerful creatures in hybrid form to embrace transformation

## Transformation selection: Seraph for Wisdom builds

Seraph requires Wisdom 13 and provides flight, healing, and support. Level 1 grants **Celestial Form** (Wisdom becomes 16 if lower, Constitution becomes 14 if lower) and **Angelic Wings** (fly speed = walking speed). This provides unparalleled mobility—positioning for Spirit Guardians, escaping grapples, reaching injured allies, avoiding ground hazards.

Level 1 flaw is **Planar Binding**: disadvantage on death saves. Death sends soul to divine plane (character becomes NPC)—encourage teammates to stabilize or heal you before death saves finish.

Level 2 optimal boons (uses = Wisdom modifier per long rest):
- **Divine Retribution** (reaction): Grant ally extra attack after their attack action, dealing +1d8 radiant. Excellent for empowering your Warlocks' Eldritch Blast or Wizard's attacks.
- **Divine Protection** (reaction): Grant ally +4 AC vs one attack. Can turn hits into misses.
- **Divine Clemency** (reaction): Cast healing spell on damaged ally immediately.

Level 3 optimal boons:
- **Radiant Strike**: +1d6 radiant per melee hit (+2d6 vs Fiend/Fey/Undead). Stacks with Divine Smite for Paladins, enhances Cleric melee attacks.
- **Cleanse Affliction**: Remove one condition when healing an ally—poisoned, paralyzed, stunned, blinded, etc. Transforms healing into multi-purpose support.
- **Bow of Celestial Judgment**: Summon radiant longbow with +1d8 radiant (+2d8 vs evil creatures), no ammunition needed. Perfect for ranged Wisdom builds.

Level 4 auras (20-foot radius, 1/long rest per creature):
- **Aura of Merciful Blessing**: Ally reduced to 0 HP stays at 1 HP instead. Prevents death automatically—essential for deadly Grim Hollow encounters.
- **Aura of Celestial Resilience**: Ally turns failed save into success. Counters save-or-die effects.
- **Aura of Holy Purge**: Ally's hit becomes critical. Enables massive damage spikes.

**Optimal timing:** Begin at character levels 7-11 when you have strong class features. Progress Level 2 at levels 11-14. Advance Level 3 at levels 14-16. Reach Level 4 at levels 17-20.

**Milestones:**
- Defeat powerful force of darkness (fiend, undead, aberration)
- Create hallowed landmark or shrine for pilgrims
- Establish parish of worshipers or followers
- Redeem a soul beyond redemption
- Establish portal to Divine Plane

## Race selection for optimized STR/WIS builds

**Laneshi** provides the only racial +1 Strength, +1 Wisdom combination in Grim Hollow. The Warrior subrace grants additional +1 Strength (total +2 Strength, +1 Wisdom), Amphibious trait, 30-foot swim speed, Darkvision 60 feet, Speak with Animals 1/day, **Skirmish Tactics** (bonus action Disengage when hit by weapon attack), Laneshi Weapon Training (spears, tridents, javelins, light armor), and Animal Handling proficiency.

This is optimal for Paladins, Fighters, or Barbarians who want Wisdom for Perception and multiclassing while prioritizing Strength. Skirmish Tactics provides mobility without taking the Mobile feat. Amphibious enables underwater adventures. The thematic fit—amphibious warriors who bond with beasts—suits Grim Hollow's coastal Bürach Empire or marshland regions.

Laneshi Mystic subrace grants +1 Strength, +2 Wisdom (total), Duality of Spirit (psychic resistance, advantage on Intelligence/Wisdom/Charisma saves vs magic, but first use per long rest imposes DC 12 Wisdom save or be stunned until end of next turn), and Laneshi Magic (1 necromancy cantrip, 2 at level 5, Wisdom spellcasting). This is exceptional for Wisdom-primary builds like Cleric or Druid who want some Strength for melee effectiveness.

**Downcast** (fallen angels) provide +2 Wisdom universally. The Maliganti subrace adds +1 Strength—giving +2 Wisdom, +1 Strength total—along with Divine Learning (Religion proficiency, Thaumaturgy cantrip), Divine Sangromancy (when ally within 30 feet regains HP, spend Hit Die to add result to HP gained), Lingering Divinity (necrotic resistance), and Branding Smite 1/day. This perfectly fits Strength/Wisdom Paladins or Clerics thematically (fallen angels seeking redemption in Grim Hollow's godless world).

**Ogresh** grant +2 Charisma, +1 Constitution, +1 Wisdom—excellent for Paladins who need Charisma for spells/auras. Powerful Build (count as Large for carrying capacity), advantage on saves vs charmed, proficiency in 2 from Persuasion/Insight/Deception/Performance, and **A Friendly Ear** (once per short rest, 1-minute conversation forces Wisdom save or target is charmed for 1 hour and reveals information, unaware of charm attempt). This creates a social powerhouse Paladin or Cleric who can interrogate NPCs and buff party saves.

**Variant Human** remains viable for free feat at level 1. Taking Polearm Master or Brawny immediately provides combat effectiveness, and you can allocate +1 Strength, +1 Wisdom to start with 16/16.

## Level 1 feat optimization

**For Strength builds:**

**Brawny** (prerequisite Strength 13+): Count as one size larger for carrying capacity/push/drag/lift, +1d4 damage on Strength-based melee attacks, add Strength modifier to Intimidation checks vs same size or smaller. This is the best damage boost for level 1 martial characters—+1d4 (average +2.5) damage per hit dramatically increases early-game effectiveness when your bonus is only +3.

**Polearm Master**: Bonus action attack (1d4 + Strength) with polearms, reaction attack (full damage) when enemy enters your reach. This provides battlefield control (locking down 10-foot radius), extra damage output, and combines with Sentinel for complete lockdown. Optimal for Fighters and Paladins who want to protect ranged allies.

**Sentinel**: Reduce enemy speed to 0 when you hit with opportunity attack, make opportunity attacks against enemies who attack allies within 5 feet, make opportunity attacks even when enemies Disengage. Excellent "sticky tank" mechanics. Combine with Polearm Master for reaction attack when enemies enter reach (10 feet) and reduce speed to 0 (they can't advance further).

**For Wisdom builds:**

**Resilient (Constitution)**: +1 Constitution, proficiency in Constitution saves. Essential for maintaining concentration on Spirit Guardians, Bless, and other key spells. Constitution save proficiency also helps against poison, disease, and many monster abilities. If starting Cleric for proficiency, take War Caster instead.

**War Caster**: Advantage on Constitution saves for concentration, can perform somatic components with weapons/shield in hands, cast spells as opportunity attacks. Advantage on concentration saves is massive—turning a DC 10 save into ~95% success rate. Opportunity attack spell (Inflict Wounds, Guiding Bolt, Toll the Dead) provides excellent reaction damage.

**Bloodhound** (Grim Hollow feat, prerequisite Wisdom 13+): Aware of creatures within 10 feet while conscious, no disadvantage on Perception checks from invisible creatures within 10 feet, advantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Survival) checks using sound or smell. This prevents surprise attacks—critical in Grim Hollow's ambush-heavy encounters—and enhances tracking/investigation.

## Multiclassing strategies for maximum effectiveness

**Paladin 6-7 / Cleric 13-14** combines martial prowess with full healing:

Start Paladin for heavy armor, martial weapons, and Fighting Style. Level 5 Extra Attack is mandatory—delaying this weakens you significantly. Level 6 Aura of Protection (+Charisma to all saves for party within 10 feet) is transformative. Level 7 oath feature (varies by subclass). After level 7, pivot to Cleric for spell progression.

Cleric levels grant higher spell slots (more powerful Divine Smites), full healing spell list, Spirit Guardians at Cleric 5 (character level 12), and channel divinity options. Final split is Paladin 6-7 / Cleric 13-14, providing Extra Attack, Aura of Protection, oath features, 7th-level spell slots, powerful healing, and double channel divinity per rest.

**Cleric 17-18 / Paladin 2-3** prioritizes spellcasting with martial dip:

Start Cleric for Wisdom save proficiency and Constitution save proficiency. Level 5 brings Spirit Guardians. After establishing spellcasting foundation (levels 1-5), dip Paladin for Divine Smite, Fighting Style, and Lay on Hands. 2-level dip is efficient (smite + fighting style). 3-level dip adds oath spells and channel divinity.

This maintains higher spell slot progression—Cleric 18 casts 9th-level spells, while Paladin 18 / Cleric 2 caps at 5th-level slots. Trade martial features for spellcasting power. Use Divine Smite on critical hits for massive burst damage. Lay on Hands provides 10+ HP healing pool for emergencies.

**Ranger 17 / Druid 3** combines poison specialization with healing:

Take Ranger to 5 for Extra Attack, then dip Druid for healing spells (Cure Wounds, Healing Word), wild shape utility (scouting as beast), and spell variety (Entangle, Goodberry, Pass Without Trace). Return to Ranger for full conclave features. Alternatively, take Druid 6 for Circle features and better wild shape, sacrificing high-level Ranger spells.

This fills your party's healing gap while maintaining poison damage and ranged excellence. Wild shape provides scouting (crucial for Grim Hollow's deadly exploration), and Ranger spells (Hunter's Mark, Pass Without Trace, Greater Invisibility) stack with Druid spells (Healing Spirit, Summon Beast, Polymorph).

## Detailed build progression: Inquisition Cleric 1-20

**Levels 1-4 (Survival Phase):**
Start with Wisdom 16 (17 with Bloodhound feat), Strength 14, Constitution 14. Heavy armor (scale mail → splint → plate), shield, warhammer or mace. At level 1, you have AC 18-19, 1d8+2 damage with Witch Hunter's Strike adding 1d8 force (2d8+2 total vs normal enemies, 3d8+2 vs concentrating casters). Prepared spells: Bless, Shield of Faith, Cure Wounds, Healing Word, Guiding Bolt.

Level 2 Channel Divinity: Spell Shield. Before big fights, grant the Wizard or Warlock temporary HP and spell resistance. Level 3 brings 2nd-level spells: Spiritual Weapon (bonus action attack), Lesser Restoration, Hold Person. Level 4 ASI: +2 Wisdom (18 total).

**Levels 5-10 (Power Spike):**
Level 5 grants 3rd-level spells: **Spirit Guardians** (your signature spell—3d8 radiant, half speed, 15-foot radius, concentration), Revivify (resurrect dead ally), Dispel Magic (counter enemy magic). Spirit Guardians transforms you into a walking hazard—enemies starting turn in radius take 3d8 damage (Wisdom save half). Cast this, wade into melee, attack with Witch Hunter's Strike, bonus action Spiritual Weapon.

Level 6 delivers Rebuke Invoker—now enemy casters within 60 feet take automatic damage when casting (Constitution save or 1d8 per spell level + Wisdom modifier). Level 7-8 provide 4th-level spells (Banishment, Freedom of Movement) and Divine Strike (+1d8 force). Level 8 ASI: +2 Wisdom (20 total). Level 9-10 bring 5th-level spells: Greater Restoration, Mass Healing Word, Raise Dead.

**Levels 11-16 (Full Power):**
Level 11 grants 6th-level spells: Heal (70 HP instant healing), Heroes' Feast (buff entire party), Blade Barrier. Spirit Guardians remains your most-cast spell (upcast to 6th level: 6d8 damage). Begin Seraph transformation for flight and reaction abilities. Level 12 ASI: Resilient (Constitution) or War Caster.

Level 13 provides 7th-level spells including Resurrection (no material components for Cleric). Level 14 Witch Hunter's Strike scales to 2d8 (+3d8 vs concentrating casters). Progress Seraph to Level 2 for Divine Protection (reaction +4 AC to ally). Level 15-16 bring 8th-level spells and ASI.

**Levels 17-20 (Epic Tier):**
Level 17 Supernal Safeguard: multi-target Spell Shield (protect multiple allies simultaneously). 9th-level spell: Mass Heal (700 HP distributed across allies). Complete Seraph Level 4 for Aura of Merciful Blessing (prevent ally deaths). Level 19 ASI: max Constitution or take Lucky feat.

At level 20, your combat routine is: cast Spirit Guardians (6d8 to 9d8 damage to all enemies in radius), position near enemy caster to trigger Rebuke Invoker (automatic reaction damage), attack with warhammer dealing 1d8+5+3d8 force (+4d8 vs concentrating casters), bonus action Spiritual Weapon (1d8+5), use Spell Shield or Divine Protection to protect allies under fire. You're nearly unkillable with 20 Wisdom (saves +11), Plate +3 (AC 21), shield (AC 23+), Constitution proficiency, and party Aura of Protection if multiclassed Paladin.

## Scaling advantages in Grim Hollow's deadly encounters

Grim Hollow's encounter design assumes **attrition**—multiple fights without rest, limited resources, scarce healing. Cleric and Paladin builds excel because they provide sustainable healing without consuming all spell slots. Lay on Hands (Paladin) and Channel Divinity healing (Life Cleric) don't use spell slots, preserving them for offensive spells or emergency Cure Wounds.

**Spirit Guardians** creates concentration-free area denial. Enemies treat your 15-foot radius as hazardous—they must choose between approaching (taking 3d8-6d8 damage per turn, half on save) or staying back (allowing your ranged allies free rain). This dramatically reduces incoming damage by controlling enemy positioning.

**Transformation power spikes** occur at character levels matching transformation levels. Lycanthrope at character level 7 (transformation level 2) grants Hunter's Howl or Iron Pelt—major damage or survivability boost exactly when encounters become deadly. Seraph at character level 11 (transformation level 2) grants reaction abilities when enemies start dealing multi-attacks and your allies need protection.

Grim Hollow's **curse system** and **disease mechanics** demand restoration magic. Lesser/Greater Restoration are critical—only Clerics, Paladins, and Druids access these reliably. Your party's Warlocks and Wizard cannot remove curses or diseases, making this utility essential.

The **dead gods** setting means resurrection is extremely rare—NPCs can't cast Raise Dead or Resurrection for you. Only your character can bring back dead allies, creating immense narrative weight. This makes survivability-focused builds (high AC tanks) more valuable than glass cannon damage dealers.

## Skills and exploration utilities your party needs

Your Warlocks have Charisma skills (Deception, Intimidation, Persuasion) and possibly Arcana/Investigation. Your Wizard has Intelligence skills (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature). The Ranger provides Survival, Stealth, and Perception. You must cover:

**Athletics** (Strength): Grappling enemies (prevent them from reaching casters), climbing without rope, swimming with heavy armor, jumping gaps, breaking down doors. Only you have Strength 14-16+; everyone else is Strength 8-10. Athletics checks are essential for environmental challenges—climbing crumbling towers in Grim Hollow's Ostoyan ruins, swimming through flooded Bürach waterways, forcing open trapped Castinellan Inquisition cells.

**Medicine** (Wisdom): Stabilizing dying allies, identifying diseases/poisons, treating wounds between combats. With Bloodhound feat or high Wisdom, you're the party's emergency medic. Medicine checks can supplement healing spells when spell slots are scarce.

**Perception** (Wisdom): Spotting ambushes, noticing traps, detecting hidden enemies, finding secret doors. Grim Hollow punishes unaware parties with ambushes—Perception is literally life-or-death. Your Wisdom 18-20 makes you the party's "eyes," especially with Bloodhound feat granting advantage on sound/smell-based checks.

**Religion** (Intelligence): Identifying undead weaknesses, recalling divine lore, understanding cursed artifacts. Grim Hollow has extensive undead threats and religious themes (dead gods, cults, Arch Seraphs). Clerics have proficiency; others don't. Religion checks provide crucial tactical information—knowing a vampire's staking vulnerability, recognizing lich phylactery, recalling seraph hierarchies.

**Insight** (Wisdom): Reading NPC intentions, detecting lies, understanding motivations. Grim Hollow is morally complex—NPCs have hidden agendas, allies betray, obvious villains might be victims. Insight separates reliable information from manipulation, crucial for social encounters and investigation.

Take skills at character creation (Clerics choose from Insight, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion; Paladins from Athletics, Insight, Intimidation, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion). Prioritize Perception and Medicine for utility. Insight helps social encounters. Athletics is situational but irreplaceable when needed.

## Utility features outside combat

**Wild Shape** (Circle of Mutation Druid): Scouting as CR 1+ beasts with flight, burrowing, or swim speeds. Infiltrate enemy camps as rat, fly above battlefields as hawk, explore underwater as octopus. With Endless Evolution (level 10), cast spells while shaped—maintain utility even in beast form.

**Angelic Wings** (Seraph Transformation): Constant flight at walking speed (no resource cost). Reach injured allies mid-combat, escape grapples, fly over traps, scout from above. Flight bypasses most exploration challenges—pits, chasms, rivers, cliffs.

**Kindred Form** (Lycanthrope Transformation): Full animal transformation (bear, wolf, boar) for scouting. Speak with animals while transformed (with appropriate boons). Unlike druids who only know beast forms, lycanthropes can scout, communicate with animal NPCs, and infiltrate without raising suspicion.

**Spell Shield** (Inquisition Cleric Channel Divinity): Pre-buff allies before walking into obviously magical threat. Grant Warlock temporary HP + spell resistance before entering lich's lair, fighting enemy caster, or traversing magical trap hall.

**Divine Sangromancy** (Downcast racial trait): When ally regains HP within 30 feet, spend Hit Die to add result to healing. This amplifies all healing—turn 2d8+5 Cure Wounds into 2d8+5+1d8 effectively. Synergizes with Healer feat or other healing boosts.

**Pass Without Trace** (Druid/Ranger): +10 bonus to Dexterity (Stealth) for entire party for 1 hour. This allows heavy armor Cleric or Paladin to stealth effectively (Plate armor's disadvantage canceled by +10 bonus = net positive). Essential for avoiding deadly encounters or ambushing enemies.

## Party synergy: How these builds support your Warlocks and Wizard

**Protecting ranged casters:**

Position yourself between enemies and casters. Polearm Master + Sentinel: when enemy enters your 10-foot reach, make opportunity attack and reduce speed to 0 (they stop and can't advance). Protective Taunt (Bulwark Warrior): creatures you attack have disadvantage on attacks not targeting you. Aura of Rampant Sickness (Pestilence Paladin): impose disadvantage on enemy attacks via reaction.

**Enhancing ally damage:**

Divine Retribution (Seraph): reaction to grant ally extra attack (+1d8 radiant). Use on Warlock's Eldritch Blast turn (4 attacks at high level) or Wizard's high-level spell. Bless: +1d4 to attack rolls and saves for 3 allies (lasts 10 minutes, huge value). Aura of Protection (Paladin 6): +Charisma modifier to ALL saves for allies within 10 feet—improves concentration saves, saving throw spells landing, and survivability.

**Debilitating enemies:**

Aura of Rampant Sickness: impose disadvantage on enemy saves via reaction—improves Warlock's save-or-suck spells (Hex, Hold Person, Banishment). Entropic Infection: strip necrotic resistance and add +2d6 necrotic when target takes necrotic damage—pairs with Warlock's Eldritch Blast with Agonizing Blast + Hex dealing necrotic.

**Emergency healing:**

Healing Word (bonus action, 1d4+Wisdom at range): When Warlock or Wizard drops to 0 HP, bonus action heal from anywhere on battlefield. They stand up immediately, can take turn normally. Aura of Merciful Blessing (Seraph Level 4): Ally reduced to 0 HP stays at 1 HP instead—prevents death automatically once per long rest.

**Resource sustainability:**

Spell Shield grants temporary HP + spell resistance—reduces incoming spell damage on allies, preserving their HP. Spirit Guardians forces enemies to choose between approaching (taking damage) or staying back—reduces melee attacks on casters. Your presence reduces damage party takes, stretching their limited resources (no short rest healing for Wizard).

## Final recommendations for immediate play

**For players who want straightforward optimization:** Inquisition Domain Cleric (pure), Variant Human with Resilient (Constitution) or War Caster, Strength 14 / Wisdom 16+ / Constitution 14. Start with heavy armor, shield, warhammer. Max Wisdom first, then Constitution. Take Seraph transformation at levels 11-14. Your role is tank healer—position in melee, protect allies with Spell Shield, maintain Spirit Guardians, heal as needed.

**For players who want aggressive damage:** Oath of Pestilence Paladin (pure or multiclass Cleric after level 7), Variant Human with Brawny or Polearm Master, Strength 16 / Constitution 14 / Charisma 14. Focus on smite burst damage, use Channel Divinity for control, protect party with auras. Take Lycanthrope transformation at levels 7-11 for Hunter's Howl (+1d6 damage) and natural weapons.

**For players who want versatility:** Circle of Mutation Druid (pure), Variant Human with Resilient (Constitution), Wisdom 16 / Constitution 14 / Dexterity 14. Wild shape for scouting and combat, cast spells for healing/utility, use mutations to adapt to any situation. At level 10, you're casting spells in wild shape—full hybrid effectiveness. Take Seraph transformation at levels 11-14 for flight redundancy and healing reactions.

**For players who want ranged power:** Green Reaper Ranger 17 / Circle of Mutation Druid 3, Variant Human with Sharpshooter, Dexterity 16 / Wisdom 16 / Constitution 14. Focus on ranged poison damage (2d8+4d6+2d6 per hit with Hunter's Mark), use Pain Tolerance at level 15 to negate attacks. Druid dip provides healing and wild shape utility. At level 17+, you're unkillable with Pain Tolerance while dealing massive sustained damage.

Every build provides essential healing (full spells or Lay on Hands), fills skill gaps (Athletics, Perception, Medicine), enhances party effectiveness (auras, buffs, reactions), and scales gracefully through Grim Hollow's deadly 1-20 progression. The transformations provide narrative depth and mechanical power spikes at appropriate tiers without overwhelming lower levels. Your ranged casters will thrive with you protecting, healing, and supporting them through Etharis's darkest challenges.
