# Video Retention Analyzer Skill

## Description
Analyzes video scripts for retention issues based on VidIQ best practices. Identifies pacing problems, late payoffs, and predictable drop points. Provides structural recommendations.

## When to Use
- During script planning phase
- After writing first draft
- When optimizing for viewer retention
- To fix drop-off issues in existing videos

## What This Skill Does
1. Analyzes script structure and timing
2. Identifies when strongest evidence appears
3. Maps predictable drop points (1:11, 2:30, etc.)
4. Checks for retention hooks at critical moments
5. Evaluates pacing (segment length)
6. Provides payoff-first restructuring recommendations

## VidIQ Best Practices Applied
- **9-11 Minute Target Length** - VidIQ data shows this range performs best for retention (not 8-12)
- **0:45 Payoff Rule** - Strongest evidence must appear before 45 seconds (CRITICAL)
- **1:11 Drop Prevention** - Retention hook required at this timestamp (viewers drop here)
- **2-3 Minute Segments** - No section longer than 3 minutes
- **Payoff-First Structure** - Show result before explaining process
- **Modern Relevance Throughout** - Not saved for conclusion

## Analysis Report Format
```
RETENTION ANALYSIS REPORT
Video Length: 11:29 ⚠️ (Target: 9-11 minutes)

⚠️ CRITICAL ISSUES:
1. Video length: 11:29 (OVER TARGET - compress to 9-11 min range)
2. First Major Payoff: 3:27 (TOO LATE - must be at 0:45 or earlier)
3. No Retention Hook at 1:11 drop point (viewers leave here)
4. Segment 1 too long: 3 min 1 sec (should be < 3 min)

✅ STRENGTHS:
- Hook clearly states what's being tested
- Evidence is strong when it appears
- Conclusion ties to modern relevance

---

RECOMMENDED RESTRUCTURE:

CURRENT:
0:00 - Setup (what we're checking)
0:26 - Claim 1 setup
3:27 - First major evidence (1832 condemnation)

OPTIMIZED:
0:00 - Hook with strongest evidence shown immediately
      "Vance claims Christianity invented rights. But in 1832,
      the Pope condemned religious freedom as 'error.'
      Here's the document." [SHOW MIRARI VOS]
0:45 - How I verified this
1:10 - "But it gets worse..." [retention hook]
1:15 - Full Claim 1 breakdown

---

RETENTION HOOKS TO ADD:

At 1:11: "But wait—it gets worse for Vance's second claim..."
At 3:45: "And here's what nobody talks about..."
At 6:30: "This is where it connects to today..."

---

SEGMENT LENGTH OPTIMIZATION:

CURRENT:
- Intro: 26 sec ✅
- Claim 1: 3:01 ⚠️ Too long
- Claim 2: 2:42 ✅
- Claim 3: 3:59 ⚠️ Too long
- Conclusion: 1:23 ✅

RECOMMENDED:
Break Claim 1 into two 1:30 segments
Break Claim 3 into two 2:00 segments
Add transition hooks between segments
```

## Predictable Drop Points Checked
- 0:30 - If no hook, viewers leave
- 1:11 - Classic drop point for 10+ min videos
- 2:30 - Mid-video fatigue
- 7:00 - "I get the point" drop
- End - Needs strong CTA for retention

## Payoff-First Template
Provides restructured outline (9-11 minute target):
```
0:00-0:15  Hook (immediate drama)
0:15-0:45  Strongest Evidence Shown (CRITICAL PAYOFF POINT)
0:45-1:10  "Here's how I verified this"
[Retention hook at 1:10 - "But it gets worse..."]
1:10-3:30  Deep-dive on evidence (Claim 1)
3:30-6:00  Second major finding (Claim 2)
6:00-8:30  Third major finding (Claim 3)
8:30-10:00 Modern relevance + synthesis
10:00-11:00 Conclusion + Strong CTA
```
