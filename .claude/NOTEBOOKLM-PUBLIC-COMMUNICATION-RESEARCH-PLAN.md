## Complete Textbook Collection (12 Books)

### Category 1: Historical Thinking & Pedagogy (4 books)

#### 1. **Historical Thinking and Other Unnatural Acts** by Sam Wineburg
- **Publisher:** Temple University Press (2001)
- **Price:** ~$30
- **Why:** THE foundational text on how people think historically vs. how historians think
- **Key for:** Understanding cognitive barriers your audience faces
- **[Amazon Link](https://www.amazon.com/Historical-Thinking-Other-Unnatural-Acts/dp/1566398568)**

#### 2. **The Big Six Historical Thinking Concepts** by Peter Seixas & Tom Morton
- **Publisher:** Nelson Education (2013)
- **Price:** ~$35
- **Why:** Practical framework - significance, evidence, continuity/change, cause/consequence, perspective, ethics
- **Key for:** Structuring comment responses around core historical concepts
- **[Archive.org Link](https://archive.org/details/bigsixhistorical0000seix)**

#### 3. **Public History: A Textbook of Practice** (2nd Edition) by Thomas Cauvin
- **Publisher:** Routledge (2022)
- **Price:** ~$45 paperback, ~$40 Kindle
- **Why:** Comprehensive textbook on public history practice, widely used in universities
- **Key for:** Translating academic research for public audiences, practical engagement strategies
- **[Amazon Link](https://www.amazon.com/Public-History-Textbook-Thomas-Cauvin/dp/0367492539)**

#### 4. **The Public History Reader** edited by Hilda Kean & Paul Martin
- **Publisher:** Routledge (2013)
- **Price:** ~$55
- **Why:** Collection of essays exploring public history as everyday practice, not just academic discipline
- **Key for:** Multiple perspectives on engaging non-academic audiences
- **[Amazon Link](https://www.amazon.com/Public-History-Reader-Routledge-Readers/dp/041552041X)**

**Category 1 Total: ~$165**

## Cross-Disciplinary Prompt Strategy

### The Power of Single Notebook Prompts

Instead of asking each discipline separately, ask ONE question that synthesizes ALL frameworks.

**Example:**
```
"A nationalist commenter claims 'Country X stole our land in 1960.'

Based on ALL 12 sources, create a complete response strategy:

PSYCHOLOGY (Klein, Kahan): Why is this commenter resistant to correction? What identity stakes are involved?

HISTORICAL THINKING (Wineburg, Seixas): Which of the six concepts apply? How do I teach source evaluation in a comment?

PEDAGOGY (Cauvin, Kean/Martin, Wineburg, Seixas): How do effective public historians handle rival narratives? What strategies work for contested histories?

SCIENCE COMM (Besley, Potochnik, Bucchi, Lewandowsky): Which engagement model fits? What does debunking research say?

RHETORIC (Crick, Warnick): How do I balance ethos/logos/pathos? What digital rhetoric strategies apply?

Synthesize all frameworks into ONE actionable response with:
1. Comment classification
2. Engagement decision (engage deeply/briefly/not at all)
3. If engaging: exact response structure with reasoning
4. Expected outcome and metrics

Cite specific pages from relevant sources."
```

**This is impossible with multiple notebooks - you'd need to ask 5 separate questions and manually synthesize.**
