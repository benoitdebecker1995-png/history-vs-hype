# YouTube Metadata Generator - History vs Hype

Generate optimized titles, descriptions, tags, and timestamps for History vs Hype videos.

## Activation

Use when user says:
- "Generate YouTube metadata"
- "Create title and description"
- "Make tags for this video"
- "/youtube-metadata"

## STEP 1: Get Script

Ask for:
1. **Final script file** (or script text)
2. **Video runtime** (total length)
3. **Part number** (if series - e.g., "Part 2 of 2")

## STEP 2: Generate Title

### Title Formula

**Pattern 1 (Fact-Check Format):**
```
[Person/Claim]: I Fact-Checked [Number] Claims with Primary Sources
```

**Examples:**
- "JD Vance's Colonial Myths: I Fact-Checked 3 Claims with Primary Sources"
- "Christianity & Human Rights: What JD Vance Got Wrong (Part 2)"

**Pattern 2 (Myth-Busting Format):**
```
[Historical Myth]: What [Primary Sources] Actually Show
```

**Examples:**
- "Did Christianity Invent Human Rights? What Papal Documents Actually Show"
- "The Belize-Guatemala Border: What the 1859 Treaty Actually Says"

**Pattern 3 (Authority Challenge Format - HIGHEST PERFORMING):**
```
[Authority Figures] Blame [X]. [Contradiction/Truth].
```

**Examples:**
- "Politicians Blame This 1916 Map. It Was Never Used."
- "Historians Credit This Treaty. It Caused the War."

**Why this works:**
- Challenges authority (politicians, historians)
- Creates curiosity gap
- 40-50 characters (optimal)
- Implies hidden truth/myth-busting
- VidIQ data shows lower competition for myth-busting angle vs. explanatory titles

### Title Requirements
- ✅ 60-70 characters (optimal for mobile)
- ✅ Keyword in first 40 characters
- ✅ Makes claim clear (what you're debunking)
- ✅ Signals evidence ("Primary Sources," "What Documents Show," "I Fact-Checked")
- ✅ Includes person/topic name for search

### Title Options

Provide user with 3 title options:

```markdown

**Option 1 (Recommended):**
[Title with character count]

**Option 2 (Alternative):**
[Title with character count]

**Option 3 (SEO-focused):**
[Title with character count]
```

## STEP 4: Generate Tags

### Tag Strategy

**Tag Types:**

**1. Main Topic Tags (5-7 tags):**
- Person/figure name: "JD Vance", "VP Vance"
- Main topic: "Christianity and human rights", "Colonial history"
- Claim type: "fact check", "myth debunking"

**2. Specific Content Tags (8-10 tags):**
- Specific documents: "papal bulls", "Mirari Vos", "Treaty of Tripoli"
- Historical figures: "Pope Gregory XVI", "James Madison", "Martin Luther"
- Historical events: "Vatican II", "Haitian Revolution", "witch trials"

**3. Broad Discovery Tags (5-7 tags):**
- General topics: "history", "Christianity", "religious freedom", "slavery"
- Content type: "primary sources", "historical documents", "evidence-based"

**4. Channel Tags (2-3 tags):**
- "History vs Hype"
- "historical analysis"
- "academic history"

### Tag Output Format

```markdown
## TAGS (Copy-paste ready)

JD Vance, VP Vance, Christianity and human rights, fact check, papal bulls, Mirari Vos, Vatican II, James Madison, Treaty of Tripoli, religious freedom, founding fathers, slavery, witch trials, Martin Luther, John Calvin, primary sources, historical documents, colonial history, Christian history, history, evidence-based history, History vs Hype, historical analysis, myth debunking
```

**Tag Requirements:**
- ✅ 20-30 tags total
- ✅ Mix of specific and broad
- ✅ Include person/topic variations
- ✅ No single-word generic tags ("video", "YouTube")
- ✅ Comma-separated for easy copy-paste

## STEP 5: Generate Timestamps

### Timestamp Rules

**Extract from script:**
- Hook/Cold Open (always 0:00)
- Each major claim/section
- Modern relevance section
- Conclusion/CTA

**Format:**
```
0:00 - Hook: What JD Vance Claimed
0:45 - Claim 1: Did Christianity Invent Human Rights?
4:15 - Claim 2: Founders & Christianity in Government
7:00 - Claim 3: Christian Civilization Most Moral?
10:30 - Why This Matters Today
12:00 - Conclusion & Sources
```

**Timestamp Requirements:**
- ✅ Clear section titles (not vague "Introduction")
- ✅ Every 2-4 minutes minimum
- ✅ Modern stakes gets its own timestamp
- ✅ Conclusion/CTA clearly marked

```markdown

**Founding Documents:**
- Madison's Memorial and Remonstrance: https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/01-08-02-0163

**Academic Sources:**
- Samuel Moyn, "Christian Human Rights" (University of Pennsylvania Press, 2015)
- Matthew Restall, "Seven Myths of the Spanish Conquest"
- David Sehat, "The Myth of American Religious Freedom" (Oxford University Press)

**Historical Sources:**
```

**Source List Requirements:**
- ✅ Working URLs for all primary sources
- ✅ Full citations for books (author, title, publisher, year)
- ✅ Grouped by type (papal, founding, academic, etc.)
- ✅ Alphabetical within groups

## Common Patterns

### For Multi-Part Series:

**Title Pattern:**
"[Topic]: [Claim] (Part [X] of [Y])"

**Description Addition:**
"This is Part [X] of [Y]. In Part 1, I fact-checked [previous claims]. In this video, I'm examining [new claims]."

**Cross-link parts:**
"▶️ Part 1: [URL]
▶️ Part 2: [URL]"

### For Follow-Up Videos:

**Title Pattern:**
"[Topic] Follow-Up: What I Got Wrong (And What New Evidence Shows)"

**Description Addition:**
"After my video on [topic], viewers sent me [new evidence/corrections]. Here's what I found."
