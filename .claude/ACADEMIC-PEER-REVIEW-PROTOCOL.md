# Academic Peer Review Protocol for Video Scripts
**Model:** Journal peer review adapted for historical YouTube content
**Standard:** Equivalent to submitting to *Journal of Modern History* or *Holocaust and Genocide Studies*

---

## OVERVIEW: How Academic Peer Review Works

### Traditional Academic Publishing:
```
Author submits paper → Editor assigns reviewers →
Anonymous peer review → Revise & resubmit →
Acceptance (if passes review)
```

### Your Video Workflow:
```
Script draft → AI peer review (3 reviewers) →
Address comments → Revise →
Final approval → Film
```

**Goal:** Catch errors, weak arguments, and missing context BEFORE filming

---

## PEER REVIEW PROCESS (3 Reviewers)

### Reviewer 1: **Source Verification Specialist**
**Role:** Verify every source meets academic standards

**Checks:**
1. **Source Tier Verification**
   - Are primary sources authentic?
   - Are secondary sources from credible scholars?
   - Are translations from reputable publishers?

2. **Citation Accuracy**
   - Do quotes match original text word-for-word?
   - Are page numbers correct?
   - Are archival references exact?

3. **Source Sufficiency**
   - Does each major claim have 2+ independent sources?
   - Are sources from same scholarly tradition or diverse?
   - Are there better/more recent sources available?

**Output:** SOURCE VERIFICATION REPORT

---

### Reviewer 2: **Historical Methodology Critic**
**Role:** Evaluate argument quality and historical reasoning

**Checks:**
1. **Logical Fallacies**
   - Post hoc ergo propter hoc ("after, therefore because")
   - Cherry-picking evidence
   - False dichotomies
   - Strawman arguments

2. **Contextualization**
   - Are events placed in proper historical context?
   - Are anachronistic interpretations avoided?
   - Are comparisons historically appropriate?

3. **Counter-Evidence**
   - Are opposing interpretations acknowledged?
   - Is contradictory evidence addressed?
   - Are debates among historians noted?

4. **Qualification of Claims**
   - Are contested claims labeled as such?
   - Are certainty levels appropriate ("likely" vs "definitely")?
   - Are limitations of evidence acknowledged?

**Output:** METHODOLOGY REVIEW REPORT

---

### Reviewer 3: **Completeness & Balance Assessor**
**Role:** Check for omissions and bias

**Checks:**
1. **Critical Omissions**
   - What relevant facts are NOT mentioned?
   - What context is missing?
   - What counter-arguments are ignored?

2. **Framing Bias**
   - Is language neutral or loaded?
   - Are all sides presented fairly?
   - Are value judgments clearly marked as such?

3. **Scholarly Consensus**
   - Does script align with mainstream scholarship?
   - If departing from consensus, is this justified?
   - Are fringe theories properly labeled?

**Output:** COMPLETENESS REVIEW REPORT
