# Verified Claims Database

This database stores fact-checked claims for reuse across videos. Each claim is dated, sourced, and searchable.

**Purpose:**
- Avoid re-researching the same facts across multiple videos
- Maintain consistency across videos on related topics
- Quick reference during scriptwriting
- Identify research gaps when revisiting topics

**Organization:**
- Claims organized by topic cluster
- Date-stamped (research date, not event date)
- Minimum 2 sources per claim
- Flag if claim becomes outdated or contested

---

## How to Use This Database

### When Writing a Script
1. Search this file for related topic keywords
2. Check if claim already verified
3. Use existing verification or mark for new research

### When Fact-Checking
1. Add newly verified claims to appropriate section
2. Include exact sources with links
3. Date-stamp the verification

### When Revisiting a Topic
1. Review all claims in that section
2. Check if any are outdated (> 2 years old for current events)
3. Identify what new research is needed

#### Guatemala Recognition of Belize
**Verified:** 2025-11-30
**Used in:** Belize vs Guatemala (First video - correction)
**Tier:** 1 (Government sources, academic)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Claim:** Guatemala officially recognized Belize's sovereignty and independence in September 1991, ten years after Belize gained independence from Britain on September 21, 1981. Guatemala was the only country to vote against Belize's UN admission in 1981.

**Sources:**
1. MIT Cascon Case Study - https://web.mit.edu/cascon/cases/case_bel.html
2. OAS Timeline - http://www.oas.org/sap/peacefund/belizeandguatemala/timeline.html
3. Country Studies - Belize Relations with Guatemala - https://countrystudies.us/belize/85.htm

**Notes:** Critical distinction—Guatemala refused recognition 1981-1991, but has recognized Belize since September 1991. Using present tense "refuses" is incorrect post-1991.

#### Atlantic Highway to Puerto Barrios
**Verified:** 2025-11-30
**Used in:** Belize vs Guatemala comment response
**Tier:** 2 (U.S. government historical documents)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Claim:** Guatemala built the Atlantic Highway from Guatemala City to Puerto Barrios during President Jacobo Árbenz's government (1951-1954). The highway was "almost completed when Árbenz was ousted in 1954, and was completed by his successor." This was separate from the Inter-American Highway and was NOT the treaty road promised in the 1859 Wyke-Aycinena Treaty (which would have gone through Belize).

**Sources:**
1. FHWA Inter-American Highway Background - https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/infrastructure/blazer009.cfm
2. Wikipedia - Inter-American Highway - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inter-American_Highway
3. State Department Historical Documents 1934 - https://history.state.gov/historicaldocuments/frus1934v04/d584

**Notes:** Two different roads—treaty road (never built) vs. Atlantic Highway (built by Guatemala). The Atlantic Highway proves Guatemala found alternative Caribbean access, but doesn't absolve Britain of treaty violation.

### Somaliland - 1960 Union Legal Defects

#### Act of Union Never Properly Ratified
**Verified:** 2026-01-05
**Used in:** Somaliland recognition video, comment responses
**Tier:** 1 (Primary legal sources)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Claim:** Neither the Somaliland "Law of Union" nor Somalia's "Act of Union" was signed by representatives of both territories. Somalia's Act was approved "in principle" only on June 30, 1960, and never enacted into law. The decree-law of July 1, 1960 lapsed because it wasn't converted under Article 63(3) of the Constitution within five days.

**Sources:**
1. Cotran, "Legal Problems Arising out of the Formation of the Somali Republic," *International and Comparative Law Quarterly*, Vol. 12, No. 3 (July 1963), page 1012 - [JSTOR](https://www.jstor.org/stable/756301)
2. Contini, *The Somali Republic: An Experiment in Legal Integration* (Frank Cass & Co., 1969), pages 9-10

**Notes:** Contini documented these failures but concluded "a full and lawful union was formed by the will of the peoples" and that Law No. 5 (1961) cured the defect retroactively. Somaliland's position is that you cannot cure what was void ab initio.

#### Article 10 Safeguards Never Implemented
**Verified:** 2026-01-05
**Used in:** Somaliland comment response (Burji-Isaaq)
**Tier:** 1 (Primary legal documents)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Claim:** Somaliland's Law of Union Article 10 promised a Special Commission with "an equal number of former members" from both legislatures to ensure uniformity in laws, institutions, and public services. This commission was NEVER formed as promised. Law No. 5 of January 31, 1961 explicitly repealed Somaliland's Law of Union (except Article 11(4)), establishing constitutional supremacy that nullified these protections. The equal representation formula was "jettisoned."

**Sources:**
1. Act of Union 1960 Law No. 1, pages 350-351
2. Ibrahim Hashi Jama, Act of Union Commentary, pages 1-3
3. Cotran, page 1012

**Notes:** This is a stronger argument than procedural defects alone — it shows substantive promises to Somaliland were never fulfilled.

#### Historical Naming Conventions (Somali/Berberi/Zanj)
**Verified:** 2026-01-05
**Used in:** Somaliland comment response
**Tier:** 2 (Academic sources)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Claim:** The name "Somali" first appears in 15th-century Ethiopian records (Emperor Yeshaq I's hymn) referring to the Sultanate of Adal in the north. Arab geographers used "Berberi" for northern Hamitic peoples and "Zanj" for the south. There was never a centralized political authority ruling the entire Somali region before European colonization.

**Sources:**
1. Lewis, *A Modern History of Somalia*, pages 20, 25
2. Mahmoud, *Understanding the Secession of Somaliland*, page 25
3. Prunier, *The Country That Does Not Exist*, page 3

**Notes:** Pre-colonial Somali society was an "ordered anarchy" of independent clans and regional sultanates. Different colonial powers (British/Italian) created fundamentally incompatible administrative systems over 76 years.

#### 2001 UN Report Confirmed Isaaq Genocide
**Verified:** 2026-01-05
**Used in:** Somaliland comment response
**Tier:** 1 (UN investigation)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Claim:** In 2001, a UN investigation led by Senior Human Rights Advisor Chris Mburu concluded that genocide was "conceived, planned and perpetrated by the Somali Government against the Isaaq people of northern Somalia between 1987 and 1989." Estimated 50,000-200,000 civilian deaths. Hargeisa was 90% destroyed, Burao 70% destroyed. Over 200 mass graves identified. One million landmines planted in Isaaq territory.

**Sources:**
1. UN Report on Isaaq Genocide - [Isaaq Genocide Info](https://isaaqgenocide.info/learn/doc/un-report/)
2. Pulitzer Center - [In the Valley of Death](https://pulitzercenter.org/stories/valley-death-somalilands-forgotten-genocide)

### Medieval Flat Earth Myth

#### Medieval Europeans knew Earth was spherical
**Verified:** 2025-12-29
**Used in:** Medieval Flat Earth Myth (Video #19)
**Tier:** 1 (Primary sources + scholarly consensus)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Sources:**
1. Russell, Jeffrey Burton - *Inventing the Flat Earth: Columbus and Modern Historians* (Praeger, 1991), pp. 1-5
   - "With extraordinary few exceptions no educated person in the history of Western Civilization from the third century B.C. onward believed that the earth was flat."
2. Gould, Stephen Jay - "The Late Birth of a Flat Earth" in *Dinosaur in a Haystack* (1996)
   - "From the seventh century to the fourteenth, every important medieval thinker concerned about the natural world stated more or less explicitly that the world was a round globe."
3. Aquinas, Thomas - *Summa Theologica* (c. 1265-1274)
   - "Both an astronomer and a physical scientist may demonstrate the same conclusion, for instance that the earth is spherical."
4. Lindberg, David C. & Numbers, Ronald - Historians of science
   - "There was scarcely a Christian scholar of the Middle Ages who did not acknowledge [Earth's] sphericity and even know its approximate circumference."

**Notes:** The rare exceptions (Lactantius, Cosmas Indicopleustes) were marginal figures. Cosmas was unknown in Western Europe until 1706 Latin translation, English 1897.

#### Washington Irving invented the Columbus flat-earth story (1828)
**Verified:** 2025-12-29
**Used in:** Medieval Flat Earth Myth (Video #19)
**Tier:** 1 (Primary source + scholarly analysis)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Sources:**
1. Russell, Jeffrey Burton - *Inventing the Flat Earth* (1991)
   - "In 1828, Washington Irving's highly romanticized biography, *A History of the Life and Voyages of Christopher Columbus*, was published and mistaken by many for a scholarly work."
2. Irving, Washington - *A History of the Life and Voyages of Christopher Columbus* (1828)
   - Contains fictional scene of Columbus facing council at Salamanca where clerics allegedly opposed his voyage because Earth was flat

**Notes:** Irving was a novelist, not a historian. The dramatized scene never occurred. The actual dispute with Columbus was about Earth's SIZE (distance to Asia), not its shape. Columbus estimated 3,000 nautical miles; his critics correctly argued the distance was much greater.

#### The flat-earth myth was codified by Draper and White (1874, 1896)
**Verified:** 2025-12-29
**Used in:** Medieval Flat Earth Myth (Video #19)
**Tier:** 1 (Primary sources + scholarly consensus)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Sources:**
1. Draper, John William - *History of the Conflict Between Religion and Science* (1874)
   - First president of American Chemical Society; anti-Catholic agenda
   - Book went through 50 editions in 50 years
   - Created the "conflict thesis" embedding flat-earth myth
2. White, Andrew Dickson - *A History of the Warfare of Science with Theology in Christendom* (1896)
   - First president of Cornell University
   - Multi-volume, exhaustively referenced but inaccurate
   - Made flat-earth myth appear scholarly
3. Numbers, Ronald - Historian of science
   - "Historians of science have known for years that White's and Draper's accounts are more propaganda than history."

**Notes:** The "conflict thesis" (intrinsic warfare between science and religion) is considered discredited by modern historians of science. Russell found "very few mentioned the flat-earth myth before 1870, but almost all texts after 1880 featured the legend."

#### Sacrobosco's *De Sphaera Mundi* was standard astronomy textbook for 400 years
**Verified:** 2025-12-29
**Used in:** Medieval Flat Earth Myth (Video #19)
**Tier:** 1 (Documented historical fact)
**Status:** ✅ Current

**Sources:**
1. Mathematical Association of America
   - "*De Sphaera Mundi* was required reading by students in all western European universities for the next four hundred years."
2. Sphere research project data
   - Written c. 1230 by John of Sacrobosco
   - 300+ surviving manuscripts
   - 359 known printed editions after 1472
   - Estimated 350,000+ printed copies
   - Used from 13th to 17th century

**Notes:** Describes Earth as spherical, based on Ptolemy's *Almagest* and Islamic astronomy. Standard curriculum text at Paris, Oxford, Bologna, Salamanca and other medieval universities.
