# Breakout & Retention Audit Protocol

**Status:** MANDATORY for all scripts before filming
**Added:** 2025-12-29

---

## Purpose

Every full script draft must undergo this audit before being marked READY FOR FILMING. This is not optional.

The goal is to maximize breakout potential beyond the existing audience by ensuring:
- Ignition creates immediate tension
- Retention bridges pull viewers forward
- Every element advances the argument
- Opposing views are presented fairly
- The ending is repeatable

---

## Audit Checklist

### 1. IGNITION (First 45 Seconds)

**Check:** Does every line in the opening create tension or contradiction?

**Flag:** Any line that EXPLAINS instead of CONFRONTS.

**Action:** Rewrite flagged lines to increase friction without increasing length.

**Examples:**
| ❌ Informational | ✅ Adversarial |
|-----------------|----------------|
| "This is one of the most strategically contested corridors on Earth." | "This is where 12% of global trade passes—and where five powers are now competing for control." |
| "...that almost no one officially recognizes." | "...that the world pretends doesn't exist." |

---

### 2. RETENTION (Act Transitions)

**Check:** Does every act end with an explicit question or unresolved tension?

**Flag:** Soft exits like:
- "Scholars disagree."
- "It's complicated."
- "There's debate about this."
- "The answer isn't clear."

**Action:** Replace with forward-driving bridges that pull the viewer into the next act.

**Examples:**
| ❌ Soft Exit | ✅ Forward Bridge |
|-------------|------------------|
| "Whether that difference is fatal—scholars disagree." | "Whether that difference is fatal depends on who's deciding. And for twenty years, the AU has been the one deciding." |
| "But the defect itself isn't disputed." | "So how does Somalia defend a union built on paperwork that doesn't exist?" |

---

### 3. CAUSAL CLARITY

**Check:** Does every document, quote, or precedent clearly advance the argument?

**Flag:** Background sentences without explicit relevance.

**Action:** Tighten or rewrite so the causal link is explicit.

**Examples:**
| ❌ Background Only | ✅ Causal Link Explicit |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| "Britain controlled the Somaliland Protectorate from 1884." | "Britain controlled the Somaliland Protectorate from 1884—a separate colony with its own borders." |
| "Eugene Cotran was a legal scholar who studied this in the 1960s." | "Eugene Cotran was a legal scholar who examined the union documents while they were still fresh." |
| "The international community accepted that framing." | "The international community accepted that framing—because they'd never recognized the suppression as legal." |

---

### 4. STEELMAN INTEGRITY

**Check:** Are opposing arguments presented at full strength?

**Flag:** Phrasing that sounds:
- Dismissive ("But that's not really the point...")
- Rushed (quick pivot back to your argument)
- Performative (going through motions without engagement)
- Prematurely conclusive ("The question is whether they outweigh...")

**Action:** Revise tone only. Do not change content.

**Examples:**
| ❌ Sounds Dismissive | ✅ Genuinely Engaged |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| "These are legitimate arguments. The question is whether they outweigh the documentary evidence..." | "These are legitimate arguments—and they're why this case is harder than either side admits. The question is whether practice and recognition can cure a treaty defect this fundamental." |

---

### 5. ENDING RESONANCE

**Check:** Can the final takeaway be repeated verbatim by a viewer?

**Flag:** Endings that are:
- Vague ("The question remains...")
- Passive ("Whether anyone will act...")
- Ambiguous ("It's unclear what happens next...")

**Action:** Frame the unresolved issue as a CHOICE, INCENTIVE, or PRESSURE—not ambiguity.

**Examples:**
| ❌ Passive/Vague | ✅ Repeatable Takeaway |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| "The question is whether anyone will act on them." | "The only thing missing is the political will to read them." |

---

## Constraints

- DO NOT add new facts unless explicitly instructed
- DO NOT increase total runtime
- PREFER line-level edits over structural rewrites
- If a section is already optimal, LEAVE IT UNCHANGED

---

## Output Requirements

After completing the audit:

1. Update script version number (e.g., v4.0 → v5.0)
2. Add "(Breakout & Retention Audit Complete)" to version name
3. Include CHANGE LOG at end of script with:
   - Which sections were modified
   - Original vs. revised text
   - Why each change improves ignition, retention, or clarity
4. Mark script READY FOR FILMING only after all edits implemented

---

## Integration with Workflow

This audit runs AFTER:
- Script draft complete
- Spoken delivery pass (RULE 7)
- Fact-check verification

This audit runs BEFORE:
- Marking script READY FOR FILMING
- Creating B-roll checklist
- Generating YouTube metadata
