# Map Framing Checklist (BLOCKING for Territorial Videos)

**Added:** 2025-12-28
**Source:** RealLifeLore transcript analysis
**Applies to:** All territorial dispute videos (channel's 9-12x performing category)

---

## Pre-Script Quality Gate

Before writing ANY territorial dispute script, verify you can complete ALL of these:

### 1. Geographic Hook (First 30 Seconds)
- [ ] **"Easy to Overlook" or "Zoom In" opening** - Start with normal-looking map, then reveal the anomaly
- [ ] **Specific measurement** in first 60 seconds (distance, size, population, time to travel)
- [ ] **Comparison anchor** - Territory compared to familiar reference (country, state, time zone)

**Example patterns:**
- "Spain looks pretty normal when you look at it on a map, but things begin to get very strange when you zoom in..."
- "Because the main part of the country is so huge and obvious to pinpoint, there's a much smaller part further west that's a lot easier for many of us to overlook..."

### 2. Strategic Implication Stack (Before 2:00)
- [ ] **Minimum 3 strategic implications** of the territory's location
- [ ] **Military/economic/political stakes** clearly stated
- [ ] **Why it matters TODAY** - not just historical curiosity

**Must answer:**
- What can be controlled FROM this location?
- What can be blocked/denied?
- Who is threatened and why?

**Example from Kaliningrad:**
1. Only year-round ice-free Russian port on Baltic
2. Headquarters of Russian Baltic Fleet
3. Iskander-M missiles can reach Warsaw, Berlin, Copenhagen, Stockholm
4. 65km gap is only land connection for Baltic NATO states

### 3. "How Did This Happen?" Transition
- [ ] **Explicit pivot** from current situation to historical cause
- [ ] **Framed as puzzle** viewer now wants solved

**Transition patterns:**
- "But how did this little chunk of land hundreds of kilometers deep into Europe even end up as Russian in the first place?"
- "How did Kaliningrad become... well, Kaliningrad?"
- "How did this weird situation come to be?"

### 4. Historical Cause → Current Oddity Chain
- [ ] **Clear timeline** with specific dates
- [ ] **Before/after names** if territory changed hands (Königsberg → Kaliningrad)
- [ ] **Duration markers** ("for nearly 684 years before...")
- [ ] **Causal explanation** - not just "this happened" but WHY

### 5. Numbers as Authority Markers
- [ ] **Minimum 5 specific measurements** throughout script:
  - Distances (km, miles)
  - Population figures
  - Time spans (years, centuries)
  - Percentages
  - Dates

**RealLifeLore density:** ~1 specific number every 30-45 seconds

### 6. "This Picture Doesn't Make Sense" Moment
- [ ] **At least one visual contradiction** highlighted
- [ ] **Borders/territories that look wrong** on a map
- [ ] **Viewer prompted to notice the anomaly**

**Pattern:** "In fact, this entire picture doesn't make a lot of sense when you add in the correct borders. You have Spain here and Morocco over here of course, but you also have Spain here..."

## Integration with Kraut Depth Check

Map Framing Checklist handles the **geographic WHAT**.
Kraut Depth Check handles the **causal WHY**.

**Both are BLOCKING for territorial videos.**

A territorial video must pass:
1. ✅ Map Framing Checklist (above)
2. ✅ Kraut Depth Check (see workflow.md Gate 2)

**Sequence:**
1. Open with geographic hook (RealLifeLore style)
2. Stack strategic implications (stakes)
3. "How did this happen?" transition
4. Deep causal explanation (Kraut style - "consequently," "thereby," comparative analysis)
5. Return to modern implications
6. "Why accurate history matters" close

---

## Red Flags - Script NOT Ready

❌ Opens with historical background instead of current map oddity
❌ No specific measurements in first 90 seconds
❌ Strategic implications stated but not stacked (only 1-2 reasons)
❌ "How did this happen?" transition missing or weak
❌ Territory described but viewer not shown WHY it matters
❌ Numbers are vague ("hundreds of years" instead of "684 years")

---

## Quick Self-Test

Before proceeding to Kraut Depth Check, answer:

1. **Could a viewer draw this on a map from my description?** (Specific enough?)
2. **Does viewer understand stakes within 2 minutes?** (Implications stacked?)
3. **Is there a clear "that's weird" moment?** (Anomaly highlighted?)
4. **Are there at least 5 specific numbers?** (Authority established?)

If any answer is NO → revise before proceeding.
