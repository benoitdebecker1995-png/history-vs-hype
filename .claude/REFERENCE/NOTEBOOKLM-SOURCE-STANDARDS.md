## Academic Quality Standards (USER PRIORITY)

### University Press Requirements

**Acceptable Publishers:**
- Cambridge University Press
- Oxford University Press
- University of Chicago Press
- Harvard University Press
- Yale University Press
- Princeton University Press
- Stanford University Press
- Cornell University Press
- Other major university presses

**Author Credentials to Look For:**
- Endowed chairs (e.g., "Hilldale Professor," "Andreas Idreos Professor")
- Leading authorities in field (check Google Scholar citations)
- Authors of multiple academic monographs
- Editors of major journals or volumes

**Critical Editions (Primary Sources):**
- Scholarly translations with commentary
- Textual apparatus showing variants
- Introduction by recognized scholar
- Published by university press or academic society

**Price is NOT a constraint:**
- Budget $150-250 per video for source purchases
- Quality over cost always
- Use Internet Archive where possible, but buy if needed

### Source Hierarchy (Mandatory)

### Tier 1: Primary Sources (MUST HAVE 3+)

| Type | Examples | Where to Find |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Treaties/Laws** | Constitutional acts, bilateral agreements, UN resolutions | Government archives, UN Treaty Series, Wikisource |
| **Court Rulings** | ICJ judgments, arbitration awards | ICJ website, Oxford Reports on International Law |
| **Official Documents** | Census data, diplomatic cables, proclamations | National archives, FRUS, Avalon Project |
| **Contemporary Accounts** | Letters, diaries, newspaper reports from the time | Archive.org, ProQuest Historical Newspapers |

### Tier 2: Peer-Reviewed Academic (MUST HAVE 3+)

| Type | Examples | Where to Find |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Monographs** | University press books on the topic | JSTOR, Project MUSE, university library |
| **Journal Articles** | Peer-reviewed analysis | JSTOR, Google Scholar, university library |
| **Edited Volumes** | Chapters by specialists | Same as above |
| **Dissertations** | PhD theses with original research | ProQuest Dissertations, university repositories |

### Tier 3: Expert Sources (RECOMMENDED)

| Type | Examples | Where to Find |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Think Tank Reports** | Brookings, RAND, Chatham House | Direct from organizations |
| **Legal Encyclopedias** | Oxford Public International Law | University library |
| **Expert Interviews** | Oral histories, ADST transcripts | ADST.org, C-SPAN archives |

### Tier 4: Avoid or Verify Heavily

| Type | Risk | If Using |
|------|------|----------|
| Wikipedia | Good for leads, not citation | Trace to original sources |
| News articles | Journalist interpretation | Verify with primary source |
| YouTube videos | Other creators' interpretations | Only for claims extraction |
| Blogs/podcasts | Unverified | Generally avoid |

## Source Verification Checklist

Before uploading to NotebookLM:

- [ ] At least 3 primary sources identified
- [ ] At least 3 peer-reviewed academic sources identified
- [ ] Each source has full bibliographic information
- [ ] Access method confirmed (downloaded or accessible)
- [ ] Key chapters/sections identified (don't upload entire books blindly)
- [ ] No Wikipedia or news articles as primary citations
- [ ] Sources cover BOTH sides of any contested claims

## NotebookLM Upload Strategy

### What to Upload

| Upload | Don't Upload |
|--------|--------------|
| Primary source PDFs (full text) | Entire 500-page books |
| Specific chapters from books | Introductions/conclusions only |
| Journal article PDFs | Abstracts only |
| Key sections (50-100 pages max per source) | Tangentially related material |

### Naming Convention

```
[P1] Constitutional-Act-542-1992.pdf
[P2] Treaty-of-X-1923.pdf
[A1] Stein-CzechoSlovakia-Ch8-9.pdf
[A2] Kraus-Stanger-Ch3-5.pdf
```

### Maximum Sources

NotebookLM limit: 50 sources, 25M words total

**Recommended per video:** 10-20 sources, focused on topic

## NotebookLM Best Practices (UPDATED 2025-12-31)

### Key Features to Leverage

**1. Customized Audio Overviews**
- Don't just click "Generate" — click "Customize" first
- Give specific instructions to AI podcast hosts
- Example: "Focus on 8th-9th century sources, emphasize surprising quotes, exclude modern scholarship meta-discussion"
- After generation, use **Interactive Mode** to ask clarifying questions in real-time

**2. 2 Million Token Context Window (Gemini 2.0 Flash)**
- Can handle 50 sources, 25M words total
- Upload ALL sources to leverage cross-source synthesis
- Ask questions that span multiple sources: "How does Russell's analysis compare to Numbers?"
- Trace developments across centuries: "Track sphericity knowledge from Bede (725) to Aquinas (1270)"

**3. Citation Grounding**
- Every answer includes clickable citations to exact source locations
- Click citations to get page numbers for script references
- Verify quotes before marking ✅ VERIFIED in research documents
- Use citation links as proof of academic grounding

**4. Study Guides for Dense Texts**
- Click "Generate Study Guide" for complex academic books
- Use as overview before diving into specific prompts
- Helps identify key sections to focus on
- Particularly useful for 500+ page monographs

**5. Multi-Format Upload**
- **PDFs:** Academic books, journal articles, primary source scans
- **Text files:** Transcriptions of manuscripts, treaty texts
- **URLs:** Web-based primary sources, archival documents
- **Audio:** Lectures for automatic transcription
- **Google Drive:** Sync entire folders of sources

**6. Save to Notes Strategy**
- Pin important AI responses to notebook
- Combine multiple notes into comprehensive summaries
- Use notes as draft sections for VERIFIED-RESEARCH.md
- Build up research document incrementally

### Optimal Workflow

**Phase 1: Upload (Organized by theme)**
- Create separate notebooks for different source types
- Use naming convention: `[P1] Treaty-Name.pdf`, `[A1] Author-Book-Chapters.pdf`
- Upload 10-20 sources per video (focused, not exhaustive)

**Phase 2: Audio Overviews**
- Generate customized overview for each notebook
- Listen while taking notes on surprising findings
- Use Interactive Mode to clarify confusing points
- Download audio for future reference

**Phase 3: Targeted Queries**
- Use specific prompts for each claim to verify
- Click all citations to get page numbers
- Save verified quotes to notes
- Cross-reference multiple sources for contested claims

**Phase 4: Synthesis**
- Ask cross-source questions
- Generate study guides for particularly dense texts
- Create comprehensive summaries
- Export notes to VERIFIED-RESEARCH.md

**Phase 5: Verification**
- Mark claims as ✅ VERIFIED with page numbers from citations
- Only proceed to script writing when 90%+ verified
- Keep NotebookLM open during writing for fact-checking
