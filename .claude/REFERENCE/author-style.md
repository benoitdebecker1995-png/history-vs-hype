# Author Style Reference

**Purpose:** Document the channel's emerging authorial voice as distinct from borrowed creator techniques. This is a higher-level "author override" layer that reflects patterns in actual finished scripts.

**Source Analysis:** Somaliland, Belize-ICJ, Dark Ages, Fuentes, Sykes-Picot, Bir Tawil, Communism Definition scripts

*Created: 2025-12-28*

---

## CORE AUTHORIAL IDENTITY

**The Channel Voice:** Evidence-based referee between opposing camps. Not a polemicist. Not a fence-sitter. Someone who goes to the documents and reports what they actually say—then shows why both popular narratives are oversimplified.

**Key Distinction from Creators:**
- Kraut builds slowly toward revelation → You deliver payoff fast, then prove it
- RealLifeLore hooks with geography → You hook with paradox, then add geography
- Shaun pronounces moral judgment → You let documents deliver judgment

---

## AUTHOR TECHNIQUES

### Technique 1: Both-Extremes-Wrong Opening

**Pattern:**
```
"[Topic] gets thrown around constantly. But here's the problem.
One side says [Extreme A].
The other insists [Extreme B].
Modern scholarship / the documents / the evidence reveals something more complex."
```

**Examples from Scripts:**
- Somaliland: "One side says separatist region... the other says never legally part of Somalia... I think both are oversimplified."
- Dark Ages: "One side dismisses as Hollywood myth. The other insists intellectual collapse was real."
- Communism: "Either 'Marx equals Stalin' or 'real communism was never tried.'"

**Problem it Solves:** Positions you as above partisan framing. Creates space for nuanced conclusion.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Replaces Kraut's "pattern + exception" opening when topic is contested
- Compatible with Alex O'Connor's intellectual honesty

---

### Technique 2: "I Went to the Documents" Authority Marker

**Pattern:**
```
"I went to [specific source type]. The actual [treaty/report/letters]. [Archive name] records."
```

**Examples:**
- Sykes-Picot: "I went to the documents. The actual Sykes-Picot text. The Hussein-McMahon letters."
- Fuentes: "I went to the Nazi paperwork—deportation logs, statistical reports, execution records."
- Somaliland: "I'm going to show you the documents: the 1960 treaty that was never signed, the 1963 court ruling..."

**Problem it Solves:** Establishes you did original research, not just reading Wikipedia summaries.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Enhances Knowing Better's source forensics with personal authority
- More assertive than Shaun's "Let's look at what they actually said"

---

### Technique 3: Early Payoff Commitment

**Pattern:** Deliver the core answer within first 45-60 seconds. Then spend the video proving it.

**Examples:**
- Belize: Payoff at 0:25 — "ICJ will probably confirm Belize's boundary."
- Somaliland: Payoff at 0:30 — AU quote "unique and self-justified" displayed immediately
- Bir Tawil: "He wasn't crazy. This is the only place on Earth where you could even try."

**Problem it Solves:** Prevents "when does this get interesting?" dropout. Viewer stays for HOW, not IF.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- SOFTENS Kraut: Kraut builds 10+ minutes before major reveal; you reveal immediately
- Compatible with RealLifeLore's quick hook delivery

---

### Technique 4: Punchy Declarative Fragments

**Pattern:** Short, incomplete sentences after key evidence. Period-separated emphasis.

**Examples:**
- "Neither was signed by both sides. It gets worse."
- "Not 1916. Fifteen years."
- "Not 'declared.' Restored."
- "A sitting judge. 1963. No valid union."
- "That text was never created."

**Problem it Solves:** Creates spoken rhythm. Emphasizes key points. Prevents monotonous academic drone.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Similar to Shaun's "This is a lie, obviously" but without the moral pronouncement
- Sharper than Kraut's flowing sentences

---

### Technique 5: "Reading Directly From" Citation

**Pattern:**
```
"[Context]. Reading directly from [source type]:
'[Exact quote]'
[Page/date/reference]"
```

**Examples:**
- "Reading directly from the letter: 'Subject to the above modifications, Great Britain is prepared to recognise...'"
- "Reading from the judgment: 'The Court would set a precedent with disturbing implications...'"
- "Here's the exact language: 'The territories known as the Somaliland Protectorate shall become...'"

**Problem it Solves:** Distinguishes your channel from paraphrase-only creators. Proves access to primary sources.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Enhances Shaun's document-first approach with explicit "reading from"
- More formal than Alex O'Connor's conversational citations

---

### Technique 6: Document-As-Revelation

**Pattern:** Introduce documents as dramatic reveals, not background context.

**Examples:**
- Fuentes: "The Nazis kept receipts for that, too." → Korherr Report reveal
- Somaliland: "Remember the AU report from the opening?" → Returns to smoking gun
- Sykes-Picot: "November 23, 1915. The Smoking Gun." → Georges-Picot meeting minutes

**Problem it Solves:** Makes archival material feel exciting instead of dry.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Extends Shaun's "Here's what they actually wrote" with anticipation-building
- Adds dramatic pacing to Kraut's evidence chains

---

### Technique 7: Immediate Caveat Insertion

**Pattern:** Strong claim immediately followed by honest limitation.

**Examples:**
- "More than 35 states extended formal recognition. Now—one caveat. Some sources note this figure is 'reportedly 35'..."
- "The 915% increase. The lights didn't go out. Think about the logistics..." [immediately adds nuance]
- "Whether that difference is fatal to Somaliland's case—scholars disagree."

**Problem it Solves:** Builds credibility by acknowledging limits. Preempts "well actually" comments.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Aligns with Alex O'Connor's intellectual humility
- SOFTENS Kraut: Kraut states claims more absolutely; you qualify

---

### Technique 8: Rhetorical Question → Evidence Chain

**Pattern:**
```
"[Rhetorical question about apparent contradiction]?
[Specific evidence answering the question]."
```

**Examples:**
- "Why destroy a transit camp? If you're just moving people through, you don't bulldoze the evidence and plant forests."
- "If 1.2 million people were just 'relocated,' where are they?"
- "If your government cooperated in marking boundary points for 80 years—how do you argue the treaty was invalid?"

**Problem it Solves:** Guides viewer to conclusion without lecturing. Makes them reach the insight.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Similar to Shaun's "This doesn't make sense" pattern
- More evidence-driven than pure rhetorical questioning

---

### Technique 9: "That's Not My Interpretation" Disclaimer

**Pattern:** After citing damning evidence, explicitly disclaim that you're interpreting it.

**Examples:**
- "That's not my interpretation. That's the African Union's own investigation."
- "That's not speculation—that's Buringh and Van Zanden's quantitative analysis."
- "The documents show this. I'm just reading them."

**Problem it Solves:** Deflects accusations of bias. Lets evidence carry moral weight.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- LIMITS Shaun: Shaun pronounces judgment ("What a load of rubbish"); you stay neutral
- Aligns with channel positioning as evidence-based, not activist

---

### Technique 10: Timeline Precision Markers

**Pattern:** Extremely specific dates, durations, and time-sequence language.

**Examples:**
- "January 11, 1943" / "November 23, 1915" / "June 26, 1960"
- "For 80 years" / "For 31 years" / "Twenty years later"
- "Six months later—May 1916" / "Eighteen months after Sykes-Picot"

**Problem it Solves:** Establishes authority through precision. Prevents vague "at some point" accusations.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Aligns with Historia Civilis's numbered precision
- More consistent than RealLifeLore (who sometimes approximates)

---

### Technique 11: Steelman → Undercut Pattern

**Pattern:**
```
"Let me present [opposing side] fairly. The strongest version of this argument matters.
[Present their 3 best points with citations]
These are legitimate arguments. The question is whether they outweigh [your evidence]."
```

**Examples:**
- Somaliland Act 3: Full section devoted to Somalia's legal arguments before returning to treaty evidence
- Communism: Acknowledges Soviet economic growth before documenting human cost
- Dark Ages: Acknowledges literacy DID decline before showing it was already low

**Problem it Solves:** Preempts accusations of one-sidedness. Makes your case stronger by surviving the best counterargument.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Direct application of Alex O'Connor and Knowing Better techniques
- More structured than Kraut's occasional acknowledgments

---

### Technique 12: Return-to-Extremes Conclusion

**Pattern:** End by explicitly revisiting both extremes from opening and showing how evidence complicates each.

**Examples:**
- Somaliland: "That first narrative—Somaliland is just a separatist region? That undercuts it. But the second narrative—never part of Somalia? That complicates it."
- Dark Ages: Returns to the 48% who called it "dark" and shows what evidence actually shows
- Communism: "Marx identified real contradictions. AND the USSR committed atrocities. Both can be true."

**Problem it Solves:** Completes the structural arc. Viewer feels the thesis was earned, not asserted.

**Interaction with Creator Models:**
- Unique to your channel—no creator model does this as consistently
- Creates satisfying narrative closure

## WHERE AUTHOR STYLE MODIFIES CREATOR MODELS

### Softens Kraut

| Kraut Does | You Do Instead |
|------------|----------------|
| 10+ minute slow build to major reveal | Payoff in first 60 seconds |
| Absolute declarative statements | Immediate caveat insertion |
| Sweeping civilizational claims | Document-specific precision |
| 30-45 minute videos | 8-15 minutes (same density, compressed) |

**Keep from Kraut:** Causal chain language ("consequently," "thereby"), comparative analysis, modern echoes

---

### Sharpens RealLifeLore

| RealLifeLore Does | You Add |
|-------------------|---------|
| Geographic hook only | Geographic hook + document authority |
| Strategic implications | Strategic implications + legal framework |
| Approximated numbers | Exact numbers with sources |
| Entertainment focus | Evidence-based argument |

**Keep from RealLifeLore:** Map-first opening, zoom-in reveal, "How did this happen?" pivot

---

### Limits Shaun

| Shaun Does | You Avoid |
|------------|-----------|
| "What a load of rubbish honestly" | "That's not my interpretation—that's their own investigation" |
| Sardonic asides throughout | Reserved, let evidence deliver judgment |
| Moral pronouncements | Document-as-verdict |
| "They didn't care" pattern | Show the evidence, let viewer conclude |

**Keep from Shaun:** Document-first argumentation, simple-story-then-demolish structure, post-war lie forensics

---

## WHEN TO APPLY AUTHOR TECHNIQUES

| Situation | Techniques to Use |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Opening any video** | Both-Extremes-Wrong + "I went to documents" |
| **Presenting key evidence** | "Reading directly from" + Document-as-Revelation |
| **After strong claim** | Immediate Caveat Insertion |
| **Guiding to conclusion** | Rhetorical Question → Evidence Chain |
| **Presenting opponent's view** | Steelman → Undercut |
| **Citing damning evidence** | "That's not my interpretation" |
| **Closing any video** | Return-to-Extremes |

## AUTHOR VOICE QUICK REFERENCE

**Tone:** Knowledgeable referee, not partisan advocate
**Pace:** Fast payoff, dense evidence, compressed runtime
**Authority:** "I went to the documents" personal research framing
**Honesty:** Immediate caveats, steelmanning, uncertainty acknowledgment
**Structure:** Both extremes wrong → evidence → return to extremes
**Precision:** Exact dates, page numbers, archive references
**Judgment:** Let documents deliver verdict; avoid personal moral pronouncements
