## Cross-Video Synthesis

### Patterns in 3+ Videos

**1. Exhaustive/Definitive Scope Signals (4/5 videos)**
- "Entire History," "Full History," "Biggest"
- Implication: Viewers value one-stop resources; exhaustive framing reduces perceived competition

**2. Causal/Structural Framing (4/5 videos)**
- Promise to explain WHY, not just WHAT
- "Why... Guarantee," "How did this originate"
- Implication: Mechanism revelation > event narration for educational content

**3. Geographic/Territorial Center (5/5 videos)**
- All center on places, borders, or territorial entities
- Implication: Geography provides natural scope constraint; enables map-based visuals

**4. Low Specificity in Titles (4/5 videos)**
- Use categories ("Middle East," "Africa") not proper nouns
- Implication: Category labels capture broader search volume

### Differences Suggesting Multiple Valid Paths

| Dimension | Variation Observed |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **News dependency** | Pure reactive (Benin) to pure evergreen (Cherokee) |
| **Format length** | Short explainer to multi-hour documentary |
| **Promise type** | Exhaustive, Explanatory, Revelatory, Clarifying all present |

**Implication:** No single formula; match approach to topic nature

### Non-Repeatable Patterns

1. **Channel authority** - RealLifeLore's millions of subscribers provide algorithmic boost
2. **News timing** - Benin's December 2025 window cannot be reproduced
3. **Topic saturation** - Western Sahara undersaturation is temporal advantage
