# User Voice Profile - History vs Hype

**Created:** 2026-01-16
**Source:** Unscripted channel intro + user input
**Status:** MANDATORY for all scripts

---

## CORE VOICE IDENTITY

**Who you are:** A passionate history tutor who's "sick and tired of seeing misinformation" and wants to show people "the historical method" and "cut through all of the ideological bullshit."

**Your mission (in your words):**
> "I actually show the primary sources and try to bring the academic historical method to the wider audience to show people why history matters and why recognizing certain historical patterns are important."

## NATURAL SPEECH PATTERNS (From Unscripted Video)

### Fillers You Actually Use
- "Um" (sentence starts, thinking)
- "Uh" (mid-sentence pauses)
- "like" (casual emphasis)
- "basically" (summarizing)
- "I guess" (hedging)
- "I think" (opinions)

### Connectors You Use
- "So" (conclusions, transitions)
- "And" (continuing thoughts)
- "But" (contrasts)
- "because" (explanations)

### Your Signature Phrases
From your unscripted speech:
- "I'm sick and tired of seeing..."
- "it pains me often to see..."
- "claims that rot your brain"
- "very little basis in historical reality"
- "known, at least in the historical community, to be false"
- "just because somebody decided to make like a viral video"
- "send them my way"
- "it's always good to get a second opinion"
- "I hope you stick around"

## PHRASES TO USE

### ✅ Approved (You said YES or use naturally)

**Emphasis:**
- "This is crucial"
- "Now, I want to be clear"
- "The reality is..."
- "Think about that for a second" (depends on context)

**Surprising facts:**
- "This might surprise you but..."

**Transitions:**
- "On top of that"
- "Furthermore"
- "That being said" / "That having said"

**Showing documents:**
- "As you can see"
- "In this document..."

**Acknowledging other side:**
- "We have to keep in mind"
- "If we are being fair"
- "From their point of view"

**Making points land:**
- "To summarize"
- "In conclusion"
- "If you take away only one thing..."

**Polite CTAs:**
- "please subscribe"
- "please look into these things"
- "please send me constructive feedback"

---

## PHRASES TO AVOID

### ❌ Never Use (You said NO)

- "Let me show you something"
- "Here's where it gets interesting"
- "And that's the key insight"
- "Buckle up"
- "Stay with me here"
- "Here's the thing"

### ❌ Also Avoid (Based on channel DNA)

- "You won't believe..."
- "SHOCKING"
- "drop your thoughts below"
- "smash that subscribe"
- Any flowery/poetic language
- Over-the-top enthusiasm

## SENTENCE STRUCTURE

### How You Actually Talk

**Longer, flowing sentences with multiple clauses:**
> "I'm sick and tired of seeing misinformation, um especially historical misinformation, uh very often generated by AI that have these like crazy fantastical claims that rot your brain."

**Self-corrections are natural:**
> "the the the first version of that teacher"
> "to to to learn"

**Direct statements:**
> "They don't post their sources or they misinterpreted sources."
> "Some of them are just not true."

**First-person ownership:**
> "I'm passionate about history and the historical process"
> "It pains me often to see people propagate some claims"

### Script Translation

**DON'T write:**
> "Let me show you what the documents reveal about this fascinating historical controversy."

**DO write:**
> "The documents show something different. And it's not what most people think."

## FORMALITY LEVEL

**Your natural register:** Educated casual

- Academic enough to cite sources properly
- Casual enough to say "rot your brain" and "ideological bullshit"
- Direct enough to say "just not true"
- Humble enough to say "I'm not a history teacher although I do tutor it"

**Script calibration:**
- Slightly more polished than unscripted speech
- Keep the directness
- Keep first-person ownership ("I found," "I think," "it pains me")
- Don't over-formalize

## DOCUMENT PRESENTATION

### Your Philosophy (in your words)
> "I go a step further and show you where these stories come from and what sources historians use to come to certain narratives."

### How to Present Sources

**Setup:**
- "As you can see in this document..."
- "Here's what the [treaty/letter/record] actually says:"

**Attribution:**
- Name the historian + their work
- Don't just cite—explain why this source matters

**Translation:**
- After complex quotes: explain in plain language
- "In simpler terms..."
- "Translation:"

---

## MISSION STATEMENT FOR SCRIPTS

Every script should reflect:

1. **Source transparency** — Show where claims come from
2. **Method visibility** — Demonstrate how historians work
3. **Pattern recognition** — Connect past to present
4. **BS cutting** — No ideology, just evidence
5. **Accessibility** — Academic rigor in conversational delivery

---

## QUICK REFERENCE: VOICE CHECKLIST

Before finalizing any script, verify:

- [ ] Would I actually say this out loud?
- [ ] Does it sound like me explaining to a friend?
- [ ] No forbidden phrases ("let me show you," "here's the thing," etc.)
- [ ] Uses my approved transitions ("on top of that," "if we are being fair")
- [ ] First-person ownership where appropriate
- [ ] Polite CTAs ("please subscribe")
- [ ] Documents introduced with "as you can see" or similar
- [ ] Complex terms explained immediately
- [ ] No fake enthusiasm or sensationalism
- [ ] Directness preserved ("just not true," "the reality is")
