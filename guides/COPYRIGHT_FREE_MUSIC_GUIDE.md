# Copyright-Free Music Guide for History vs Hype

**Purpose:** Find background music that won't trigger copyright claims on YouTube

**Your needs:** Subtle, professional background tracks for educational documentary content

---

## ⚠️ Important: What "Copyright-Free" Means

### Three Categories:

**1. Public Domain**
- Copyright has expired (usually 70+ years old)
- No restrictions, completely free
- No attribution required

**2. Creative Commons (CC)**
- Free to use with conditions
- **READ THE LICENSE** - some require attribution, some don't allow commercial use
- Most common: CC BY (attribution required)

**3. Royalty-Free / Licensed**
- You pay once, use forever
- No ongoing royalties
- Still copyrighted, but you have permission

**❌ AVOID:**
- "No Copyright Sounds" on random YouTube channels (often unreliable)
- Music from Spotify/Apple Music (always copyrighted)
- Popular songs (will get flagged immediately)

**Why it's best for beginners:**

- ✅ Searchable by mood (perfect for documentaries)

## 🚫 What to AVOID for Educational Content

**❌ Don't use:**
- Upbeat pop music (wrong tone)
- Music with vocals (competes with your voice)
- Overly dramatic orchestral (feels manipulative)
- Electronic/EDM (wrong genre for history)
- Anything louder than -18dB during talking
- Music that changes tempo/volume dramatically

**Your channel needs:**
- Subtle and professional
- Lets your voice be the focus
- Reinforces serious, academic tone
- Doesn't distract from evidence

## 📋 Step-by-Step: Adding Music to Your Video

### Phase 1: Planning (During Script)
- [ ] Mark where music should start/stop
- [ ] Note desired mood for each section
- [ ] Decide: Music throughout, or only in transitions?

**Recommendation for your channel:**
- Light ambient throughout (very quiet, -22dB)
- No music during key evidence moments (papal documents, quotes)
- Music OK during your talking head analysis

### Phase 2: Selecting Tracks
- [ ] Search based on mood needed
- [ ] Download 3-5 options per section
- [ ] Check license requirements
- [ ] Test with a 30-second section of your video

### Phase 3: Editing
- [ ] Import music into DaVinci Resolve
- [ ] Place on separate audio track
- [ ] Set volume: -22dB for background, -18dB for more prominent sections
- [ ] Fade in/out smoothly (2-second fades)
- [ ] Cut music during critical moments (primary source reveals)

### Phase 4: Mixing
- [ ] Balance voice vs. music
- [ ] Remove music during evidence presentation if it distracts
- [ ] Test: Can you hear every word clearly?
- [ ] Export and listen on different devices (phone, laptop, TV)

### Phase 5: Attribution (If Required)
Add to video description:
```
MUSIC:
[Track 1 Name] by [Artist]
Source: [Website]
License: [License type]
[License URL if required]

[Track 2 Name] by [Artist]
...
```

## 🎚️ Audio Mixing Guidelines

### Volume Levels:
- **Your voice:** -6dB to -3dB (loudest)
- **Background music during talking:** -22dB to -18dB (very subtle)
- **Music during intro/outro (no talking):** -12dB to -9dB
- **Sound effects (if any):** -15dB

### The Golden Rule:
**If the music makes it harder to understand your words, it's too loud.**

### Testing:
- Play video on laptop speakers (not headphones)
- Can you hear every word clearly?
- Does music add atmosphere without distraction?
- If you answered no to either: Lower the music 3dB

## 🆘 If You Get a Copyright Claim

### Types of Claims:

**1. Content ID Claim (Yellow icon)**
- Not a strike, but monetization goes to copyright holder
- **Fix:** Replace the music, dispute if you have license, or accept it

**2. Copyright Strike (Red icon)**
- Serious - affects your channel standing
- **Fix:** Remove video immediately, replace music, re-upload

### How to Avoid Claims:

- Use only sources listed in this guide
- Keep licenses/attribution documented
- Test as "Unlisted" first
- When in doubt, use YouTube Audio Library (100% safe)

## 💡 Pro Tips for Documentary-Style Music

### 1. Less is More
- Don't feel obligated to have music throughout
- Silence can be powerful during key evidence moments
- Music should enhance, not fill space

### 2. Match Your Brand
- Your channel is academic, serious, evidence-based
- Music should reflect: professional, understated, authoritative
- Avoid anything that feels trendy or YouTube-cliché

### 3. Consistency
- Use similar style across videos
- Creates recognizable brand sound
- Consider a signature intro/outro track

### 4. Volume is Critical
- Too loud: distracts from content
- Too quiet: pointless
- Sweet spot: You notice it's gone when you mute it, but don't consciously hear it while watching

### 5. Test Before Publishing
- Watch video on phone speakers
- Can you understand every word?
- Does music feel appropriate for the subject?
- Would you want this playing during a university lecture?
