### ⚠️ CLAIM 4: Guatemala built highway to Puerto Barrios in 1930s-1960s
**Commenter says:** "The main highway connecting Guatemala City to the Caribbean coast (Puerto Barrios) was built by Guatemala as part of the Inter-American Highway system. Construction began in the 1930s and was largely completed by the 1950s, with the final sections finished by the 1960s."

**What video said:** "Years went by and Britain built nothing." (line 171) + "No road was ever built, not even with Guatemala's own money." (lines 199-204)

**PARTIALLY CORRECT - NEEDS CLARIFICATION:**

**Two different roads:**
1. **Treaty road (1859)**: From Guatemala City through Belize to Caribbean coast near Belize - NEVER BUILT (video is correct)
2. **Atlantic Highway**: Guatemala City to Puerto Barrios - BUILT BY GUATEMALA

**Atlantic Highway timeline:**
- Planned and constructed during President Jacobo Árbenz government (1951-1954)
- Purpose: Compete with United Fruit Company's transportation monopoly
- "Almost completed when Árbenz was ousted in 1954, and was completed by his successor"
- This was NOT the "Inter-American Highway" (which ran Mexico-Guatemala-El Salvador through interior)
- This was NOT built in "1930s" as commenter states - it was 1950s

**Inter-American Highway timeline:**
- Program initiated 1932, completed 1967
- Ran through Guatemala's interior (Guatemala City), not to Puerto Barrios/Caribbean
- President Ubico interested in 1934, construction began late 1930s

**Clarification for response:**
- Video correctly states the TREATY ROAD (through Belize) was never built
- Guatemala DID build alternative Atlantic Highway (1951-1954) to Puerto Barrios
- This proves Guatemala found alternative access to Caribbean (not through Belize)
- But doesn't contradict that Britain violated the specific 1859 treaty obligation
