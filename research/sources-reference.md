# Academic Sources & References

## Armenia-Azerbaijan / Nagorno-Karabakh Conflict

### Essential Comprehensive Works

**Thomas de Waal**
- *"Black Garden: Armenia and Azerbaijan Through Peace and War"* (2003, updated 2013)
- Standard reference on the conflict
- 200+ sources including Soviet archives, interviews, historical documents
- Extensive bibliography for tracing primary sources

**George Bournoutian**
- *"A Concise History of the Armenian People"* (2002)
- Multiple journal articles on Armenian demographics
- Cites 1823 Russian Imperial Survey
- Publications on Armenian demographics under imperial rule

**Victor Schnirelmann**
- *"Who Gets the Past? Competition for Ancestors Among Non-Russian Intellectuals in Russia"* (1996)
- Documents Bunyadov fraud allegations
- Analysis of historical narrative construction in former Soviet space
- Documentation of academic manipulation techniques

**Laurence Broers**
- *"Armenia and Azerbaijan: Anatomy of a Rivalry"* (2019)
- Recent academic synthesis with updated bibliography
- Covers historiography debates and competing narratives
- Current scholarly consensus

**Ronald Grigor Suny**
- *"Looking Toward Ararat: Armenia in Modern History"* (1993)
- Comprehensive historical analysis with extensive sources
- Covers demographic changes and imperial policies
- Strong bibliography on Armenian population movements

**Audrey L. Altstadt**
- *"The Azerbaijani Turks"* (1992)
- Academic analysis from Azerbaijani perspective
- Contains Soviet archival sources and demographic data
- Useful for cross-referencing claims

## Source Quality Indicators

### Reliable Indicators
✓ Published by university press
✓ Peer-reviewed journal
✓ Author has relevant PhD
✓ Extensive citations/bibliography
✓ Published by respected institution
✓ Cited extensively by other scholars
✓ Recent publication (or classic work still referenced)

### Warning Signs
⚠ Self-published
⚠ No citations or bibliography
⚠ Published by advocacy organization
⚠ Author's only work on topic
⚠ Contradicts scholarly consensus without strong evidence
⚠ Extremely political language
⚠ Cherry-picks evidence

## Notes on Using These Sources

1. **Always verify citations** - Secondary sources sometimes misrepresent primaries
2. **Check publication date** - Recent scholarship may supersede older work
3. **Understand author perspective** - All historians have viewpoints, acknowledge it
4. **Multiple sources for contested claims** - Don't rely on single scholar for controversial points
5. **Primary sources are gold** - Try to access original documents when possible
6. **Context matters** - Historical documents must be understood in their time
