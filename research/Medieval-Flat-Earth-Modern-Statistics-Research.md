## Claim 1: "Trust in science dropped 15 percent between 2020-2023"

### ✅ VERIFIED (with minor adjustment)

**Pew Research Center (November 2023):**
- "Percentage of American adults who say science has a 'mostly positive' effect on society fell to 57%, down 8 percentage points since November 2021 and **16 percentage points since just before the pandemic**"
- The drop is actually **16 percentage points** from pre-pandemic to 2023, not 15%
- Script claim is essentially accurate

**Source:** [Americans' Trust in Scientists and Views of Science Decline in 2023 | Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/science/2023/11/14/americans-trust-in-scientists-positive-views-of-science-continue-to-decline/)

## Claim 3: Research shows a "gateway effect" for conspiracy beliefs

### ⚠️ PARTIALLY VERIFIED (concept supported, specific term not standard)

**Research Found:**

The concept is supported by academic research, though "gateway effect" is not the standard term in the literature. Research describes:

**"Gateway for radicalization":**
> "Research indicates there is an overlap between conspiracy theories, with some acting as gateways for radicalization - QAnon and the great replacement theory, for example, have proved deadly more than once. In the context of flat earth theories, alignment with certain political rhetoric can serve as a gateway for the community to adopt similar ideologies across political, scientific, and social realms, further polarizing public discourse."

**Studies:**
1. **Topics in Cognitive Science (2024):** Framework studying how conspiracy communities promote "contagious sense of understanding"
2. **Flat Earth Society Forum Analysis (2024):** 2.5 million comments showing anti-institutional, anti-climate sentiment
3. **YouTube Susceptibility Study:** How flat earth videos spread through recommendation algorithms

**Better phrasing for script:**
- "Research shows conspiracy theories can act as gateways for radicalization"
- "Studies show belief in one conspiracy theory makes it easier to believe others"

**Sources:**
- [Conspiracy Theory as Individual and Group Behavior - Topics in Cognitive Science 2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/tops.12662)
- [Online Conspiracy Theories and Climate Change - ResearchGate 2024](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/391940966_Online_Conspiracy_Theories_and_Perceptions_of_Institutional_Power_and_Climate_Change_A_Study_of_the_Flat_Earth_Society's_Forum)
